# Stump / Stumpf — Thurgau parishes to Tallinn and the Baltic German Erbe line

**Europe-only** essay for [Marc Francois Stump](../people/marc-francois-stump.md) and [Olga Caroline Erbe](../people/olga-caroline-erbe.md): Swiss Protestant forebears in canton Thurgau, Marc's career as a French teacher in Imperial Russian Tallinn, marriage into a Baltic German household, and children baptised in the Tallinn Dome congregation. **Structured tree:** [family-tree.json](../family-tree.json).

**Geographic note:** casual family memory sometimes says "Emmental" for Swiss German lines. The strict-ancestor GEDCOM anchors the Stump patriline in **Sulgen / Erlen / Buchackern, Thurgau** — not the Emmental (canton Bern). If a parish book ever ties this branch to Bern, the narrative can be retitled; until then, Thurgau is the documented canton.

**Iran / dentistry** — son [Étienne Stump](../people/etienne-stump.md) and the Persia hub remain in [topics/persia.md](../topics/persia.md) and [saginian-burgess-bottin-stump.md](saginian-burgess-bottin-stump.md) §4; this file stops at the Baltic household and Marc's death.

## 2. From Thurgau to Tallinn — Marc François Stump

Marc François Stump was born in **1834** — but where, exactly, depends on which record you trust. The Swiss extract gives **Erlen, Thurgau, 10 March 1834** (*Markus Franz* Stump, per FamilySearch). The Tallinn Dome parish digest, quoted in the [Estonian Biographical Center report (2005)](../sources/estonian-biographical-center-stump-report-2005.md), gives **16 February 1834 at Yverdon**, with a possible 10 March baptism note — and an explicit warning about Gregorian versus Julian dating between Switzerland and the Russian Empire.

What is certain is where he ended up. By **March 1865** Marc was sitting his examinations at the **University of Tartu** — French language and literature, with prescribed minors — earning a certificate for gymnasium teaching. From **1868** he was ***Oberlehrer*** of French at the **Tallinn government gymnasium**, the senior teaching post in the city's main secondary school. A Swiss Protestant from Thurgau, teaching French to Baltic German and Russian students in the capital of the Estonian governorate.

He built a life in Tallinn: property on **Tatari** and **Mere 123**, tenants (a bakery, a pharmacist), litigation with a neighbour named Mühlberg in 1900. Marc was **Reformed**; his wife was **Lutheran** — a confessional difference that the parish records duly noted.

## 3. Olga Caroline Erbe — a Baltic German marriage

[Olga Caroline Erbe](../people/olga-caroline-erbe.md) was born **11 November 1844** in **St. Petersburg** and died **15 October 1894** in **Tallinn**. Through her the Stump household connected to two established Baltic German families.

Her father [Hermann Eberhard Erbe](../people/hermann-eberhard-erbe.md) was born in **Rakvere**, married in Tallinn in 1839, and held the post of **Domschloßvogt** — a cathedral-chapter administrative role (the primary appointment file has not been located). He died in **Riga** in 1872. Her mother [Emilie Ida Eylandt](../people/emilie-ida-eylandt.md) was born and died in Tallinn; the Eylandt–Busse parents married in **Viljandi** in 1799.

Marc and Olga married in **1868** in Tallinn (union F70 in the tree; EBC parish summary). His Reformed confession beside her Lutheran status is recorded in the same register entry. The marriage joined a Swiss immigrant teacher to the Baltic German civic world — a pattern of integration through professional standing that their son Étienne would repeat, in an entirely different country, a generation later.

## 4. The Tallinn household and the road to Tehran

The EBC report lists five children baptised in the Tallinn Dome congregation: **[Jean Jacque](../people/jean-jacque-stump.md)** (died 1874 as an infant), **[Arthur Roger](../people/arthur-roger-stump.md)** (later Moscow and Berlin), **[Marguerite Emilie](../people/marguerite-emilie-stump.md)** (married [von Frey](../people/cuno-von-frey.md)), **[Antoinette](../people/antoinette-stump.md)** (died 1881), and **[Étienne Stump](../people/etienne-stump.md)** — born **7 March 1880** in Tallinn, per the parish digest.

By 1910, a Tallinn obituary for Étienne's mother Louise already noted "Dr Etienne Stump and wife in Teheran." The son of a Swiss French teacher and a Baltic German Erbe had somehow ended up practising dentistry in Qajar Iran — the first link in what would become the Persia line of the family tree.

Sibling IDs confirmed: [Jean Jacque](../people/jean-jacque-stump.md) (`@I398@`), [Arthur Roger](../people/arthur-roger-stump.md) (`@I399@`), [Marguerite Emilie](../people/marguerite-emilie-stump.md) (`@I400@`), [Antoinette](../people/antoinette-stump.md) (`@I401@`).
