# Certi Ricordi — Certain Memories

A family history scrapbook compiled by [Fulvia Zerauschek Lewis](../people/fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md), ~1990s. The visual companion to her [1996 memoir](../sources/corpus/famhist-nonna-memoir-1996/extracted.pdf.md) — where the memoir was text addressed to Peter, this album is images and handwritten Italian addressed to everyone who came after.

**Corpus:** [certi-ricordi-zerauschek-addobbati](../sources/corpus/certi-ricordi-zerauschek-addobbati/reference.md)

---

> *Zerauschek - Addobbati*
> *• Certi Ricordi*

Zerauschek - Addobbati — Certain Memories. Title page with a photograph of a swimmer in the Adriatic.

## Epigrafe — Epigraph

> *Diceva sempre Cesare:*
> *"Meglio essere il primo al proprio paese... che il secondo a Roma!"*

Caesar always said: "Better to be first in your own country... than second in Rome!"

*The thesis statement of the whole album: the Zerauschek-Addobbati were leading citizens of Zara, their own small world.*

---

## Dedica — Dedication

> *Quindi:*
> *... per chi non lo sapesse ......*

Therefore: ...for those who don't know......

*Fulvia addressing the next generation directly — this album exists because the grandchildren don't know the family history.*

## Famiglia Addobbati — Group portrait and family anecdotes

Multiple photographs and handwritten notes. Family group photo labelled: Vittoria – Pietro – Ester – Noemi – Elsa – Irma / Nonna Ottilia – Bice – Nonno Pietro. Portrait of Zio Casimiro Boare (brother of Nonna Ottilia) and photo of Nonna Ottilia.

> *Zio Pierello quasi finiva alla Corte Marziale Austriaca per aver alla Mensa Ufficiali schiaffeggiato un suo Superiore. Se non fosse per lo zio Field maresciallo....*
>
> *Pietro Addobbati, secondogenito destinato al Sacerdozio. Educato al seminario Tommaseo fu giudicato non idoneo per aver messo un topo morto nel piatto del Vescovo ad un pranzo in suo onore. W el Topo!!*
>
> *Zio Casimiro Boare fratello della nonna Ottilia*
>
> *Famoso insegnante di Latino e Greco e così pigro che tenendo le mani in tasca, girava le pagine col naso. Le sue lezioni erano appassionanti. Forse doveva insegnare per passione perché era proprietario terriero. (Non ò mai sentito altre persone che come lui e la zia Carmela a pranzo e a cena avevano sei "mene.") La nonna Ottilia prendeva in prestito da leci soldi per arrivare alla fine del mese.*

Uncle Pierello nearly ended up at the Austrian Court Martial for slapping a superior officer at the Officers' Mess. If it weren't for uncle the Field Marshal...

[Pietro Addobbati](../people/pietro-pio-addobbati.md), second-born, destined for the priesthood. Educated at the Tommaseo seminary, he was judged unfit for having placed a dead mouse on the Bishop's plate at a lunch in his honour. Long live the Mouse!!

Uncle Casimiro Boare, brother of Nonna Ottilia. Famous teacher of Latin and Greek, and so lazy that keeping his hands in his pockets, he turned the pages with his nose. His lessons were riveting. Perhaps he taught for passion because he was a landowner. (I never heard of anyone else who, like him and Aunt Carmela, had six courses at lunch and dinner.) Nonna Ottilia used to borrow money from him to make it to the end of the month.

>

>

## Caffè Centrale — Chess, business, and "Tono Zerauschek"

Postcard of Via Larga (Calle Larga) and continuation of the printed text about the Caffè Centrale. Handwritten annotation by Fulvia.

> *Il Caffè Centrale: come era prima che il Nonno lo comprasse esaltato la possibilità di un restauro lo ricostruisce in uno stile moderno (!!!!) Anche questo rapporto molto ai bombardamenti anche se l'interno forse era bene come il resto*

The Caffè Centrale: as it was before Nonno bought it — excited by the possibility of a restoration, he rebuilt it in a modern style (!!!!) This too suffered greatly from the bombings, even if the interior was perhaps fine like the rest.

Among the group: "Tono Zerauschek, Enrico e Bepi Predolin, Crivellari, Pipi Tolja, Rudi Cettinee e altri."*

## Le fabbriche del Nonno — The cigarette factory and Ausonia

Handwritten notes about [Antonio Zerauschek](../people/antonio-zerauschek.md)'s factories and an Ausonia business letter.

> *La prima delle fabbriche del Nonno*
>
> *Il Nonno, dopo una litigata con i soci durante la quale à quasi spaccato una sedia in testa al Bussi (con il quale peraltro à rimasto amico) à lasciato la fabbrica di Sigarette e è partito con la Nonna. The first of Nonno's factories.

Nonno, after a quarrel with his partners during which he nearly cracked a chair over Bussi's head (with whom, incidentally, he remained friends), left the cigarette factory and set off with Nonna. > *Zara, 30 maggio 1939.—*
> *Accusiamo ricevuta della preg/ v/ 25 corr/, giuntaci il 29 dalla quale abbiamo ritirato gli ordini acclusi che avranno ogni cura nell'espletamento.— Vi confermiamo che l'ordine Marangio sarà spedito secondo le preg/ v/.—*
> *... F.C.C. AUSONIA — ZARA*
> *A. Zerauschek*

> *Quello che scriveva le lettere in Inglese per i contatti con l'America era il Signor Pollack, ebreo, che à anche insegnato al Guido ad andare a cavallo*

The one who wrote the letters in English for the contacts with America was Mr. Pollack, a Jew, who also taught Guido to ride a horse.

## Ausonia — Profumo de Dalmazia

Printed page (p. 243) from *Profumo de Dalmazia* by Gioia Colussi, describing the Fabbrica di Cioccolato Ausonia. Two period advertisements: the Ausonia logo with a child's silhouette, and a display ad promoting Zaratini liqueurs and Marasca cherry liqueur.

> *"No voio café negro, no ma cicolata" recitava una canzonetta spalatina e, a Zara, per la gioia de tuti i fioi a cui ghe piase la ciocolata e de tuti i bonculovich sedotti dal suo delizioso sapore e caldo profumo, il padre del bimbo raffigurato nella carta intestata della Ditta aprì La Fabbrica di Cioccolato e Cacao Ausonia. "I don't want black coffee, I want chocolate" went a Spalatina song, and in Zara, for the joy of all the children who like chocolate and all the bonculovich seduced by its delicious taste and warm aroma, the father of the child depicted on the company letterhead opened the Fabbrica di Cioccolato e Cacao Ausonia. Founded in 1928 by Antonio Zerauschek in Zara, with offices and factory at Via Fabbriche No. 2.

*Fulvia's note: "Trade mark → Zio Guido aged 2 o 3" — the child in the Ausonia logo was Uncle Guido as a toddler.*

## Cripto-Telegrammi — Adventures on the road to Lusaka

Three handwritten notes about the chaos of visiting Africa. Some guests send "crypto-telegrams" that embarrass the whole colony and have to travel Salisbury–Lusaka–Umtali–Beira–Lusaka. On the normally deserted roads they meet dozens of cars at 200 km/h full of excited Portuguese men going to a bullfight, who spray stones and break the windshield. In Beira there are no Alfa Romeo parts — they build a plastic windshield and wait eighteen months for the real glass from Italy.

> *Certi invece, mandano cripto-Telegrammi che "imbarazzano" l'intiera Colonia e devono fare: Salisbury - Lusaka - Lusaka - Umtali - Beira - Lusaka*
>
> *In più, sulle strade normalmente deserte, anno incontro a dozzine di auto che viaggiano a 200 Km l'ore, piene di uomini portoghesi, eccitatissimi, che vanno a una corrida. Ci schizzano con sassi che ci rompe il parabrezza.*
>
> *Passiamo tre giorni a Beira. Non ci sono Alfa Romeo. Alla fine ci ne costruiscono uno di plastica e ci vorranno 18 mesi prima dell'arrivo dall'Italia di quello di vetro vero. Intanto lui, visto che la Nico non lo tien più, el nua nel Oceano (Freddo)*

Some, instead, send crypto-telegrams that "embarrass" the whole colony and have to go: Salisbury – Lusaka – Lusaka – Umtali – Beira – Lusaka.

On top of that, on the normally deserted roads, they run into dozens of cars travelling at 200 km/h, full of excited Portuguese men going to a bullfight. They spray us with stones that break the windshield.

We spend three days in Beira. There are no Alfa Romeos. In the end they build us a plastic one, and it will take eighteen months for the real glass one to arrive from Italy. Meanwhile he, seeing that Nico can no longer hold him back, swims in the Ocean (cold!).
