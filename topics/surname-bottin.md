# Surname: Bottin

A French surname that crossed into the Italian-speaking world of nineteenth-century Tehran — carried by the man who built the Shah's arsenal and married into the Anglo-Armenian Burgess family. The name sits at the hinge point of the [Persia line](persia.md): the generation that connected British diplomacy and Armenian court life to French technical expertise, before the Stump dentistry chapter that followed.

![Julien Bottin and Henriette Bottin (née Burgess)](../media/images/portraits/julien-bottin-henriette-bottin-burgess.jpg)

---

## Etymology

From Old French **botte** or **botine** — a small boot, legging, or gaiter. The suffix **-in** is a French diminutive. The surname was an occupational name for a maker or seller of boots and leggings, or possibly a nickname for someone known for wearing them. The related Italian form **Bottini** derives from **botte** (barrel, cask), an occupational name for a cooper — but the French form *Bottin* is consistently associated with footwear rather than cooperage.

A secondary derivation connects some Bottin bearers to the Venetian Terraferma, where the name appears in Lucca, Tuscany, from at least 1500. The French and Italian lines may be distinct in origin despite the shared spelling.

**Classification:** occupational (French: bootmaker / seller).

## Variant spellings

| Form | Context |
|------|---------|
| **Bottin** | Standard French form; the form used in this family |
| **Bottini** | Italian variant (different root: *botte* = barrel); common in Tuscany and Lombardy |
| **Botin** | Older French; documented in Normandy from 1180–1195 |
| **Botti** | Italian shortened form |
| **Bottari** / **Bottaro** | Italian agent forms (barrel-related) |

## Geographic distribution

The surname is most concentrated in **Italy** (38% of bearers) and **France**, with a significant population in **Brazil** (emigration). In France it ranks approximately 6,789th, with an estimated 1,000–1,500 bearers. The name is also associated with the famous **Bottin** directory — the French equivalent of Who's Who — founded by Sébastien Bottin in 1796, which became synonymous with commercial directories in the Francophone world.

## In this tree

**[Julien Bottin](../people/julien-bottin.md)** was a French engineer who arrived in Qajar Iran as part of the wave of European technical advisors modernizing the Persian military-industrial apparatus. He secured a contract as **arsenal engineer** at the War Ministry in Tehran — 2,400 tomans salary, 30 students to train, responsibility for a powder factory and cartridge works. He was also active in Tehran's nascent Freemasonry, helping found the lodge *Réveil de l'Iran* in 1906 during the Constitutional Revolution.

Julien married **[Fanny Burgess](../people/fanny-burgess.md)**, granddaughter of [Daoud Khan Saginian](../people/daoud-khan-saginian.md) and daughter of [Edward Burgess](../people/edward-burgess.md) — connecting French technical expertise to the Anglo-Armenian court family that had been embedded in Persian power since the 1830s. Their daughter **[Louise Bottin](../people/louise-bottin.md)** married **[Étienne Stump](../people/etienne-stump.md)**, completing the chain that the Persia hub carries in its title: [Saginian](surname-saginian.md) → Burgess → Bottin → [Stump](surname-stump.md).
