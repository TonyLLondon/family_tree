# Surname: Coolbear

A hyper-local Essex surname whose entire genealogy can be traced to a single parish cluster around **Mundon and Maldon**, on the Blackwater estuary. The name drifted through four spellings in two generations — an unusually well-documented example of pre-standardisation surname fluidity in English parish registers.

---

## Etymology

The [SurnameDB entry](https://www.surnamedb.com/Surname/coolbear) traces the name to Old English compound elements: **Cole** (from the personal name *Cola*) + **baer** (pasture, from Old English *bǣr*). The compound would mean roughly "Cola's pasture" — a topographic or locational surname for someone associated with that piece of land. SurnameDB places the origin in Devon before migration to Essex, though the mechanism of that migration is not documented.

**Classification:** locational / topographic (Old English compound).

---

## Variant spellings

The spelling history of this surname in a single family is a textbook case of pre-twentieth-century fluidity:

| Form | Period | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Colbia** | 1830s | Parish registers, Mundon/Tollesbury, Essex |
| **Coolbar** | 1841 | Census, Mundon |
| **Colbear** | 1840s | Birth index, Mundon |
| **Coolbear** | 1851 onward | Census, standardised form |
| **Cool Bear** | WWI | Ministry of Pensions card (space inserted) |
| **Colbear** | WWI | RAF service file (AIR 79/1122) — filed under this spelling |

The shift from *Colbia* to *Coolbear* happened within the lifetime of a single generation. **John Colbia** (b. c. 1807, Purleigh, Essex) married Mary Barnard at Tollesbury in 1832; their children were recorded as Coolbar, Colbear, and Coolbear depending on the clerk. By 1851 the spelling had stabilised to *Coolbear*, and it remained fixed thereafter — though the military bureaucracy of 1914–1918 managed to introduce two further variants (*Colbear* on the RAF record, *Cool Bear* on the pensions card).

*Source: Wilkinson-Ransome family tree; TNA AIR 79/1122; WO 372 medal index cards; Ministry of Pensions card P.F. 23 M.*

## Geographic distribution

The surname is extremely rare. No Forebears entry exists for *Coolbear*, suggesting fewer than the database's threshold for listing (probably under 100 bearers globally). The historical concentration is entirely in the **Maldon district of Essex** — Mundon, Purleigh, Tollesbury — a stretch of flat marshland and tidal creeks on the Blackwater estuary. The family radiated outward only slowly: to Southend-on-Sea (Archibald's post-war address), to Hendon (his death), and to Holloway/Islington (his widow's pensions address).

---

## The Mundon cluster

The documented origin point is the marriage of **John Colbia** (b. c. 1807, Purleigh) and **Mary Barnard** (b. c. 1807, Tollesbury) on **29 September 1832** at Tollesbury. Their children, all born in Mundon, carry the evolving spellings:

- **John Colbear** (b. 1840, Mundon) — stayed in Essex
- **David Coolbear** (b. 1843, Mundon) — emigrated to Utah with his mother

**[George Gridley Coolbear](../people/george-gridley-coolbear-i105.md)** — father of [Archibald Percy Coolbear](../people/archibald-percy-coolbear.md) — fits squarely into this Maldon-district lineage, the same parish cluster where the Colbia/Coolbear family is documented from the 1830s. The [Wilkinson-Ransome family tree](https://www.wilkinsonransomefamilytree.co.uk/fam9112.html) provides the most detailed published reconstruction of this cluster.

## In this tree

**[Archibald Percy Coolbear](../people/archibald-percy-coolbear.md)** (1892–1928) — chimney sweep turned soldier, one of the founding generation of the RAF — was born in Maldon and carried the surname into the military records of the First World War, where the bureaucracy produced yet more variants. His daughter **[May Coolbear](../people/may-coolbear-i100.md)** married into the Holborn Evans family in 1940, making the Coolbear surname one of the tributaries feeding into the London branch of this tree.

The full military biography and evidence trail — RAF service file, medal rolls, pensions card, the Gallipoli and Western Front service — is on [Archibald's person page](../people/archibald-percy-coolbear.md).
