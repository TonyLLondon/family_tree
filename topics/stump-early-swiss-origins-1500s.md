# Early Swiss STUMP Origins: Multi-Regional Families 1500s-1560s

> **⚠️ Important note:** The individuals documented here are **not proven direct ancestors** of our STUMP lineage. The earliest *confirmed* direct ancestor is [Hanssen Stump](../people/hanssen-stump-1580.md) (c. 1580, Sulgen). These early Swiss STUMP families provide valuable **surname context and migration evidence**, but no primary record directly links them to the later Thurgau line.

The **STUMP surname** was already well-established across **multiple Swiss cantons** by the **early 1500s**, long before the documented concentration in Thurgau during the 1560s-1590s. Systematic research through Swiss parish records reveals **STUMP families** in **Zürich**, **Basel**, and **St. Gallen** during the **Renaissance** and **Reformation** periods, showing the surname was broadly distributed across Switzerland.

## The Zällerin Connection: Suggestive But Unproven

### Shared Surname

**Margreth Zällerin** appears in both early and later STUMP records:
- **Cunrat Stump's wife** (Zürich, died 1550) — **Margreth Zällerin**
- **Blaesi STUMP's mother** (Thurgau, early 1600s) — **Margreth Zaellerin**

**This is suggestive but not proof.** The Zällerin/Zeller surname was common across Swiss cantons in this period. The overlap could indicate:
- **Direct family connection** — the same or related Zällerin family married STUMP men in both locations
- **Coincidence** — a common surname appearing in unrelated marriages
- **Migration route** — Zürich families moving to Thurgau via shared social networks

### What Would Be Needed to Prove It

Bridging this gap would require:
1. A baptism record for Hanssen Stump (c. 1580) naming his father
2. Records showing a son of Cunrat migrating from Zürich to Thurgau
3. Marriage records linking specific Zällerin women across cantons

## Implications for STUMP Genealogy

### Proven Direct Lineage

The **confirmed direct lineage** spans from Hanssen Stump (c. 1580) to Marc François Stump (1834) — approximately **254 years** across **nine documented generations**. The earlier Swiss STUMP families documented here extend the *surname context* back to the early 1500s but are **not part of the proven direct line**.

### Contextual Value

These early records demonstrate:
- The STUMP surname was broadly established across Switzerland before the Thurgau concentration
- Multiple independent STUMP families existed in major urban centres during the Reformation
- The Thurgau establishment (1560s–1590s) likely represents a migration from one of these earlier centres
- The Zällerin surname overlap is the strongest (though still circumstantial) link between the early and later families

### What Would Bridge the Gap

The critical missing piece is a record naming **Hanssen Stump's father** (c. 1580, Sulgen). Sulgen's parish registers begin only in 1590, meaning Hanssen was born before systematic record-keeping started. Without this single link, the connection to any earlier STUMP family remains hypothetical.
