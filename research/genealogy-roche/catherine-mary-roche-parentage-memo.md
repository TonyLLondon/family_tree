## Conclusion in brief

The memo argues **very high confidence** that **Catherine Mary “Katie” Roche** was the **third daughter** of **William Roche** (Dublin solicitor, 72 Harcourt Street; later Crown Solicitor, Limerick) and **Eliza (Elizabeth) Knight**.

## Evidence strands summarized

| Strand | Role |
|--------|------|
| Burke’s *Commoners* (1833) | William Roche m. Eliza Knight — establishes the couple (secondary). |
| St Andrew’s RC baptisms (NLI) | Mary (1838), Hellen (1839), parents Wm & Elizth — sequence matches “third daughter” for Catherine ~1841. |
| Bombay marriage notice (1869) | “Katie Mary, third daughter of William Roche, Esq., of 72 Harcourt-street” — ties India marriage to Dublin family. |
| William Roche will (1894) | Executors sons Thomas and George — consistent with a large sibling set. |
| Directories | William Roche solicitor 72 Harcourt St; Crown Solicitor Limerick. |

## Gaps flagged in the memo

- Original **1832–1835 Dublin marriage** register entry (William × Eliza) not yet in hand.
- **1841 baptism** line for Catherine: logic from sibling order + notice; direct transcript called out as still desirable.

## Pitfall (vault)

**William Roche of Jamestown, Kilkenny** (m. Mary Murphy 1844) is a **different** man from the **Dublin solicitor** — do not merge on dates alone (also noted in [roche-knight wishlist](../../sources/wishlist/roche-knight.md)).
