# General Simeone Gilberto Addobbati (1851–1926) analytical dossier

## Executive summary

General Simeone Gilberto Addobbati (German administrative form: **Simeon Addobbati**) was a senior Habsburg officer born **4 February 1851** in Zara (Dalmatia) and deceased **20 June 1926**. His general-officer status and senior rank chronology are confirmed by the Austrian State Archives’ published list of the k.k./k.u.k. generality: **Generalmajor** effective **1 Nov 1906** (seniority **28 Oct 1906**), **retired (pens.)** effective **1 Nov 1909**, then granted **Titular Feldmarschalleutnant** on **17 Dec 1910**. citeturn0search44

A crucial near-primary record is your scanned **Kriegsarchiv letter dated 20 Dec 1968** (reference Zl. 49.082/1968). It summarises his service postings (including teaching assignments and Landwehr commands), awards, and marriage; most importantly, it states that his **Qualifikationsliste/Dienstbeschreibung was transferred in 1926 to the Italian Embassy in Vienna**, implying that the most detailed personnel file may no longer be held in Austria. This aligns with ÖStA guidance that officers’ Qualifikationslisten were frequently **“ausgefolgt” (transferred out)** after 1918 when officers assumed successor‑state citizenship. citeturn5search37turn11search2

Unresolved points requiring primary confirmation are: **campaigns served (unspecified)**, **exact death-place and burial location (unspecified)**, and the **current Italian repository/call reference for the transferred Qualifikationsliste (unspecified)**.

**Family placement inside HR‑DAZD‑342.** The same DAZD inventory identifies Simeone as son of **Pietro Paolo Addobbati** (a provincial accounting clerk) and states that Simeone married **Elisa Luxardo**, had **no children**, and that the **obituary (osmrtnica) dated 21 June 1926** lists mourners including a nephew physician **Pietro Addobbati**, his sister **Anna**, and Luxardo in‑laws. citeturn13search33 It also states that the fond was produced across four generations, with the largest portion created by **Pietro Addobbati (born 1769), son of Luigi**, providing a direct archival bridge to your “Dr Pietro 1769–1832” anchor. citeturn13search33
