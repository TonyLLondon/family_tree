## Luxardo violence in Zadar (September–November 1944) vs Riccardo’s location

Secondary journalism and regional histories describe liquidations of **Nicolò Luxardo** (30 September 1944) and **Pietro Luxardo** (arrested 1 November 1944; killed 12 November 1944) in the context of the **Italian-Yugoslav transition** and partisan takeover of Zara/Zadar. Examples (for **general context only**, not proof Riccardo witnessed anything):

- [Il Giornale — "Zara, così scomparvero i due fratelli Luxardo"](https://www.ilgiornale.it/news/zara-cos-scomparvero-i-due-fratelli-luxardo.html)
- [Slobodna Dalmacija — Luxardo family, art restitution angle](https://www.zadarski.hr/kultura/slikarstvo/obitelj-luxardo-cije-su-pretke-u-zadru-1944-likvidirala-ozna-trazi-ukradene-umjetnine-zasto-mi-hrvatska-ne-vrati-sliku-naseg-michelangela-1024905)
- English-language summary: [Wikipedia — Girolamo Luxardo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girolamo_Luxardo) (Foibe context).

**Timeline intersection:** Riccardo was on **Lošinj and Silba** from **early 1944 through April 1945** per his IRO narrative. The Luxardo brothers’ deaths in **Zadar city** fall in **September–November 1944**. So he was **not physically in Zara during those specific killings** — unless undocumented travel contradicts his filed story (no evidence of that in the vault).

**How to read Fulvia’s memoir** (“as a non-partner he escaped the fate of the Luxardo brothers”):

- **Structural:** Riccardo was a **legal agent / employee**, not a named **principal** of a major firm — the memoir contrasts his position with **business owners** targeted in the liquidation of the Italian economic elite.
- **Not a claim** that he was in the same room or the same week as the Luxardos unless family oral tradition says otherwise; the IRO file supports **absence from Zara** during the worst wave.

## Split opera house (1945–1947)

**Hrvatsko narodno kazalište u Splitu** traces its institutional continuity to **1 July 1945** (post-liberation reorganisation; roots in the “Theatre of National Liberation of Dalmatia” on Vis, 1944). Official history portal: [hnkarhiva.sistemi.hr — Povijest HNK](http://hnkarhiva.sistemi.hr/O-nama/Povijest-HNK.html).

Riccardo’s dates (**singer from Apr 1945 to Apr 1947** with Split named) overlap the **founding season**: first performance **27 September 1945** (*Matija Gubec…*). His engagement could have been **early wartime/improvised ensemble before July 1945** and/or the **new HNK** — the IRO text does not name the employer’s legal entity.

**Archival chase:** HNK Split archive / **arhiva@hnk-split.hr** (verify current contact on [hnk-split.hr](https://www.hnk-split.hr/)) — request **payrolls, contracts, or chorus lists 1945–1947** under surname variants **Zerauschek**, **Zerausch**, Italian **Riccardo**.
