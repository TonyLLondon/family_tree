- **Sitter-specific Persian hits:** Repeated queries for Tamar under Armenian transliterations (ساگینیان / ساقینیان, etc.) did **not** return portrait- or sitter-specific catalogue trails in the memo’s search pass.
- **Absence-of-hit argument:** If the work sat in a major Iranian public collection with a Persian catalogue entry, the memo expects at least one durable web or index trace; none appeared in this run.
- **Hakop Hovnatanyan context:** Persian Wikipedia and academic indexing on **هاکوپ هوناتانیان** give useful **anchor works** (e.g. Naser al-Din portrait linked to Vienna/Belvedere inventory in secondary summaries; Ali b. Abi Talib image tied to Isfahan decorative-arts context in article text). That frames **style and documentary footprint** comparisons but does not identify Tamar’s portrait.
- **“Cormick / McCormick” bridge:** Persian sources frequently discuss court physicians rendered as کرمیک / کورمیک / مککورمیک (John and William Cormick’s line, smallpox treatise, “political physicians” framing). That supports **plausibility of the English caption’s doctor identifier** but **does not confirm Tamar as sitter**.
