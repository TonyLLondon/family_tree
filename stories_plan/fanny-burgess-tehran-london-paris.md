# Fanny Burgess: Tehran, London, Paris, and Back

## Pitch

The most densely documented untold biography on the direct line. Fanny was born in Tehran around 1854, just before her father Edward Burgess died on the road from Tehran to Tabriz — she was barely past her first birthday. Raised by Anna Saginian alone in Qajar Persia, she survived smallpox as an infant, studied English at the American Nestorian mission in Urmia, lived with the Cormick family in Tabriz, and in 1880 travelled to England with her mother — the trip that produced Anna's extraordinary interview for the NYPL. She married Julien Bottin in Tehran in 1884 and bore five children there. By the 1901 London census she was living in England without Julien. She sent children to school in Paris. The marriage ended in divorce in 1919. Her last known letter is from 1929. She died in Tehran in 1938 and is buried in the Protestant Cemetery.

This is a story about a woman raised between cultures — Armenian-Georgian mother, English father she never knew, Qajar childhood, French husband, London and Paris interlude, and a return to the only city that was ever really home.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **Tehran, c. 1854** — birth in Qajar Tehran, Edward Burgess's last days, the three-month takhteravan journey that Anna and Edward made to Tabriz while Fanny was still an infant.
2. **Orphaned at one** — Edward dies in Tabriz. Anna's account from the 1880 interview: six weeks of care by Dr. Cormick, death, burial in the Armenian cemetery. Fanny grows up without a father.
3. **English and missions** — smallpox survival, Urmia and the American Nestorian mission, the project of teaching Fanny English as her father had wished. Tabriz years with the Cormick household.
4. **England, 1880** — Anna and Fanny travel to England. The Leicester interview for the NYPL. The Anna & Fanny plate portrait. What England looked like to women who had grown up in Qajar Iran.
5. **Marriage to Julien** — 1884, Tehran. Five children born in the city. The Bottin household: French language, War Ministry connections, Alliance Française circles, the 1896 family photograph.
6. **London without Julien** — 1901 census captures Fanny in London, husband absent. Paris schools for the children. A marriage pulling apart across continents.
7. **Divorce and return** — 1919 divorce. Last letter 1929. Death in Tehran, 1938. Protestant Cemetery. A life that began and ended in the same city, spanning the entire Qajar-to-Pahlavi transition.
