# Vera Obolensky: Revolution, Tehran, and the Mahmoudieh Guesthouse

## Pitch

White Russian princess who fled the 1917 Revolution to Iran, lost her first husband (Prince Yuri Obolensky, "a relative of Tolstoy") to malaria, ran a 40-room European-style guesthouse on the Mahmoudieh estate with Dr. Étienne Stump, married Stump, and after his death emigrated to New Jersey where she died in 1974. Her son Georges published *Forever Russian: Memoirs of a Vagabond Prince* (2013). The vault has portraits, the NYT death notice, the memoir extract, and the Mahmoudieh house album.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **The Russian Empire** — Vera Nemtchinova's birth c. 1895, the world before 1917.
2. **Revolution and flight** — 1917, Caucasus route south to Iran, the first wave of White émigrés.
3. **Prince Yuri** — marriage, Tehran, the Obolensky name and Tolstoy connection, Georges born 1920.
4. **Yuri's death** — malaria c. 1925, Vera widowed with a young son in Tehran.
5. **The Mahmoudieh guesthouse** — 40 rooms, European travellers, Stump's property and Vera's management, effectively Tehran's first foreign-run hotel.
6. **Marriage to Stump** — the partnership becomes personal, Henriette Bottin's exit, the Stump–Obolensky household.
7. **After Stump** — Étienne's death 1951, Vera leaves Iran for Frankfurt then New Jersey, Georges' American life.
8. **Forever Russian** — Georges' memoir, the NYT death notice, the Tolstoy thread.
