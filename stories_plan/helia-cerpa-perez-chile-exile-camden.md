# Helia Cerpa-Pérez: Allende, Coup, Exile, Camden

## Pitch

The only modern political-exile story in the vault. Helia grew up in Parral — Neruda's birthplace, later the site of a DINA torture facility — worked for the Allende government, fled Chile after the September 1973 coup through the Italian embassy system, resettled in London, and spent decades as a housing advocate on the Ossulston Estate in Camden. Her uncles on both sides were recognised victims of political imprisonment and torture (Valech I and II). She died in 2025.

This is the most emotionally immediate narrative in the family archive: living memory, political violence, exile, and civic reinvention.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **Parral** — birthplace, Neruda country, small-town Maule.
2. **Allende's Chile** — Unidad Popular, women's expanding roles, Helia's generation drawn into government service.
3. **11 September 1973** — the coup, stadiums, embassies, the DINA apparatus arriving in Parral itself.
4. **The Italian embassy** — Piero De Masi, 700 sheltered, mattresses bought with personal funds, safe-conduct passages.
5. **Rome** — Chilean exile hub, political renovation of the Socialist Party, Italy's first non-European refugee intake.
6. **London** — Labour's reversal of Heath's policy, Chile Solidarity Campaign, JWG, WUS scholarships, Chalk Farm squats, the Primrose Hill mural.
7. **Ossulston Estate** — Grade II modernist council housing, TRA work, DMC minutes 2010–2023, the 2014 Cabinet deputation.
8. **El piquete de Londres** — Pinochet arrested 1998, 503 days of protest, exile community remobilised.
9. **The Valech lists** — Patricio Arnaldo and Luis Omar Pérez Escobar (Helia's uncles, mother's side); Hugo Amado Cerpa González (father's side). What the double-surname matches mean.
