# David John Lewis — Northern Rhodesia and the Congo Crisis

## Pitch

The WWII Italy story is already shipped. But David John Lewis had a dramatic second act: colonial district officer in Northern Rhodesia (Zambia), front-line during the **1960 Congo crisis** when refugees flooded across the border at Ndola, correspondence with Kenneth Kaunda through independence, and later a Keep Britain Tidy career in England. The media archive for this arc is enormous and episodic — it's practically a ready-made scrolly.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **From the Po to Africa** — demobilisation, transition from soldier to colonial administrator.
2. **Northern Rhodesia** — district administration, the bush, the Provincial system.
3. **Ndola and the copper belt** — industrial landscape at the edge of empire.
4. **The Congo crisis, 1960** — Lumumba, secession, refugees crossing into Northern Rhodesia, Dag Hammarskjöld's death at Ndola (Sep 1961).
5. **David Lewis at the border** — the refugee operation, BESL presentation, press coverage.
6. **Kaunda and independence** — the handover, correspondence, the transition from Northern Rhodesia to Zambia (1964).
7. **Return to England** — the colonial officer's repatriation, Keep Britain Tidy, a different kind of public service.

Appendix: Evidence, Medals and decorations (combined WWII + colonial), Sources.
