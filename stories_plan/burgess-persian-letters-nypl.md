# The Burgess Persian Letters — An Archive Recovered

## Pitch

A different kind of story: not a biography but a documentary narrative about the **archive itself**. The NYPL holds the Burgess family's Persian-language correspondence — letters from Edward Burgess, Anna Saginian, and their circle. The Schwartz article series in the NYPL Bulletin (9 parts + appendix) unpacked this collection. The vault holds the complete PDFs, 28 Farsi page scans, Anna and Fanny portrait plates, and the Charles Burgess portrait. This story would trace how the letters survived, what they contain, and what they reveal about the family's Persian-language world — a meta-narrative about evidence and recovery.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **The letters** — what the NYPL collection contains, how it got there.
2. **The Schwartz series** — the NYPL Bulletin articles that opened the archive to scholarship.
3. **The Farsi originals** — what it meant for an Anglo-Armenian-Persian family to write in Persian; the calligraphy, the forms.
4. **Anna's voice** — the 1880 interview (NYPL appendix), Anna Saginian speaking about her family in English for an American audience.
5. **The portraits** — Anna and Fanny plate, Charles Burgess painted in Persia.
6. **What the letters reveal** — trade, family, the "intelligence newspaper," the Tabriz–Tehran corridor.
7. **Recovery** — from private correspondence to public archive to family history project.
