# General Simeone Gilberto Addobbati — a Habsburg Military Life

## Pitch

Simeone Gilberto Addobbati (1851–1926) rose through the k.u.k. military from Dalmatian cadet schools to Generalmajor (1906) and titular Feldmarschalleutnant (1910). His career crossed the Landwehr, garrison commands at Ragusa (Dubrovnik) and Kotor, and a Prague brigade. After retirement, the Italian Embassy in Rome acquired his service file (Qualifikationsliste) from the Kriegsarchiv in 1926 — a detail that speaks to the contested identities of Italian-speaking Habsburg officers in the aftermath of WWI. The research dossier and Kriegsarchiv media scans are ready.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **Dalmatia, 1851** — birth in the Addobbati *cittadini* network, the Habsburg military as a career path for Italian-speaking subjects.
2. **Cadet schools** — the pipeline from provincial families into the imperial officer corps.
3. **Landwehr service** — garrison life in Ragusa and Kotor, the Adriatic frontier.
4. **Generalmajor, 1906** — Prague brigade command, the apogee of a provincial career in an empire about to collapse.
5. **Feldmarschalleutnant (titular), 1910** — honorary rank on retirement, the decorations list.
6. **The Qualifikationsliste, 1926** — after WWI, after the empire's dissolution, the Italian Embassy claims his service file from the Austrian Kriegsarchiv. What this transfer meant for identity and memory.
