# Anna Herenda: Pag, Zara, and Nine Children

## Pitch

Anna Herenda was born on the island of Pag in 1859, married Antonio Zerauschek (senior) at 19, and raised eight or nine children in Zara whose fates spanned La Scala singing, shipyards, merchant navy, Spanish flu, a fatal fall, and Iron Curtain separation. The Fulvia memoir (1996) preserves vivid details: Anna's famous blond hair that reached the floor, the "orphaned at 16" narrative that contradicts the marriage register (both parents listed as living), her sudden death at 64. This is a matriarch story anchored in the Dalmatian register network and the Zerauschek collection of 64 parish images.

## Proposed sections (scrolly beats)

1. **Pag island** — the setting, an Adriatic outpost under Habsburg rule, salt and stone.
2. **The orphan claim** — the memoir says orphaned at 16, raised brothers, cheated out of the family business. But the 1878 register says both parents alive. Estrangement, not death?
3. **Marriage to Antonio** — 26 January 1878, Pago. Anna described as "Negoziante" (merchant). The register details.
4. **Zara, Via del Cristo** — the family's urban life in Habsburg Dalmatia by 1889. Antonio the carpenter, the furniture factory.
5. **Nine children** — the roster of fates: Antonio ("Nonno"), Marie (died from a fall at 18), Emma (Spanish flu), Giuseppina (married a La Scala tenor), Giovanni "Zio Nini" (shipyard), Giusit, Besto (merchant navy, South Africa), Ginco/Riccardo.
6. **The long blond hair** — the memoir's most vivid image: brushing her hair while boys played, a foot on the ground when loose. Her mother ended life wearing a wig. Anna died at 64 with her hair still below her knees.
7. **Death and dispersal** — Anna dies c. 1923, confirmed by ITS/IRO file listing her as mother of Riccardo Zerauschek (1951, Trieste displaced persons). The children scatter: Riccardo's IRO file, Iron Curtain, exile.
