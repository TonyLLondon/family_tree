# Julien Bottin: Arsenal, Freemasonry, and French Tehran

## Pitch

Julien Bottin married Fanny Burgess (Edward and Anna's daughter, raised after Edward's 1855 death) and entered the Qajar War Ministry on contract — managing the arsenal, a powder factory, training Iranian students in modern manufacturing. He co-founded the Masonic "Orient de Tehran" and moved in Alliance Française circles. The existing Saginian arc story covers the Bottin generation briefly, but Julien's own life — French industrial expertise sold to a modernising Islamic monarchy, Freemasonry as a social network for European professionals in Tehran, the household with Fanny and their children — deserves its own scrolly.
