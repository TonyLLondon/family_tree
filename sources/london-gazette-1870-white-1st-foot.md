# London Gazette — White appointed to 1st Foot

**Issue:** 23598, 15 March 1870, page 1738  
**URL:** https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/23598/page/1738/data.pdf  
**Corpus:** [sources/corpus/london-gazette-1870-03-15-white-1st-foot/](corpus/london-gazette-1870-03-15-white-1st-foot/)

## The notice

> Assistant-Surgeon John Middleton, M.D., transferred from 1st Foot, **vice William O'Byrne White, appointed to 1st Foot**.

"Vice" in Gazette usage means "in place of." Middleton fills a vacancy in the 1st Foot created when White was appointed to that regiment. This confirms White was officially an Assistant-Surgeon of the **1st (Royal) Regiment of Foot (Royal Scots)** by March 1870 — five years into his AMD commission (started 31 March 1865).

## Significance

This is the **primary source** confirming White's regimental assignment. It directly fills the gap flagged in [sources/wishlist/white-india.md](wishlist/white-india.md) ("regimental attachments, posting history, confirming Jabalpur") — achieved via the London Gazette rather than IOR/L/MIL at the British Library.

## Station list corroboration

War Office station lists show the **2nd Battalion, 1st Foot** was based at **Jubbulpore (Jabalpur)** from 1866 through at least 1879. White's son Gerald was born at Jabalpur in **February 1878**. Medical officers moved with their regiments, so White's presence at Jabalpur in 1878 follows directly from his attachment to the 2nd Bn/1st Foot.

Earlier lists show the 25th Foot occupied Jabalpur in 1873–1874; the 1st Foot's 2nd Battalion relieved them and held the station through the late 1870s.

## The regiment

The **1st (Royal) Regiment of Foot** — the Royal Scots — was the senior line regiment of the British Army ("Primus in Indis"). Assignment to it was a mark of professional standing within the AMD. Medical officers serving in the Central Provinces faced malaria and cholera outbreaks alongside standard garrison duties.
