## Summary

Open-access academic chapter (Ca' Foscari Edizioni) covering the legal and institutional structure of the *cittadini originari* class in the Venetian Republic. Explains the **Libro d'Argento** (Silver Book, established 1315) as the equivalent registration system for citizens below the patrician *Libro d'Oro*. Details admission criteria (three generations of legitimate descent, liberal arts or mercantile livelihood, no manual trades), the role of the **Maggior Consiglio** in confirming status, and the range of posts reserved for *cittadini* (Senate secretaries, *Cancelliere Grando*, notaries, chancellery officials).

## Use in this research

Provides the institutional framework for understanding what it meant for the Addobbati to be admitted to Zara's *cittadini originari* council in 1733. The Dalmatian variant of the *cittadini* system mirrored the Venetian model: hereditary registration, liberal-professional entry requirements, and reserved access to civic administrative offices. The chapter is the primary scholarly reference for contextualising the family's tier in the Venetian social hierarchy.
