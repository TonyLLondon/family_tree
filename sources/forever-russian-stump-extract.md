# Forever Russian — Stump extract (Henderson)

Typed extract from **Georges Obolensky**, *Forever Russian: Memoirs of a Vagabond Prince* (AuthorHouse, 2013), prepared by JPH (7 Jan 2014). Covers extracts from **Chapter 1** and **Chapter 9** relevant to the Stump family.

## Key facts

- **1925** — Prince Yuri Obolensky dies of malaria; Georges nearly 5 years old.
- After Yuri's death, Vera asked to manage a **40+ room B&B** on Dr Stump's estate. Stump was "the only dentist in Teheran."
- Georges grew up attending a **French grammar school** (Mlle. Tomassin).
- **1930** — Georges sent to France aged 10.
- **1954** — Georges visits Tehran on business; stays with Vera. Stump had died in **1951**.
- Vera eventually left Tehran for **Frankfurt**, then **New Jersey** with Georges and his wife Rhoda.
- **Vera died in America in 1975** (note: the NYT notice says **6 Nov 1974** — minor discrepancy with this extract).
