# 405 Women Detained at the Estadio Nacional, 1973

Official list of 405 women detained at the Estadio Nacional in Santiago during the Chilean military dictatorship, published on 31 December 2018. The list is maintained by the **Corporación Estadio Nacional Memoria Nacional Ex Prisioneros Políticos** (president: Wally Kunstmann) and was first made public by *Cambio21* journalist Alfredo Peña.

**CERPA PEREZ HELIA DEL C** — [Helia del Carmen Cerpa Pérez](../people/helia-cerpa-perez.md) — appears on the list. She was detained at the Estadio Nacional after the 11 September 1973 coup, held in the women's changing room (*camarín*) beside the stadium swimming pool at the corner of Pedro de Valdivia and Avenida Grecia, Ñuñoa, Santiago. The camarín norte is now one of nine designated memory sites at the Estadio Nacional (National Historic Monument since 2003).

The Corporación has since recovered **507 names** (433 Chilean, 74 foreign) of women detainees. An estimated 1,200 women passed through the stadium between 12 September and 9 November 1973; the former DINA director Manuel Contreras cited 509 (445 Chilean, 64 foreign). Copper memorial plaques with detainees' names are installed in the Camarín Norte.

## Key facts from the article

- ~20,000 people total passed through the Estadio Nacional (unofficial estimate)
- Women were initially held in coliseum changing rooms; on 26 September 1973 all were transferred to the pool-area camarines (~30 m × 7 m)
- Many women were subjected to sexual violence
- The Corporación installed copper plaques with prisoners' names in the changing room
