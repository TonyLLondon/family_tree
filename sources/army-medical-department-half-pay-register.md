**Qualifications decoded:**
- **LRCP Ed.** = Licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians of Edinburgh
- **LRCSI** = Licentiate of the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland (Dublin)

Both are the specifically Irish/Scottish licensing route, consistent with training at the Catholic University School of Medicine (Cecilia Street, Dublin, opened 1855) and Edinburgh — the standard path for Irish Catholic medical graduates who did not attend Protestant Trinity College or the non-denominational Queen's Colleges.

## Rank structure context

Under the 1860 Royal Warrant: Surgeon Major = relative rank of Lieutenant-Colonel. The 1879 Warrant introduced the "Brigade-Surgeon" grade (relative rank Lt. Col.) between Surgeon Major and Deputy Surgeon-General. White's retirement title "hon. Brig.-Surg." is the honorary version of this grade granted on retirement after the qualifying period.
