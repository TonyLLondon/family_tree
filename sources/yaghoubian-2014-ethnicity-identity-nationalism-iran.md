## Relevance

Chapter 4 (pp. 123–167) is a **social biography of Sevak Saginian** (1922–), son of Zohrab (Zora/Sohrab) Saginian, based on **oral interviews conducted by the author in 1995–1998** and Sevak's own written memoirs. The chapter includes a detailed scholarly summary of the Saginian family's Qajar-era military history (pp. 125–127) with endnoted citations.

### Burial

David Khan and Solayman Khan both buried in the **Saginian mausoleum at Surb Astvatsatsin** (Holy Mother of God church), Tabriz (nn. 10–11). Corresponds to Armen Saginian's "Mary, Mother of God" cemetery.

### Key source cited

Isma'il Ra'in, *Iraniyan-i Armani* (Armenians of Iran) — contains the portrait painting of Sartip David Khan with the Persian caption: *"Sartip David Khan Sakinian, who was commander of Iranian armed forces [farmandeh-ye qoshun-e Iran] in the provinces of Shiraz and Isfahan during the reign of Fath Ali Shah, and who died at age 78"* (p. 47 of Ra'in).

### Other Saginian references

- **Alex Saginian** (Zohrab's brother) opened the first movie house in Iran, "Cinema Soleil," in Tabriz in 1916 (p. 129, n. 21).
- Zohrab was secretary and interpreter for **Yeprem Khan** during the Constitutional Revolution; Yeprem Khan was killed trying to reach Zohrab, who was wounded in battle (p. 128, n. 19).
- Saginians related to the **Setkhanian** military family through Sandokht Badmagurian's family (n. 35).
