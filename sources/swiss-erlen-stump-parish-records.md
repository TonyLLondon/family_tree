# Swiss parish records — Stump, Haag, Montagnon & Chenevard families (FamilySearch)

FamilySearch indexed records from the Erlen/Sulgen/Hüttwilen Reformed Churches (Thurgau), the Canton de Vaud, and the Céligny Reformed Church (Geneva) covering the Stump, Haag, Montagnon, Chenevard, and Dumartheray families across six generations. Full indexed data (names, dates, places, relationships) available via individual record ARKs. **Images restricted** to FamilySearch Centers for collection 4138674; collections 1520585/1520587/1520589 are indexes without linked originals.

Each record has its own corpus bundle with `source.yaml` and `reference.md` containing the full indexed data.

## Key findings

- **Marc's baptism** confirms birth 10 March 1834, christened 25 April 1834 at Erlen, parents Jakob Stump + Jeanne Benigne Montagnon.
- **Jakob's baptism** establishes paternal grandparents: **Hans Ulrich Stump** + **Anna Margaretha Hagg**, christened 15 June 1800 at Riedt bei Erlen.
- **Ulrich's baptism** traces to great-grandparents: **Hans Konrad Stump** + **Anna Margaretha Jung**, christened 25 October 1767 at Riedt bei Erlen. He died 18 December 1807 at Sulgen, when Jakob was seven.
- **Marriage record** confirms Ulrich × Margaretha Hagg on 26 April 1792 at the Reformed church in Sulgen.
- **Jeanne Montagnon's baptism** corrects the legacy export: born **15 July 1805** in **Vaud**, Switzerland (not "1800" in "France"). Parents: **Louis Montagnon** + **Jeanne Françoise Dumartheray**.
- **Family register** identifies great-grandparents on the maternal side: **Jean François Louis Montagnon** + **Susanne Cheneveau** (paternal), **Jean Gabriel Nicolas Dumartheray** + **Elisabeth Borrel** (maternal).
- **Parentage correction:** An earlier pass attributed Jean Louis Montagnon's parents to Jean Michel Montagnon + Catherine Vougaz based on a burial record (6NQ2-TV9P, d. 1817). Subsequent research proved that burial is for a *different* Jean Louis Montagnon (b. 1743, son of Jean Michel, married Jeanne Françoise Vougaz 1780). Our ancestor **Jean François Louis Montagnon** was the son of **Jean Pierre Montagnon** (b. 1692, d. 1737 Aubonne) + **Jeanne Cusin**, confirmed by multiple family register entries at Céligny (6NQL-FNTW et al.). He was born ~1737 and buried 4 Feb 1786 at Aubonne.
- **Jaqueline Suzanne Chenevard** (née Chenevard, also Cheneveau/Chanevaz) — full name and baptism found: 26 Oct 1732, Céligny, Geneva (6NQF-WB45). Parents: **Benjamin Chenevard** (b. ~1688, d. 2 Dec 1747 Céligny, father: Antoine Chenevard) + **Christine Nerfin** (father: Humbert Nerfin). Marriage register dated 9 Mar 1720 (6NQL-M318).
- **Hans Conrad Haag's baptism** at Hüttwilen identifies his parents: **Johann Conrad Haag** + **Anna Barbara Erbig**, adding another generation to the Haag line.
- **Anna Margaretha Jung's baptism** at Sulgen (24 Jul 1735) identifies her parents: **Hans Jacob Jung** + **Maria Keller**. Her burial (18 Dec 1807) — same day as son Hans Ulrich — confirms her identity.
- **Margaretha Freyenmuth's baptism** at Wigoltingen (15 Sep 1743) identifies her parents: **Hans Jacob Freyenmuth** + **Anna Margaretha Keller**.
- **Dumartheray family register** (4 Aug 1767) identifies Gabriel Nicolas Dumartheray's father as **Jaques Dumartheray** and wife Jeanne Elisabeth Borel's father as **Gabriel Borel**.
- **Jeanne Françoise Dumartheray** (Marc's maternal grandmother) born 1775, baptised 25 March 1775.
- **Jean François Montagnon** (b. ~1667, d. 15 Sep 1732, Aubonne) — his baptism record (22 Dec 1667, as "François Montagnon") names his father **Jean Montagnon**, pushing the Montagnon line back to the 1630s/40s. His wife **Jeane Marechal** (b. 1665, d. 11 Jul 1742) is confirmed by her burial record. Jean François is the probable father of Jean Pierre Montagnon (b. 1692) — the link rests on strong circumstantial evidence (sole Montagnon patriarch at Aubonne, consistent birth spacing) but no explicit parent-child record. Aubonne baptismal registers for the 1690s appear unindexed.
- **François Louis Montagnon** (d. 26 Mar 1745, Aubonne) — burial record (6NQ2-535L) explicitly names "Pierre Montagnon" as his father, confirming Jean Pierre had a son who died young.
