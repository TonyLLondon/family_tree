## Summary

Analysis and transcription of the 1817 Nin noble-family petition to the Habsburg Heraldic Commission. Granić identifies the 52 families that submitted genealogies and supporting documents; the Habsburg commission ultimately ruled that Dalmatian communal nobility did not constitute nobility under Austrian law, applying this standard uniformly.

**Addobbati entries (pp. 232–233 approx.):** Four Addobbati are named:

| Name | Father | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| Dr. Petar Addobbati | Lujo | Lawyer (*causidico*); Knight of the Golden Spur 1786; Nin admission 1804 |
| Ivan Vicko Addobbati | Lujo | Nin admission 1804 |
| Petar Addobbati | Ivan Vicko | Second generation, post-1804 |
| Josip Addobbati | Ivan Vicko | Second generation, post-1804 |

Also records: Lujo's brothers **Salustio** and **Don Giovanni** admitted to the Nin council in **1793**. The family submitted their **1745 Bergamo testimonial** and the **1786 Golden Spur diploma** as supporting evidence.

## Use in this research

Primary source for the Addobbati family's engagement with the Nin noble council. Names, relationships, and dates on the 1817 petition cross-reference directly with documents in HR-DAZD-342 ([dazd-addobbati-family-fonds](corpus/dazd-addobbati-family-fonds/)).
