# Antonio Zerauschek obituary — *ZARA* (A.N.D.A.Z.), 1973

A second obituary for [Antonio Zerauschek](../people/antonio-zerauschek.md), published in *ZARA* — the periodical of the **Associazione Nostalgica degli Amici Zaratini** (A.N.D.A.Z.) in Ancona — **Anno XXI, N. 4–5, Aprile–Maggio 1973**. Distinct from the *Difesa Adriatica* obituary by Giuseppe Zilotto (same year).

The unnamed author arrived in Zara in 1928 as a young graduate and observed Zerauschek from a distance as one of the city's "greats." He provides the only known account of Antonio's start as a **"garzone di bottega"** (shop boy) at the **Mazzocchi** commercial establishment under partner **Tonti**. He places Antonio alongside **Luxardo**, **Drioli**, and other "musketeers" of Zaratine industry, calling him **"il vero d'Artagnan dell'industria zaratina"** — completing liquors with cigarettes and chocolate.

The article also records a single personal encounter at a Zaratini exile reunion in Florence, organised by **Don Luigi Stefani**, where Antonio arrived alone, listened without speaking, and at the end quietly handed the author a cheque to cover organisational expenses.

The copy was mailed to **Rivolta Zerauschek Liana** (= [Giuliana Zerauschek](../people/fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md)'s sister, married Rivolta) at **Via Masaccio 112, 50132 Firenze**.
