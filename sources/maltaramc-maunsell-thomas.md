# Malta RAMC — Thomas Maunsell

**URL:** https://www.maltaramc.com/regsurg/m/maunsellt.html  
**Corpus:** [sources/corpus/maltaramc-maunsell-thomas/](corpus/maltaramc-maunsell-thomas/)

Profile of Surgeon General **Thomas Maunsell** (1839–1937, Dingle, County Kerry). Used as a **comparative case** and contextual anchor for the Irish Catholic AMD career pathway.

## Relevance

Maunsell's profile establishes the full pipeline:
- Born **Dingle, County Kerry** (same county as William O'Byrne White's retirement town, Tralee)
- Attended **Cardinal Newman's Catholic University of Ireland** (Cecilia Street, Dublin) — the institution that produced Irish Catholic medical graduates for the AMD
- Commissioned AMD **1 October 1860** (five years before White)
- Rose to **Surgeon General** — the ceiling above White's Lt. Colonel grade, showing the full career arc was possible for Catholic officers
- Served Burma expedition (1886–1887) and Chitral Relief Force (1895)

The Kerry/Cecilia Street/AMD chain in Maunsell's case makes it plausible that White also trained at Cecilia Street before taking his LRCSI — though White's exact school is not yet confirmed from primary sources.
