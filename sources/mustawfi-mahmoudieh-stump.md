## Summary (from the saved email)

- **Dr. Stump** appears in a “well known Persian memoir,” mentioned as **dentist of this family** and in connection with his house outside Tehran, **Mahmoudieh**.
- **Mahmoudieh** was built by **Muhammad Shah Qajar** (reign 1834–48) toward the end of his life; he died before living there; originally called **Muhammadieh**; reputedly “unlucky”; several owners; before Stump, **Mahmoud Alamir** (title *Ehtesham al-Saltana*); Alamir renamed it Mahmoudieh and sold to **Dr. Stump**.
- Location described as between **Tajrish** (Bagh-e Ferdous) and **Evin** (area now largely built up).
- Memoir: **Abdullah Mustawfi**, *Sharh-e Zendegani-ye Man* (*An account of my life*), **3 volumes**; this material is in **vol. 1**.
