## Summary

Neighbourhood history article in Hamshahri's *Darvazeh Tehron* column. Traces the Mahmoudieh district from two competing name etymologies through the Qajar palace, the ownership chain, and Dr. Stump's acquisition and subdivision.

Key chain: in **Fath-Ali Shah**'s time, the Zaferaniyeh–Mahmoudieh lands belonged to the **Mo'ayyer al-Mamalek** family → **Mohammad Shah** built **Qasr-e Mohammadieh** in the southern portion; he **died of gout** (نقرس) upon entering it → palace abandoned → sold post-1848 to **Mohammad Rahim Khan Ala-ud-Dowleh** (*vazir-e darbar* of Naser al-Din Shah) → his son **Mahmoud Alamir** (*Ehtesham al-Saltana*) built a new palace and renamed the estate **Mahmoudieh** → sold to **Mirza Fath-Ali Khan Sahebdivan** → passed through various notables → **Dr. Ashtump** (Stump), Swiss dentist to Ahmad Shah, bought a large portion from **Mohammad Hossein Amin al-Zarb** (محمدحسین امین‌الضرب, 1872–1932) → Stump built a house and farmed there → after Stump's death, heirs subdivided into today's neighbourhood.

## Details not in other vault sources

- **Mo'ayyer al-Mamalek** land ownership in Fath-Ali Shah's era (pre-Mohammad Shah).
- Mohammad Shah died of **gout** (نقرس) on entering the palace — the Mahdavi email says "died before he could live in it"; this is a slightly different version (he entered, then died).
- Stump's seller identified as **Mohammad Hossein Amin al-Zarb** — the son (1872–1932), not the father Haj Mohammad Hassan (1837–1898).
