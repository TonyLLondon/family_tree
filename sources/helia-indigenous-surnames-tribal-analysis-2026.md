## Detailed Surname Analysis

### 1. MARILAO - Primary Indigenous Connection

**Record Volume**: 6,325+ entries (1769-2008)  
**Geographic Distribution**: Ñuble, Biobío, O'Higgins regions  
**Tribal Affiliation**: **Mapuche proper** (central Mapuche communities)  

#### Linguistic Analysis
**From Mapudungun (Mapuche language):**
- **Mari** = "ten" (complete/perfect number in Mapuche cosmology)
- **Lao/Lahuen** = "medicine/healing plant/remedy"  
- **Combined Meaning**: "Perfect healer" or "Complete medicine woman"

**Alternative Etymology:**
- **Mariluan** = "ten guanacos" (traditional wealth measurement)
- **Mari + lawün** = "ten remedies" (keeper of medicinal knowledge)

#### Tribal Territory & Cultural Context
**MAPUCHE PROPER CHARACTERISTICS:**
- **Geographic Range**: Biobío to Cautín rivers (traditional heartland)
- **Social Organization**: Patrilineal clans (lof) with territorial autonomy
- **Economic Base**: Agriculture (maize, beans, quinoa) + livestock (llamas, guanacos)
- **Spiritual System**: Shamanic traditions with female machi (medicine women)
- **Colonial Resistance**: Active participants in Arauco War (1550-1656)

**POST-WAR INTEGRATION (1656-1850):**
- **Parlamentos system** - treaty negotiations with Spanish colonial government
- **Cultural adaptation** - adoption of Spanish names while preserving indigenous surnames
- **Economic integration** - trade relationships with colonial settlements
- **Religious syncretism** - Catholic practices blended with traditional spirituality
