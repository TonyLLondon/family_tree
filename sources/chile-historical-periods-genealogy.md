# Chilean Historical Periods & Genealogical Research

**Context:** Understanding historical periods for Chilean family genealogy  
**Significance:** Our ancestors lived through major Chilean historical transitions

## Chilean Historical Timeline

### **Spanish Colonial Period (1541-1818)**

**1541-1700: Early Colonial**
- Spanish conquest and initial settlement
- Indigenous-Spanish contact and mestizaje
- Encomienda system and agricultural development

**1700-1810: Late Colonial**  
- Bourbon administrative reforms
- Established colonial society
- **Our ancestors:** Pablo Escobar (~1720s), Juan Perez (~1690s-1740s)

**1810-1818: Independence Period**
- Chilean independence movement
- Wars of liberation (O'Higgins, San Martín)
- **Our ancestors:** Justo Cerpa, José González — **lived through independence**

### **Chilean Republican Period (1818-present)**

**1818-1880: Early Republican**
- New Chilean nation-building  
- Constitutional development
- **Our ancestors:** Most documented generations (1820s-1880s)

**1880-1930: Civil Registration Era**
- Formal civil record-keeping begins
- **Our research period:** Most records from this era
- Transition from church records to civil documentation

## Genealogical Record Types by Period

### **Colonial Records (1541-1818)**
- **Catholic Church baptisms, marriages, burials**
- Parish registers (libros parroquiales)
- **Our examples:** Pablo Thimoteo Escobar baptism 1746, Theodoro Escobar baptism 1786

### **Republican Records (1818-1880)**  
- **Mixed church and early civil records**
- Increasing civil documentation
- **Our examples:** Early burial records 1840s-1860s

### **Civil Registration Era (1880-present)**
- **Formal civil birth/death/marriage registration**
- Standardized forms with parent information
- **Our examples:** Most death records showing parents (1880s-1890s)

## Research Implications

### **Record Availability**
- **Colonial period:** Church records, but fewer survive and harder access
- **Early republican:** Mixed church/civil, variable availability  
- **Civil registration era:** Systematic documentation, FamilySearch digitized

### **Genealogical Methodology**
- **Work backward** from civil registration (1880s) to colonial records
- **Cross-reference** church and civil records during transition period
- **Geographic continuity** helps trace families across historical periods

### **Historical Context Value**
Understanding these periods helps:
- **Interpret record types** and availability
- **Understand migration patterns** (colonial settlements → republican development)
- **Appreciate historical significance** of multi-generational family continuity
