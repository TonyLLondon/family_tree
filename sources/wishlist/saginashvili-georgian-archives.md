# Saginashvili — Georgian National Archives remote research request

**Goal:** Trace the Saginashvili (საგინაშვილი) family in Tbilisi-area church and civil records before Daoud Khan's 1811 flight to Iran, and identify his parents.

People: [Daoud Khan Saginian](../../people/daoud-khan-saginian.md) · [Anna Saginian](../../people/anna-saginian.md)

## Background

Daoud Khan Saginian (David Saginashvili / Saginskilli) was born in 1790 in Tbilisi, Georgia (Yaghoubian 2014, n. 5). He served in the Russian army after the 1801 annexation of Georgia, then fled to Qajar Iran in early 1811 with his brother **Zaal Saginashvili** and companion Solayman Khan Saham al-Dowleh. In Iran, the family integrated into the Armenian community, the surname shifted from Georgian **Saginashvili/Saginskilli** to Armenian **Saginian**, and Daoud Khan rose to the rank of Sartip (Brigadier General) under Fath Ali Shah.

He died in 1867 in Tabriz (Yaghoubian 2014, n. 10) and was buried in the Saginian family mausoleum at Surb Astvatsatsin (Holy Mother of God church). His parents are completely unknown. This is the deepest dead end on the Burgess/Saginian branch.

## Parish register search tool

The Georgian National Archives has an online parish register search at:
**[archival-services.gov.ge/saeklesio/](https://archival-services.gov.ge/saeklesio/)**

This covers the **Metrical books of the Georgian-Imereti Synod Cantor** (1819–1921). Relevant districts to search:

| District | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **Tbilisi** (city) | Primary — where the family lived |
| **Tbilisi District** | Broader surrounding area |
| **Mtskheta** | Ancient capital, 20km north of Tbilisi, seat of the Catholicos |
| **Gori District** | Major military garrison; Saginashvilis served in the Russian army |

Note: the metric books start at **1819**, eight years after Daoud Khan fled. His baptism (c. 1790s) predates this collection. But family members who remained in Georgia would appear in these registers.

```text
Dear colleagues at the National Archives of Georgia,

I am conducting genealogical research into the Saginashvili (საგინაშვილი) family of Tbilisi and would like to request a thematic search of your holdings.

THE FAMILY

Davit (David) Saginashvili (დავით საგინაშვილი), also known as Daoud Khan Saginian in Persian and Armenian sources, was born in 1790 in Tbilisi (Yaghoubian 2014, citing oral interviews and Ra’in’s Iraniyan-i Armani). He had a younger brother named Zaal Saginashvili (ზაალ საგინაშვილი). Both brothers attended a Russian military academy in Tbilisi as teenagers following the 1801 annexation of Georgia. In early 1811, they left Georgia — reportedly obtaining leave from their Russian commander on a pretext — and travelled to Iran via Akhalkalaki, Yerevan, and Gyumri, arriving in Tabriz. A family memoir describes them as belonging to the noble Saginashvili family of Georgia.

In Iran, David entered Qajar military service under Crown Prince Abbas Mirza and rose to the rank of Sartip (Brigadier General), commanding Iranian forces in Shiraz and Isfahan under Fath Ali Shah. He also held the titles farmandeh (supreme military commander) of those provinces and sarperast-e Aramane (guardian of the Armenians). He died in 1867 in Tabriz and was buried in the Saginian family mausoleum at Surb Astvatsatsin (Holy Mother of God church), Tabriz. The family adopted the Armenian form of the surname, Saginian, in Iran.

His parents’ names are unknown. Confirming these and finding family history in Georgia is the primary research objective.

DESCENDANTS (for context)

David’s oldest son, Goorgen Khan Saginian, served as a sarhang (colonel) in the Qajar army and died in his forties. Goorgen Khan’s son Solayman Khan Saginian (d. 1913, Tabriz) served as sarhang and head of the telegraph system in Iranian Azerbaijan from 1875, and was also buried at Surb Astvatsatsin. Solayman Khan’s son Zohrab Saginian (b. 1883, Tabriz; d. 1970) served as Armenian representative to the Iranian Majles from 1924 to 1943. The family’s prominence in Iran is well documented, but their Georgian origins before 1811 remain almost entirely unknown.

WHAT I AM LOOKING FOR

1. Church registers (metric books): Any entries for the surname საგინაშვილი (Saginashvili) or variant spellings (Сагинашвили, Сагинскилли) in Tbilisi-area parish registers — births, marriages, deaths. The priority period is 1780–1815, but any entries for this surname in any period would be valuable, as the name appears to be uncommon.

2. Military records: Any mention of Davit or Zaal Saginashvili in records of the Tbilisi Military Governor (Fond 16, 1801–1860), including service records, muster rolls, military academy enrolments, or records relating to their departure from Georgia in 1811.

3. Nobility registers: Any Saginashvili entries in the Tbilisi Assembly of Noble Deputies (Fond 213) or the Head of Nobility records (Fond 214), which would clarify the family’s claimed noble status.

4. Anchiskhati Church records (Fond 1683, from 1806) and Sioni Cathedral records (Fond 1682, from 1817): any Saginashvili entries in either church.

SPECIFIC FONDS OF INTEREST

- Fond 16 — Chancellery of Civil Administration of the Tbilisi Military Governor (1801–1860)
- Fond 213 — Tbilisi Assembly of Noble Deputies (1818–1917)
- Fond 214 — Head of the Nobility of the Tbilisi Governorate (1817–1917)
- Fond 489 — Georgian-Imeretian Synodal Office (1814–1917)
- Fond 1682 — Sioni Cathedral, Tbilisi (1817–1921)
- Fond 1683 — Anchiskhati Church, Tbilisi (1806–1921)

PAYMENT AND DELIVERY

Please let me know any fees for this work in advance. I am happy to pay appropriate and reasonable costs.

SCHOLARLY CONTEXT

The Saginashvili/Saginian family is documented in peer-reviewed scholarship:

- Yaghoubian, David N. (2014). Ethnicity, Identity, and the Development of Nationalism in Iran. Syracuse University Press. Chapter 4, pp. 125–127, endnotes 5–11. (Based on oral interviews with Sevak Saginian, 1995–1998. Confirms David Khan born Tbilisi 1790, died Tabriz 1867, and the family lineage through Goorgen Khan, Solayman Khan, and Zohrab.)

- Maeda, Hirotake (2019). “Lives of the Enikolopians.” In Amanat & Ashraf (eds.), The Persianate World. Brill. pp. 169–195. (Names David Saginean and Zaal Saginean as Georgian companions who fled to Iran in 1811.)

- Ra’in, Isma’il. Iraniyan-i Armani (Armenians of Iran). p. 47. (Contains a portrait painting of Sartip David Khan Saginian with a Persian-language caption confirming his military commands and death at age 78.)

- Wolff, Joseph (1845). Narrative of a Mission to Bokhara. Chapter XXVI. (Identifies Daoud Khan as “a Colonel in the Russian service… a genuine Georgian” in Tabriz, 1843.)

The Saginashvili name appears to be relatively uncommon, which may make the search more tractable. I would be very grateful for any information you can provide about this family’s Georgian origins.

Thank you for your time.

With kind regards,
Tony Lewis
```
