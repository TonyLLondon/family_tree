## DIGITISED — viewable online

### PROB 11/1865/205 — Will of Dr John Cormick (1836)

**Prerogative Court of Canterbury**
Will of John Cormick, "late Surgeon on the Madras Establishment at Mearneh of Khorosan, Persia", proved 6 August 1836.
Cormick was physician to Crown Prince Abbas Mirza at Tabriz — Edward Burgess's predecessor and colleague in the British community. The will may name executors or beneficiaries from the Tabriz circle.

- TNA ref: PROB 11/1865/205 (ID: D282237)

## HIGH PRIORITY ADDITIONS (January 2026)

### **Opars Persian Gazette Subscription — Edward's Actual Newspaper**

**🏆 PRIORITY 1** — Access to **all 471 issues** of the *Ruznameh-ye Vaqaye-e Ettefaqiyeh* (1851-1861) that Edward edited.

- **URL:** https://www.opars.com/collection/ruznamah-i-vaqayi%CA%BB-i-ittifaqiyah-qajar/
- **Content:** Complete digitized Persian gazette (1267-1277 H.Q. / 1851-1861 CE)
- **Significance:** Edward's own publication - **"Burjīs Ṣāḥib"** (برجیس صاحب) listed as translator
- **Details:** 600-700 print run, published Thursdays, printed by **Haji Abd al-Mohammad** (same person Edward threatened about deadlines!)
- **Value:** See Edward's actual editorial work, publication changes (Friday→Thursday), and Iran's first systematic exposure to European news
- **Status:** 💰 Requires purchase/subscription through Opars (Persian digital archives)

## Not yet searched / future angles

- **National Probate Calendar** (post-1858, gov.uk) — Edward's will if proved by his widow Mary Ann after 1858. If he died c. 1855 (before civil probate), his will would be in PROB 11 but none of the indexed results match "Edward Burgess of Persia". The will may never have been proved in England, or may be indexed under his residence in England rather than his place of death.
- **FO 96** — Drafts and minutes on Persia: FO 96/2/19 (1838), FO 96/5/7 (1845), FO 96/9/12 (1850), FO 96/11/13 (1852), FO 96/24/47 (1855) — may name Burgess in internal FO discussions
- **FO 566** — FO registers of correspondence (index to FO 60) — would list Burgess by name in dispatch summaries. Not individually indexed in Discovery but available at Kew.
- **London Gazette** — Edward's consular appointment announcement (searchable at thegazette.co.uk). Also search for Charles Henry Burgess (brother, Leicestershire) and Henry Burgess (father, merchant/bankruptcy).
- **FO 560** — Bushire consulate registers of births, baptisms, marriages, deaths (1849–1947) — in case any Burgess family events were registered at Bushire rather than Tabriz
- **T 1 / T 38** — Treasury papers and departmental accounts: consular pay for Tabriz, 1830s–1850s. Would confirm Burgess's official status and salary.
- **FO 366** — FO establishment files: staffing lists for Persia consulates. Would confirm Burgess's title (agent, vice-consul, dragoman) and dates of service.
- **TS 59/66** — Tabriz Consulate: rent, ownership etc (1918–1926). Too late for Edward but might reference the earlier history of the consulate building.
- **RGS correspondence** — Keith Edward Abbott (consul at Tabriz) corresponded with the Royal Geographical Society from Tabriz in 1856–58 (RGS/CB4/2). Abbott was Edward Burgess's superior. His papers might reference Burgess.
