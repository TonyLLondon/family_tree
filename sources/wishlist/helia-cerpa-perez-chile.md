# Wishlist — Helia Cerpa Pérez & Chilean exile sources

Outstanding sources for [Helia Cerpa-Pérez](../../people/helia-cerpa-perez.md) (`I19`). Current evidence establishes three documentary milestones: detention at the Estadio Nacional (1973), formal ban from re-entering Chile (1985), and decades of Camden housing advocacy (2010–2023). The gaps are in between.

---

## 1. Italian embassy refugee roster — Archivo Nacional de Chile

**What:** The Comisión Chilena de Derechos Humanos archive, held at the Archivo Nacional, contains the actual refugee rosters (*nóminas de refugiados*) from different embassies in Santiago, 1973–1975 — including the Italian embassy. Stored in **caja 271**.

**Why it matters:** Would confirm whether Helia entered the Italian embassy on calle Miguel Claro 1359, Providencia, and when she left Chile. This is the single most important unlocated source.

**Access:** Archivo Nacional de Chile, Miraflores 50, Santiago. Contact: `consultas@archivonacional.gob.cl`. Online catalog: [archivonacional.gob.cl](https://www.archivonacional.gob.cl/). The [exile exhibition page](https://www.archivonacional.gob.cl/galeria/exilio-chileno-durante-la-dictadura-civil-militar-archivos-de-la-represion-y-de-la) confirms the holding.

**Action:** Request a search for "Cerpa Pérez, Helia del Carmen" in the Italian embassy refugee roster, caja 271. May require citing the relevant fondo and series.

## 3. Intermediate banned lists (1986–1988) — Contraloría / Museo de la Memoria

**What:** The individual decrees that removed people from the *Prohibición de ingreso* list, or the intermediate lists themselves (after the 6th edition where Helia appears). The list shrank from 4,360 → 3,878 → 3,717 → 623 → final 512.

**Why it matters:** Would establish exactly when Helia's ban was lifted — whether in the mass reductions of 1987 or in the final Decree Nº 203 of 1 September 1988.

**Access:**
- **Museo de la Memoria y los Derechos Humanos** — holds 18+ digitised prohibition decrees from the Contraloría General series (archivommdh.cl). The full series may contain the removal decree naming Helia. Contact: `archivo@mmdh.cl`.
- **Contraloría General de la República** — original decree files. Contact: [contraloria.cl](https://www.contraloria.cl/).
- **FASIC archive** (Fundación de Ayuda Social de las Iglesias Cristianas) — holds Comité Pro-Retorno de Exiliados (COPROREX) working papers. May contain case-level exile data. Contact: [fasic.cl](http://www.fasic.cl/).

**Action:** Search for "Cerpa Pérez, Helia del Carmen" across the Contraloría decree series at archivommdh.cl and request the Museo de la Memoria archive search the full prohibition-decree collection.

## 4. Piero De Masi memoir — *Santiago. 1 febbraio 1973 – 27 gennaio 1974*

**What:** Personal memoir by the Italian chargé d'affaires who sheltered ~700 people and evacuated ~600 to Italy. Published by Bonanno Editore (2013); Spanish edition by Editorial Cuadernos Del Laberinto (2017).

**Why it matters:** May name individual refugees who passed through the embassy. Covers February 1973 – January 1974, the window during which Helia would have been evacuated.

**Access:** Purchase. Italian edition ISBN likely findable via Bonanno Editore. Spanish edition: [cuadernosdelaberinto.com](http://www.cuadernosdelaberinto.com/autores/paginas/piero_de_masi.html).

**Action:** Purchase or library-borrow. Search index and text for "Cerpa" or any general roster of refugees.

## 5. *Cien voces rompen el silencio* — Kunstmann & Torres

**What:** *Cien voces rompen el silencio: testimonios de expresas y expresos políticos de la dictadura militar en Chile (1973–1990)*, Wally Kunstmann Torres & Victoria Torres. DIBAM, 2008, 730 pp; FCE, 2023, 715 pp. Over 100 first-person testimonies from political prisoners.

**Why it matters:** Kunstmann is the president of the Corporación Estadio Nacional Memoria Nacional — the same person who holds the 405-name women's list that includes Helia. Helia may have contributed a testimony, or other testimonies from the camarín de mujeres may name her.

**Access:** Purchase (FCE 2023 edition). Also available in Chilean university libraries.

**Action:** Search index for "Cerpa" and read the women's camarín testimonies for any mention of Helia or other Cerpa names.

## 6. *Camarines de Mujeres* — Vidaurrazaga & Pequeño (2019)

**What:** *Camarines de Mujeres: Memorias de prisioneras políticas del Estadio Nacional*, Tamara Vidaurrazaga & Andrea Pequeño. Instituto de la Mujer / Fondart, 2019, 268 pp. Seven women's testimonies from the camarín de mujeres.

**Why it matters:** The most detailed published account of women's detention in the camarín. May reference Helia or her cell-mates.

**Access:** [PDF on academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu/39899589/Camarines_de_Mujeres_Memorias_de_prisioneras_pol%C3%ADticas_del_Estadio_Nacional). Already cited in person page but not yet read in full.

**Action:** Read in full. Search for "Cerpa" and any references to women from Parral or the Maule region.

## 7. Corporación Estadio Nacional — memorial plaques and extended list

**What:** The Corporación has recovered 507 names (as of March 2019) of women detained at the Estadio Nacional. Copper memorial plaques bearing "some" of these names are installed in the Camarín Norte. The full 507-name list may be longer than the 405 published by Cambio21.

**Why it matters:** Would confirm whether Helia's name is on the physical memorial and provide the complete recovered list (vs the 405 published in 2018).

**Access:** Corporación Estadio Nacional Memoria Nacional Ex Prisioneros Políticos. Website: [memoriaestadionacional.cl](https://www.memoriaestadionacional.cl/). President: Wally Kunstmann Torres. Contact via the website or through the Museo de la Memoria.

**Action:** Contact the Corporación to (a) confirm Helia's name on the plaques and (b) obtain the full 507-name list. Also ask whether the Corporación holds any detail on individual women's arrest and release circumstances.

## 8. *Santiago, Italia* documentary — Moretti (2018)

**What:** Documentary by Nanni Moretti on the Italian embassy operation. Interviews refugees now living in Italy and the diplomats who saved them. David di Donatello Best Documentary 2019.

**Why it matters:** May visually identify or name Helia among the embassy refugees, particularly if it includes group photographs or registry footage from the embassy period.

**Access:** Streaming or DVD. Italian release; widely available in European markets.

**Action:** Watch and note any references to refugees from Parral/Maule, any group photographs, and any embassy documentation shown on screen.

## 9. Carlos Cerpa Miranda — confirm family connection

**What:** Carlos Cerpa Miranda appears immediately above Helia on page 12 of the 1985 banned list. He is now a political opinion columnist for *El Mostrador* and *El Maipo*, described as *"Ex concejal y ex director laboral Banco del Estado."*

**Why it matters:** The Cerpa surname has only ~3,000 bearers in Chile. A former state-bank labour director and municipal councilman who was banned alongside Helia is very likely a paternal-line relative. Also on the Valech I list: **Cerpa Miranda, Manuel Jesús** (p. 57) — same double surname, therefore a brother of Carlos. Two Cerpa Mirandas targeted by the regime alongside Helia.

**Access:** Carlos Cerpa Miranda is a public figure, contactable via *El Mostrador* or *El Maipo*.

**Action:** Family to contact directly, or cross-reference Chilean civil registration for shared grandparents (Francisco Cerpa / Ana González are Helia's paternal grandparents).

## 10. UK arrival / Home Office records

**What:** Helia's entry into the United Kingdom — Home Office asylum or refugee records, JWG/WUS resettlement programme files.

**Why it matters:** Would establish when she arrived in Britain, whether via the JWG programme or independently, and her legal status on entry.

**Access:**
- **The National Archives (TNA)** — Home Office: Aliens Department files (HO series). JWG records may be in TNA or at the Living Refugee Archive (University of East London).
- **Living Refugee Archive** (UEL) — holds JWG and WUS records. Website: [livingrefugeearchive.org](https://www.livingrefugeearchive.org/). Has oral histories from Chilean refugees.

**Action:** Search the Living Refugee Archive catalog for JWG case files or resettlement records that might name Helia. Consider whether an oral-history interview from a family member would be accepted by the archive.
