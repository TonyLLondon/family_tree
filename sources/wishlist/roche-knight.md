## What is already documented

- **Burke’s *History of the Commoners* (1833)** — under Thomas Roche of Limerick: **William**, solicitor in Dublin, m. **Eliza**, another daughter and co-heiress of **— Knight**, esq.; her brother **Stephen** m. **Catherine**, co-heiress of the same Knight. Extract: [Roche of Limerick — Forgotten Victorians](../roche-of-limerick-forgotten-victorians.md) · [corpus](../corpus/roche-of-limerick-forgotten-victorians-da2674483d/).
- **legacy export** links Catherine to **F68** (William × Eliza, marriage **1 Mar 1832**, St Andrew’s, Westland Row); her **1841 Dublin birth** in the export has **no attached source** (unlike 1911 census / 1937 death).
- **FamilySearch Family Tree** id on `@I136@` in the anchor legacy export: **`GQFY-HS8`** — may carry user citations worth mining or replacing with primary images.

Burke proves **the couple**; it does **not** list **issue** for William and Eliza. Proving **Catherine** is their daughter needs a **primary** record naming her with those parents.

## Priority — primary records to obtain

1. **Dublin RC baptism ~1841** — **Catherine (Mary) Roche**; parents should read **William Roche** and **Eliza** / **Elizabeth Knight**. Search [IrishGenealogy.ie](https://www.irishgenealogy.ie) church records, NLI registers, Findmypast. Parish unknown until indexed hits appear.
2. **Marriage 1 Mar 1832** — **St Andrew’s, Westland Row** (**F68**). Confirms **Eliza Knight** as bride and ties the legacy export family to Burke’s solicitor.
3. **Bombay Presidency marriage 8 Feb 1869** — William O’Byrne White × Catherine Mary Roche. Register image may give **father’s name** and residence. Overlaps [white-india.md](white-india.md) Priority 4.
4. **Will or administration** — **William Roche d. 14 Apr 1894 Dublin**; may name **Catherine** (e.g. as Mrs White). National Archives of Ireland / calendars of wills.
5. **Civil death 1937 Tralee, Kerry** — legacy export cites Irish deaths index **vol. 5, p. 412**; full register entry sometimes weak on parents for the elderly but useful for age, informant, consistency.

## Pitfall

- **William Roche (c. 1814–1894) of Jamestown, Glenmore, Kilkenny** (m. Mary Murphy 1844) is a **different** man from the **Dublin solicitor**; do not merge on dates alone.
