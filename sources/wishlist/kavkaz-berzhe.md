## The newspaper *Kavkaz* (Кавказ)

**What it is:** The first Russian-language newspaper published in the Caucasus, based in Tiflis (Tbilisi). Published 1846–1918 (~16,400 issues, ~71,400 pages). Founded on the initiative of Prince Mikhail Vorontsov, the imperial Governor-General. Covered official documents, regional news, and historical / archaeological / cultural writing by Caucasian intellectuals.

**Digital archive:** [Kavkaz Digital Archive](https://gpa.eastview.com/kvz/) (East View). Full-image pages with searchable Old Russian text. Requires institutional subscription.

**Access routes:**
- University library database (East View Global Press Archive) — free via institutional login
- British Library reading rooms (where Tony originally spotted it)
- Inter-library loan request for specific issue ranges

## Adolf Petrovich Berzhe (1828–1886)

Russian Orientalist historian and archaeographer specialising in the Caucasus. Studied Oriental languages at St Petersburg University; dispatched to the Caucasus 1851 to work in Vorontsov's chancellery. Chairman of the Caucasian Archaeographical Commission (Tiflis) 1864–1886. Published in *Kavkaz*, *Russkaya Starina*, and the *Caucasian Calendar*.

**Wikipedia:** [Adolf Berzhe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolf_Berzhe)
