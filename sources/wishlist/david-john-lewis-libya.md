# David John Lewis — Libya / Tripolitania sources wishlist

Person: [David John Lewis](../../people/david-john-lewis.md) · Topic: [British Administration of Tripolitania and the Rise of King Idris](../../topics/libya-tripolitania-british-administration.md)

**Known:** Appointed HM Colonial Administrative Service 1948. Seconded to Foreign Office Administration of African Territories. District Officer in charge of Sabratha District, Tripolitania (1948–1950). Magistrate, Western Province, Tripolitania (1951). OHMS appointment Jan 1949. Thomas Cook travel documents (1950), gun licences (1950–51), customs paperwork in the manual inbox.

**In vault:** 13 photographs from the 1949 Emir Idris reception ([libya-idris-1949/](../../media/docs/david-john-lewis-colonial-service/libya-idris-1949/)); CVs citing the posting; topic page.

---

## Priority 1 — TNA (archive visit or paid download)

| Source | Reference | What it gives |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Foreign Office Administration of African Territories — registered files** | [FO 1015](https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C8314) (1,033 files, 1915–1952) | District Officer reports, intelligence summaries, and administrative correspondence from the British administration of Tripolitania and Cyrenaica. Lewis's reports from Sabratha District (1948–1950) would be filed here. Responsibility transferred from War Office to Foreign Office in 1949. Open records at Kew; not digitised. Keyword searches in Discovery for "Sabratha" or "Lewis" within this series may narrow the file list. |
| **Four-power Commission: Tripolitania** | [FO 1015/102](https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C3501475) | Report of the Four-power Commission of Investigation for the Former Italian Colonies: Tripolitania (1948). Context for the political situation when Lewis arrived. |
| **Four-power Commission: Libya** | [FO 1015/104](https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C3501477) | Report on Libya (1948). |
| **Embassy and Residency, Libya — general correspondence** | [FO 1021](https://discovery.nationalarchives.gov.uk/details/r/C8320) (1947–1970) | British Residency in Benghazi correspondence covering the BMA–Emirate–Kingdom transition. May contain references to district-level staff or events. |

## Priority 2 — published histories

| Source | What it gives | Cost / access | Status |
|--------|---------------|---------------|--------|
| **Richard Synge, *Operation Idris: Inside the British Administration of Cyrenaica and Libya, 1942–52*** (Silphium Press, 2015; new edition 2021, 248 pp., 30+ photographs) | Based on the diaries of the author's father (a BMA administrator). Covers British political strategy, the Anglo-Senussi relationship, and the transition to independence. Might name district officers or describe the Tripolitanian reception ceremonies visible in the family photographs. | £18.00 / £13.50 for BILNAS members. [Publisher](https://www.bilnas.org/shop/silphium-press/operation-idris-inside-the-british-administration-of-cyrenaica-and-libya-1942-52-new-edition/) · [Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/Operation-Idris-British-Administration-Cyrenaica/dp/1900971259) | Not yet acquired |
| **Adrian Pelt, *Libyan Independence and the United Nations: A Case of Planned Decolonization*** (Yale University Press, 1970) | The UN Commissioner's own account of shepherding Libya to independence 1949–1951. Pelt toured districts and met local officials; there is a chance he names the District Officer at Sabratha. | Secondhand / university libraries. [LIAS overview](https://liasinstitute.com/libyan-independence-and-the-united-nations-by-adrian-pelt/) | Not yet acquired |

## Priority 3 — archives and collections

| Source | What it gives | Where to look |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| **Blackley papers** | Papers of Brigadier Travers Robert Blackley (1899–1982), Chief Administrator of Tripolitania 1943–1951 — Lewis's ultimate superior. Staff lists, administrative orders, and correspondence might name district officers by posting. | King's College London Archives |
| **British Institute for Libyan & Northern African Studies (BILNAS)** | The institute published *Operation Idris* and maintains a library and research network focused on Libya. A direct enquiry about British District Officers in Tripolitania 1948–1951 might yield leads or connect with researchers who have worked in FO 1015. | [bilnas.org](https://www.bilnas.org/) |
| **Society for Libyan Studies** | Scholarly society with close links to the archaeological work at Sabratha (Kenyon/Ward-Perkins). May hold or know of administrative records that overlap with the excavation period. | [societyforlibyanstudies.org](https://www.societyforlibyanstudies.org/) |
