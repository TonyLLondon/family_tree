## Key passage — Catherine Mary Roche's parents (p. 4 of extract)

Under the entry for **Thomas Roche of Limerick** (son of Stephen Roche; m. Hellen Ankettle):

> "**3. William, a solicitor in Dublin, married Eliza, another daughter and co-heir of — Knight, esq.**"

This matches GEDCOM `@I136@`'s parents **exactly**: Catherine Mary Roche's father is recorded as **William Roche** and mother as **Eliza Knight**. The Burke's entry confirms:
- William Roche was a **solicitor practising in Dublin**
- Eliza's maiden name was **Knight** — she was co-heiress of a "— Knight, esq." (first name not given in Burke's) with her sister Catherine (who married Stephen Roche above)
- The Roche family seated at **Granagh Castle, Kilkenny**; earlier seats in **Limerick and Clare**

## Significance

- Establishes Catherine Mary Roche as part of the documented **Roche of Limerick** Catholic merchant-gentry family (Burke's 1833)
- Her father William's career as a **Dublin solicitor** is echoed two generations later in her son **Gerald Sebastian White** becoming a solicitor in Madras — a family professional tradition in law
- The **Knight** co-heiress connection (Eliza and her sister Catherine both married Roche brothers from the same family) is a tight cousin-network marriage typical of Catholic gentry in this period
