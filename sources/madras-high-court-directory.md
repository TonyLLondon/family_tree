# Madras High Court — Officers of the Court (printed directory, ~1930s)

## Dating

Chief Justice **Sir H.O.C. Beasley** held office **1929–1937** (knighted 1930; succeeded by Sir Alfred Leach 1937). The directory reads "Sir H.O.C. Beasley" — the "Sir" establishes it was printed **after the knighthood in 1930**, so the directory dates to **1930–1937**.

The same directory lists Puisne Judges including S. Burn, I.C.S., V. Mockett, Barr., V. Pandrang Rao, I.C.S., and others — a mix of ICS and Bar appointments typical of the late colonial High Court bench.

## Gerald White as Registrar

The Registrar of the High Court was the senior administrative law officer of the court — responsible for case management, court records, and official proceedings — ranking directly below the judges. This was not an ICS post: Gerald's obituary identifies him as a **Solicitor** (qualified private practitioner), suggesting he was appointed as a legally qualified administrator rather than a civil servant.

The Registrar functioned as the institutional memory and procedural backbone of the court, a position requiring both legal knowledge and administrative continuity.
