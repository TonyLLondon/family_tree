# Nómina de Exonerados Políticos — Montos Pagados Julio 2010

Transparency disclosure of all beneficiaries of Chile's **exonerado político** reparation programme, released under the *Ley de Transparencia* (Law 20.285, 2008). Published on Scribd as "Consejo Trans Par en CIA CELPA1 Ver2". Lists beneficiary name, former employer, date of political dismissal, and monthly pension amount (Chilean pesos) for July 2010. The programme, established by **Ley 19.234** (1993), provides reparation pensions to persons dismissed from public-sector or state-enterprise employment for political reasons during the Pinochet dictatorship (11 Sep 1973 – 10 Mar 1990). Approximately 157,624 beneficiaries as of 2010.

**Helia del Carmen Cerpa Pérez** appears as **"CERPA PEREZ HELIA DEL CARMEN"** — dismissed from the **Servicio de Registro Civil e Identificación** (SRCEI) on **16 April 1974**, receiving 140,868 CLP/month (~US$270). The SRCEI is Chile's civil registry, founded 1884, responsible for births, marriages, deaths, identity cards, and passports. Under Allende it was headed by Heriberto Benquis Camhi (PS, 1971–11 Sep 1973); the junta replaced him the day after the coup. The service operates regional offices in every commune, including Parral — Helia's birthplace. She was 27 when Allende took office and 30 at the coup.

**Marcial Cerpa González** also appears — dismissed from **SOCOAGRO** (agricultural cooperative) on 30 November 1973 (279,792 CLP). Shares the exact double surname of Helia's father [Miguel Cerpa González](../people/miguel-cerpa-gonzalez-i346.md) — almost certainly his brother, and another family member purged in the first weeks of the dictatorship.
