# OAS IACHR — Chile 1985: Right of Residence and Movement

Chapter VI(a) of the OAS Inter-American Commission on Human Rights report on Chile (OEA/Ser.L/V/II.66 Doc.17, 9 September 1985). Documents the regime's systematic violation of the right to residence and movement through expulsions, entry bans, and forced exile.

The report describes the **banned-list system** under which Helia Cerpa Pérez was prohibited from entering Chile. The "most recent list known to the Commission" (dated September 1985) contained **3,878 names** — the same list reproduced in the *Cartilla del Retornado* Nº 3. The IACHR declared these measures a violation of **Article VIII of the American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man**.

Key passages:

- **§55–57:** The September 1984 list of 4,982 persons was delivered to airlines, which had to consult International Police before selling tickets to Chile. By September 1985, the list had shrunk to 3,878.
- **§58:** "The question for the exiles whose names appear on that list persists, since they do not know with certainty what has been the action, the event, the situation or even which hidden enemy is the one who has motivated their inclusion on it."
- **§60:** "The system of lists of names of persons whose entry into the national territory is prohibited continues to be applied... in practice, Chilean authorities themselves recommend that before traveling to Chile exiles ascertain whether or not there will be impediments to their return."

Legal instruments: **Decreto Ley 81** (1973), **Decreto Ley 604** (10 August 1974), **24th Transitory Provision** of the 1980 Constitution. Case law established these powers as discretionary and exclusive to the Executive; the Judiciary repeatedly declined jurisdiction.
