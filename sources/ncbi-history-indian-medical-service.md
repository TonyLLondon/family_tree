# NCBI — History of the Indian Medical Service

**URL:** https://ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5165998/
**Corpus:** [sources/corpus/ncbi-history-indian-medical-service/](corpus/ncbi-history-indian-medical-service/)

Peer-reviewed article on the history of the Indian Medical Service. Secondary source for the institutional structure of the AMD/IMS and rank progression in the period relevant to William O'Byrne White (1865–1885).

## Key facts for this vault

**Pre-1892 rank sequence (IMS/AMD):**
- Assistant Surgeon → Surgeon → Surgeon Major
- 1892: medical ranks merged with military ranks (Lieutenant, Captain, Major)
- 1898: medical ranks discontinued in favour of full military ranks
- 1897: IMS unified from three Presidency services

**Irish representation:**
- Ireland-born officers: 10.5% of IMS (1837–1854), rising to **26.2%** (1855–1884), then 12.6% (1885–1896)
- William O'Byrne White's service (1865–1885) falls exactly in the **peak Irish period (26.2%)**
