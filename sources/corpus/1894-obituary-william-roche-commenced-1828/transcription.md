# Death of Mr William Roche, Solicitor

*Unknown newspaper (possibly Freeman's Journal or Irish Times)*, c. 14 April 1894

---

We deeply regret to announce the death, at the advanced age of 88, of Mr **William Roche**, of 72 Harcourt-street, senior partner in the firm of **William Roche and Sons**, Solicitors. The deceased gentleman had a long and honourable career in his profession, and was for many years **Crown Solicitor for the County and City of Limerick**, and **Solicitor for the War Department in Ireland**. He had been **President of the Incorporated Law Society**, and for many years **Chairman of the Solicitors' Benevolent Association**. During the lengthened period of his professional life, which commenced in the year **1828**, he won the respect and esteem of all with whom he was associated, and we are confident that his death will be regretted by a large circle of professional and private friends.

---

**Notes:**

- Says career "commenced in the year 1828" — one year earlier than the Limerick Chronicle version ("Trinity term of 1829"). Possibly admitted as an apprentice or clerk in 1828, formally admitted as solicitor in 1829.
- Same four offices as the Limerick Chronicle: Crown Solicitor for Limerick, Solicitor for the War Department, President of the Incorporated Law Society, Chairman of the Solicitors' Benevolent Association.
