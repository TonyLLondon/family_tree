## Notes

### The family stabilised

By 1871 the older daughter Margaret (who appeared on the 1861 census age 6) has died — she was buried at St Fagan's in December 1863. The four surviving children are Elizabeth (11), [Catherine](../../people/catherine-griffiths.md) (9), Margaret (5, a second daughter given the same name), and David (2).

### Thomas's occupation

Still an **Iron Works Lab** (ironworks labourer) — the same occupation as 1861. By 1881 he would shift to **Colliery Lab** as the local economy transitioned from iron to coal.

### Anne's birthplace: "Llan Ferbyn"

The enumerator wrote **"Llan Ferbyn, Carmarthenshire"** — an unusual rendering. On the 1881 census the same place is written as "Llanfair". This is probably **Llanfair-ar-y-bryn** near Llandovery, though Llanfihangel-ar-arth remains possible without a certificate. The 1861 enumerator left her birth town blank.

### Address continuity

Still on **Clive Street** (called "Clive Place" in 1861), Cwmdare. No boarder this time — the Thomas Thomas who lodged in 1861 has moved on.
