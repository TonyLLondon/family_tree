## Notes

- **Alma** (I106, née Newcomb, first married Coolbear, now **South**) has remarried after George Gridley's death in 1892. Her second husband **Reuben South** is a farm labourer from **Tolleshunt Knights** — the same village as Alma's own 1891 birthplace. They likely knew each other from the local community. The marriage probably took place ~1894 (Henry South born ~1895 in Tollesbury).
- **Alma's birthplace** is given here as **Tiptree, Essex** — not Tolleshunt Knights as in 1891. Tiptree is the neighbouring village (~3 miles). Both are small settlements in the Blackwater area; the variation may reflect different parts of the same rural area, or Alma may have been born at the boundary.
- **Archibald Coolbear** (I103) — age 9, born ~1892, Maldon. Listed as Reuben's **step-son**. This is the future WWI soldier and RAF airman, currently a child on an Essex farm. He would later become a chimney sweep and enlist in the Essex Regiment in December 1914.
- **Harold Coolbear** (~1882) — now 19, working as a dayman on the farm alongside his stepfather. This is the Harold A from the 1891 census.
- **Ernest Coolbear** (~1890) — age 11, still at school.
- **Henry South** (~1895, Tollesbury) — Alma and Reuben's son together. Born in Tollesbury, suggesting the family lived there before moving to Little Totham.
- **Missing Coolbear children:** Kate Alma (~1877), Florence Ann (~1880), Alice E (~1884), Lily R (~1886), and Jubilee Victoria (~1888) are all absent — likely in domestic service or married. Kate would be ~24, Florence ~21.
- The household has moved from **Maldon** (1891) to **Little Totham** — deeper into the Essex farming country around the Blackwater estuary.
