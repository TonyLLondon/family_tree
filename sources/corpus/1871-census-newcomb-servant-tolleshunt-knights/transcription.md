## Notes

- **Alma Newcomb** (I106) — age 16, servant at Park Farm. Born **Tolleshunt Knights**, confirming her birthplace from the 1891 census exactly. Age 16 gives birth year ~1855 (consistent with ~1854 from other censuses — the one-year variance is normal for Victorian age reporting).
- She is serving on a substantial farm — 117 acres, employing 3 men and a boy. John Tanner is from Thaxted (north Essex), his wife Harriet from Hertfordshire. The Tanner children were born in Tolleshunt Knights, so the family had been at Park Farm for at least five years.
- **Same pattern as Ann Gridley:** Both women who married into the Coolbear family were in domestic service as teenagers before marriage. Ann Gridley was a farm servant at 15 in Bicknacre; Alma Newcomb is a farm servant at 16 in her home village. Alma married George Gridley Coolbear ~1879, about eight years after this census.
- Alma's **parents** — the Newcomb family — are not in this household. They should be findable in Tolleshunt Knights in earlier censuses (1861, 1851).
- The 1901 census (after George Gridley's death) gives Alma's birthplace as **Tiptree** — Tiptree is adjacent to Tolleshunt Knights, so "Tolleshunt Knights" and "Tiptree" refer to essentially the same area.
