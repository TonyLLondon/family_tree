# Southern Reporter / Dublin Evening Mail, April 1855 — Roche as Viscount Fermoy claimants

Published in the *Southern Reporter* (26 Apr 1855) and reprinted from the *Dublin Evening Mail*, shortly after **Edmond Burke Roche** was created **Baron Fermoy** (a new creation, 1855):

> We are authorised to **contradict** an erroneous statement which has appeared in one of the Cork papers, as to the lineal descendants of the last **Viscount Fermoy** and Baron **Roche**. The late Messrs. **Roche, of Cork, Stephen and James**, with their brothers of Limerick, **Thomas** now living, and **William**, deceased, and for many years member of Parliament for that city, are the **lineal descendants of the Viscount Fermoy**, who was attainted and died abroad, and **Mr. Stephen Roche, of Ryehill, county of Galway, and Moyvanine and Cluenties, county of Limerick**, as eldest son of the late Stephen Roche, of Cork, **is the lineal male heir of the late Viscount Fermoy**; and as such is inheritor of the **signet ring** of that noble family, whose landed possessions, as is well known, were entirely forfeited. **The gentleman who has lately received the title is not related to this family.** — *Evening Mail.*

**Key facts:**
- The Limerick/Cork Roches (Stephen, James, Thomas, William) publicly declared themselves the **lineal descendants of the Viscount Fermoy**
- **Stephen Roche of Ryehill, Galway, and Moyvanine and Cluenties, Limerick** named as the **lineal male heir** of the attainted Viscount
- He held the **signet ring** of the Viscount Fermoy
- The newly created **Baron Fermoy** (Edmond Burke Roche) was **"not related to this family"** — a different Roche line entirely
- Thomas Roche described as "now living" (he died December 1855)
