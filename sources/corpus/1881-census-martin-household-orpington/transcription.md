## Notes

- **Two families under one roof.** Peter Martin (41, "Married") is a **master chimney sweep** — a widower from his first marriage, with four children surnamed Martin: Samuel (20, journeyman sweep), Catherine (16, rag cutter), Emma (11), Peter Jr (9). His first wife died between ~1872 (Peter Jr's birth) and ~1876.
- **Clara Webb** (27, "Unmarried," born **Chelsfield**, Kent) is listed as **housekeeper** but has three children with Peter: Arthur (4), William (2), Albert (3 months) — all surnamed **Webb** because Clara and Peter are not married. By the 1891 census, Clara is listed as "Clara Martin, Wife" and all Webb children have been re-surnamed Martin.
- **No marriage record found** between Peter Martin and Clara Webb. They may have married informally, or the children were simply re-surnamed when the relationship became accepted. By 1891 the household presents as a unified Martin family.
- **Peter's birthplace discrepancy:** **Sevenoaks** (1881) vs **Woolwich** (1891). Both are in Kent but ~25 miles apart. Sevenoaks is inland; Woolwich is on the Thames in south-east London. One or both may be inaccurate, or Peter may have been born in one and raised in the other.
- **Samuel Martin** (20, journeyman sweep) — Peter's eldest, already qualified in the trade. Not present in 1891 (would be ~30). Same for Catherine (rag cutter at a paper mill — there were mills along the River Cray near Orpington) and Emma.
- **Alpine Cottage, Frog Lane** — the same address as 1891 ("1 Alfrons Cottages" appears to be the same property with a different name or transcription).
- **Rosina Martin** (I104) was not yet born (b. 1891). Her mother Clara Webb was from **Chelsfield**, a village ~2 miles south of Orpington.
