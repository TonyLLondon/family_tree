## **Practical next steps to verify the sitter name and when the name was written** 

Because Persian-language catalogues did not surface an independent record for “Tamar Saginian,” verification should be treated as an authentication workflow rather than a simple citation lookup. 

First,  treat  the  “Tamar  Saginian”  label  as **hypothesis** anchored  in  a  secondary  caption,  not  as independently established fact. 1 Then proceed: 

Establish object-based documentation (highest priority). Photograph the painting’s verso, stretcher, frame, and any labels under raking light; record all inscriptions exactly (including spelling variants). The Austrian Belvedere-related thesis demonstrates how unsigned/undated portraits can still have rich object histories through inventory and transfer documentation—your portrait may have analogous paperwork if it ever travelled through an institution or a documented private collection. 23 

Pursue the “Cormick/McCormick” identity trail in Iranian records. Persian sources consistently connect “Cormick/  ”کورمیک/کرمیک with  Abbas  Mirza’s  court  and  Qajar-era  medical-modernisation  context (including treatises and vaccination activity). Use this as a keyword bridge in Iranian archival catalogues: search for “ ”کورمیک ویلیام / کرمیک جان / کورمیک / کرمیک alongside “/ فرنگی حکیم / مخصوص طبیب دربار / دارالفنون  .” 6 If Tamar is claimed to be the physician’s wife, genealogical confirmation may appear in marriage, burial, church, or consular records (depending on the physician’s nationality and community). 

Target likely repositories, with concrete asks.
