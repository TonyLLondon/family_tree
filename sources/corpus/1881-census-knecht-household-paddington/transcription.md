**Notes on age:** George's age is recorded as **45**, implying a birth year of ~1836. This is inconsistent with the 1871 Census (age 42, implying ~1829). Self-reported ages on Victorian censuses routinely vary; the 1871 figure places his birth closer to 1829, the 1881 figure to 1836. Eliza's and George V.'s ages are illegible in the scan.

**Notes on birthplace — Eliza:** In the 1871 Census (Pembroke Street, Islington), Eliza's birthplace was recorded as **Ireland**. Here it is given as **Middlesex, Paddington**. This discrepancy may reflect a change in self-reporting (common among Irish-born residents facing anti-Irish prejudice) or an enumerator error. Her maiden name, **Glasscock**, is English in origin; the GRO marriage index records her marriage to George Knecht at Pancras, Q3 1856.

**Notes on birthplace — George:** The entry "Hesse Nassau, Subject" records George as a subject of the German Empire, born in the Prussian province of **Hesse-Nassau** (created 1868 from the former Electorate of Hesse and Duchy of Nassau). In 1871 his birthplace was given simply as "Germany."

**Notes on occupation:** George's trade has changed from **Gas Worker** (1871 Census, Islington) to **Cabinet Maker**. Both are manual trades consistent with London's working-class economy.

**Notes on location:** The family has moved from **Pembroke Street, Islington** (1871) to **Paddington**. By the 1901 Census, they will have returned to Islington — the same Pembroke Street address where their son-in-law [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md) and daughter [Dorothea](../../people/dorothea-knecht.md) are also found.

**Dorothea's absence:** [Dorothea Knecht](../../people/dorothea-knecht.md), George's daughter, married [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md) in Q4 1878 and would have been living with her husband by 1881. She does not appear in this household.
