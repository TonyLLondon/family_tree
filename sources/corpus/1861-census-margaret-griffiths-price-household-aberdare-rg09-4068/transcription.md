## Analysis

Margaret Griffiths is identifiable as **I655** (wife of David Griffiths, I654) based on:

- **Age:** 75 in 1861 → born ~1786, consistent with all prior census records and the probable 1787 christening at Conwil-gaio.
- **Birthplace:** "Cilycwm, Carmarthenshire" — Cilycwm (Cil-y-cwm) is the parish immediately adjacent to Cynwyl Gaeo/Caio. The 1851 census gave "Cayo, Carmarthenshire." Both are in the northern Tywi valley near Llandovery. This is normal census imprecision for neighbouring parishes.
- **Widow:** David Griffiths was buried Dec 1857 (probable). Margaret is now a widow.
- **Location:** Cynon Row, Cwmdare, Aberdare — the same hamlet where her son Thomas Griffiths (I422) was living at Clive Place (schedule 114, same enumeration district).

**Sarah Price née Griffiths** (b. ~1826, "Castletown") is a probable daughter of David and Margaret Griffiths:

- "Castletown" is the English rendering of **Trecastle** (Tre-castell), the village in Llywel parish where David and Margaret lived at Tresgob/Bishop Town.
- Her age (~35, b. ~1826) fills the 12-year gap between children John (1816) and Thomas I (1828).
- Margaret Griffiths is living in Sarah's household, suggesting a mother–daughter relationship.
- Sarah's eldest children (John, ~1847, and Margaret, ~1850) were born at "Castletown" and "Glyntawe" — both in the Llywel/upper Swansea valley area, suggesting the Price family lived near the Griffiths before migrating to Aberdare.
