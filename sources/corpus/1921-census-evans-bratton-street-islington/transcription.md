## Notes on Alfred's occupation

Findmypast's index transcribes Alfred's personal occupation as **"Caster Manager"**. The original handwriting on the census schedule appears to read **"Coster Monger"** (costermonger — a street seller of fruit and vegetables from a barrow). The reading "Coster Monger" is strongly supported by:

- His **1939 National Register** entry, which records his occupation as **Costermonger** (RG101/0291F/005, schedule 61).
- The **"Own Account"** employer status, consistent with self-employed street selling.
- The Clerkenwell/Islington locality, which was a centre of the costermonger trade since the eighteenth century.

If this reading is correct, Alfred was already working as a costermonger by age 28, having changed from his 1912 occupation of **barman** (recorded at marriage, St David's Church).

## Notes on Annie's occupation

Annie's employer, the **Star Brush Company** at **Eden Grove, Holloway**, placed her workplace within walking distance of the Fensom family's Lower Holloway roots. By 1939 she had moved into the printing trade as a **Printers Maker** (1939 Register).

## Notes on the children

The census reveals **three children** in the household. The existing family tree recorded only the middle child, **Joseph David Evans** (I99). The eldest son **Alfred Charles** (born ~April 1913, Islington) and youngest daughter **Annie** (born ~April 1917, Islington) were previously unknown.

Joseph's age of 6 years 9 months on 19 June 1921 implies a birth date around **September 1914**, slightly earlier than the previous estimate of "about November 1914." The GRO birth index quarter (Q4: Oct–Dec 1914) is consistent with a September birth registered in the following quarter.
