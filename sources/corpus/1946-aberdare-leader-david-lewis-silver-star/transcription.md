# U.S. Silver Star Awarded to Aberdare Major — Aberdare Leader, 2 March 1946

**Source:** Aberdare Leader, 2 March 1946. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1946-aberdare-leader-david-lewis-silver-star.png).

## Transcription

> **U.S. SILVER STAR**
>
> **Awarded to Aberdare Major**
>
> [*Photograph of Major David J. Lewis in uniform*]
>
> Some weeks ago we reported that Major David J. Lewis, son of Mr. & Mrs. Samuel Lewis, Pendarren St., Aberdare, had been awarded the United States Silver Star.
>
> Now his parents have received a copy of a general order from the Headquarters of the Mediterranean Theatre of Operations, United States Army, describing how their son won the award.
>
> Major Lewis, attached to the 1st Battalion of the Welch Regt., was awarded the Silver Star for heroic achievement in connection with military operations in Italy on April 25, 1945. During the establishment of a bridgehead over the Po River, Major Lewis was ordered to seize and hold Faccenda, a small village in enclosed country, to protect the right flank of the bridgehead. On approaching the objective, the company was suddenly subjected to heavy machine gun and mortar fire, imposing a check on the whole advance. With speed and aggressive determination Major Lewis led a platoon to assault the strong German positions in and around farm buildings, and by his personal efforts and example successfully over-ran the enemy, who fled, leaving seven dead and six prisoners. Later, an enemy counter-attack formed south of Faccenda, with the evident intention of destroying Major Lewis' company. Again showing the highest qualities of leadership and initiative, Major Lewis gave the enemy no opportunity to launch his attack, but immediately placed himself at the head of the reserve platoon and assaulted the enemy, who speedily withdrew. Throughout the day, Major Lewis' offensive spirit and total disregard for his own personal safety were an inspiration to his whole company, and a complete deterrent to the enemy's further efforts to threaten the bridgehead.

## Notes

This is the **first published account** of the Silver Star citation in the British press. The article includes a **portrait photograph** of David in uniform — one of the earliest published images of him. The *Aberdare Leader* notes "some weeks ago we reported" the award, meaning there was an earlier, shorter announcement (probably January or February 1946) that has not yet been located.

The text is a near-verbatim reproduction of **General Orders No. 285, HQ Mediterranean Theater of Operations, United States Army, 10 November 1945** — the same citation later gazetted in the *London Gazette* (Second Supplement 38018, 18 July 1947, p. 3319) and recorded in the Military Times *Hall of Valor* database. The article confirms:

- **1st Battalion, The Welch Regiment** — attached to the 61st Infantry Brigade, 6th Armoured Division
- **25 April 1945** — the date of the action at Faccenda, during the Po bridgehead
- **The action:** ordered to seize and hold Faccenda to protect the bridgehead's right flank; led a platoon assault on German positions in farm buildings; overran the enemy (7 killed, 6 prisoners); then led a second assault against a counterattack forming south of the village
- **Parents:** Mr. & Mrs. **Samuel Lewis**, **Pendarren Street, Aberdare** — confirming the family home address

The general order text sent to David's parents in Aberdare is the same document referenced in [Battalion Orders No. 113](../../media/docs/david-john-lewis-war/service-history/DJ-BattOrd041245.doc) (Sirmione, 4 December 1945), which formally announced the Silver Star. David's own laconic summary to his son Ivor, decades later: *"The same as everyone else; except someone saw me."*
