## What this note establishes

This two-page editorial preface by Abrahamian frames the *Brief Notice* historically and explains its archival journey. Key claims:

- **Author identification:** Charles Burgess (1808–53) was probably **"the first Englishman to trade with Iran through the Black Sea."**
- **Military background:** Charles first went to Persia in **1827** as a young military adviser in Abbas Mirza's *Nizami Jadid* army; dismissed after two years when financial and political pressure forced Abbas Mirza to release most British advisers.
- **Trade pivot:** He then turned to trade, raised capital from family and local money-lenders, and persuaded his younger brother **Edward** to join him in Iran.
- **Bankruptcy and abandonment:** The Balkan upheavals of **1835–6** wrecked his ventures; Charles returned to Britain, leaving Edward as a "hostage" for his Iranian creditors.
- **Edward's later career (summary):** village supervisor in western Azerbaijan → language tutor to royal princes → custodian of royal stables in Tabriz → court translator and official editor of the *Ruznama-i Vagayi-i Ittifagiyya* under Nasir al-Din Shah. "By the late 1840s, he had paid off his family debts but remained in Iran because of his deep attachments to the country. He died in Tabriz in 1855."
- **Publication history of the *Brief Notice*:** Prepared in **1832** for Charles's contact in the House of Commons, **T.H. Villiers**, who was expected to publish it as a **Parliamentary Paper**. Villiers's **death in the same year** ended this. A few copies were published **privately in 1833**; very few entered public archives. One copy was given by the Burgess family to the **New York Public Library**, where it is held in the rare manuscript room. Benjamin Schwartz's 1942 *Letters from Persia* did **not** include the *Brief Notice*.

## Cross-reference: date discrepancy on Charles

Abrahamian gives Charles's lifespan as **1808–1853**. Denis Wright (*The English Amongst the Persians*, Ch. 7) says Charles died "at about the same time" as Edward (June 1855), living "for some years at **Aix-la-Chapelle**" from "advanced stages of syphilis." The vault's earlier note gave **1806–1854**. All three are secondary; no primary death record is in corpus — see [`people/edward-burgess.md`](../../../../people/edward-burgess.md) open questions.
