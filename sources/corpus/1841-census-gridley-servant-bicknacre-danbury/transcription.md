## Notes

- **Ann Gridley** (I108) — age 15, **Female Servant** on John Chapman's farm. Born in Essex (the 1841 census records only county, not parish). Her birthplace of **Tillingham** (given in the 1851 census) is ~15 miles east of Bicknacre, on the Dengie Peninsula. Going into service at 14–15 was standard for working-class girls in rural Essex — she would have left her parents' household to work on this farm.
- **John Chapman** is a farmer at Bicknacre, a hamlet of Danbury parish, in the Chelmsford registration district. He has six children aged 1–14 and two female servants (Harriet Matthams, 30, and Ann Gridley, 15).
- Ann's parents — the Gridley family — are **not in this household**. They would need to be found separately, most likely in Tillingham or the wider Dengie Peninsula area. The surname Gridley is uncommon enough to narrow any 1841 search.
- By ~1844 Ann married **George Coolbear** (carpenter, Hazeleigh), and their first son was given her maiden name: **George Gridley Coolbear**.
- **George Coolbear is on the previous page** (page 18, schedule 30) of this same enumeration book — age 15, living with William and Hannah Stebbing as an apparent apprentice carpenter. They were neighbours in Bicknacre before they married. See [George Coolbear transcription](../1841-census-coolbear-apprentice-bicknacre-danbury/transcription.md).
