# A Couple of Deserters — Thomas Cushen

**Merthyr Express**, 29 September 1906

> **A COUPLE OF DESERTERS.** — John Keefe and Thomas Cushen were charged with being deserters from the Welsh Regiment. — P.S. Evans proved the arrest of the defendants at Dowlais the previous night. Both admitted the offence, and were remanded to await an escort.

Given the location (Dowlais) and the Welsh Regiment connection, this is likely a grandson or nephew — possibly a son of Thomas Jr (~1851) or another branch of the family. The co-defendant John Keefe has an Irish surname consistent with the Dowlais Irish community. Both men admitted desertion and were remanded for a military escort — a common procedure for returning absconders to their regiment.*
