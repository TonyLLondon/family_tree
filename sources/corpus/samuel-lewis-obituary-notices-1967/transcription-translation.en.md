# Samuel Lewis — obituary notices, August 1967

Three newspaper clippings from South Wales newspapers, August 1967.

---

## 1. English death notice (newspaper column)

> **LEWIS.** — At Maesyffynnon, Aberaman, on August 7, **Samuel**, formerly of 32
> Penydarren St., Aberdare, devoted husband of **Elizabeth Lily** and dear father
> of **David John** and **Brynmor Keith**. Funeral Friday, Aug. 11. Friends
> please meet at Glyntaff Crematorium, at 11 a.m. No flowers. Donations to
> Blind please.

## 2. Welsh chapel obituary — Carmel, Trecynon

Original Welsh text (transcribed from rotated clipping):

> Mr. Samuel Lewis, Carmel, Trecynon, gan Mr. D. Llewellyn Jones, Trecynon.
> Cyfaill diffuant a phethau crefyddol oedd y cyfan iddo. Un o nodweddion ei
> fywyd oedd ei ffyddlondeb di-dor i'r Achos yng Ngharmel. Cafod y Cyfarfod
> Gweddi a'r Seiat lle blaenaf yn ei fywyd trwy'r blynyddoedd. Bu'n Flaenor
> teimlwydd am 35 mlynedd a llanw y swydd gydag urddas. Cymriad cywir a
> chesiai fyw ei grefydd. Bu'n Ysgrifennydd Gohebol yr eglwys am maith ond yn
> herwydd aflechyd gorfu iddo roi fynny'r swydd. Fydd llanw y bwlch yr eglwys.
> Cydymdemlir yn ddwys a'r weddw a'r plant.

### English translation

Mr. Samuel Lewis, Carmel, Trecynon, by Mr. D. Llewellyn Jones, Trecynon.

A sincere friend — religious matters were everything to him. One of the
hallmarks of his life was his unwavering faithfulness to the Cause at Carmel
[chapel]. The Prayer Meeting and the Seiat [fellowship meeting] held the
foremost place in his life throughout the years. He was a sensitive Deacon
(Blaenor) for 35 years and filled the position with dignity. A true character
who sought to live his religion. He served as Corresponding Secretary of the
church for a long time, but due to illness was forced to give up the position.
It will be hard to fill the gap in the church. Deepest sympathy is expressed to
the widow and children.

## 3. Aberdare Leader, 18 August 1967, p. 8 — "Week's Obituary: Church secretary for 30 years"

> **MR. S. LEWIS, ABERAMAN**
>
> CREMATION took place at Glyntaff on Friday of Mr. Samuel Lewis, aged 80,
> of Maesyffynnon, Aberaman.
>
> The officiating minister was the Rev. Glyndwr Richards, M.A., B.D., a
> former pastor of Carmel Calvinistic Methodist Church, Trecynon.
>
> Mr. Lewis died at Maesyffynnon, where he and his wife Elizabeth had been
> residents for the past 16 months. Before that they had lived at 32,
> Pendarren Street, Aberdare, for almost 40 years.
>
> Mr. Lewis was a devout member of Carmel, being the school superintendent
> for 10 years before being made a deacon in 1932. He was the church
> secretary for several years.
>
> He had lived all his life at Aberdare and until his retirement in 1954 was
> employed by the Scottish Legal Life Assurance Society as district manager.
>
> He also had strong associations with the Independent Order of Oddfellows
> Lily of the Valley Lodge, and apart from being a committeeman for seven
> years, he held some of the Order's highest offices.
>
> At the evening service at Carmel, Trecynon, on Sunday evening, Mr. E. R.
> Walters paid tribute to Mr. Lewis's tremendous sincerity and loyalty and
> his unfailing willingness to serve God and his church.
>
> Mourners: David John and Brynmor Keith Lewis, sons; Brynmor Lewis, John
> Hennessey, Ron Evans, Alan M. Evans and Keith M. Evans, nephews.
>
> Messrs. I. G. Powell, Tom Thomas, Stephen Barnes, Ted Walters, Philip
> Walters, J. L. Thomas, Bill Billingham, Herbert Price, Tom Evans, Penri
> Williams and Robert Harris, friends.
>
> Absent through illness was Mr. Tom Lewis, brother.
>
> Bearers: Messrs. Norman Reynolds, David Hall, George Forward and David
> Pugh.
>
> At Maesyffynnon with the widow were Jeanette Lewis, daughter-in-law; Anne
> Southey, May Hennessey, May Evans, nieces; and Miss A. Powell.
>
> Mrs. Lewis greatly appreciates the care and many kindnesses received by
> her late husband from the matron and staff at Maesyffynnon, and the
> attention given by Dr. A. Richards.
>
> There were no flowers, the request being made that donations be sent to
> the Blind Institute.
