## Notes

### William O'Byrne White — age and career

William (I135) is recorded at age **67**, consistent with a birth of ~May 1844 (he would turn 67 two months after census night). He had been retired from the Army Medical Department since **25 April 1885** — twenty-six years by this census — but still described himself by his medical qualifications rather than as "retired." The qualifications in the occupation column appear to read **LRCPI LRCSI**, consistent with the 1901 return.

### Katie (Catherine Mary Roche) — fertility return

**Catherine Mary Roche** (I136) is recorded as **Katie** — the familiar form of Catherine. Age 69 (born ~1842, consistent with her documented 1841 birth year in Dublin). The marriage duration of **42 years** confirms the **February 1869 Bombay** marriage.

The fertility columns — **8 children born alive, 6 still living** — are recorded against both husband and wife (standard 1911 practice). Two children had died by 1911. The identities of all eight children remain partially documented: **Gerald Sebastian White** (I12, born 1878 Jabalpur) and **Kathleen** (present in this household) are accounted for; the remaining six have no person pages.

### Kathleen White — still at home

Kathleen is now **29**, consistent with age 19 in the 1901 census (born ~1882). Still single and living at home. Her birthplace is specified more precisely as **Tipperary Town** rather than just "Tipperary" (1901). She was born during William's India service — either during home leave or while Catherine was back in Ireland.

### [?] Hanlon — boarder

A 21-year-old female boarder whose first name is difficult to read from the handwriting. Her occupation appears to relate to nursing or medical attendance — which would make practical sense in the household of an elderly retired surgeon (William was 67, Catherine 69). Birthplace appears to read **Donegal** but the handwriting is uncertain. Not related to the White family.

### Margaret Sullivan — servant

A domestic servant/cook from **County Cork**, age 18. The Irish Language column records her as speaking **both Irish and English** — she is the only member of the household with Irish, which fits a young woman from rural Cork. A different servant from the 1901 census (Ellen Sullivan of Kerry); the shared surname Sullivan is coincidental — Sullivan is the most common surname in Kerry and Cork.
