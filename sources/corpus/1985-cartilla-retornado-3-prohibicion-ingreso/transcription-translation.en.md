# Cartilla del Retornado Nº 3 — Transcription and Translation

**Source:** *Cartilla del Retornado* Nº 3, November 1985, published by the Comité Pro-Retorno de Exiliados with support from the Vicaría de la Solidaridad.

**Original language:** Spanish

## Presentación / Presentation (page 3)

### Original

> Cada cierto tiempo, concretamente a partir del crecimiento de la movilización popular y de la difusión más ampliada de la violación de los derechos de las personas en el ámbito nacional, el régimen ha ido cediendo en cuanto al problema del exilio chileno. Esto, cuantitativamente. De acuerdo al listado entregado el 15 de julio y a las modificaciones que le introdujera el 17 de septiembre, serían 3.878 los chilenos impedidos de ingresar al territorio nacional.
>
> Sin embargo, junto al listado antes aludido, el régimen entregó un nuevo instructivo que reglamenta acerca de la reconsideración de las medidas de prohibición de ingreso, las que lesionan aún más la situación de los exiliados, y que se analizan en el presente documento.
>
> La gravedad de esta situación y la necesidad de dar a conocer más profusamente los nombres de las personas afectadas por estas medidas, ha hecho que el Comité Pro Retorno de Exiliados decidiera publicar dentro de su serie Cartilla del Retornado los documentos antes señalados.
>
> La Vicaría de la Solidaridad, compartiendo la inquietud del Comité al respecto, ha accedido a apoyar la publicación de la presente Cartilla.
>
> Octubre de 1985

### Translation

From time to time, specifically as popular mobilisation has grown and awareness of violations of individual rights at the national level has spread, the regime has been making concessions on the problem of Chilean exile — though only quantitatively. According to the list issued on 15 July and the amendments introduced on 17 September, there would be 3,878 Chileans barred from entering the national territory.

However, alongside the aforementioned list, the regime issued a new directive regulating the reconsideration of entry-ban measures — measures that further harm the situation of exiles, and which are analysed in this document.

The gravity of this situation and the need to disseminate more widely the names of those affected by these measures prompted the Committee for the Return of Exiles to publish the aforementioned documents as part of its *Cartilla del Retornado* series.

The Vicariate of Solidarity, sharing the Committee's concern, has agreed to support the publication of this Booklet.

October 1985

## Helia's entry — page 12

### Surrounding entries (right column, page 12)

Transcribed from the scanned image:

> Cerpa Miranda, Carlos Alberto
> **Cerpa Pérez, Helia del Carmen**
> Cervino Arévalo, Domingo Leonardo

Helia del Carmen Cerpa Pérez appears as entry **"Cerpa Pérez, Helia del Carmen"** on page 12 of the *Sexto listado* (6th edition, 15 July 1985) of Chileans banned from entering Chile, issued by the Policía de Investigaciones under the Pinochet military government. The list at this edition contained 4,360 names. She was among 3,878 persons still barred as of the September 1985 amendments.

## Context note

The *Cartilla del Retornado* series was published by the **Comité Pro-Retorno de Exiliados** (Committee for the Return of Exiles), a Chilean exile-rights organisation, with backing from the **Vicaría de la Solidaridad** (Vicariate of Solidarity), the Catholic Church's human-rights body during the dictatorship. The banned list was compiled by the **Policía de Investigaciones de Chile** (Chilean Investigative Police) and enforced under the *Disposición vigésima cuarta transitoria* (24th Transitory Provision) of the 1980 Constitution, which gave the regime power to ban citizens from re-entering the country. The booklet also includes a presidential directive ("Instructivo") dated 3 July 1985 from the Ministry of the Interior (signed by Minister Ricardo García Rodríguez) setting out the procedure by which exiles could petition for reconsideration of their ban.

Helia's presence on this list confirms that as of July 1985 she was formally prohibited from returning to Chile — twelve years after her detention at the Estadio Nacional and subsequent exile to Italy and then London.

Immediately above Helia's entry on the page appears **Cerpa Miranda, Carlos Alberto**. A Chilean political columnist of the same name now writes for *El Mostrador* and *El Maipo*. Given the extreme rarity of the Cerpa surname, he is very likely a relative (same paternal surname Cerpa, different maternal surname Miranda vs Pérez).

The regime deliberately timed Decree Nº 203 after voter-registration rolls had closed for the 5 October 1988 plebiscite, preventing returning exiles from voting on whether Pinochet would remain in power. The "No" won 55.99% to 44.01%.

The legal framework for the bans comprised three instruments: **Decreto Ley 81** (1973), **Decreto Ley 604** (10 August 1974), and the **Disposición vigésima cuarta transitoria** (24th Transitory Provision) of the 1980 Constitution. The OAS Inter-American Commission on Human Rights repeatedly declared these measures a violation of Article VIII of the American Declaration of the Rights and Duties of Man (right to residence and movement).
