## **STUMP 50330** 

## **FAMILY HISTORY RESEARCH. 01.09.2005** 

Aim of the research was to find information about the Stump family (when they migrated to Estonia, names of Etienne Stump’s parents and siblings and when he left Estonia) within the time limit allocated for the research. 

We started with using the card files and indexed records of Tallinn Lutheran congregations. From a list of nobles in the Tallinn Dome congregation we found that one nobleman had married with Marguerite Emilie Stump. This lead us to use the records of the Tallinn Dome congregation. From there we found the birth entries of Etienne and his siblings as well as the marriage entry of his parents. Then we used the register of parishioners of Tallinn Dome congregation, address board records of Tallinn, one university file and some court and other files to find out more information about the family events. 

It is possible to continue the research with finding more biographical information about the persons in the family tree, however, it is probably not possible to find more ancestors and descendants from Estonian records. There are eight files about real estate in Tallinn that the Stump family owned. Those can be used for more information. 

NB! Inseparable part of the report is the text of the principles of the report. We would like to turn your attention to the most important ones among those principles: 

-  Not all children are always listed. If all are listed then we have made a respective remark about that. 

-  Place of registration (marked with _sr:_ , meaning registration in some farm or manorial district, being a member of some commune) is not always actual place of residence, but legal place of registration. 

-  Almost always the data can be complemented, exact birth and death dates and children can be found out etc, if it is not specified otherwise. 

-  Finding vital information from the years 1949-1992 in most cases requires knowing at least approximate year and place when and where something happened.

Marc François (ooMarkus Franz, Marc Francois, Marcus Franz, Marc, Markus Franz, Marc François) Stump. * _16.02.1834_ in Yverdon. There is a date _10.03.1834_ below his birth date in the register of parishioners of Tallinn Dome congregation 1864-1901. Usually it is a date of baptism, but we are not sure about this in his case. Also his birth date might be according to the new (Gregorian) calendar as in Switzerland the new calendar was used already since 1584, but in the Russian empire since 1918. It is not noted in the above-mentioned register of parishioners when he or his family members joined the congregation. The first remark about him and his family are receiving the Holy Communion on _10.04.1881_ and this is the only additional information about him (except for the name and birth date and place) in this source. There are two dates about her wife receiving the Holy Communion – _09.04.1878_ and _10.04.1881_ . It seems the table about receiving the Holy Communion is not quite complete. There is no information about the children ever receiving the Holy Communion. In the 1902-1926 register of parishioners of the Tallinn Dome congregation his name Markus Franz is crossed through and Marc François is written instead. It is not easy to determine when Marc came to Estonia. There are some records that can be used, but there are no passenger files or continuous lists of foreigners etc. For that reason we did not look for that information. 

The oldest document about Marc Stump in Estonia that we found was from _03.03.1865_ when Marc applied to be accepted to the Tartu university to exams that he needed to pass for becoming a French teacher at a gymnasium. On _13.03.1865_ he passed the following exams: 

- French language 

- French literature 

- History of the French literature 

- minors: 

- religion 

- Bible and Greece history 

- World history 

- World geography 

- arithmetic 

His grades were 4-5 (on the scale of 1-5) for French and 4 for the minor subjects. On _24.03.1865_ he received a certificate about the exams that he has passed. Marc + _03.04.1903_ (maybe according to new calendar) in Yverdon. There is no remark in the 1902-1926 register of parishioners about his leaving the congregation, thus he either was temporarily in Yverdon when he died or he had not officially changed the congregation. There is a word _Schweiz_ (Switzerland) written below his name in the same source. It might mean that he lived there for some time when the register of parishioners was used or that his birth place Yverdon was in Switzerland. 

At least since 1868 he was a senior teacher ( _Oberlehrer_ ) of French in the Tallinn government’s gymnasium. According to the parish registers of Tallinn Dome congregation he was of the reformed confession and his wife was of Lutheran confession. 

In 1900 Marc lived in Tallinn at Tatari Street and was a collegiums councilor. According to other documents he lived in Tallinn at Mere 123. He had a house in Tallinn (probably the house was at Tatari Street and he himself lived at Mere Street) and its was partly rented to Magnus Grube’s bakery in May 1894 (which was still there in 1900). In 1897 an pharmacist Treublut rented the basement of that house and it was still there in 1900. Stump’s neighbor Konstantin Mühlberg sued him for disturbing his peace with a tinning workshop and claimed 600 rubles for that. More information is available about that matter and a couple more cases like that.
