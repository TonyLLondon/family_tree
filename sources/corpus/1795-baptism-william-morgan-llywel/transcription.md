## Notes

- This is almost certainly the baptism of [William Morgan](../../people/william-morgan-llanfairarybryn.md) (I657), farmer of Coedefaur, Llanfairarybryn. The 1851 census gives his age as 55 (born ~1796) and his birthplace as Llanfairarybryn, Carmarthenshire. Llywel parish is immediately adjacent to Llanfairarybryn — the boundary runs between the two parishes — and a baptism date of 15 Sep 1795 is consistent with the 1851 census age.
- **Morgan Morgans** — the father. The patronymic name means "Morgan son of Morgan." A Morgan Morgans was baptized at Llywel on 27 Dec 1776 (parents Morgan and Rachel), which would make him 18–19 when William was born. Young but plausible for rural Wales.
- **Lettuce** — the mother. This is a form of **Lettice** (Latin *Laetitia*), a name common in Wales. She is also named as mother in the baptism of [Thomas Morgan](../../people/thomas-morgan-nantyglo.md) at Llywel on 15 Feb 1794 (FMP `R_732790425`), making Thomas and William brothers. She does not appear on the 1841 or 1851 censuses, where William's "Mother" is named **Anne Morgan** (age 70/84, widow). Anne's relationship is uncertain — she may be Morgan Morgans' second wife (stepmother) or Lettice's mother (William's maternal grandmother). There is no evidence Lettice died young.
- The 1841 census age (40, born ~1801) is less reliable — the 1841 census rounded adult ages to the nearest 5, and the 1851 census (age 55, born ~1796) is much closer to this 1795 baptism.
