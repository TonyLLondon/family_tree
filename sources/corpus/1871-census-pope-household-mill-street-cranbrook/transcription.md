## Notes

- **George Pope** (I296) is a **shoemaker master** — a self-employed craftsman, not a factory worker. Age 46 gives a birth year of ~1825.
- **Sarah A. Pope** (I297, née **Quaife**, confirmed by GRO marriage index: George Pope × Sarah Anne Quaife, Q4 1847, Cranbrook, Vol 5, p. 163). Age 43 gives birth year ~1828. She is a dressmaker — both parents are skilled tradespeople.
- **Minnie Pope** (I293) — this is **Minnie Flora Pope**, later wife of David William Fensom. Age 5 gives birth year ~1866, consistent with the GRO registration in Q4 1865.
- All seven family members were born in Cranbrook — the family is deeply rooted in this Kentish market town.
- The children are listed slightly out of birth-year order: George (~1854), Arthur (~1864), Frank (~1859), Minnie (~1866), Gertrude (~1868). This ordering (eldest, then younger boys, then girls) may reflect the enumerator's choice rather than strict age order.
- A **Frederick Pope** (lodger, unmarried, 17, civil servant, born Guildford, Surrey) appears immediately above this household at the same address — possibly a relative boarding with the family.
- George Pope is described as a "master" shoemaker, meaning he ran his own workshop and likely employed journeymen or apprentices. Cranbrook was a small market town in the Weald of Kent, historically associated with the cloth trade but by the 1870s a mixed agricultural and artisan economy.
