## Notes on the 1841 Census format

The 1841 Census did not record relationships, marital status, or precise birthplaces — only whether the person was born in the county. Ages of those over 15 were rounded down to the nearest 5 years. Occupations were recorded but were not included in the Findmypast transcription provided.

## Interpretation

- **William Pope** (40, b. ~1801, Kent) — I591. George's **father**. The Findmypast transcript does not include his occupation, but the census image suggests he may have been a shoemaker (matching his son George's later trade). Age 40 under the 1841 rounding convention means he was 40–44, giving a birth year of ~1797–1801.
- **Sarah Pope** (35, b. ~1806, Kent) — I592. George's **mother**. Her maiden name is not yet known. Age 35 under rounding means 35–39, giving birth year ~1802–1806.
- **George Pope** (15, b. ~1826, Kent) — I296. Our George, later master shoemaker of Cranbrook and father of Minnie Flora Pope. Age 15 is exact (under-15s were recorded precisely), giving birth year ~1826, consistent with ~1825 from later censuses.
- **William Pope** (10, b. ~1831, Kent) — George's younger brother.
- **William Pope** (75, b. ~1766, Kent) — almost certainly the **grandfather**, i.e. William (40)'s father. Three generations of William Pope under one roof. Age 75 under rounding means 75–79, giving birth year ~1762–1766.
- **Mark Smith** (25, b. ~1816, Kent) — likely a lodger or journeyman; relationship to the family unclear.
- **Elizabeth Dungey** (80, b. ~1761, Kent) — an elderly woman in the household. She could be William Sr's wife (though the surname difference suggests not), a relative, or a boarder. Age 80 under rounding means 80–84.

## Three generations

This household captures three generations of Pope men in Cranbrook:
1. **William Pope Sr** (~1766) — grandfather
2. **William Pope** (~1801) — father
3. **George Pope** (~1826) — our line, future master shoemaker

The shoemaking trade may well have passed through all three generations.
