**Note on "William" (row 4):** The age of 24 is consistent with our [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md) (b. Q1 1857), yet he was also enumerated at 3 Swinton Street, St Pancras with his wife [Dorothea](../../people/dorothea-knecht.md) in the same 1881 census. Dual enumeration is possible (the census being taken on a single night), or this may be a separate person — a lodger or step-relative with the same name. The occupation is hard to read and does not match William I289's recorded trade of "Brass Founder."

**Note on Catherine May (row 6) and Thomas (row 7):** These are previously unrecorded children, born after the 1871 census. Catherine May (b. ~1869) and Thomas (b. ~1864) were not in the 1871 enumeration, which is puzzling for Thomas — he may have been away or his age is misread.

**Absent family members:** Jessie (b. ~1849) and Alfred Jr (b. ~1853) have left the household.

## Notes

### Identity

This is the household of **Alfred Evans** (I606), now sixty years old and still working as a painter. The family remained at Paradise Place, the same street recorded in the 1871 census.
