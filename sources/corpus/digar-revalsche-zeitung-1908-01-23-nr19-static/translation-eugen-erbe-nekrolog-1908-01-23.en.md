# Obituary: Former city councillor Eugen Erbe †

**Former city councillor Eugen Erbe, deceased.**

Yesterday a sudden death cut down a man who for decades played a significant part in the municipal life of our city and ranked among the most versatile and tireless workers in the field of urban administration. Former city councillor Eugen Erbe succumbed to the effects of a stroke that struck him yesterday and tore the man, barely sixty years old, from his family and from his public work before his time.

Born on 5 November 1847 as the son of the **Schloßvogt** (castle bailiff) and **Syndikus** (city attorney) of Reval, the deceased, after completing his gymnasium course, went to the University of Dorpat, where he devoted himself to the study of jurisprudence—a field to which his whole mental cast, with its marked bent for theoretical dialectic, particularly inclined him. Already at the university, where he belonged to the **„Estonia“** student corporation, he stood out for the sharpness of his power of distinction with which he approached questions debated in student life, whether in convention or in academic discussion. It was no accident that, as a long-serving honorary judge, he was most often sought out precisely in those cases that were especially complicated and involved.

After completing the university in **1871** with the degree of **Candidate of Jurisprudence**, he began in his native city the usual legal career and in succession served as **trainee at the Manngericht**, secretary of the **Wierland–Jerwen Manngericht**, secretary of the **Reval lower court**, and from **1872** onward simultaneously as **superior-court and council advocate** in Reval. Gradually his municipal activity moved ever more to the fore and step by step became the exclusive sphere of his public life. Elected to the **city council** in **1883**, in **1887–89** he was also a **councillor** (*Ratsherr*) and from **1889** **Syndikus** of the council.

Above all, it is his broad, long-sustained labour in the **Reval city administration** that ties his name most closely to the history of his native town. Thanks to favourable private circumstances, and thus able to devote himself fully to municipal affairs without any secondary office or sideline, he—as city councillor, as **deputy mayor** (*stellvertretender Stadthaupt*), and finally, in troubled times, as the city’s representative officially charged with the functions of **mayor**—spared no effort to meet his varied and often highly difficult duties. Numerous careful memoranda on city matters, many commission motions with detailed reasoning, various exposés on pressing municipal questions are the outward milestones of this restless work. Moreover, as **president** (*Präses*) of the **city treasury** (*Stadtkasse*), the daily, time-consuming work in that branch lay with him, and whenever one had business at the city office, one always found him at his post. All of this secures him in the annals of our municipal administrative history a title of honour of lasting duration.

Yet thorns were not spared him in this field of labour either. Twice, and from different quarters, he had to learn, painfully enough, that the trust of political groups is no fixed possession and is subject to painful fluctuations, however good one’s intentions may be. In these experiences lay the tragedy of his life—a life whose motto was restless, tireless work, and to which nevertheless the satisfaction of full inner and outer fulfilment was denied.

All the more is it **duty**, in view of his passing, **to keep in grateful memory** what a vast sum of diligence and labour, of knowledge and experience, of perseverance and conscientiousness down to the smallest detail has been taken from us in Eugen Erbe.

**May he rest in peace!**

*Reval, 23 January.*
