# 1871 Census — George G Coolbear, lodger, Wantz Road, St Peter, Maldon

**Date:** 2 April 1871
**Address:** Wantz Road, St Peter parish, Maldon, Essex (appears to be an inn — schedule 27 is headed by Thomas Hallister, inn keeper)
**Reference:** RG 10/1674, folio 28, page 5, schedule 29

## Entry

| Name | Relation | Condition | Sex | Age | Occupation | Birthplace |
|------|----------|-----------|-----|-----|-----------|------------|
| George G Coolbear | Lodger | — | M | 26 | Bill poster | Hazeleigh, Essex |

## Notes

- **George Gridley Coolbear** (I105) is a **lodger** at what appears to be an inn on Wantz Road, Maldon. He is **unmarried** (marital status blank) and **alone** — no wife, no children with him.
- He is already a **bill poster** at 26 — the same trade he would practise throughout his life.
- His birthplace is again confirmed as **Hazeleigh, Essex**. Age 26 gives birth year ~1845.
- **Critical finding:** George is single and childless in April 1871. Yet in the 1881 Census, his household includes **Ellen Alice Coolbear** (13, b. ~1868, Maldon) and **Charles Coolbear** (10, b. ~1871, Witham). These children cannot be from a prior marriage of George's, since he was unmarried in 1871. They must either be:
  - **Alma's children** from a prior relationship who took the Coolbear surname after the marriage (but Alma was only ~14 in 1868 — very young)
  - Children whose birth years in 1881 are overstated (they may be younger than recorded)
  - Or **Alma's age in 1881 was understated** — she may have been older than 27, which would make her old enough to have had Ellen Alice in 1868
- The other lodgers at the same address include men from Kent and Suffolk — this is transient accommodation for working men.
