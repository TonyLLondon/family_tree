# Looking Ahead — Paddington Times, 22 October 1897

**Source:** Paddington Times, 22 October 1897. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1897-paddington-times-knecht-subsidence-chichester-mews.png).

## Transcription

> **LOOKING AHEAD.**
>
> A letter was read from Mr G. Knecht, giving the Vestry notice that in the event of any subsidence of his house in consequence of the proposed sewer works at the entrance of Chichester-mews, he would hold the Vestry responsible, and asking to be informed whether the Vestry considered it necessary for him to remove from the house during the progress of the works. — The Works Committee reported that they had already informed Mr Knecht that they had no advice to offer with reference to his letter. — Mr Whur said there was no danger of any subsidence of the house in question. — The action of the Works Committee was approved.

## Notes

George Knecht wrote to the Paddington Vestry less than a year before his death (September 1898), warning that proposed sewer works at the entrance to **Chichester Mews** — directly adjacent to his house at **19 Chichester Road** — might cause subsidence. He asked whether the vestry thought it necessary for him to vacate during construction. The Works Committee brushed him off ("no advice to offer"), and Mr Whur reassured the vestry there was no danger. The vestry approved the committee's inaction.

George was simultaneously a vestryman and a property owner affected by vestry decisions — a dual role that must have made for awkward meetings. The letter shows him still engaged and protective of his property at around age 70, and confirms he was living at 19 Chichester Road in late 1897, consistent with all other records from 1881 onward.
