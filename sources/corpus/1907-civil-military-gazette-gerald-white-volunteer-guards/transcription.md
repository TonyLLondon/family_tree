# Madras Volunteer Guards — Civil & Military Gazette (Lahore), 14 May 1907

**Source:** Civil & Military Gazette (Lahore), 14 May 1907, p. 9. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast.

## Transcription (OCR extract)

> Madras Volunteer Guards — Second-Lieutenant Harold Greenwood Ogden to be Lieutenant, vice Robinson, resigned. Gerald Sebastian White, gentleman, to be Second-Lieutenant.

## Notes

This gazette notice records Gerald Sebastian White's commission as **Second-Lieutenant in the Madras Volunteer Guards**, a part-time territorial force for European civilians in the Madras Presidency.

Key points:

- Uses his **full name "Gerald Sebastian White"** — important corroboration of identity alongside the "Gerald S. White" used in court records.
- Described as **"gentleman"** — the standard gazette designation for a civilian (i.e. not transferring from another military unit), confirming his primary role was legal/civilian.
- Dated **May 1907**, approximately one year after his admission as a Solicitor of the High Court of Madras (March 1906), showing he was settling into the expatriate community.
