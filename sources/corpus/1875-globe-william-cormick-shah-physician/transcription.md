## Notes

This notice records **Queen Victoria granting William Cormick, M.D., Royal licence** to accept and wear a Persian decoration — a formal requirement for British subjects receiving foreign honours. Key points:

- Confirms William Cormick held the title **"doctor in medicine"** and was **"actually employed beyond her Majesty's dominions, in the medical service of His Majesty the Shah of Persia."**
- Dated **October 1875**, two years before his death (1877), placing him still in active service to the Shah at this late date.
- The notice originates from the **London Gazette** (the Globe is reporting a Gazette announcement), so the full text with the specific decoration name should be recoverable from the Gazette itself.
- This is the first contemporary British newspaper confirmation of William Cormick's role as physician to the Shah, independent of the family oral history recorded in Anna Saginian's 1880 interview.
