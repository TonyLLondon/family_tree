# 1983 — Lurgan Mail — "Moira waits and sees…"

**Newspaper:** Lurgan Mail, 1 September 1983, page 1
**Reference:** BL/0005200/19830901/005

## Extract (relevant passage)

> …winners won't be announced until early October and national trophies will be awarded to the winning city, large town, small town, large village and small village. The Keep Britain Tidy Fulvia Lewis trophy will be awarded to the place which the judges consider has made the greatest effort in litter control.

## Notes

Front-page article about Moira (County Armagh) awaiting results in the Britain in Bloom competition. By 1983 the trophy was referred to simply as "the Fulvia Lewis trophy" — no longer needing to explain who she was. The same October 1983 ceremony at the Café Royal saw the trophy presented to Cheltenham by Princess Michael of Kent (reported in the *Daily Telegraph* and *The Times*, 4 October 1983).
