## Notes

### William O'Byrne White — identification

This is **William O'Byrne White** (I135), the retired Army Medical Department surgeon who settled in Tralee after his 1885 retirement. Born ~May 1844 in Carlow Town — the census age of 57 is consistent (he would turn 57 in May 1901, two months after census night). His medical qualifications — **LRCPI** (Licentiate of the Royal College of Physicians of Ireland) and **LRCSI** (Licentiate of the Royal College of Surgeons in Ireland) — are written in the occupation column. The Army half-pay register records his qualifications as **LRCP Ed.** (Edinburgh) + **LRCSI**; the enumerator may have written LRCPI for the Irish college rather than the Edinburgh one, or William may have held both.

### Catherine White — née Roche

**Catherine Mary Roche** (I136), born 1841 in Dublin. Age 59 is consistent with an 1841/1842 birth year. The couple married **8 February 1869** in the **Bombay Presidency** during William's Army Medical Department service in India. The 1911 census would confirm 42 years of marriage by that date.

### Kathleen White — daughter born in Tipperary

Kathleen White, age 19 (born ~1882), is not yet on a person page. Her birthplace of **Tipperary** is notable: William was posted in India until his 1885 retirement, so a birth in Ireland in 1882 suggests either that Catherine returned to Ireland before William's retirement, or that the family took home leave. Gerald Sebastian White (I12, born Jabalpur February 1878) was the last child born in India. The 1911 census specifies **Tipperary Town** for Kathleen's birthplace.

### Ellen Sullivan — servant

A domestic servant from Kerry, age 18. Local hire for the Denny Street household — Kerry servants were standard for Tralee households.

### Household size

Only three family members are present: the head, wife, and one daughter. Son Gerald (I12, then age 23) was presumably already in England or India pursuing his legal career. The 1911 census would record 8 children born alive and 6 still living — at least five children are absent from this household, either grown and living elsewhere, or deceased.
