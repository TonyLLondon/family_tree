## Notes

- This is the only marriage of a William Morgan to a bride named **Sarah** in the Llanfairarybryn parish registers during the period 1820–1837. Five different William Morgan marriages appear at Llywel and Llanfairarybryn between 1820 and 1833 — some certainly involve different men bearing this extremely common Welsh name — but this is the only one matching our Sarah.
- **Sarah Watkins** — this gives Sarah's maiden name as **Watkins**. On subsequent censuses she appears as Sarah Morgan (her married name). The 1881 census records her birthplace as **Builth, Brecknockshire**, while the 1851 census gives **Llanddaysaint, Carmarthenshire**. These are different places ~25 miles apart; the discrepancy is unresolved.
- The couple's eldest known child, [Anne Morgan](../../people/anne-morgan.md) (b. ~1829–1830), was born shortly before this marriage. Pre-marital pregnancy was common in rural Wales.
- Both parties gave their residence as Llanfairarybryn, confirming Sarah was already living in the parish by 1831.
- William was a widower by this date — he may have been previously married to Margaret David (Llywel, 1820) or another bride. The 1841 census shows a **Jinken Morgan** (age 20, born ~1821) in the household who could be a child from an earlier marriage.
