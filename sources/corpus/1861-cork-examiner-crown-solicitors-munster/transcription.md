# Crown Solicitors for Munster Circuit

*Cork Examiner*, 19 April 1861

---

We (*Dublin Evening Post*) have learned that the Crown Solicitorship of Munster Circuit, vacant by the death of Sir Matthew Barrington, Bart., has been divided into counties. Mr. **William Roche**, of Harcourt-street, who has been long and intimately connected with the City of Limerick, for which he is Clerk of the Crown, is to be **Crown Solicitor** for the County and the City of Limerick, resigning the office of Clerk of the Crown. We have heard that Mr. Murphy has been appointed Crown Solicitor for Kerry and Clare, and Mr. Gilman for the County of Cork.
