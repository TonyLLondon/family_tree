# Carmel (C.M.)

*Aberdare Leader*, 9 March 1940

---

CARMEL (C.M.). — Mr. Llewelyn Jones, Llewelyn Street, was on Sunday evening, elected financial secretary of Carmel (C.M.) Chapel in succession to Mr. **Samuel Lewis** who has resigned after ten years' service, owing to ill-health. Mr. Jones, who is a son of the late Mr. Edward Jones, a former deacon and precentor, is also precentor of the church. — The Guild on Tuesday week held a debate on "Should the church take part in politics?" in which the Rev. Glyndwr Richards (pastor) and Mr. E. J. Williams participated. Mr. David Wilson presided.

---

**Notes:**

- **Mr. Samuel Lewis** (1887–1967): treeId I17 — ten years as financial secretary places the start of his tenure at ~1930, shortly before his election as deacon in October 1932. His resignation "owing to ill-health" at age 52 is consistent with the physical toll of decades of underground mining (he was a face worker at Bwllfa until at least 1921).
- Rev. Glyndwr Richards: pastor of Carmel — the same minister who would officiate at Samuel's cremation in 1967.
- The debate topic — "Should the church take part in politics?" — is evocative in March 1940, six months into the war.
