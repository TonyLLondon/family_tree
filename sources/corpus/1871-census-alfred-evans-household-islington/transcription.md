Catherine's birthplace is given as **Essex, Clapton** here — in the 1851 census it was "Middlesex, Clapton." Clapton straddled the county boundary.

**Note on Jessie:** Now 22 and working as a **Mantle Maker** (cloak/coat maker) — a skilled garment trade.

## Notes

### Identity

This is the household of **Alfred Evans** (I606), father of [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md) (I289). William, now fourteen, is recorded as a **Printer** — his first known trade. Within seven years he married [Dorothea Knecht](../../people/dorothea-knecht.md) and had shifted to the brass trades (recorded as "Brass Founder" by 1881). Both printing and brasswork were concentrated in the Clerkenwell/Finsbury corridor.

### Family growth

Two sons have been added since 1861: **Reuben** and **Walter**. The full birth order is Jessie (~1849), Alfred Jr (~1853), Arthur (~1855), William (Q1 1857), Reuben (~1861), Walter (~1864).

### Address

**Paradise Place** is in the same Islington East sub-district where the family had lived since at least 1851. Alfred remained at this address through the 1881 census.
