## Notes

**Lewis Lewis (head):** Age 58, born Briton Ferry — a coastal town ~3 miles up the River Neath from Aberavon. Occupation "Porter" — likely dock or railway work at Port Talbot. The GEDCOM (I427) records him as a "carpenter" at Gelligaer with census references RG09/4046 (1861), RG10/5388 (1871), RG11/5308 (1881); those references are now known to be **wrong** — they belong to a different Lewis Lewis. The actual 1871 reference is RG10/5424, Aberavon. The FamilySearch baptism record for David (ARK [1:1:X1BM-ZYH](https://familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:X1BM-ZYH)) independently confirms Lewis Lewis as father.

**Rachel Lewis (wife):** Age 41, born Carmarthen, Carmarthenshire. Her maiden name was **Stephen** — confirmed by her 1825 baptism at Llanarthne, Carmarthenshire (daughter of William and Mary Stephen, farmer) and by GRO MarriageFinder (Q4 1856, Neath). The GEDCOM's "James" (I428) was wrong. She gave her age as 41, implying a birth of ~1830, but she was actually baptised in 1825 — she understated her age by about five years. The true age gap was twelve years (Lewis b. 1813, Rachel b. 1825).

**David Lewis (son):** Age 13, Scholar, born Aberavon. This is [David John Lewis (1857)](../../../people/david-john-lewis-1857.md), baptised at Aberavon 11 August 1857. He is the eldest child in the household. By 1881 he had migrated to the Aberdare coalfield — on every subsequent census (1891, 1901, 1911) he reported his birthplace as "Aberdare" rather than "Aberavon," the two names being easily confused (both "Aber-" towns in Glamorganshire).

**Nine children:** All born at Aberavon. David is the eldest visible (13); the youngest, Samuel, is 2. No child named **Henry** appears — relevant because the strongest 1881 census candidate for David (RG11/5324, 46 Glanaman Road, Aberdare) lists brothers "Henry" and "John." John is present here (age 9) but Henry is not, which weakens that 1881 match unless Henry comes from an earlier marriage of Lewis Lewis.

**The 17-year age gap** between Lewis (58) and Rachel (41) raises the possibility that Lewis had a prior wife and older children not present in 1871. If a "Henry Lewis" (born ~1856) existed from a first marriage, he would have been ~15 in 1871 and potentially already working away from home.
