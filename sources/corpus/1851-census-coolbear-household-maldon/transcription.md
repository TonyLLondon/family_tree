## Notes

- **George Coolbear** (I107) — the GEDCOM's "George Coalbear," now confirmed. He is a **journeyman carpenter** aged 28, born in **Hazeleigh, Essex**. A journeyman is a qualified tradesman working for a master — similar to his son George Gridley, who also started as a journeyman (bill poster) before becoming self-employed. Age 28 gives birth year ~1823.
- **Ann Coolbear** née **Gridley** (I108) — the GEDCOM's "Ann Gridley," confirmed. Born in **Tillingham, Essex**, a village on the Dengie Peninsula near the Essex coast, ~10 miles east of Maldon. Her maiden name is preserved in her eldest son's middle name: **George Gridley Coolbear**.
- **George Gridley Coolbear** (I105) — age 6, born Hazeleigh. Birth year ~1845 is consistent with all later censuses. He was born in Hazeleigh (his father's home village) before the family moved to Purleigh and then Maldon.
- **Samuel Olonzo Coolbear** (~1847, Purleigh) — a remarkable middle name. "Olonzo" (likely a rendering of "Alonzo") is an exotic choice for a rural Essex carpenter's son. It may reflect literary fashion — Alonzo was a popular romantic name in early Victorian penny dreadfuls and novels.
- **Elizabeth Ann Coolbear** (infant, ~1851, Purleigh) — the youngest, born in Purleigh.
- **Family movement:** George Gridley was born in Hazeleigh (~1845), but Samuel and Elizabeth Ann were born in Purleigh (~1847, ~1851). The family moved from Hazeleigh to Purleigh and then to Maldon (Butt Lane) by 1851. All three parishes are within a few miles of each other in the Blackwater estuary area.
- **David Coolbear** (seen in the 1861 census, b. ~1819, Purleigh, wheelwright) is almost certainly George Sr's **brother** — both from the Hazeleigh/Purleigh cluster, with the same rare surname and similar skilled trades (carpenter / wheelwright).
