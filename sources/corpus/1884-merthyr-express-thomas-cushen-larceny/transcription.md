# Larceny — Thomas Cushen

**Merthyr Express**, 14 June 1884

> **LARCENY.** — Thos. Cushen, a tramping labourer, was charged with stealing on the 3rd inst., one jacket value 21s., the property of Margaret Perry, and one jacket value 10s., the property of Mary A. Cowling. Pleading guilty in each case, defendant was committed for 14 days hard labour for each offence.

*Transcribed from [image](../../media/docs/1884-merthyr-express-thomas-cushen-larceny.png). This Thomas Cushen is described as "a tramping labourer" — an itinerant worker. Thomas Sr (b. ~1821, Cork) would have been about 63 in 1884 and is recorded as a labourer in 1881, living with his son Thomas Jr. This could equally be Thomas Jr (~1851), who was 33 and the household head. The designation "tramping" suggests someone on the move, possibly after work dried up at Dowlais during the ironworks' contraction in the 1880s.*
