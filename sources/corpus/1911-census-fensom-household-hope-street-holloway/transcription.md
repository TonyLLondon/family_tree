## Notes

- **Minnie's birthplace is given as "Cranbrook, Kent"** (Findmypast indexes this as "Cimbook Kent"). Her maiden name was **Pope** (confirmed via GRO marriage index: David William Fensom and Minnie Florence Pope, Q3 1886, Islington, Vol 1B, p. 355). She was born **Minnie Flora Pope** in 1865 at Cranbrook (GRO birth index). Her parents were **George Pope** (shoemaker, Cranbrook) and **Sarah Ann Quaife** (dressmaker, Cranbrook) — confirmed via GRO marriage index (George Pope × Sarah Anne Quaife, Q4 1847, Cranbrook) and census records. The GEDCOM parents (Edward Pope & Emma Day from Hammersmith) were a different family.
- David William is still a **plumber** at 46, the trade he began as an apprentice at 16 in 1881.
- **Fred Fensom** (I583, 20) has become a **painter** — a building trade like his father's plumbing.
- **Findmypast indexes Gertie's age as 25 (born 1886)**, but this appears to be a misread of **15** in the handwriting. Age 15 (born ~1896) is consistent with the 1901 census where Gertie was 6 (born ~1895).
- **Three eldest children have left home:** David Jr (~24), Minnie Jr (~22), and [Annie](../../../people/annie-fensom-i291.md) (18, [boarding with the Watts family](../../../sources/corpus/1911-census-annie-fensom-boarder-north-road-holloway/transcription.md) at 17 North Road).
- **Four children born since the 1901 census:** Telly (~1902), Rose (~1904), Ellisie (~1906), Allen (~1908).
- **Name mapping 1901→1911:** William (3) appears as "Bell" (13) — likely "Bill," a nickname for William. Gwynne (1) appears as "Millie" (11) — possibly a middle name or nickname (Gwyneth Millicent?).
- The form is signed by David William himself, with the postal address **9 Hope St, Lower Holloway, London N**. The dwelling has **6 rooms**.
- **11 children born alive** across all censuses: David (~1887), Minnie (~1889), Fred (~1891), Annie (1893), Gertie (~1895), William (~1898), Gwynne/Millie (~1900), Telly (~1902), Rose (~1904), Ellisie (~1906), Allen (~1908).
