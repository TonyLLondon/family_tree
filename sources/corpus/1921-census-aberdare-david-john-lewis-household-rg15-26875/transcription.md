## Notes

### Widower status

David John Lewis (head, b. ~1859) is recorded as **Widower**. His wife [Catherine Griffiths](../../people/catherine-griffiths.md) (I174) was alive at the 1911 census. This establishes that Catherine died **between April 1911 and June 1921**.

### The 1921 coal lockout

The census was taken on 19 June 1921, squarely during the **1921 coal lockout** (1 April – 1 July 1921). The lockout followed the government's decontrol of the coal industry on 31 March 1921 and the miners' refusal to accept wage cuts. All three working men in the household — David John (surface labourer), Samuel (coal miner), and Thomas John (coal miner) — are recorded as **"Out of Work."** The employer for all three is a variant of **Bwllfa Colliery**, the Powell Duffryn-associated pit at Cwmaman near Aberdare.

### Bwllfa Colliery

Samuel and Thomas John are both listed under **"Bwllfa Co No 1"** / **"Bwllfa Coy (No 1)"**, while David John (the head) is under **"Aberdare Bwllfa Co."** Bwllfa No. 1 was one of the principal deep mines in the Aberdare valley, sunk in the 1840s and later absorbed by Powell Duffryn Associated Collieries.

### David John Lewis — age discrepancy

The census gives David John Lewis's birth year as **1859** (age 62 on 19 June 1921). The tree has his baptism at **11 August 1857** at Aberafon. Self-reported ages on census returns routinely differ by ±2 years from parish records.

### David John Lewis (row 6) — relationship

The youngest David John Lewis (age 2 years 8 months) is listed as **"Son"** of the head, but is almost certainly the **grandson** — the child of Samuel and Lily (daughter-in-law). The age of 2 years 8 months on 19 June 1921 is precisely consistent with a birth date of **2 October 1918**. Census schedules sometimes recorded grandchildren as "Son" when the householder filled in the form loosely.

### Lily Lewis — identity

"Lily Lewis" is [Elizabeth Lilian Cushen](../../people/elizabeth-lilian-cushen.md) (I28), known as **Lily**, recorded here under her married name. Her birthplace is given as Merthyr, consistent with her birth registration at Merthyr Tydfil in August 1886.

### Thomas John Lewis — full name

The tree previously recorded this individual as **"Thomas J. Lewis"** (I335). This census confirms the full name as **Thomas John Lewis**.

### Surface work

David John Lewis (head) is recorded as a **Surface Labourer** at the colliery. In the 1911 census he was a **Coal Miner (Below Ground)**. By 1921, at 62, he had moved to surface work — a common trajectory as miners aged out of the underground face.
