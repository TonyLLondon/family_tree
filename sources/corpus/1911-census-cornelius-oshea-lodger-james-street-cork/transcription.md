## The lodging house

The household at 4 James Street is headed by **Robert John Rice** (44, Kilkenny), described as a **Clothes Dealer** (or Merchant), with wife Catherine (42, Cork) and two children. Below the family are **20 male lodgers** — all Roman Catholic, ranging in age from 25 to 75, working as general labourers, or with no occupation given. This is a **common lodging house**, not a private lodging arrangement. Cornelius is one man among twenty in a commercial establishment.

## Notes

### Occupation — "School Master"

Cornelius still identifies himself as a **School Master** on the 1911 census — the same profession recorded on [Mary O'Shea's 1893 birth certificate](../../../media/docs/Ireland%20birth%20certificate%20Mary%20O%27Shea%201893-02-19%20Monkstown%20parents%20Cornelius%20O%27Shea%20Catherine%20Enright%20copy%201967.jpg) as "National Teacher." Yet he was **absent from the 1905 Teachers' Census** — the national register of all serving N.T.s. By 1911, he is not teaching at any school; the title is either a claim to former standing, or he had found informal teaching work that did not require Board registration.

### Marriage and fertility — cross-checked

His return states **Married, 19 years, 3 children born alive, 3 still living** — a perfect match with his wife [Catherine O'Shea](../../../people/catherine-enright.md)'s 1911 return at Monkstown (also: Married, 19 years, 3 born, 3 living). The marriage was 2 June 1892; 18 years and 10 months rounds to 19. The three daughters — [Mary](../../../people/mary-oshea.md) (1893), Teresa Rose (1894), and Madeline (1897) — all survived.

### Age discrepancy

Age 52 (born ~1859) is three years younger than the documented baptism date of **19 September 1856** at Inchigeelagh, which would make him 54. Census self-reported ages in this range routinely vary by 2–3 years.

### Irish speaker

Cornelius speaks **Irish and English** — consistent with his origin in **Inchigeelagh**, in the Muskerry Gaeltacht west of Macroom. His mother-in-law Catherine Enright (79, in the Monkstown household) also speaks Irish and English. His wife Catherine (40, Monkstown head) and daughters do not — the language stopped at the older generation.

### The separation

On census night 2 April 1911, Cornelius was in a common lodging house in Cork City with 20 other men. His wife Catherine was heading a household of nine in Monkstown — herself, two daughters, her elderly mother, and five paying boarders. They reported identical marriage and fertility details, confirming the union was legally intact. The separation appears economic rather than formal: Catherine ran the boarding house as the family's income, while Cornelius lived cheaply in the city. His death record (12 June 1929, at the **Little Sisters of the Poor**, Cork City) confirms he remained in the city's institutional care network until the end.
