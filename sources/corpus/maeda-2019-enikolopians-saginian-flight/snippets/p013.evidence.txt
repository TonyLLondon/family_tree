   In Georgian society of the sixteenth to eighteenth centuries, the role of the
Armenians was quite important as the closest “other,” and many courtiers of
Armenian origin served the Georgian kings as writers and translators.41 The
Enikolopians started learning languages in their childhood and through their
knowledge of Armenian, Georgian, Persian, Turkish, and Arabic, they often
played a diplomatic role. Using traditional skills and probably his personal ex-
periences from his youth, Mirza Gurgen Khan mainly served as a negotiator
with the neighboring Muslim states that were still dominant over the Southern
Caucasus at that time.


5      In the Transitional Period from Persian to Russian: Sons of
       Mirza Gurgen
