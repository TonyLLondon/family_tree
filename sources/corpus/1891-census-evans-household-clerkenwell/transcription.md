## Notes

### Identity

- **Clerkenwell parish** — William's home territory and the centre of London's brass trades. ✓

### The six-year gap

Between William Henry (~1882) and the next surviving child (~1888) lies a gap of about six years. This may indicate children who died in infancy — not uncommon in the overcrowded Clerkenwell tenements of the 1880s, where infant mortality rates regularly exceeded 150 per 1,000.

### Dorothea no longer working

In the 1871 Census (age 10) and the 1881 Census (age 20), Dorothea was recorded as a **flower maker** (artificial flowers). By 1891, with four children to care for, she has no listed occupation.

### Clerkenwell to Islington

The family is still in **Clerkenwell** in 1891 — the parish where William was born and worked. By the 1901 Census they had moved to **44 Pembroke Street, Islington**, the street where Dorothea had grown up in the Knecht household. The move to Islington, probably in the mid-1890s, accompanied the arrival of the remaining six children (Alfred in 1893, then Ernest, Arthur, Lilian Emily, and Frank Edward through 1900).
