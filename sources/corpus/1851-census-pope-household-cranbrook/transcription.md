## Notes

- **George Pope** (I296) is 26, giving a birth year of ~1825, consistent across all four censuses (1851–1871). He is a **shoemaker** — not yet styled "master" as he would be from 1861 onward, suggesting he was still a journeyman or had recently established his own workshop.
- **Sarah A Pope** (I297, née **Quaife**, per GRO marriage index Q4 1847) is 24, giving a birth year of ~1827. This is one year earlier than the 1871 estimate (~1828) and two years earlier than the 1861 estimate (~1829). The slight variation is typical of census age reporting. A birth year of ~1827–1829 is the range.
- **Ellen M Pope** (~1848) — the eldest daughter, also seen in 1861 (aged 13). She would leave home by 1871.
- **Sarah A Pope** (~1849) — a second daughter named after her mother. She does **not** appear in the 1861 or 1871 censuses, indicating she likely **died in infancy or early childhood** between 1851 and 1861.
- George and Sarah Ann married around **1847** or shortly before, given that Ellen was born ~1848. They were both about 22–23 at marriage.
- The family is at the very bottom of the page; they are in the **Hamlet of Court Lodge** area of Cranbrook parish. Their neighbour (schedule 5) is Thomas Kennard, a carpenter employing 2 men — a similar artisan-class household.
