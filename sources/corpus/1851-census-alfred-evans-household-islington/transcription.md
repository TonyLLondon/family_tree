**Note on wife's name:** The enumerator wrote "Cathe" — confirmed as Catherine in the 1861 and 1871 censuses. **Birthplace "Clapton"** is the hamlet within the ancient parish of Hackney; later censuses give "Hackney" or "Clapton" interchangeably. The 1871 census records it as "Essex, Clapton" — Clapton straddled the Middlesex/Essex boundary.

## Notes

### Identity

This is the household of **Alfred Evans** (father of [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md), I289) and his wife **Catherine** (née Burton). Daughter Jessie, born ~1849 in St Luke's (the parish between Clerkenwell and Islington), is their eldest known child. William was not yet born (b. Q1 1857).

### Address

12 Windsor Street, Islington — in the Islington East sub-district, within the parliamentary borough of Finsbury. The family remained in this part of Islington for at least thirty years (see 1861, 1871, 1881 censuses).

### Occupation

Alfred is recorded as **Painter & Glazier** — a building trade. Later censuses describe him as "House Painter" (1861, 1871, 1881).
