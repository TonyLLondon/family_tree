## Notes

- **George Gridley Coolbear** (I105) — a **bill poster and hawker**, not the agricultural labourer one might expect in rural Essex. Bill posting was a precursor to modern advertising — he pasted printed bills and placards on walls and hoardings. Hawking (itinerant selling) was his second trade. Age 36 gives birth year ~1845, consistent with the GEDCOM's 1844. Birthplace confirmed as **Hazeleigh, Essex**.
- **Alma Coolbear** (I106, née **Newcomb**) — aged 27, born Essex (no specific town). She is described as "bill poster & hawker wife," meaning she assisted in the trade. Nine years younger than George.
- **Marriage date discrepancy:** The GEDCOM records the marriage as **1879**, but the two eldest children — Ellen Alice (b. ~1868) and Charles (b. ~1871) — predate that by over a decade. Possible explanations: (a) the GEDCOM date is wrong and they married earlier (~1867); (b) Ellen Alice and Charles are from George's prior marriage and Alma is their stepmother (consistent with the age gap — Alma would have been ~14 when Ellen Alice was born, too young to be her mother); (c) they cohabited before marrying.
- **Ellen Alice Coolbear** (~1868, Maldon) — given Alma's age (she'd have been ~14 at Ellen Alice's birth), Ellen Alice is almost certainly from a **prior relationship** of George's. A first wife who died between ~1871 (when Charles was born) and 1879 (when George married Alma) would explain the pattern.
- **Charles Coolbear** (~1871, Witham, Essex) — born in Witham, ~20 miles from Maldon. The family was itinerant (hawking) so a different birth town makes sense. Same logic applies — Charles may be from the first marriage.
- **George Coolbear Jr** (~1876, Maldon) — if the 1879 marriage date is correct, he also predates the marriage. But if Alma was ~22 in 1876 and cohabiting with George, she could be his mother.
- **Kate Alma Coolbear** (~1877) — middle name **Alma** after her mother, strongly suggesting Alma IS her biological mother.
- **Florence Ann Coolbear** (~1880) — born after the 1879 marriage; definitely George and Alma's child.
- **Alma's birthplace** is given only as "Essex" with no specific town. The 1921 Census (via Rosina's record) doesn't help here. Alma's maiden name Newcomb and an Essex origin will need to be traced through marriage and birth records.

## Neighbours

The household is on Bambridge Road in the All Saints parish of Maldon, a working-class area. Schedule 90 is George Lewin (groom, Hatfield Peverel); schedule 91 is Jane Jacoby (blacksmith's widow, Maldon).
