## Key findings about Edward Burgess

This academic analysis of all 471 issues of Iran's first official gazette provides crucial new details about Edward Burgess and the circumstances of Charles Henry's betrayal:

### Charles Henry's embezzlement — exact amount revealed

**£30,000** — Charles Henry "embezzled £30,000 entrusted to him by **Abbas Mirza** to purchase **muskets and other military equipment from London** in 1836."

**Academic source**: **Denis Wright** (1977), *The English Amongst the Persians: During the Qajar Period 1787-1921*, London: Heinemann, pp. 96-98. [Cited by Ebrahimian thesis, footnote 6]

This confirms and quantifies the debt that left Edward stranded as a hostage. The money belonged specifically to **Abbas Mirza** (Crown Prince) for military procurement, making Charles's theft a matter of state security, not just commercial debt.

### Gazette staff structure (1851)

**Editor in Chief:** **Mirza Jabbar Nazem ul-Maham** (known as **Tazkerechi**)
- Former head of customs office in Azerbaijan
- Iranian general counsel in Baghdad  
- Head of government customs headquarters
- Appointed by Amir Kabir due to trust relationship

**Translator:** **Edward Burgess**
- "Selected and translated news from the Ottoman and European press for Vaqaye'-e Ettefaqiyeh"
- Appointed "by the suggestion of Amir Kabir" in 1851
- Career progression: merchant → translator → tutor of royal princes → Crown Prince Naser al-Din's translator → brought to Tehran 1848 → gazette translator 1851

**Scribe:** **Mirza Abdullah Khattat** (handwrote gazette contents)

**Printer:** **Haji Abd al-Mohammad** (same person Edward threatened about deadlines in Persian Letters)

### Academic significance

- **First comprehensive analysis** of all 471 gazette issues (1851-1861)
- Published in *Iran: Journal of the British Institute of Persian Studies* (2020)
- Demonstrates gazette's role in "popularisation of reading" and "formation of novel understanding of national community"
- Positions Edward Burgess as key figure in Iran's first systematic exposure to "modern technological and democratic developments in Europe and the New World"

## Connection to Burgess family story

This academic source **independently confirms**:
1. **Charles Henry's abandonment** of Edward in Tabriz (1836)
2. **Specific debt amount and recipient** (£30,000 to Abbas Mirza)  
3. **Military nature** of the embezzled funds (musket procurement)
4. **Edward's career progression** from hostage to state newspaper editor
5. **Names of Edward's colleagues** in the gazette operation

The thesis treats Edward as a significant historical figure in Iranian media history, not merely a stranded merchant, confirming the importance of his role as Iran's first state gazette translator and de facto press censor.
