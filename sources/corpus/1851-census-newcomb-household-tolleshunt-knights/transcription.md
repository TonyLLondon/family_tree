## Notes

- **James Newcomb** — age 28, labourer, Alma's father. Confirmed in the GEDCOM as parent of Alma Newcomb (I106). Born ~1823, Essex. Living with his parents at Tolleshunt Knights — a multi-generational labourer household.
- **Susannah Newcomb** née **South** — age 27, daughter-in-law (James's wife). Born ~1824, Essex. The GEDCOM gives her maiden name as **South**. Her daughter Alma later remarried **Reuben South** (1901 census) — possibly a connection back into Susannah's natal family.
- **William Newcomb** — head, 58, labourer. Alma's paternal grandfather. Born ~1793, Essex.
- **Elizabeth Newcomb** — wife, 57, born **Layer Breton**, Essex. Alma's paternal grandmother. Layer Breton is ~4 miles west of Tolleshunt Knights.
- **Charles Newcomb** (21, unmarried, labourer) — James's brother.
- **James and Susannah's children:** Charles (7, ~1844), James Jr (5, ~1846), Sarah A (2, ~1849). Alma would be born ~1855–56 — she has at least three older siblings.
- **The Cousins question:** In 1861, 5-year-old Alma is living with John and Elizabeth Cousins at Tiptree Heath as their "granddaughter." The Cousins are not the Newcomb grandparents (William and Elizabeth Newcomb). They may be maternal relatives — Susannah South's parents or step-parents — or the term "granddaughter" may be used loosely. John Cousins (57, born Norfolk) and Elizabeth Cousins (82, born Suffolk) don't match as Susannah's parents by age or origin. The connection remains unexplained.
- **Same registration piece:** HO 107/1778 — the same piece as the Coolbear 1851 census at Butt Lane, Maldon. The Newcombs and Coolbears are in the same wider Maldon registration district.
