# Fire at Bell Inn, Silver-street

**Chelmsford Chronicle**, 27 July 1883

> **FIRE.** — The property about the Bell Inn, Silver-street, was on Wednesday, about midday, placed in great jeopardy by a fire occurring at a cottage in Church-walk, one of a row of three lath and plaster tenements of other times. It appears that Mrs. Saville, wife of the tenant, left two little children at home, and they probably set light to some shavings, and a conflagration ensued. The attention of a fruit hawker, **George Coolbear**, was attracted by an unusual light. On entering the house and discovering the cause he raised an alarm, and with the assistance of buckets of water succeeded in reducing the flames before they could spread beyond the room, and upon Supt. Smith's arrival with a hand pump the extinguishing was completed. All Saints' Church is contiguous to the spot where the fire occurred.
