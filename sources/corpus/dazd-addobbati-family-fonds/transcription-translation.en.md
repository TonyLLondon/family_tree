## Addobbati family

### **2.2. History**

The Addobbati family moved to Dalmatia at the beginning of the 18th century from the city of Bergamo, following many families from that city who relocated to Zadar and Dalmatia.[1] In the literature they are described as a Zadar burgher family, although the family was admitted to the nobility of the town of Nin in 1804.[2] They first appear in Zadar parish registers in 1730, when the marriage of Daniele Addobbati and Margherita Lanfranchi is recorded.[3] In the following year, 1731, two births are registered: Zuanni Addobbati, son of Lorenzo and Anzola Plavino, and Francesca Zuanni Addobbati, daughter of Daniele and Margherita Lanfranchi.[4] We may conclude that a large family group settled in Zadar. One Addobbati family from Bergamo appears in the registers of Turanj in 1728.[5] There is no information on that first generation of Addobbati either in the fonds or in the literature.

**Luigi Addobbati**, the oldest family member whose documents appear in the fonds, was born to Daniele Addobbati and Margherita Lanfranchi. He was a lawyer by profession, and in 1789…

**Pietro Addobbati**, Luigi’s son, died in 1832 at age 63, which implies a birth year of 1769.[8] That date is cast in doubt by his obtaining a degree *in utroque iure* from the University of Padua in 1786,[9] i.e. at age 17. ### English

… we conclude that he too, like his father and brother, was a lawyer.[14]

**Pietro Paolo Addobbati** was a clerk (*computista*) in the provincial accounts office in Zadar. He had a son Simeone.

**Simeone Gilberto Addobbati** served in the military. He retired with the rank of lieutenant–field marshal (*tenente maresciallo*). His marriage to Elisa Luxardo produced no children. On his obituary notice[15] of 21 June 1926, the mourners listed beside his wife Elisa include his nephew, Dr Pietro Addobbati,[16] brother-in-law Michelangelo Luxardo, sister Anna, and uncle Mariano Rovaro-Brizzi. Simeone is the last member of the Addobbati family whose documents appear in this fonds.

Members of the Addobbati family remained active in Zadar until the Second World War, holding high office. They were prominent figures in the Dalmatian autonomist movement in Zadar in the second half of the 19th century, unlike the Addobbati of Makarska, who favoured a pro-Croat orientation.[17]

### **2.3. History of the fonds**

The Addobbati family fonds was formed through the activity of four generations of the family, chiefly through Pietro Addobbati, son of Luigi, born 1769. In the registers of archival fonds from 1984 and 2006 the surname is entered as *Addobati*. In the documents it also appears as *Adobati*, *Addobati*, *Adobbati* and *Addobbati*. The compiler considers the root of the surname to lie in the verb *addobbare* (“to adorn”) and that the correct form is **Addobbati**.

### **2.4. Acquisition**

There is no information on when the Addobbati family fonds was received by the Archive. It first appears in the *Register of archival fonds and collections in the Republic of Croatia* (1984).

### English

## **3.1. Content profile**

The material of the Addobbati family fonds was produced by family members over four generations. Given the small quantity, the content is thin. Most documents belong to Pietro Addobbati—especially on his education and public activity. The genealogies of related families, mostly authenticated before a notary, are noteworthy; they most likely supported the petition for admission to the Noble Council.

## **3.2. Appraisal**

The quantity is too small to isolate and appraise separate archival complexes separately. The material is nevertheless an essential source for the history of the Addobbati family.

## **3.3. Selection, disposal and retention**

Nothing has been weeded from the fonds; retention is permanent.

## **3.4. Arrangement plan**

The records span 1732–1930. Before arrangement they stood in a single bundle. There was no prior grouping or sign of earlier arrangement. Each item was identified individually, after which series could be formed. Because of the modest quantity and sparse content, the material was described analytically, grouped by generation, and within each generational series ordered chronologically. The last three series are collections of documents gathered during the lives of brothers Pietro and Vincenzo. Lacking grounds to assign those documents to Pietro’s or Vincenzo’s series, separate series were created. Seven series were formed in all. Any departure from Italian—the language of most documents—is noted in the *Napomena* (Note) field.

**Series I** is entitled *Documents of Luigi Addobbati*. Apart from the petition to the Noble Council, all items concern his professional activity. Date span 1789–1792. Item **sig. 1** includes copies of documents from 1410.

## Appendix A — Inventory item 49 (diplomatic)

<a id="sig-49-diplomatic"></a>

**Facsimile:** [page-13.png](pages/page-13.png) (inventory table, signatura **49**).  
**Reference extract:** same wording and line breaks as [`extracted.pdf.md`](extracted.pdf.md) (machine extract from `original.pdf`).

Diplomatic transcription — *Sadržaj* column only, with line breaks as in the typewritten column:

```text
- Notarski  spis sestara Canona koji
imenuju prokuratora Donata Mascherija,
Kanonika Nina nakon smrti svoga oca
Alberta Canone koji je bio brat po majci
PietraAddobbatijaMargherite
```

**Diplomatic particulars**

| Feature | Reading |
|--------|---------|
| Spacing | Double space after `Notarski` (as printed). |
| `svoga` | Printed possessive before `oca`. |
| Run-in string | **`PietraAddobbatijaMargherite`** — no spaces between *Pietra*, *Addobbatija*, *Margherite* in the facsimile. |
| Syntax | No comma before the final cluster; the cluster almost certainly intends *Pietra Addobbatija* and *Margherite* (or *Margherite [Canone]*) as distinct names but was keyed without breaks. |

**Editorial sense** (for genealogy use): the sisters appoint Donato Mascheri, **canon of Nin**, as procurator after their father **Alberto Canone** died; Alberto was **brother of Margherita** in the maternal line of **Pietro Addobbati** (the jammed ending matches that interpretation). The **normalized** Croatian in the main table spells this out.
