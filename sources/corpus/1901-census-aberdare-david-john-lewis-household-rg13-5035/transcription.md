## Notes

### Samuel Lewis — coal miner at 13

Samuel (I17, b. 1887-06-23) is listed as a **Coal Miner** at age 13 — the earliest documented evidence of his working life. On census night 31 March 1901 he was three months short of his fourteenth birthday. The age of 13 is consistent with his June 1887 birth; the census-derived birth year of "1888" is rounding from the age column.

### Thomas Lewis (1891) — absent, presumed dead

The tree records a child **Thomas Lewis** (I336, b. 1891). He does not appear in this household. If alive he would be about 10. His absence confirms he died **before March 1901** — within his first decade. He was one of the seven child deaths Catherine would report on the 1911 fertility return.

### Rachel Lewis — infant, only census appearance

**Rachel Lewis** (I421, b. 1901) appears as an infant, age 0. She is absent from the 1911 census household, meaning she died **between March 1901 and April 1911** — another of the seven.

### James Griffiths Lewis — middle name confirmed

**James Griffiths Lewis** (I334) has his full name recorded here. The middle name **Griffiths** is Catherine's maiden name, given as a family name to the third surviving son.

### Two boarders

The household includes two boarders — **John Lewis** (widower, 35, from Merthyr) and **William Lewis** (single, 30, from Aberdare), both coal miners. Taking in lodgers was a standard coalfield income supplement. The Findmypast transcript listed the second boarder's first name as "William" and the first as "Samuel" aged 66; the original image shows **John** (35, widower) and **William** (30, single). The Findmypast transcript names and ages for the boarders may differ from the image — the image is the primary source.

### David John Lewis — age discrepancy

Age 42 (b. ~1859) vs tree baptism 11 August 1857. The same ±2 year discrepancy appears on the 1921 census. Self-reported ages on census returns are routinely inexact.

### Catherine Lewis — birthplace

Catherine's birthplace is given as **Aberdare, Glamorganshire**, not Merthyr. This is consistent with earlier census appearances (1871, 1881) locating her in Aberdare-area households, though her birth was registered in the Merthyr Tydfil registration district (which included Aberdare).

### Neighbouring households on the page

The Lewis household at schedule 242 is the last entry on page 40. The preceding households on Glen Road include schedule 238 (Evan Evans household, colliery engine driver) and schedule 239 (further Lewis/Evans households). The page header confirms: Civil Parish **Aberdare**, County Borough **Aberdare**, Ward **Gadlys**, Parliamentary Borough **Merthyr Tydfil**.
