# Floral Britain

## Arbroath Competing for 'Britain In Bloom' Trophies

*Arbroath Herald*, 26 July 1974

---

Arbroath is one of a record number of 281 areas — three more than last year — who are competing in the 1974 "Britain in Bloom" competition organised by the British Tourist Authority.

Since last year, Arbroath has taken on more than ever a floral look. The Brothock Valley has been planted, and the rosebeds along the dual carriageway are magnificent. The laying out of grass and the planting of trees and flowers at other parts of the town that have been an eyesore for years, has transformed these desolate areas. A special word is due the Brothock Bridge development where the controversial flower-beds are now beautifully planted with low-flowering geraniums set in grass.

The annual "Britain in Bloom" campaign was launched eleven years ago by the former British Travel Association, which offered a national trophy to the city, town or village judged to have done most to improve its overall appearance by the imaginative use of flowers, shrubs and trees.

The competition is now run under the sponsorship of the British Tourist Authority, which awards three national trophies to winning areas. One of these trophies has been donated by the Worshipful Company of Gardeners.

A fourth national trophy is donated by Gordon Ford, chairman of the "Britain in Bloom" committee for the South West of England. Mr Ford's trophy will go to an area in which the shopkeepers and other commercial interests have made special effort.

A trophy donated by **Mrs Fulvia Lewis**, wife of 'Keep Britain Tidy' Director General, **David Lewis**, will be presented to the finalist most active in litter prevention.

Regional finalists in the national competition will be selected in separate competitions run by the Scottish, English, Wales and Isle of Man Tourist Boards. These will be inspected by a panel of national judges during the first part of September.

The results of the BTA's national competition will be announced at a special ceremony in London in October.

In 1973 the results of the "Britain in Bloom" competition were: Winning City — Aberdeen, Aberdeenshire; Winning Towns — Bridlington, Yorkshire, and Falmouth, Cornwall; Winning Village — Ryton, County Durham. The Gordon Ford trophy went to Bath, Somerset. The 'Keep Britain Tidy' trophy was won by Bridlington, Yorkshire.

**Notes:**

- Earliest located press reference (1974) to the "Fulvia Lewis Keep Britain Tidy Trophy" — confirms it existed by the second year of Britain in Bloom under BTA sponsorship.
- David Lewis named as "Director General" of Keep Britain Tidy.
- The 1973 Keep Britain Tidy trophy was won by Bridlington, Yorkshire — the first known winner of the Fulvia Lewis trophy.
