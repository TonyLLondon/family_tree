## Notes

- **George Coolbear** (I107) — age 15, living with William and Hannah Stebbing, apparently as an **apprentice carpenter**. The occupation column appears to read "Ct Carpr" — by 1851 he is a **journeyman carpenter**, the next step up from apprentice. Born in Essex (the 1841 census records only county). His birthplace of **Hazeleigh** (given in 1851) is ~8 miles north-east of Bicknacre.
- **The meeting place:** This is the same hamlet — Hamlet of Bicknacre, Danbury — where **Ann Gridley** (I108, George's future wife) is working as a farm servant with the Chapman family **on the very next page** (page 19, schedule 2660). George is an apprentice carpenter, Ann is a farm servant, both teenagers, both from elsewhere in Essex. They married ~1844, three years after this census, and named their first son **George Gridley Coolbear** after Ann's maiden name.
- **William and Hannah Stebbing** — George's hosts. William (25) may be a carpenter himself (George's master), or a relative by marriage. The Stebbing surname is common in Essex. No occupation is listed for William, which is unusual.
- **William Curtis** — also on this page (page 18), a 41-year-old **carpenter** in Bicknacre. He could be George's actual master if William Stebbing is not a carpenter. Apprentices sometimes lodged with families other than their masters.
