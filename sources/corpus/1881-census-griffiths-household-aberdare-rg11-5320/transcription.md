## Notes

### Transition from iron to coal

Thomas is now a **Colliery Lab** (colliery labourer) at 51 — he was an ironworks labourer in 1861 and 1871. The Aberdare economy had shifted decisively to coal.

### Three daughters in tinplate

**Eliza** (21), **Catherine** (18), and **Margaret A** (15) are all working as **tin workers** in what was probably the same Aberdare tinplate works. Catherine would marry [David John Lewis](../../people/david-john-lewis-1857.md) three years later (Q1 1884).

### Sarah Morgan — Anne's mother, key to the maiden name

**Sarah Morgan** (75, widow) is listed as **"Mother In Law"** — in 1881 census parlance this means Thomas's mother-in-law, i.e. Anne's mother. Her surname **Morgan** confirms Anne's maiden name. Her birthplace is transcribed as **Builth**, Brecknockshire — meaning **Builth Wells** (Llanfair-ym-Muallt), the market town on the River Wye in central Breconshire. Her occupation is given as "House Property" — she owned rental property.

### David Morgan — nephew

**David Morgan** (16, coal miner, born Aberdare) is recorded as a **nephew**. As a Morgan born in Aberdare, he is almost certainly the son of one of Anne's siblings — a Morgan who also migrated to the coalfield.

### Margaret Griffiths — niece (not the daughter)

There are **two Margarets** in this household. **Margaret A Griffiths** (line 5, age 15, daughter, tin worker) is the surviving daughter. **Margaret Griffiths** (line 8, age 16, niece, brick worker) is a different person — probably the daughter of one of Thomas's siblings. She works in a brickworks, not the tinplate works.

### John Morgan — cousin

**John Morgan** (34, coal miner, born Llanfair, Carmarthenshire) is listed as a **cousin**. Born in the same "Llanfair" as Anne, he is almost certainly her cousin — another Morgan from the family's home parish.

### Address

First time a house number is given: **11 Clive Street**. The family has lived on this street (earlier called "Clive Place") since at least 1861.
