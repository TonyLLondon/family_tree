# Killed at the Front — Private D. W. Cushen

**Merthyr Express**, 12 June 1915

> **KILLED AT THE FRONT.** — News has been received at Dowlais that Private David James Vines, 2nd Welsh Regt., was killed in action on May 22nd. A single man, 36 years of age, he was employed in the Old Works when he went to the front from 8 Ivor-street. Deceased, who was a reservist and had served in the South African War, had been in France since January. His mother has a large family, and Mrs. Owen, 87 Ivor-street, his eldest sister, received the sad information. Another brother is Private John Thos. Vines, who is with the 9th Batt. Welsh Regt. at Perham Down Camp. — Another Dowlais man, Private **D. W. Cushen**, of the South Wales Borderers, the son of Mr. **John J. Cushen**, 28 Graig Cwm, was killed in action at Richebourg l'Avoué on May 9th. His age was 25 years, and he had been nearly three years in the Regulars, whilst previous to that he had served with the Monmouth Royal Engineers.

*Transcribed from [image](../../media/docs/1915-merthyr-express-dw-cushen-killed-in-action.png). Private D. W. Cushen was the son of **John J. Cushen, 28 Graig Cwm, Dowlais**. John J. Cushen is almost certainly the John Cushen born ~1867, youngest son of [Thomas Cushen](../../people/thomas-cushen.md) and [Ann Mainwaring](../../people/ann-mainwaring.md) — making D. W. Cushen a nephew of [David Cushen](../../people/david-cushen.md). D. W. Cushen was killed at the **Battle of Aubers Ridge** (9 May 1915), one of the bloodiest engagements of the South Wales Borderers. He had previously served with the Monmouth Royal Engineers (a Territorial unit) before joining the Regulars.*
