# Southern Reporter — Death of William Roche, M.P. for Limerick

**Date:** 30 April 1850 (and multiple papers, April–May 1850)
**Newspaper:** Southern Reporter and Cork Commercial Courier
**FMP:** [article link](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/search-newspapers/article/BL/0000876/18500430/035)

## Transcription (combined from multiple papers)

**Southern Reporter, 30 Apr 1850:**
> Died, this morning, at his residence, in **Lower Glentworth-street**, **William Roche**, Esq., J.P., formerly M.P. for Limerick.

**Montrose Review, 3 May 1850:**
> 27 — At Limerick, William Roche, Esq., late M.P. for that city. Mr. Roche took leave of public life in July 1841. The late Daniel O'Connell declared in Limerick that "**William Roche was the only man in Ireland from whom he would not demand a pledge.**"

**North & South Shields Gazette, 24 May 1850:**
> Died, at Limerick, William Roche, Esq., late M.P. for that city. **He was the first Roman Catholic** [M.P. for Limerick].

**Witness (Edinburgh), 4 May 1850:**
> William Roche, Esq., J.P., formerly M.P. for Limerick, died on Friday evening.

## Notes

William Roche (1775–1850) died **27 April 1850** at Lower Glentworth Street, Limerick. He was Thomas Roche's brother and banking partner. He entered Parliament in December 1832 as the first Catholic M.P. for Limerick and left government in July 1841. He died unmarried and childless. The O'Connell quote — "the only man in Ireland from whom he would not demand a pledge" — appeared in multiple papers.
