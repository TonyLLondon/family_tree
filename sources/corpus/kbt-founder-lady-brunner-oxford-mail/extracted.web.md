LADY Brunner OBE, who has died at the age of 98, was the founder of the Keep Britain Tidy group.

She was also chairman of the committee that founded the WI's Denman College in Marcham, near Abingdon, in 1947.

Elizabeth Brunner was educated briefly at Oxford High School for Girls when the family moved to Oxford from London in 1916.

After studying acting in Oxford and London she performed in Ibsen's *The Pretenders* and Du Maurier's Trilby, and appeared in a silent film adaptation of Charlotte Bronte's *Shirley*. She married Felix Brunner in 1926, who was knighted in 1929 and they had five sons. He died in 1982.

The family moved to Grey's Court near Henley in 1937 -- the home they later gave to the National Trust, but continued to live in for the rest of their lives.

Lady Brunner held the office of chairman of the Oxfordshire Federation of Women's Institutes five times, becoming President of the National Federation in 1951.

After founding the Keep Britain Tidy Group in 1955, she tirelessly campaigned against litter in towns and countryside, maintaining her involvement for the rest of her life. In 1964 she was awarded an OBE for her devotion to the group.

She was a Justice of the Peace for more than 30 years as well as chairman of both the Women's Group on Public Welfare and Henley and District Housing trust.

Lady Brunner leaves behind three of her five sons -- John, Barnabas, and Hugo, the Lord Lieutenant of Oxfordshire.

He described her as an active, fun, generous person who was devoted to good causes and the community of Oxfordshire.
