## §1 — Framing: Stump among the second generation of foreign dentists

> "Dr. Stump [Ashtump] can be counted, alongside Dr. Stepanian and Dr. Melczarski, after the Swedish Dr. Bertrand Hybenette — dentist to Naser al-Din Shah — as part of the **second generation of foreign-trained dentists in Iran**, who for many years during the reigns of Ahmad Shah Qajar, Reza Shah Pahlavi, and the early years of Mohammad Reza Shah's reign, practiced in Iran and introduced many of the first treatment methods, equipment, and modern instruments of their day, playing a significant role in advancing the science of dentistry and establishing modern dental treatments in Iran."

### Names cited

| Persian spelling | Identification |
|---|---|
| دکتر استپانیان | **Dr. Hartioun Stepanian** — Armenian dentist, studied in the US |
| دکتر ملچارسکی | **Dr. Falak Kush Melczarski** — Polish, Reza Shah's personal dentist |
| دکتر برتراند هیبنت سوئدی | **Dr. Bertrand Hybenette** — Swedish, Naser al-Din Shah's dentist |

## §2 — Origins and arrival in Iran (citing Dr. Najm-Abadi)

> "Among the foreign dentists who were active in Iran in the last years of the previous century and the early years of the current one, little has been written about **Dr. 'Atkin Ashtump'** [اتکین اشتومپ]. His name has been rendered variously in sources as Ashtamp, Estump, or Estamp."

> "According to **Dr. Mahmoud Najm-Abadi**, he was of **Austrian origin but a Swiss national**, and his clinic was at the corner of **Manuchehri and Ferdowsi Streets**; he lived in a large garden between **Fisherabad Street and Iranshahr Street** that was known by his name. He was hired as **Atabak's personal dentist** following the trip of **Mirza Ali-Asghar Atabak (Amin al-Sultan)** and **Mehdi-Qoli Hedayat (Mokhber al-Saltaneh)** to America, and came to Iran."

> "Since this trip took place in **1904 CE / 1283 SH**, in the late Mozaffar al-Din Shah period, it appears Dr. Stump arrived in Iran around that year. He is also referred to as **Ahmad Shah's dentist**, and apparently held this position in later years as well."

### Key facts

- **First name rendered as "Atkin"** (اتکین) — likely a Persianisation of "Étienne"
- **Hired in New York** during Atabak's 1904 trip to the US (see §5 below)
- **Arrival date: c. 1904** — this contradicts the person page's "c. 1921"
- Najm-Abadi: **Austrian origin, Swiss citizen** — the vault [person page](../people/etienne-stump.md) records **Swiss** nationality and treats Austrian/German labels in memoirs and encyclopedias as frequent misattributions.
- Clinic: corner of Manuchehri & Ferdowsi streets, Tehran
- Residence: large garden between Fisherabad & Iranshahr streets

## §3 — Cyril Elgood on Stump at Dar al-Funun

> "**Cyril Lloyd Elgood**, British physician and historian in Iran, wrote about his appointment as Ahmad Shah's dentist and his teaching at the Dar al-Funun:"

> "'The Government of Iran was by no means satisfied with the steps that had been taken thus far to raise the general level of medicine, and wished to advance it further. For this purpose **new professors were engaged to breathe new life into the Dar al-Funun**... Among the new professors was **Dr. Stamp [Stump]**, who came to Iran as the **Shah's dentist** and was chosen as the **first teacher of dentistry** in the medical school.'"

### Source identification

This is a direct quote from **Cyril Elgood, *A Medical History of Persia and the Eastern Caliphate from the Earliest Times Until the Year A.D. 1932*** (Cambridge University Press, 1951). The Beytoote article gives the Persian translation; the passage above is reverse-translated. → See [§ Referenced materials](#referenced-materials) for locating the English original.

## §4 — Dr. Mohsen Sayyah's memoirs (meeting Stump c. 1927)

> "**Dr. Mohsen Sayyah**, in his memoirs published in the **Journal of the Faculty of Dentistry** [مجله دانشکده دندانپزشکی], wrote at length about his meeting with Dr. Stump around **1306 SH [1927 CE]** and named him as **one of only three qualified dentists in Tehran**. According to Dr. Sayyah, his home was a large garden north of Ferdowsi Square and his clinic was on **Ala al-Dowleh Street**. Sayyah wrote of Stump's warm manner and his boasting of his skill in **horsemanship, hunting, and bridge**, and noted that he **never spoke of where or how he had studied** or of his background."

> "Dr. Sayyah continues — citing Dr. Najm-Abadi — about the trip of Atabak and Mokhber al-Saltaneh Hedayat to America and Stump's hiring **in New York** and his coming to Iran as Atabak's personal dentist, and states that **after the assassination of Atabak** [1907], he remained in Iran treating **notables, elites, and ordinary people** alike."

> "Dr. Sayyah called Stump the **undisputed leader of dentistry** in Iran at that time and wrote that he had **amassed great wealth** from this profession, and in his later years built a **magnificent villa in the old Mahmoudieh garden between Tajrish and Evin** and owned valuable antiques and precious carpets. Sayyah also noted the **bitter relations** between Dr. Stump and **Dr. Falak Kush Melczarski** (a Pole, Reza Shah's personal dentist), which led to Stump's refusal to participate in the **examining board for evaluating empirical dentists** and his non-involvement in the **founding and establishment of the dental school**."

### Key facts

- **Hired in New York** — not just "in America"
- Stayed after Atabak's assassination (1907)
- Only **three qualified dentists** in all of Tehran c. 1927
- Rivalry with Melczarski explains why Stump was excluded from founding the formal dental school
- Mahmoudieh villa: confirms location between Tajrish and Evin

## §5 — The Kamareh'i parliamentary diary (1917–1920)

> "In the memoirs of an MP named **Seyyed Mohammad Kamareh'i**, between the years **1296 to 1299 SH [1917–1920]**, we repeatedly see descriptions of visits to Stump's clinic, which was always **packed with patients**. He apparently had a **European assistant named Monsieur Gol** [Mr. Gol]:"

> "'I boarded the **wagon** [tram] and got off at the end of Lalezar Street, walked past the house of Moshir al-Dowleh to Stump's clinic — by the time my turn came it was sunset. He took the teeth, and **Monsieur Gol said: "I have just imported new porcelain teeth of a new type; it would be good for you to get a set."**'"
>
> "'I said: if they are better than this grade, I will take them. He said: they are better. I asked the price.'"
>
> "'He said: **thirty-five tomans**. We agreed I would come back in three or four days to see them.'"

### Key facts

- **Monsieur Gol** was Stump's European assistant (later ran the practice after Stump's death)
- The clinic was near the end of Lalezar Street, near Moshir al-Dowleh's house
- Porcelain denture sets cost 35 tomans (a significant sum at the time)

---

## §6 — WWI arrest by Russian forces (1915)

> "In **1294 SH [1915]**, in the thick of World War I, Dr. Stump was **temporarily arrested by the Russians**:"

> "'After a few months the same thing happened to the **Ottoman Ambassador Plenipotentiary**, and Russian soldiers arrested him together with the **Austrian military attaché** and **Dr. Ashtamp [Stump], dentist of the Imperial Court** [دندانساز دربار شاهنشاهی], in the **village of Vardavard between the road from Karaj to Tehran**, and handed them over to the headquarters of **General Baratov**.'"

> "'Russian soldiers ignored the protest of the Iranian government. The Ottoman Ambassador and the Austrian military attaché were expelled from Iran. The former was sent via Sweden to the Ottoman Empire and the latter to a **prisoner-of-war camp for officers** in Russia. **Friday, 1 Shawwal 1333 AH — 19 Mordad 1294 SH — 11 August 1915 CE**.'"

> "But Stump was apparently released very quickly:"

> "'**Dr. Ashtamp [Stump], a Swiss national** who was the dentist of **Sultan Ahmad Shah**, was among those detained and initially sent to **Qazvin**, but after a short time was released and returned to Tehran.'"

### Key facts

- Described as "dentist of the Imperial Court" (دندانساز دربار شاهنشاهی)
- Arrested 11 August 1915 alongside the Ottoman ambassador and Austrian military attaché
- His Swiss nationality (not Austrian) apparently secured his quick release
- Detained in Qazvin, then released back to Tehran

## §7 — Dental school proposal (c. 1916)

> "A document from **1334 AH [c. 1916]** is preserved in the **National Archives** [آرشیو ملی], according to which **Dr. Stump and Dr. Hartioun Stepanian** submitted a **proposal for founding a dental school**, including a **curriculum and proposed location**, to the **Ministry of Education** [وزارت معارف]."

> "This was **several years before** the establishment of the dental school by Dr. Melczarski, and it appears their initiative at that time **did not come to fruition**."

### Key facts

- Stump and Stepanian jointly proposed a dental school **years before Melczarski** founded one
- Document survives in Iran's National Archives — potentially retrievable

---

## §8 — Public health writing and professional recognition

> "**Dr. Esmail Yazdi** in his book ***Siri dar Tarikh-e Dandanpezeshki-ye Iran*** [A Journey through the History of Iranian Dentistry] refers to Dr. Stump's activities in **public education on oral and dental hygiene** and reproduces one of his articles on this subject published in **1301 SH [1922]** in the magazine ***Nasvan*** [نسوان, a women's magazine]. He also became a **Fellow of the International College of Dentists** in **1308 SH [1929]**."

### Key facts

- Stump published a dental hygiene article in *Nasvan* magazine (1922)
- Elected Fellow of the **International College of Dentists** (ICD) in 1929 — the ICD was founded in 1927, so Stump was among the earliest Fellows
- Yazdi's book reproduces the original article text

---

## §9 — Abolhassan Ebtehaj's memoirs

> "**Abolhassan Ebtehaj**, in recounting his memoirs of residing in Tehran during his studies in **1295 SH [1916]**, wrote of living in the home of **two American women** and noted that Dr. Stump also visited them, which led to his acquaintance with Dr. Stump."

### Key facts

- Stump socialised with Americans in Tehran c. 1916
- Ebtehaj was then a student (he later became Iran's most important banker/economist)

---

## §10 — Abdullah Mustawfi's memoirs

> "**Abdullah Mustawfi** also mentions Stump in his memoirs; where he records the history of the **Mahmoudieh district of Tehran**, he writes that those lands presently belong to '**Dr. Stemp the dentist**' [دکتر استمپ دندانساز], and in another passage describing his own toothache, he notes that his dentist was Stump:"

> "'Thirty years ago when there were not many dentists and hardly any city-dweller went to a barber for tooth-pulling, when I went to inspect the district of Savojbolagh and stayed ten or twenty days, my teeth had **pyorrhea** [پیوره, periodontitis] and **Dr. Stemp, the German** [آلمانی], was my dentist — and like all sufferers of this disease, every so often one of my teeth was pulled **with European forceps and a prior injection**.'"

### Key facts

- Mustawfi calls Stump "German" (آلمانی) rather than Austrian or Swiss — indicating casual usage
- Confirms Stump used **injection anaesthesia** and **metal forceps** — modern Western technique
- Confirms Stump as **owner of the Mahmoudieh lands** (already in our corpus via the legacy email)

---

## §11 — Later wealth and fame

> "As noted, by the early years of Mohammad Reza Shah's reign and in the **1320s SH [1940s]**, he had become **very wealthy**, owned extensive lands in north Tehran, and had established a **brewery in Majidieh, Shemiran**."

> "He also owned **one of the first automobiles in Iran**. During Majles [parliament] debates, an MP stated that in **1912** there were only **two automobiles in Iran** — one belonging to Mozaffar al-Din Shah and the other to Dr. Stump. While this claim is likely inaccurate, it demonstrates Stump's **fame and wealth**."

> "Indeed, his name appears in the novel ***Savushun*** [by Simin Daneshvar, 1969], where the phrase '**to get a proper set of teeth from Dr. Ashtamp [Stump]**' is used — showing that he had achieved **widespread public renown**."

---

## §12 — Death

> "It appears that Dr. Stump **never left Iran** and lived and practiced there for **approximately half a century**. According to Dr. Najm-Abadi, he died in **1330 SH [1951 CE]** at the age of **eighty-three** in Tehran, and after his death his clinic was run by **Dr. Gol**, his assistant."

### Key facts

- Death: 1330 SH = 1951 CE — matches GEDCOM (1 Nov 1951)
- Age 83 → born c. 1868 — **conflicts with** Tallinn parish digest (**7 Mar 1880** per [EBC 2005](../../estonian-biographical-center-stump-report-2005.md)) and grave year **1880** (age 71 at death)
- Half a century in Iran: 1904–1951 = 47 years — consistent with 1904 arrival
- Assistant "Dr. Gol" (previously "Monsieur Gol") took over the practice

### Important discrepancy: birth year

The article (citing Najm-Abadi) says Stump died at **83** in 1951, implying birth c. **1868**. The [Étienne Stump](../../../people/etienne-stump.md) person page follows **Tallinn Dome** parish via [EBC 2005](../../estonian-biographical-center-stump-report-2005.md): **7 March 1880**, Tallinn — **71** at death if the year **1880** is correct. The Beytoote **83** may be error; old GEDCOM sometimes had **3 July 1880** (likely day/month confusion). Grave marker shows **1880–1951** (year only).

## Referenced materials

### 1. Dr. Mahmoud Najm-Abadi — medical history writings

- **Author:** Mahmoud Najm-Abadi (1903–2000), Iranian physician and medical historian
- **Likely source:** His multi-volume work on the history of medicine in Iran (no specific title cited in the article)
- **Status:** Published in Persian; no online full text found. Physical copies exist in Iranian university libraries.
- **Value:** Primary source for Stump's nationality, clinic location, residential address, arrival story, death date and age

### 2. Cyril Elgood — *A Medical History of Persia*

- **Full title:** *A Medical History of Persia and the Eastern Caliphate from the Earliest Times Until the Year A.D. 1932*
- **Publication:** Cambridge University Press, 1951, 616 pp.
- **Author:** Cyril Lloyd Elgood (1893–1970), British physician, honorary physician to the Shah
- **Contains:** Passage about Stump as Shah's dentist and first dental instructor at Dar al-Funun
- **Availability:** Physical copy at [Wellcome Collection](https://wellcomecollection.org/works/bgeqxv4p); [Cambridge UP](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/medical-history-of-persia-and-the-eastern-caliphate/903C69EEE9B3867991D46E654D15C63C) (possible digital access)
- **Wikipedia:** <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyril_Elgood>
- **Action:** High priority — this is an English-language source that directly names Stump and Dar al-Funun

### 3. Dr. Mohsen Sayyah — memoirs in the Journal of the Faculty of Dentistry

- **Author:** Dr. Mohsen Sayyah (محسن سیاح), Iranian dentist
- **Published in:** *Majalleh-ye Daneshkadeh-ye Dandanpezeshki* (Journal of the Faculty of Dentistry), Tehran
- **Contains:** Detailed account of meeting Stump c. 1927; descriptions of his home, clinic, personality, wealth, rivalry with Melczarski
- **Status:** No online text found. Likely held in Tehran University library archives.
- **Value:** Richest biographical detail about Stump in any single source

### 4. Seyyed Mohammad Kamareh'i — parliamentary diary

- **Full title:** *Ruznameh-ye Khaterat-e Seyyed Mohammad Kamareh'i* (روزنامه خاطرات سیدمحمد کمره‌ای)
- **Editor:** Mohammad-Javad Moradi-Nia
- **Publication:** Shirazeh Publishing, 1382 SH / 2003, 2 volumes, 1784 pp.
- **Period covered:** 1295–1299 SH (1916–1920)
- **Contains:** Multiple entries about visiting Stump's clinic; the "Monsieur Gol" porcelain dentures passage
- **Availability:** Listed on WikiFeqh and Atharebartar; physical copies likely in Iranian bookshops and libraries
- **Reference:** <https://fa.wikifeqh.ir/روزنامه%20خاطرات%20سید%20محمد%20کمره‌ای‌%20(کتاب)>

### 5. National Archives of Iran — dental school proposal (1334 AH / c. 1916)

- **Archive:** National Archives of Iran (سازمان اسناد و کتابخانه ملی ایران)
- **Contains:** Stump and Dr. Hartioun Stepanian's joint proposal to the Ministry of Education for founding a dental school, including curriculum and location
- **Status:** Physical archive only. Potentially requestable through Iranian archival services.
- **Value:** Documentary evidence that Stump and Stepanian attempted dental education reform years before Melczarski

### 6. Dr. Esmail Yazdi — *Siri dar Tarikh-e Dandanpezeshki-ye Iran*

- **Full title:** *Siri dar Tarikh-e Dandanpezeshki-ye Iran* (سیری در تاریخ دندانپزشکی ایران) — "A Journey through the History of Iranian Dentistry"
- **Author:** Dr. Esmail Yazdi (1931–2020), Tehran University professor, Harvard-trained maxillofacial surgeon, "father of modern dentistry in Iran"
- **Contains:** Stump's public dental hygiene education work; reproduces his 1922 article from *Nasvan* magazine; notes his 1929 ICD fellowship
- **Status:** Published in Persian; no online full text found
- **Author bio:** <https://www.mohammadmossadegh.com/biography/ismail-yazdi/>
- **Wikipedia (Persian):** <https://fa.wikipedia.org/wiki/اسماعیل_یزدی>

### 7. *Nasvan* magazine (1301 SH / 1922) — Stump's dental hygiene article

- **Publication:** *Nasvan* (نسوان), a women's magazine published in Tehran in the 1920s
- **Contains:** Article by Dr. Stump on oral and dental hygiene
- **Status:** Historical periodical; physical copies may exist in university or national library archives in Tehran
- **Value:** A primary source *by* Stump himself — extremely rare

### 8. Abolhassan Ebtehaj — memoirs

- **Full title:** *Khaterat-e Abolhassan Ebtehaj* (خاطرات ابوالحسن ابتهاج)
- **Author:** A. H. Ebtehaj (1899–1999), banker, Governor of Bank Melli, head of Plan Organization
- **Publication:** London, 1991, 2 volumes, 900 pp. Editor: Ali Reza Arouzi.
- **Contains:** Passage about Ebtehaj as a student c. 1916, living with American women whom Stump also visited
- **Availability:** [Open Library](https://openlibrary.org/works/OL38662880W); [Google Books](https://books.google.com/books?id=kHPIAwAAQBAJ)
- **Wikipedia:** <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abol_Hassan_Ebtehaj>
- **Action:** Google Books may allow searching for "اشتومپ" or "Stump" to find the exact passage

### 9. Abdullah Mustawfi — *Sharh-e Zendegani-ye Man*

- **Full title:** *Sharh-e Zendegani-ye Man* (شرح زندگانی من) — "An Account of My Life"
- **Author:** Abdullah Mustawfi
- **Persian edition:** 3 volumes; first published 1945, revised 1965
- **English translation:** *Administrative and Social History of the Qajar Period*, trans. Nayer Mostofi-Glenn, Mazda Publishers, 1997, 1338 pp.

### 10. Majles proceedings — automobile anecdote

- **Source:** Official records of the Majles-e Showra-ye Melli (National Consultative Assembly)
- **Contains:** An MP's claim that in 1912 only two automobiles existed in Iran — one belonging to the Shah, the other to Dr. Stump
- **Status:** Iranian parliamentary records are partially digitised. No specific session/date given.

### 11. Simin Daneshvar — *Savushun* (1969)

- **Full title:** *Savushun* (سووشون), novel, 1969
- **Author:** Simin Daneshvar (1921–2012), first major female Persian novelist
- **Contains:** The phrase "getting a proper set of teeth from Dr. Ashtamp [Stump]" — demonstrating Stump's popular fame
- **English translations:** *Savushun* trans. M. R. Ghanoonparvar (Mage, 1990); *A Persian Requiem* trans. Roxane Zand (1991)
- **Wikipedia:** <https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savushun>
- **Action:** The English translation should contain the Stump reference — searchable

### 12. WWI arrest incident — newspaper/historical source

- The article quotes a historical account of the August 1915 arrest (Ottoman ambassador, Austrian attaché, Stump) by Russian forces under General Baratov at Vardavard between Karaj and Tehran. The exact source of the quoted passage is not named but appears to be from a published historical account of WWI in Iran.
