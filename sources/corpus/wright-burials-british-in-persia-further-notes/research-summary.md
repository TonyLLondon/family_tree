# Wright "Further Notes" (2001) — Research Summary

**Full citation:** Sir Denis Wright, "Burials and Memorials in Persia: Further Notes and Photographs," *Iran* 39/1 (2001): 293-298.

**Document purpose:** Photographic supplement to Wright's main 1998 article, documenting cemetery conditions and additional graves discovered.

## Key Locations Covered

### ISFAHAN — New Julfa Cemetery (Armenian)

**Oldest British graves in Persia:**
- **William Bell**, d. 1624
- **Edward Paget**, d. 1703
- Both have Latin inscriptions, previously recorded by Frederick G. Goldsmid in *Travels and Telegraph* (London, 1874), p. 562
- Photographs taken March 1999 by H.M. Ambassador Nick Browne

### BUSHIRE — St. George's Church (Armenian)

**War damage noted:** Church and cemetery badly damaged during Iran-Iraq War of the 1980s. Many memorials from Wright's 1998 article have been destroyed.

**Surviving graves documented (2000):**
- **W.J. Bird**, d. 14.10.1890
- **Mrs Caroline Hayward**, d. 19.10.1905
- **Haick Goolzad**, b. 25.4.1875, d. 6.3.1941
- **Tigran J. Malcolm**, b. 1.3.1843, d. 4.5.1920
- **Commandant A.Th. Sterzel**, b. 25.6.1861 at Bautzen, Saxony, d. 16.9.1896

**Historical note:** Goolzad and Malcolm headstones inscribed in both English and Armenian, indicating Armenian community membership with probable British Protected Person status.

**Naval history:** Commandant Sterzel was captain of the *Persepolis*, Persia's first steamship (600 tons, built Bremerhaven 1884). Inspected by Curzon at Lingeh in 1889 with crew of 40 Persians/Arabs and 4 German officers.

### RAYSHAHR (RESHIRE) — Old British Cemetery

**Status:** Abandoned in 1963, in poor condition by 2001.

**Documented grave:**
- **Ernest H. Tayson**, d. Bushire 3.4.1906 aged 26, Leading Seaman on H.M.S. *Hermes*

## Research Significance

**For family research:** This supplement contains **no material relevant to Edward Burgess, Anna Saginian, or Tabriz locations**. Focuses on different geographic areas and time periods.

**For Persian studies:** Documents war damage to important British cemetery sites and provides photographic record of oldest British graves in Iran.
