The Goolzad and Malcolm headstones, inscribed in both English and Armenian, indicate that the deceased were members of the Bushire Armenian community and probably enjoyed British Protected Person status. Commandant Sterzel was captain of the 600-ton Persepolis, the Persian Navy’s very first steamship, built at Bremerhaven in 1884; when Curzon inspected her at Lingeh in 1889 she had a crew of forty Persian and Arabs and four German officers.‘
