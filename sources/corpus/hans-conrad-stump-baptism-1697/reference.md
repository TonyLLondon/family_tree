## Record Details

**Child:** Hans Conrad Stump  
**Baptism Date:** 30 May 1697  
**Location:** Sulgen, Thurgau, Switzerland  
**Gender:** Male  
**Father:** Caspar Stump  
**Mother:** Dorothea Brueschwiler  

## Significance

This baptism record establishes **Hans Conrad Stump** (1697-c.1735) as the father of **Hans Konrad Stump** (baptized 1730), extending the STUMP genealogy back to Generation 11 (late 17th century).

### **Five-Generation STUMP Lineage:**
1. **Caspar Stump** (1662-1729) × **Dorothea Brueschwiler** - Generation 12
2. **Hans Conrad Stump** (baptized 1697) - Generation 11  
3. **Hans Konrad Stump** (baptized 1730) - Generation 10
4. **Hans Ulrich Stump** (1767-1807) - Generation 9
5. **Hans Jacob Stump** (1800-?) - Generation 8

### **Chronological Framework:**
- **Hans Conrad baptized:** 30 May 1697 (parents Caspar & Dorothea)
- **Hans Conrad's son Hans Konrad baptized:** 30 Oct 1730 (Hans Conrad age 33)
- **Geographic consistency:** All events in Sulgen, Thurgau across multiple generations

### **Father-Son Naming Pattern:**
The consistent use of "Hans Conrad" and variations demonstrates Swiss naming traditions where the eldest son often carries the father's name, creating clear generational links.

## Family Context

**Parents:** Caspar Stump (baptized 1662) × Dorothea Brueschwiler  
**Wife:** Multiple wives documented in children's records (Anna Barbel Kaegi, Barbel Kreys, Elssbeth Straub)  
**Children:** Hans Konrad Stump (1730, mother Elssbeth Straub), plus other children with different mothers  
**Death:** Estimated c. 1735 (before wife Elssbeth's later records show widowhood)  

## Research Notes

This record confirms Hans Conrad as the son of **Caspar Stump** and **Dorothea Brueschwiler**, pushing the Swiss STUMP ancestry back to the 1660s generation. The multiple wives pattern was common in 17th-18th century Switzerland due to high mortality rates.
