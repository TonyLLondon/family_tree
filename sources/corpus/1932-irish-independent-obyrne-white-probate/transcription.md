# Probate: Col. Wm. O'Byrne White — Irish Independent, 6 August 1932

**Source:** Irish Independent, 6 August 1932 ("Other People's Money" column). Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast.

**FMP search:** [Irish Independent](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/search-newspapers/results?names=o%27byrne+white&keywords=probate&newspaper=irish%20independent&startdate=1932-08-01&enddate=1932-08-31&exactdate=true)

## Transcription (from FMP snippets — two editions)

> £6,042 — Col. Wm. O'Byrne White, late R.A.M.C., Denny St., Tralee.

## Notes

- William's estate was probated at **£6,042** — a substantial sum for 1932 Ireland (roughly equivalent to £500,000+ in 2026 purchasing power), indicating comfortable professional prosperity from decades of medical practice in Tralee plus his army pension.
- He is styled **"Col."** (Colonel) and **"late R.A.M.C."** — the Royal Army Medical Corps, into which the old Army Medical Department was reorganised in 1898. His honorary rank of Brigade-Surgeon / Lieutenant-Colonel (1885) was evidently rounded up to Colonel in the probate notice, or he may have received a further honorary promotion in retirement.
- Address: **Denny Street, Tralee** — the same address recorded in the 1901 and 1911 censuses.
