# Blooming

*Buxton Advertiser*, 7 July 1977

---

THE 1977 "Britain in Bloom" competition has yielded a bumper crop of entries, says the British Tourist Authority, who organize the annual contest. The record number, including Buxton's are competing for the distinction of being nominated Britain's top floral city, town or village.

National trophies are awarded to the city, town and village judged to have done the most to improve their overall appearance by the imaginative use of flowers, shrubs and trees. A fourth national trophy is awarded to the area in which shopkeepers and other members of the business community have made an outstanding effort.

A trophy given by **Mrs Fulvia Lewis** — wife of "**Keep Britain Tidy**" Director **David Lewis** — will be presented to the "Britain in Bloom" finalist most active in litter prevention.

---

**Notes:**

- Confirms the "Fulvia Lewis Keep Britain Tidy Trophy" was an annual award within the Britain in Bloom competition, given for litter prevention — the intersection of Fulvia's personal commitment and David's professional role.
- David Lewis named as "Keep Britain Tidy Director" (shortened from Director-General).
