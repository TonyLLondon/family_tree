# Meeting of the Citizens of Limerick

*Sun (London)*, 7 January 1802

---

At a MEETING of the CITIZENS of **LIMERICK**, convened by Public Advertisement, at the Royal Club-House, on Monday the 14th December, 1801;

The Hon. and Rev. Dean CROSBIE, in the Chair;

THE following Address to Lieutenant-General Sir **JAMES DUFF**, being moved by **Thomas** Casey, Esq. and seconded by Lancelot Hill, Esq.

Resolved Unanimously, That the said Address do stand the Address of this Meeting.

Resolved Unanimously, upon the Motion of Lancelot Hill, Esq. and seconded by **William** Ryves, Esq. That a Sword of the value of One Hundred Guineas, be presented to Lieutenant-General Sir James Duff, as a Memorial of our affectionate attachment to him, and of the eminent Services he has rendered to the Kingdom in general, and to this City in particular.

Resolved, That this Address, with the Answer of Sir James Duff, be published in the **Limerick** Newspapers, the Dublin Evening Post, Dublin Journal, Sun, Courier, Caledonian Mercury, and Glasgow Courier.

Resolved, That a Subscription be immediately entered into for defraying the Expences of the above Resolutions, and that the Gentlemen who have convened this Meeting, together with Mr. Ryves and Mr. D'Esterre, be requested to act as the Committee for carrying the same into execution.

Resolved, That our Chairman and Committee, together with such other Gentlemen as can attend, do assemble here at the hour of One o'Clock in the Forenoon of To-morrow, in order to wait on Sir James Duff with our Address.

Resolved, That the Committee be requested to receive the Subscriptions to be entered into, and apply the same.

Resolved, That this Address do remain at the Banks of Messrs. **Thomas** and Robert Maunsell, and **Thomas and William Roche**, and at the Offices of the **Limerick** Chronicle and **Limerick** Journal, in order that the same may be subscribed by the Citizens, together with their respective additions.

- The final resolution names "the Banks of … **Thomas and William Roche**" — confirming the Roche brothers operated a **bank** in Limerick as early as 1801.

- Thomas and William Roche's bank is listed alongside Thomas and Robert Maunsell's bank — two of the leading Catholic banking houses in Limerick.
