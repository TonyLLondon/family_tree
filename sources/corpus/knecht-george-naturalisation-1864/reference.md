## Critical finding: Wasserlos, Unterfranken, Bavaria

The naturalisation memorial **clearly states George was from "Wasserlos in Bavaria"**, contradicting later census returns that say "Hesse-Nassau" (1881, 1891 census). This resolves a key discrepancy.

**Wasserlos** is a real village (Kirchdorf) in the **Alzenau** district of **Unterfranken (Lower Franconia), Bavaria**. The Catholic and Protestant parishes for Wasserlos are in Alzenau.

**Implication for German research:** Focus search on **Alzenau Catholic/Lutheran church records** for baptisms 1814–1816 to find Johann Georg Knecht's baptism and parents' names. These records are not currently digitized on FamilySearch and may require on-site research at Bavarian archives or via Archion subscription.

## Analysis and research implications

### 1. Origin: Bavaria confirmed

The memorial explicitly states George was **"a subject of Vardestat [?] in Bavaria"**. This contradicts the 1891 census which says "Hesse-Nassau". Primary contemporary evidence (naturalisation 1864) trumps retrospective census (1891, 27 years later). The census may reflect:
- Enumerator error
- Post-1871 German unification administrative changes (Bavaria and Hesse-Nassau both became part of the German Empire)
- George simplifying or mis-stating his origin by that time

**Action:** Search Bavarian place-name gazetteers for "Vardestat" or similar. Consider OCR/handwriting variants: Vardestat, Vardenstat, Wardestat, etc.

### 2. Arrival date: c. 1852

George states he has been in the UK **12 years** as of 1864, placing his arrival around **1852**. This is consistent with:
- Marriage to Eliza Glasscock by 1851 census (she is listed as married with child Elizabeth Thorbourn, suggesting George may have married her 1850-51)
- Birth of daughter Eliza Elizabeth Knecht c. 1852-53 (first child of George & Eliza)

**Action:** Search shipping passenger lists 1851-1853 for "George Knecht" from German ports to London.

### 3. Occupation: railway employee

George worked for the **"Great [Northern?] Railway Company"** as a "Good Breeder" [uncertain]. The Home Office note may say "Coach Builder", which would make sense for railway carriage construction. The Great Northern Railway (GNR) was a major London-based railway serving routes from King's Cross to Yorkshire and Scotland.

**Alternative readings of occupation:**
- **"Coach Builder"** (HO clerk's note) — railway carriage carpenter/joiner
- **"Goad Breeder"** — unlikely unless specialized railway term
- **"Guard"** — railway guard/conductor (but memorial handwriting doesn't match)

**Action:**
- Search GNR staff records if accessible (may be at National Railway Museum, York, or TNA in RAIL series)
- Cross-reference 1861 census (George age ~46) for occupation
- Check trade directories for "Coach Builders" at Canonbury Terrace, Islington

### 4. Address: Canonbury, Islington

**No. 1 Canonbury Terrace, Islington Road, Middlesex** — George's residence in 1864. This is consistent with the Glasscock/Knecht family pattern of living in east/north London (Whitechapel, Shoreditch, Bethnal Green, Islington).

**Action:**
- Map this address to modern location (Islington, N1)
- Check 1861 census at this address
- Look for George & Eliza in 1871 census (they married c. 1850-51, so should appear together)

### 5. Family: married with one child (1864)

George states he is **married with one child**. By 1864:
- George married Eliza (née Glasscock, widow Thorbourn) c. 1850-51
- Eliza had daughter **Elizabeth Thorbourn** (b. c. 1848) from first marriage
- George & Eliza had daughter **Eliza Elizabeth Knecht** (b. c. 1852-53)

**So "one child" likely means:**
- One child **of their marriage** (Eliza Elizabeth), or
- One child **living with them** in 1864 (could be either stepdaughter Elizabeth or biological daughter Eliza Elizabeth, or possibly a younger child born 1853-64)

**Action:** Locate 1864 baptism/birth records for Knecht children; cross-check 1861 census for household composition.

### 6. Age: 49 in 1864 → born c. 1815

George was **49 years old** in 1864, placing his birth year around **1815** (±1 year depending on whether he'd had his birthday yet). This is consistent with:
- 1891 census: age 78 → born c. 1813
- 1898 death registration (George Knecht, age 83, Paddington) → born c. 1815

**Action:** Search Bavarian church registers (Catholic and Protestant) for **Johann Georg Knecht** baptisms 1814-1816 once the town "Vardestat" is identified.

### 7. Character references

The memorial has two additional signatures at the bottom (besides George's). These are likely the **two householder character references** required by the Naturalisation Act. Names uncertain:
- "26 Brackenbury [?]" — possibly an address
- "C.L. Bonnerefe" or "C.L. Beaufort" — possibly same person as the JP "E.L. Beaufort"

The HO note says "Regular; except an attest should be do not state that they are Householders" — suggesting the references didn't explicitly state they were householders (a technical deficiency, but not fatal).

**Action:** Cross-reference London directories 1864 for residents at "Brackenbury" addresses or named Beaufort/Bonner.
