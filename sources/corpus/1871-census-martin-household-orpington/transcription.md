## Notes

- **Peter Martin** (I596) — age 33, chimney sweep, born **Woolwich**. Birthplace matches the 1891 census; the 1881 census "Sevenoaks" was an error. He is already established at **Frog Lane, Orpington** — the same address as 1881 (Alpine Cottage, Frog Lane) and 1891 (1 Alfrons Cottages). He has an employee: William Deanson, journeyman sweep from Dartford.
- **Catherine Martin** — wife, 31, born **Bexleyheath, Kent**. This is **Peter's first wife**, whose identity was previously unknown. She died between 1871 and ~1876–77 (when Clara Webb's first child Arthur was born). Maiden name unknown from this record — would need a marriage record (Peter Martin × Catherine, ~1857–58, Woolwich or Dartford area).
- **James Martin** (12, born ~1859, "Bric?" Surrey) — the eldest child, not seen in any later census. Birthplace may be Brixton. He had left home or died by 1881.
- **William Martin** (infant, born 1871, Orpington) — died young. Peter and Clara later named another son William (~1879). Re-using a deceased child's name was standard Victorian practice.
- **William Deanson** (26, chimney sweep, servant, born Dartford) — a journeyman working for Peter. Peter is already an employer by 1871, though he's not yet titled "master" (that appears in 1881).
- **Family movement:** The eldest two children were born outside Orpington — James in Surrey (~1859) and Samuel in "Horsom?" Kent (~1862). The family settled at Frog Lane by ~1865 (Catherine Jr born Orpington). Peter may have been an itinerant sweep before establishing his Orpington base.
