# Free Baths — Paddington Times, 11 May 1878

**Source:** Paddington Times, 11 May 1878. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1878-paddington-times-knecht-free-baths-vote.png).

## Transcription

> **FREE BATHS.**
>
> The following gentlemen were nominated as Commissioners of baths and washhouses in the room of Messrs. Hunter, Hall, and Anderson, viz. Messrs. Wilkinson, who obtained 9 votes, Ellerton 7, Doughty 7, Gwyn 6, Knecht 8, Hall 24, Anderson 24, Hunter 31. The last three gentlemen were consequently re-electing.

## Notes

George Knecht stood for election as a **Commissioner of Baths and Washhouses** but received only 8 votes, insufficient to win — the three incumbents (Hunter 31, Anderson 24, Hall 24) were re-elected. This was a distinct vestry role overseeing public bathing facilities, separate from George's other committee work on highways and sanitation. The same issue of the *Paddington Times* also contained "The Sweepers" article in which George "expressed his surprise at the opposition to such a humane recommendation" — defending provision of waterproof capes for street sweepers.
