## Notes

### Identity

This is [William Evans](../../people/william-evans-i289.md) (I289) and [Dorothea Knecht](../../people/dorothea-knecht.md) (I578). Confirmed by:

- **"D. Evans", Wife, age 20, born 1861, Islington** — matches Dorothea Knecht (b. Q1 1861, Islington), married William Evans Q4 1878.
- **"W. Evans", Head, age 24, born 1857, Middlesex** — matches William Evans (b. ~1857, Clerkenwell, Middlesex).
- **Occupation "Flower Maker"** — Dorothea was recorded as an "Artificial Flower Maker" at age 10 in the 1871 Census.
- **Occupation "Brass Founder"** — William was recorded as "Brass Trimmer" (1901, 1921) and "Brass Finisher" (1911). Brass founding (casting) and brass finishing (trimming, polishing) are distinct but closely related trades within the same Clerkenwell workshop economy.
- **Married couple, no children** — consistent with marriage Q4 1878 and first recorded child (daughter Dorothea) born ~1882.

### Address

**Swinton Street** runs between Gray's Inn Road and King's Cross Road, on the boundary between Clerkenwell and St Pancras. The street is within easy walking distance of the brass workshops around Exmouth Market, Clerkenwell Green, and the Holborn metalworking quarter — placing William right in the heart of his trade. The registration district of **St Pancras** matches the district where William and Dorothea married in Q4 1878.

### William's birthplace

The Findmypast index records William's birthplace simply as "Middlesex, England" without a specific town — the original handwriting may be unclear. Later censuses consistently give **Clerkenwell** (1901, 1911), which was part of the Holborn registration district within Middlesex. The 1881 return may say "Clerkenwell" or a variant that the indexer could not read.

### Household of two

At 24 and 20, William and Dorothea had been married two to three years with no children yet recorded. Their first known child, daughter Dorothea, was born around 1882. The small household — just husband and wife — and the single address (3 Swinton Street) places them in the dense lodging-house territory near King's Cross, typical for young working couples in the Clerkenwell trades.

### Trade continuity — Dorothea

Dorothea's occupation as **Flower Maker** at age 20 extends the record of the same trade first noted when she was ten years old (1871 Census: "Artificial Flower Maker"). By 1901 and later censuses she is recorded without an occupation, consistent with the demands of raising ten children.
