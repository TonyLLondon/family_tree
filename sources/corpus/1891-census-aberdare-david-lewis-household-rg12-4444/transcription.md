## Notes

### First census as a married household

This is the earliest census return for David and Catherine Lewis as a married couple. They married about **February 1884** at Merthyr; by April 1891 three children had arrived. David is recorded simply as **"David Lewis"** — not "David John" as on later censuses.

### Thomas Lewis — alive as an infant

**Thomas Lewis** (I336, b. 1891) appears here as a newborn (age 0), born in or just before early 1891. This is the **only census on which he appears**. He is absent from the [1901 census](../1901-census-aberdare-david-john-lewis.md), confirming he died between **April 1891 and March 1901**. He was one of the seven child deaths Catherine would report on the 1911 fertility return.

### Samuel Lewis — age 3

[Samuel Lewis](../../people/samuel-lewis.md) (I17, b. 1887-06-23) appears aged 3. On census night 5 April 1891 he was three years and nine months old. Twelve years later, on the 1901 census, he would already be underground as a coal miner.

### Mary Ann Lewis — Scholar at 5

[Mary Ann Lewis](../../people/mary-ann-lewis.md) (I337, b. 1886) is recorded as a **Scholar** at age 5 — attending school in Aberdare.

### Two Gillias boarders from Hereford

**Richard Gillias** (61, married) and **William Gillias** (23, single) — likely father and son — are both **labourers underground** from **Hereford**. English-speaking miners from across the border, drawn to the Welsh coalfield for work. They speak only English while the Lewis family speak Welsh. By the 1901 census the boarders had changed to Welsh-surnamed men.

### David's age discrepancy

Age 32 (b. ~1859) vs tree baptism **11 August 1857**. The same ±2 year discrepancy persists across all four censuses now in the repo (1891, 1901, 1911, 1921).

### Catherine's age

Age 28 (b. ~1863) vs tree estimate of 1862. Consistent ±1 year across censuses.

### Address

The enumerator's book header shows **Glan Road**, Aberdare. Later censuses (1911, 1921) give the house number as **41 Glan Road** (or "Glen Road" in the 1911 spelling). The 1891 schedule does not include a house number, only the street name.
