# The Day (London) — Toast to Philip Roche and Stephen Roche

**Date:** 18 October 1809 (also Dublin Evening Post, 12 October 1809)
**Newspaper:** The Day (London)
**FMP:** [article link](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/search-newspapers/article/BL/0002433/18091018/004)

## Transcription (partial, from OCR)

> ... Charles Roche, and the memory of his grandfather, Mr. **Philip Roche, whose mercantile spirit laid, almost, the foundation of Limerick's commercial prosperity**.

> A Gentleman immediately gave — the amiable memory of **Mr. Stephen Roche, the President's Father**, which was received with reiterated applause.

## Notes

At a Limerick commercial meeting or fête, toasts were given to the memory of both **Philip Roche** (~1740–1797, builder of the Granary) and **Stephen Roche** (Stephen John Roche, 1724–1804, "the oldest merchant in that city"). The "President" whose father Stephen was toasted is likely one of Stephen's sons (possibly Thomas or William Roche, then active in Limerick civic life).

The characterisation of Philip's "mercantile spirit" as having "laid, almost, the foundation of Limerick's commercial prosperity" is a remarkable tribute, placing the Roche family at the origins of Limerick's 18th-century trading economy.
