## Notes

- This is **David William Fensom** (I292) with wife **Minnie** (I293) and seven children at 65 Georges Road, Lower Holloway. The family had moved from 11 Eden Grove (1891) and would be at 9 Hope Street by 1911.
- **Minnie's birthplace is recorded as Kent** — the first census to give a specific county rather than just "London." The 1911 census would specify this as **Cranbrook, Kent**. Her maiden name was **Pope** (confirmed via GRO marriage index: David William Fensom and Minnie Florence Pope, Q3 1886, Islington, Vol 1B, p. 355). She was born **Minnie Flora Pope** in 1865 at Cranbrook (GRO birth index).
- The household has grown from three children in 1891 to seven. **Annie Fensom** (I291, age 8) appears for the first time — confirming her January 1893 birth. New children since 1891: **Gertie** (~1895), **William** (~1898), and **Gwynne** (~1900).
- David William's occupation remains **plumber** — consistent from his apprenticeship at 16 (1881) through to the 1912 marriage register.
- The same RG 13 piece (173) contains David Sr and Georgiana at 13 Eden Grove (schedule 16, folio 58) — father and son recorded in the same census district, a few streets apart in Lower Holloway.
- Note the neighbour at 63 Georges Road (schedule 164): **Joseph W Dury**, brush maker — the brush-making trade was concentrated in this part of Holloway, the same industry [Annie](../../../people/annie-fensom-i291.md) would enter at the Star Brush Factory on Eden Grove.
