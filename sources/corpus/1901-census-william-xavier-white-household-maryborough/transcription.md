## Notes

### William Xavier White (I410) — solicitor, born India

**William Xavier White** (I410), age 30, born **India**. He is the eldest known child of [William O'Byrne White](../../../people/william-obyrne-white.md) and [Catherine Mary Roche](../../../people/catherine-mary-roche.md), born in 1870 — just one year after their February 1869 marriage in Bombay. His birthplace of India confirms that the first children were born during William Sr's Army Medical Department service in the Central Provinces. The family had at least three India-born sons: William Xavier (1870), Reginald (1876, Jabalpur), and Gerald (1878, Jabalpur) before retiring to Ireland in 1885.

His occupation — **Solicitor** — continues the professional pattern: father was a surgeon, brother Gerald became a solicitor-registrar in Madras, brother Celsus became a dentist. William Xavier practised as a **Solicitor and Notary Public** in Maryborough (now Portlaoise), the county town of Queen's County — over 100 miles from the family base in Tralee.

The head of family signature reads **W.X.C. White**, confirming the "Xavier" middle name and revealing an additional middle initial "C" not recorded in the GEDCOM.

### Mary White (wife) — from Kildare

Mary, age 30 (born ~1871), is from **County Kildare** — neither Tralee nor Cork, suggesting William Xavier met and married her in the Leinster circuit where he practised. Her maiden name is not recorded on the census. A civil marriage search in the Maryborough or Kildare registration districts ~1898–1900 would identify her.

### William White (son) — age 1

Their son, also named **William**, age 1, born **Queen's County** — born locally ~1900. Named after his father and grandfather, maintaining the William White lineage across three generations.

### Two servants — same pattern as siblings

Like his brother Celsus's Tralee household (1911), William Xavier employs two female servants:

- **Anna Connolly** (19, Queen's Co.) — listed as **Nurse**, i.e. nursemaid for the infant son.
- **Bridget Keys** (29, Queen's Co.) — **General Servant**, handling household duties.

Both are local women from Queen's County. The two-servant household indicates a solicitor's income supported a comfortable establishment in this midlands county town.

### The India birthplace

William Xavier's birthplace of "India" — without a specific city — is typical of census self-reporting for people born abroad. His parents were in the Central Provinces (Jabalpur area) during the 1870s, so he was likely born at a military station there. His younger brother Reginald's GEDCOM entry specifies Jabalpur for his 1876 birth.
