## Notes

- This is **David Fensom** (I202, b. ~1836) with his wife **Georgiana Talbot Grindlay** (I203) and five children at 8 Milton Place, Lower Holloway, Islington. By 1891 his son David William would be living at 11 Eden Grove, and by 1901 David Sr and Georgiana would be at 13 Eden Grove — the family gravitated toward that street over two decades.
- David Sr's occupation is **house decorator** — a skilled building trade. By 1901 he would be recorded as a **general labourer** at 65, likely reflecting age-related decline. His birthplace here is **Luton**, while the 1901 census gives **Harpenden** — both in Hertfordshire, about 5 miles apart.
- **David W Fensom** (I292, age 16) is already working as a **plumber** — the same trade he would give on his daughter Annie's 1912 marriage register, 31 years later. He was listed as a "lime worker" in 1891, but plumber appears to have been his core trade.
- **Georgiana E Fensom** (I205, age 19) is listed as a "general labourer, unemployed." She would later appear as a domestic servant and institutional inmate in the [1901 Census](../../../sources/corpus/1901-census-georgina-fensom-institution-islington/transcription.md).
- **Annie Fensom** (age 11, b. ~1870) is a daughter **not listed in the GEDCOM**. She sits between David William (~1865) and Ellen (~1875) in the birth order. Her later life is untraced.
- The GEDCOM lists a son **William Fensom** among David Sr's children, but he does not appear in this household. He may have left home, died young, or been born after Fanny.
- Findmypast links this David W Fensom to a **"David W Fenson"** in the 1871 census — the spelling variant **Fenson** may explain why David William's household cannot be found in the 1901 and 1911 census indexes under "Fensom."
