# County Court Judge Dead

*Irish Independent*, c. August 1920

---

The death is announced of Co. Court Judge R. W. **Brereton Barry**, K.C., 37 Northumberland Rd., in his 64th year.

The late Judge Barry, B.A., K.C., was the son of the late Mr. James Barry, solr., Limerick. He was Senior Moderator and Medalist in Trinity College in 1878, and the following year was Senior Scholar in Common and Criminal Law at the Middle Temple. He was called to the Irish Bar in 1880, and joined the Munster Circuit.

He enjoyed an extensive practice, and held the Professorship of Law at Queen's College, Cork, and subsequently held Professorships of Law in Dublin, and the post of Examiner at the old National University of Ireland.

In April 1902 he was appointed Co. Court Judge of Kildare, Carlow, Wicklow and Wexford, in succession to the late Judge Kane. The late Judge Barry was **married to a daughter of the late Mr. Roche, Crown Solicitor, Limerick**. He was an amateur actor of no little ability, and took much interest in the production of plays.

---

**Notes:**

- Confirms **Claire Roche** married Ralph (R. W.) Brereton Barry, who became a County Court Judge and K.C.
- "Married to a daughter of the late Mr. Roche, Crown Solicitor, Limerick" — independent confirmation of the Roche connection, written 26 years after William Roche's death.
- Barry was Professor of Law at Queen's College Cork, then in Dublin, and Examiner at the National University of Ireland — another major professional link for the Roche family.
