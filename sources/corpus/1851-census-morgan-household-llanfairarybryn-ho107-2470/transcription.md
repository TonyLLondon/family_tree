## Notes

- **Coedefaur** is the same farm as "Coedevan" on the 1841 census — variant spellings of the same Welsh farmstead name in the Rhandir Ganol hamlet. William Morgan was a substantial farmer: 90 acres of arable plus 173 acres of mountain grazing land, with two male farm servants and a house servant.
- **Anne Morgan** (age 84, widow) is confirmed as William's **mother** — listed as "Mother" to the head. Born ~1767 in Carmarthenshire. She was the elderly "Ann Morgan" (age 70) in the 1841 census, now with a more precise age. Her late husband (William Morgan's father) is unknown by name.
- **Sarah Morgan** (wife, age 49) — her birth county is given as **Carmarthenshire** here, but the 1881 census gives **Builth, Brecknockshire**. The 1881 record, where Sarah named a specific town, is the more reliable self-report. The 1851 enumerator may have assumed Carmarthenshire since the family lived there. Alternatively, Sarah may have been born near the Carmarthenshire/Brecknockshire border.
- **David Morgan** (son, 19, b. ~1832) and **William Morgan Jr** (son, 16, b. ~1835) — consistent with the 1841 census. William Jr is apprenticed to a druggist — he was being trained for a trade rather than farming.
- [Anne Morgan](../../people/anne-morgan.md) (daughter, b. ~1829) is absent from the household, as she was in 1841. She was ~22 and may have been in service, or already courting Thomas Griffiths; the couple's first known child Margaret was born ~1855 in Aberdare.
- **Jinken Morgan** (age 20 in 1841) is also absent — by 1851 he'd be ~30, likely married and in his own household.
