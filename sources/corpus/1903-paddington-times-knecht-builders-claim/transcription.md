# A Builder's Claim — Paddington Times, 15 May 1903

**Source:** Paddington Times, 15 May 1903. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1903-paddington-times-knecht-builders-claim-county-court.png).

## Transcription

> **TUESDAY.**
>
> **A Builder's Claim.** — Mr. Hooper, builder, of 9, Queen-street, Edgware-road, sued Mr. Knecht, of 24, Cambridge-avenue, Kilburn, for £15 10s., balance of account for work done. — In opening the case for the plaintiff, Mr. Nonweiler stated that the defendant was the leaseholder of some premises in Brindley-street, and in the early part of last year received a notice of dilapidations there. He went to the plaintiff who carried out the work at his ultimate estimate of £95. The work was passed by the lessors' surveyor, and Knecht sold the property. After this work was done defendant asked him to carry out certain painting and repairs at his private residence. He did so and not having been paid he sued for the amount. — The defendant objected to pay because the contract had not been carried out, the work not being properly finished and also because of the damage done to the premises whilst the work was being carried out. He called several witnesses to prove his statements. — His Honour gave judgment for £15, with costs.

## Notes

This is **Charles Knecht** of 24 Cambridge Avenue, Kilburn — the same address and surname as the son who gave evidence at [Eliza Knecht's inquest in 1908](../1908-paddington-times-eliza-knecht-gas-suffocation/transcription.md). This article predates the inquest by five years and independently confirms Charles at that address.

Key details about Charles:

- **Leaseholder of premises in Brindley Street** — he held commercial property, not just a residence. Brindley Street is near Harrow Road in the Paddington/Kensal area.
- **Sold the property** after having £95 of dilapidation repairs done — the lessors' surveyor signed off the work before the sale. This suggests Charles was managing investment property, following in his father George's footsteps (George owned 271 Edgware Road and 19 Chichester Road).
- **Then commissioned painting and repairs at his private residence** (24 Cambridge Avenue) — but refused to pay the £15 10s. balance, claiming the work was unfinished and damaged. The judge sided with the builder.
- **His solicitor was Mr. Nonweiler** — represented in the plaintiff's opening, suggesting this was a formal county court hearing.

George Knecht had died five years earlier (September 1898). Charles, now managing property and engaging builders, appears to have inherited or continued George's small property portfolio. The family pattern — German immigrant cabinet maker builds up local property holdings, son continues as a leaseholder — mirrors a common trajectory for successful Victorian tradesmen.
