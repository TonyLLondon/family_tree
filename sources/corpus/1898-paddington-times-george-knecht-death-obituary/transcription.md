# Death and Obituary of George Knecht — Paddington Times, 30 September 1898

**Source:** Paddington Times, Friday, 30 September 1898. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Death notice scan](../../media/docs/1898-paddington-times-george-knecht-death-notice.png) · [Obituary scan](../../media/docs/1898-paddington-times-george-knecht-obituary.png).

## Death notice

> DEATH.
>
> KNECHT. — On September 28th, at No. 19, Chichester-road, Paddington, George Knecht, for several years a member of the Paddington Vestry, in his 73rd year.

## Obituary

> OBITUARY. — Another old inhabitant of the parish has, since our last issue, been removed by death, in the person of Mr George Knecht, of No. 19, Chichester-road, Paddington. He took a great interest in all matters of a parochial nature, and during the many years he sat as a member of the Paddington Vestry used his utmost endeavours on all possible occasions to reduce the rates. He earned by his uprightness and kind nature the respect of all parties, both friends and opponents, and his loss will be keenly felt by all who knew him. He was 73 years of age. The funeral will take place at Kensal Green on Monday.

## Notes

- George died on **28 September 1898** at 19 Chichester Road, Paddington, aged **73**.
- Funeral at **Kensal Green Cemetery** on the following Monday.
- The obituary is warm but brief. It highlights his long Vestry service and his focus on keeping rates down — "used his utmost endeavours on all possible occasions to reduce the rates."
- The tribute that he earned "the respect of all parties, both friends and opponents" suggests a man who was civically engaged but not divisive.
- No mention of his German origins, his cabinet-making trade, or his family. The paper remembered him purely as a parish figure.
