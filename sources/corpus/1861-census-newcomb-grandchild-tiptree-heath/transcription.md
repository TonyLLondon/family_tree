## Notes

- **Alma Newcomb** (I106) — age 5, scholar, listed as **granddaughter** of John and Elizabeth Cousins. Born Tolleshunt Knights. She is **not living with her Newcomb parents** — instead she is with the Cousins grandparents at Tiptree Heath. This is a common rural arrangement, especially if a parent had died, remarried, or was working away.
- **Location — Tiptree Heath:** The hamlet heading on the page reads "Tiptree Heath," within the parish of Great Wigborough. This directly explains why the 1901 census gave Alma's birthplace as **Tiptree** rather than Tolleshunt Knights — she grew up at Tiptree Heath, which straddles the Tolleshunt Knights / Great Wigborough parish boundary.
- **John Cousins** (57, ag. lab., born Henstead, Norfolk) and **Elizabeth Cousins** (82, born Suffolk) — a 25-year age gap is striking. Elizabeth at 82 would have been born ~1779. This may be a transcription error in age, or she may genuinely be much older than John.
- **Susan Cousins** (35, visitor, married, mariner's wife, born Hockley, Essex) — listed as a visitor. She could potentially be connected to Alma's mother **Susanna South** (GEDCOM), but her surname is Cousins here, suggesting she married into the Cousins family. **Charles J Cousins** (grandson, 1) may be Susan's child.
- **The Cousins–Newcomb connection:** Alma's surname is **Newcomb** (her father's name), yet she's a "granddaughter" of the Cousins. This means one of the Cousins' children married a Newcomb — or, more likely, Alma's **mother** was a Cousins who married James Newcomb. The GEDCOM gives Alma's mother as **Susanna South**, which could mean Susanna's maiden name was **Cousins** and "South" was a prior or subsequent married name, or the connection runs through a different route.
- **Open question:** Where are James Newcomb and Susanna (Alma's parents) in 1861? James may be working away — possibly at sea, given Susan Cousins is described as a "mariner's wife."
