# 1841 Census of England & Wales — Tresgob, Llywell, Brecknockshire

**Census date:** 6 June 1841.

**Address:** Tresgob, parish of Llywell, Brecknockshire, Wales. The hamlet is also rendered "Trayan Glase" in the enumerator's header. "Tresgob" is Welsh for "Bishop's homestead" — the same place appears as "Bishop Town" on the 1851 census.

**Archive reference:** TNA HO 107/1367, book 10, folio 13, page 7, schedule 600. Registration district: Brecknock. Sub-district: Devynnock.

## Notes

### The 1841 census rounds adult ages down

The 1841 census instructed enumerators to round ages down to the nearest five for persons aged 15 and over. David's stated age of 60 means he was 60–64 (born ~1777–1781). On the 1851 census he gave his age as 75 (born ~1776), which is consistent. Margaret at 55 means she was 55–59 (born ~1782–1786); in 1851 she said 65 (born ~1786).

### Thomas — age 11

**Thomas Griffis** (age 11, born Breconshire) is [Thomas Griffiths](../../people/thomas-griffiths.md) (I422), born 16 June 1830 at Llywel. He would have been 10 years and 11 months on census night (6 June 1841) — consistent with the stated age of 11. He is the only child in the household; the older brother Thomas (born 1828) presumably died in infancy.

### No other children at home

Only Thomas is present. David and Margaret may have had other children who had already left home, or Thomas (1830) may have been their only surviving child.

### Surname spelling

The surname is transcribed as **Griffis** throughout — a common variant of Griffiths in this period and region.

### Margaret's birthplace

The 1841 census did not require a specific birthplace, only whether the person was born in the same county ("Y" for yes) or not. Margaret's column is left blank (neither Y nor N), making it ambiguous. The [1851 census](../1851-census-griffiths-household-llywell-ho107-2489/transcription.md) would reveal she was born in **Cayo, Carmarthenshire** — not Brecknockshire.
