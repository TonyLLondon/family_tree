## Notes

- **Peter Martin** (I596) — age 23, **travelling chimney sweeper**, born **Bexley, Kent**. This is the earliest census appearance. He is itinerant — described as "travelling" and living in "sheds" rather than a house. By 1871 he will be established at Frog Lane with an employee; by 1881 he is a **master** sweep.
- **Catherine Martin** — wife, 23, also born **Bexley, Kent**. Same age, same parish — they almost certainly grew up together. In 1871 her birthplace is given as "Bexleyheath" (a hamlet within Bexley parish); this is consistent. Catherine is Peter's first wife who died between 1871 and ~1877.
- **James Martin** (2, born **Bletchingley, Surrey**) — the eldest child. Bletchingley is ~20 miles south-west of Orpington, in the Surrey hills. Peter was sweeping across the Surrey/Kent border; James was born wherever his father happened to be working. This solves the "Bric?" birthplace in 1871 — it was Bletchingley, not Brixton.
- **Birthplace resolved:** Peter is from **Bexley** (1861, primary — closest to birth). He gives "Woolwich" in 1871 and 1891, and "Sevenoaks" in 1881. Bexley and Woolwich are adjacent parishes in north-west Kent — easy to interchange. Sevenoaks (~15 miles south) was simply wrong.
- **Social trajectory:** In 1861, a young travelling sweep living in makeshift sheds near a farm. By 1871, settled at Frog Lane with six children and an employee. By 1881, master sweep at Alpine Cottage with nine children. A remarkable rise from itinerant tradesman to established small business.
