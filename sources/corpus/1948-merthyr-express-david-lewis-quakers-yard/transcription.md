# Caedraw School's New Head / Other Posts — Merthyr Express, 21 February 1948

**Source:** Merthyr Express, 21 February 1948. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1948-merthyr-express-david-lewis-quakers-yard-appointment.png).

## Transcription (relevant extract)

> **OTHER POSTS**
>
> Other appointments made by the committee were: Mr. D. J. Lewis, Pendarren-street, Aberdare, history teacher at Quakers' Yard Grammar School; Mr. R. Meatyard, of Glamorgan-terrace, Llwynypia, Rhondda, was appointed chemistry master at Cyfarthfa Castle Grammar School; and Miss W. A. Isaac, of Huddersfield, geography and biology teacher.

## Notes

This confirms David's brief teaching career between completing his BA at Bristol (1948) and joining the Colonial Administrative Service (also 1948). He was appointed **history teacher at Quakers' Yard Grammar School** by the Merthyr Education Committee, still living at the family home at **Pendarren Street, Aberdare**. Quakers' Yard is about 8 miles south of Aberdare, in the Taff Valley between Merthyr Tydfil and Pontypridd.

David's 1968 CV lists "Post-Graduate Certificate in Education" from Bristol alongside his BA — this appointment is the immediate result of that qualification. The teaching post was short-lived; by late 1948 he was appointed to the Colonial Administrative Service and posted to Tripolitania.
