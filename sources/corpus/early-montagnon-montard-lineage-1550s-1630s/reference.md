# Early MONTAGNON/Montard Lineage Discovery, 1550s-1630s

**Source:** Switzerland, Catholic and Reformed Church Records, 1418-1996  
**Collection ID:** 4138674  
**Research Date:** 11 April 2026  
**Scope:** Extension of MONTAGNON lineage via early Swiss "Mont-" surname families

## Major Discovery

**Potential extension of MONTAGNON ancestry back to mid-1500s through related Swiss "Mont-" surname families**

## Early Swiss "Mont-" Surname Families (1556-1646)

### **Generation 1 (1550s-1560s)**

#### **Pierre Montalard** (Geneva, 1556)  
- **Record:** Baptism 13 Nov 1556, Geneva  
- **Parents:** Pierre Montalard × Jehanne  
- **Record ID:** 6JCK-279B  
- **Significance:** Earliest "Mont-" surname record found  

#### **Jean Montard** (Geneva, 1563)  
- **Record:** Thomas Montard baptism 21 Jan 1563, Geneva  
- **Parents:** Jean Montard × Anne  
- **Record ID:** 6JCK-KF9Q  
- **Significance:** Contemporary with Pierre Montalard, different branch  

#### **Martin Montard** (1573)  
- **Record:** Françoise Montard baptism 4 Oct 1573  
- **Father:** Martin Montard  
- **Record ID:** 6NQF-N5SR  
- **Significance:** Continuation of Montard surname into 1570s  

### **Generation 2-3 (1630s-1640s)**

#### **Anthoyne Montard** (Montreux, Vaud, 1633)  
- **Record:** François Montard baptism 20 Mar 1633, Montreux, Vaud  
- **Parents:** Anthoyne Montard × Françoise  
- **Record ID:** 6NQN-LGJB  
- **Significance:** **Bridges to Vaud region** where MONTAGNON families established  

#### **Matthieu Montgilland** (Rolle, Vaud, 1645-1665)  
- **Marriage:** 1646 to Claudine Resin (FVJ4-7TT)  
- **Children:** Guillaume (1645), Jean (1647), Jeane Magdeleine (1649), Pierre Jaques (1651), Elizabeth (1655), Nicolarde (1661), Susanne (1665)  
- **Location:** Rolle, Vaud - **same region as MONTAGNON families**  
- **Significance:** Large family, variant surname, geographic consistency  

### **Established MONTAGNON Families (1660s-1710s)**

#### **Jean Montagnon** (1660s-1670s)  
- **Children:** François (bapt. 1667), Elisabet (bapt. 1670)  
- **→ Leads to:** Jean Pierre Montagnon → MONTAGNON ancestral line  

#### **Sebastien Montagnon** (1690s-1710s)  
- **Children:** Jean (1698), Susanne (1700), Jane Françoise (1710)  
- **Probable brother** of Jean Montagnon  

## Analysis & Significance

### **Potential Surname Evolution**
```
1550s: Montalard (Geneva)
1560s: Montard (Geneva)  
1570s: Montard (continued)
1630s: Montard (Vaud region)
1640s: Montgilland (Rolle, Vaud)
1660s: MONTAGNON (Vaud region)
```

### **Geographic Progression**
- **1550s-1570s:** Geneva (French-speaking Reformed Protestant center)  
- **1630s:** Montreux, Vaud (transition to Vaud region)  
- **1640s:** Rolle, Vaud (established in Vaud)  
- **1660s-1800s:** Aubonne/Vaud region (MONTAGNON families)  

### **Chronological Framework**
- **~90-year gap** between early Montard families (1570s) and established MONTAGNONs (1660s)  
- **Represents 3-4 generations** of potential development  
- **Consistent Reformed Protestant** church records throughout  

### **Evidence Strength**
**Strong indicators:**
- Geographic consistency (Geneva → Vaud progression)  
- Reformed Protestant community continuity  
- Surname phonetic similarity (Mont- root)  
- Chronological sequence fits generational gaps  

**Gaps requiring research:**
- Direct genealogical connections between surname variants  
- Missing intermediate generations (1580s-1650s)  
- Specific parent-child relationships bridging surnames
