# A Contradiction

**Essex Newsman**, 15 September 1877

> **A CONTRADICTION.**
>
> *To the Editor.*
>
> SIR, — With reference to the paragraph in your last week's paper with regard to the two children that had a narrow escape of being drowned, I beg to say that the statement made by the elder child is false. I can bring seven or eight witnesses to bear proof of my being there to rescue them out of the water.
>
> **GEORGE COOLBEAR.**

---

Two weeks after the drowning rescue report, Coolbear wrote to the editor to defend his role — the elder child had apparently contested his account. His willingness to name witnesses suggests the rescue was genuine and important to his reputation.*
