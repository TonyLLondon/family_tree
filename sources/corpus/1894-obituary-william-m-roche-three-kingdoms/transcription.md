# Death of Mr. W. Roche, Solicitor

*Unknown newspaper (possibly Irish Times or Dublin Evening Mail)*, c. 14 April 1894

---

We regret to announce the death of Mr **William M. Roche**, senior member of the firm of **William Roche and Sons**, solicitors, and who was **one of the oldest practising solicitors in the three kingdoms**. He had reached the age of 88 years, was admitted to practise in **1828**, and was an ex president of the **Incorporated Law Society** and chairman for many years of the **Solicitors' Benevolent Society**. Mr Roche was also for a long term **Crown Solicitor for Limerick** and solicitor for the **War Department in Ireland**.

---

**Notes:**

- Gives his name as **William M. Roche** — a middle initial "M" not found in other sources.
- "One of the oldest practising solicitors in the three kingdoms" — i.e. England, Scotland, and Ireland.
- Admitted 1828 (consistent with the other non-Limerick Chronicle obituary; Limerick Chronicle says "Trinity term of 1829").
