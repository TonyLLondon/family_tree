# Dublin Journal — John Roche of Limerick, Oil Mills near Sixmilebridge

**Date:** 2 May 1758 (also 15 Apr, 6 May, 20 May, 23 May, 30 May 1758)
**Newspaper:** Dublin Journal
**FMP:** [article link](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/search-newspapers/article/BL/0001049/17580502/007)

## Transcription (partial, from OCR)

> To be Let for Lives or 31 Years, the Oil Mills, situate near Sixmilebridge in the County of Clare, now in the Possession of **John Roche of the City of Limerick, Merchant**, consisting of a double Oil Mill, with two large Cisterns...

## Notes

This is a first-person commercial record of **John Lysaght Roche** (1688–1760), advertising oil mills near Sixmilebridge in County Clare. The mills were evidently part of the Roche family's commercial operations — they traded in provisions, seeds, flax, and oils. The ad ran at least six times across April and May 1758, indicating a significant property.

The Sixmilebridge location in Clare is close to the Stackpole family estates at Mountcashell, Kilneen, and Kilcoman — John's wife Anna Stackpole's family held these Clare properties. Burke's 1833 notes "the fee of which estate [Mountcashell] is now in Thomas Roche, esq." — suggesting the Clare properties passed through the family.
