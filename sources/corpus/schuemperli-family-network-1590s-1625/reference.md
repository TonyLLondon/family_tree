## Analysis & Significance

### **Family Network Evidence**
The concentration of **multiple SCHUEMPERLI families** in **Sulgen, Thurgau** during the 1590s-1620s suggests:

1. **Established clan presence** in specific Swiss Reformed community  
2. **Multi-generational roots** predating our documented records  
3. **Deep regional integration** within Thurgau canton  

### **Naming Patterns & Relationships**
- **Hans** appears as patriarch name (1560s generation)  
- **Zacharias** appears in both Anna's father (died 1625) and later relative (died 1679)  
- **Heinrich** continues family line through different branch  
- **Multiple surname spellings:** Schuemperli, Schümperli, Schuemperly (consistent phonetic root)  

### **Geographic Consistency**
All records concentrated in **Sulgen, Thurgau** with some extension to nearby **Wigoltingen** and **Tägerwilen**, showing **stable regional presence** across generations.
