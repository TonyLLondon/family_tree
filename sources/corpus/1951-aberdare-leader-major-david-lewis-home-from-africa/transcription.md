# Home from North Africa

*Aberdare Leader*, 17 November 1951

---

ON Monday evening, members of Carmel (C.M.), Trecynon, gave a welcome tea to Major David John Lewis, son of Mr. and Mrs. **Samuel Lewis**, Penydarren Street, Aberdare, who is on leave from Africa.

Major Lewis saw service in the Second World War with the First Batt. Welsh Regiment in the Middle East. For the last three years, he has been in the service of the Foreign Office — on the staff concerned with the administration of the territory around Tripoli.

After his present leave, Major Lewis will take up an administrative post under the Colonial Office in Northern Rhodesia.

Trayholders were Mesdames D. Jones, J. T. Davies and — Thomas, Miss Mair Williams, Miss M. E. Jones, Mrs. Llewelyn Jones, Miss P. Davies, Mrs. E. R. Walters, Miss A. Powell, Mrs. E. J. Williams, Mrs. George Griffiths. Cutters, Mrs. J. Roberts, Mrs. J. George, Mrs. P. Evans, Mrs. G. Wilson, Mrs. E. J. Lewis. Miss E. A. Thomas. Tea brewer, Mr. Elfed Davies.

A miscellaneous programme followed under the direction of Mrs. A. Walters and Mr. E. J. Williams. Items were given by Cor Carmel (conductor, Mr. Llewelyn Jones; accompanist, Mr. D. Llewelyn Jones), and a solo was rendered by Mr. Llewelyn Jones. Messrs. David Wilson, D. Ll. Jones, and E. J. Williams read bardic verses of their own composition. Messrs. T. Marchant Harries and G. Wilson made short speeches of welcome.

Major **Lewis** and Mr. **Samuel Lewis** thanked Carmel members for their gesture.

On behalf of his parents, Major Lewis presented the church with a communion cloth embroidered with lace crocheted by his mother. Mr. David Wilson, the oldest deacon, received the cloth on behalf of the church and tendered thanks to the donors.
