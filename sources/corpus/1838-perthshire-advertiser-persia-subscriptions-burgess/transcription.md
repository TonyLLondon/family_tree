# Indian Subscriptions to the Perth Infirmary — Perthshire Advertiser, 18 January 1838

**Source:** Perthshire Advertiser, 18 January 1838, p. 3. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan](../../media/docs/1838-perthshire-advertiser-persia-subscriptions-burgess.png).

## Transcription

> INDIAN SUBSCRIPTIONS TO THE PERTH INFIRMARY. — We are happy to state that Colonel Smythe, of Methven, has received a communication from John Lindsay, Esq., London, containing a draft for £50, being the amount of contributions collected in Persia, by our townsman, Dr James P. Riach, in aid of the fund for establishing the City and County Infirmary. Mr Lindsay adds, that it must give pleasure to the friends of the Charity, to find all ranks of the British community supporting Dr Riach in his exertions for an Institution which promises important benefits to his native city. The following is a list of the contributions, the amount of which has been paid to the Treasurer: —

## Notes

This subscription list is a roll call of the **entire British community in Persia** in late 1837 / early 1838, contributing to a charitable fund for Perth Infirmary. **"Edward Burgess, Esq."** appears between **E. W. Bonham** (the British consul at Tabriz whom Edward later worked for) and **Keith Edward Abbott** (another consular official mentioned in Edward's letters).

Key identifications:
- **John McNeill** — Her Britannic Majesty's Envoy Extraordinary and Minister Plenipotentiary to Persia (1836–42). The £10 heading contribution.
- **Col. Sheil** — later Sir Justin Sheil, British Minister to Persia (1844–53), whose wife Lady Mary Sheil wrote *Glimpses of Life and Manners in Persia* (1856), independently corroborating Edward's gazette role.
- **Major Rawlinson** — almost certainly Henry Rawlinson, later Sir Henry Rawlinson, the Assyriologist and "Father of Assyriology," at this time a military adviser in Persia.
- **E. W. Bonham** — the consul at Tabriz who departed in 1842, leaving Edward in charge of consular business.
- **Edward Burgess, Esq.** — contributing £2 0s 0d, a respectable sum matching the junior officers.

This is the **earliest known appearance of Edward Burgess's name in a British newspaper** — published when he was about 28 years old and had been in Persia for seven years, already trapped by Charles's debts but establishing himself in the British community. His designation as "Esq." (not military rank) confirms his civilian merchant status at this date.
