# Sabalich, *Guida archeologica di Zara* — Addobbati (pp. 503–509)

English translation. Latin passages and proper names left in the original. Editorial notes in square brackets.

---

<!-- page 503 -->

P. 487. — The coat of arms does not fully correspond to the one shown in the "Heyer" at p. 95, plate 57, nor to the authentic one in which the sun surmounts the tower and the tower surmounts the three lilies.

The Addobbati family originates from Bergamo. The genealogy *lecta in Majori 1745 6 septembris* [read in the major council, 6 September 1745] at Bergamo, drawn from the attestations of the Notaries of Bergamo, describes the following persons: D. *Petrus* de Adobatis, Citizen of Bergamo (1495); D. *Joanes Aloysius*, created notary (1511); D. *Ioanes Baptista*, created notary (1559); D. *Iacobus*, notary, created citizen (1610); D. *Ivanes Baptista*, notary (1630); D. *Daniel* (1651); D. *Ioanes* (1676), captain of cuirassiers, who had three sons: D. *Daniele* (1702), D. *Laurentius* (1705), D. *Iosephus* (1709) — and these three transferred to Zara. The five sons of Daniele were granted the *certificate of Citizenship of Zara*. Consequently, in Zara, as time went on they acquired urban offices and honours, built up an estate, and by embracing the legal profession or entering the priesthood gained trust and esteem.

From the book of *parti prese* [resolutions] and various documents (now in the possession of the I.R. Lieutenant Colonel Simeone Addobbati, son of the late Pietro Paolo) we report, as a matter of curiosity, the various urban offices, since the designations recall times past.

<!-- page 504 -->

*Lorenzo Addobbati* (1742) Conservator of the orders; *Daniele* (1762) Procurator of the Casa di Pietà [house of charity]; and Fonticaro [grain officer] *Giuseppe* (1781) and *Luigi* (1783) Prior of the Casa di Pietà; *Luigi* (1788) Justiciary; *Antonio*, Conservator of the Hospital for the condemned; *Luigi* (1789) Provincial Procurator of the Citizens and People. The dates refer to the appointments.

It appears, however, from other documents that Angelo Daniele and Simon Salustio were also Procurators and Heads and that Dr Pietro was Conservator of the order, Head and Procurator.

A document (1756) records that Francesco Grimani invested *Lorenzo Addobbati* in the name of the Serenissima [Republic of Venice] with lands situated in the village of Nadin in the district of Zara. He was also *Rural Deputy of Health during the contagion of Dobropolci* (1770). His son *Giuseppe* was *Captain of the Craine* [border militia] (1770).

Father *Daniele* of the Preachers [Dominicans], doctorate in Theology 1789 by diploma (among the papers of the Biblioteca Paravia), became *Prior*. His portrait is in the Paravia; see p. 400.

*Luigi, son of the late Daniele* (1789) aggregated to the Noble Body of Nona [Nin]. He was granted (1804) the *Diploma of noble of Nona* with the imperial coat of arms, the coat of arms of Nona, and the coat of arms of the Addobbati family: .... *auti collati sibi tituli*, acri-

<!-- page 505 -->

*butae Nobilitatis* .... Dr *Petri* et *Joanes Vincentis fratres Addobbati q m Aloysium* [brothers Pietro and Giovanni Vincenzo Addobbati, sons of the late Aloisio/Luigi]. *)

In the era of the rapid succession of rulers — Venetian, Austrian, French, and again Austrian — the members of the aforesaid family met with the trust of the different Governments and the various diplomas and decrees praise their probity and civic valour.

*Simon Salustio* Addobbati, Head and Procurator of the University of Citizens, delivered a memorable speech at the *Extraordinary Congress of Nobles and Citizens of Zara* for the Serenissima (1797).

His brother *Antonio* is *Sardaro* [border captain] at Bilibrigh; the other, *Don Giovanni*, is Canon (1797) of the Metropolitan [Cathedral], at the nomination of Rukavina. **)

*Luigi, son of the late Daniele*, born 1 October 1744 in Zara; he swore the citizen's oath on 27 September 1762; was Prior of the Casa di Pietà on 10 June 1783; Justiciary on 13 January 1788. He resigned of his own accord and initiated a lawsuit which, through the *nunzii* [envoys] Calvi and Addobbati sent to Venice, was settled by Lodovico Manin in his favour; he was Procurator and Head of the University in 1786, Provincial Procurator

**) P. 453, *Zara Cristiana* by Bianchi.

<!-- page 506 -->

(1789). He is a *Causidico* [lawyer/advocate], signs (1789) a mandate of the Count of Zara to the villagers of Ugljan.

On 21 March 1772, in his capacity as *Procurator and Coadjutor* of the Order, the Father Provincial of the convent of Carin issued him a certificate. On 6 July 1797 he became Procurator and Head of the University of Citizens; on 22 July 1797 he was a member of the Extraordinary Civic Congress and of the Civic Deputation; on 27 July 1797 he was a member of the *Deputation by sea to go and meet the Austrians*.

*Doctor Pietro Addobbati*, born (1769), received during his studies at Padua (1786) the order of the Golden Spur and the title of *Lateran Count*. With his diploma (1790) the doctoral degree, and as a *Lawyer* he already signed (1790) a decree of Angelo Diedo to the villagers of Ugljan. In 1794 he became *Criminal Lawyer* and simultaneously *Lawyer for poor prisoners*. During the extraordinary civic congress, owing to the upheaval of the state, he was Head and Procurator of the civic order (1797). With the arrival of the Austrians, Count Thurn appointed him (1798) effective I.R. Associate Judge of the Tribunal of First Instance in Zara for Zara and District; as Salustio Addobbati [was appointed] Secretary and Chancellor of the Tribunal of Šibenik (1798). *)

*) P. 61, *Raccolta di Editti*, etc., Vol. I, Archive of ancient acts, Zara.

<!-- page 507 -->

Dalmatia under the Kingdom of Italy, and Dandolo appoints Dr Pietro (1807) *Legal Protector of prisoners* (1807), *Advocate of the First Class for the District of Zara and its territory*, as also the lawyer Simon Salustio as *Advocate of the Second [Class]* and the *causidico* Angelo Addobbati as *Advocate of the Second Class*. Napoleon creates (1812) Dr Pietro *Substitute Judge*.

In 1806 Dandolo names him "Member of the Municipal Council" on 10 July and on 28 July one of the four "Assistant Administrators". Under Venetian sovereignty, in 1793, Member of the "Economic-Agrarian Academy of the Capital of Dalmatia"; Member of the "Health Deputation" in 1815; from 1824 to 1825 "First Assessor of the Municipal Administration of Zara"; Member of "Public Charity"; Councillor of the "Building Committee of the Metropolitan Archdiocese"; in 1816 member of the "Repartition Commission", etc. etc. On every occasion he discharged his offices with praise.

In a legal case printed in Venice (1810) by Curnis (the adversary) one reads the words: … *Signor Pietro Addobbati, upright and celebrated lawyer of whom it suffices to pronounce the name to hear well-deserved praise* ….. Dr Pietro died on 4 November 1832.

<!-- page 508 -->

*Vincenzo Addobbati*, son of the late Luigi (born 1776).

*Pietro Paolo Addobbati*, son of the late Vincenzo (1815), Auditor of the Lieutenant-Governor's Accounting Office, Lieutenant of the National Guard (1848 — † 1886).

From birth certificates, marriage contracts, notarial acts, and other authenticated documents it is established that the Addobbati family, after its arrival in Zara, intermarried with the families: Lanfranchi, Plavinò, Sandri, Coltelli, Rovaro-Brizzi; and going back through the Plavinò, as demonstrated by genealogical trees authenticated in 1803 by the public notary Giov. Grisogono de Franceschi, one finds the names Canova, Pasini, Marchio, Marchi (witnessed 1589), Carboncich, Ventura.

*Abbot Francesco Addobbati* was a man of letters; a member of the "Accademia dei Ravvivati di Zara" [Academy of the Revived of Zara] in which he bore the name *lo smarito* [the bewildered]; he wrote (1756) in the book entitled "Orations and Poems in honour of Francesco Grimani, Provveditore Generale" a sonnet at p. L. XXVII.

P. 487. — The Archive of S. Grisogono [St Chrysogonus] was joined to the General Archive of Ancient Acts because, when the Benedictine Convent was suppressed (1807), the rich and precious archive with many parchments should not be dispersed (Fasti, Bianchi p. 15).

P. 489. In the *ginnasio* [high school] building one may visit the Library.

<!-- page 509 -->

Of it, Professor Krones of Graz expresses himself thus: "The library contains a true treasure of books and minor compositions that concern the history, geography, and statistics of the province; among these, no small place is held by the collection of the Ragusan Golubovich (Miscellanea Dalmato-Ragusina, XXXIII volumes)."

But great and important is also the number of manuscripts, part originals and part copies, made — with an example most worthy of imitation and gratitude — by the students of the Zadar *ginnasio*, and then donated to the library. Among other things one finds the Venetian History by the most diligent and exact archivist and chronicler of the Republic, Giovanni Giacomo Caroldo, secretary of the Council of Ten; it runs from the earliest times to the year 1383 and was transcribed by a certain Borcich from another copy which is in the Court Library in Vienna, among the so-called "Foscarini manuscripts". In these copies one also finds the Ragusan annalists Ragnina (de Araneis, Araneus, according to the Paulovich codex) and Resti. The works of the first were published by Professor Nodilo for the Academy of Zagreb, the second from the Biblioteca storica della Dalmazia.
