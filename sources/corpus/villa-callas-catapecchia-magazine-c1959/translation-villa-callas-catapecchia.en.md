# CURRENT AFFAIRS

## FOR MARIA CALLAS THIS VILLA IS A HOVEL

In this villa in Sirmione, on Lake Garda, Commendatore Giovanni Battista Meneghini is living through the bitterest and most turbulent hours of his life. The villa has three floors, plus a solarium built in the form of a turret on the terrace. In the park that surrounds it there is also a small rectangular swimming pool. Meneghini bought the property three years ago from a Veronese industrialist. Maria Callas took possession of it in the summer of 1957, inviting all her Milan friends for the inauguration. She wanted no photographers: "They can come when I can show them the villa I have in mind, not this hovel," she told a few intimate guests. Meneghini, as usual, managed to hide his embarrassment with another affectionate quip: "You would deserve the most beautiful villa in the world," he told his wife. In the *hovel* (which is in fact elegant and comfortable) Meneghini continues to receive his lawyers from Verona and Milan: the task is still to settle down to the last centesimo the division of what, until a few weeks ago, was the joint estate of the two spouses now in conflict. By now "Titta" Meneghini (as his wife used to call him) makes no further secret of the romantic *infatuation* of Maria Callas with the Greek shipping magnate Aristotle Onassis. He does not, however, dare to reproach the famous singer: "It is all Onassis's fault: he is a man capable of anything. He wants to make publicity for himself on the back of a great artist. Maria is naive and she fell for it." But not everyone agrees about the supposed naivety of Maria Callas.
