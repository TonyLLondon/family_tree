## Notes

- **Rosina Martin** (I104) — infant, the youngest child and only daughter. Born Orpington, 1891. She married [Archibald Percy Coolbear](../../../people/archibald-percy-coolbear.md) on 28 October 1911.
- **Peter Martin** — head, chimney sweep, born **Woolwich**. This is a **trade family**: Peter Sr, his eldest son Peter Jr (20), and Arthur (13) are all chimney sweeps. Archibald Percy Coolbear, who married Rosina in 1911, was also a chimney sweep — almost certainly a trade connection through the Martin family.
- **Edward Martin** — son, age 3, born Orpington. This matches the **Edward Martin** listed as Archibald's brother-in-law in the 1921 census (age 31, stoker, born Kent). He would be Rosina's brother.
- **Clara Martin** — wife, age 40. Birthplace is blank or illegible in the transcript. She would be Rosina's mother. Maiden name unknown from this record.
- The family moved from **Woolwich** to **Orpington** between ~1871 and ~1878 (Peter Jr born Woolwich, Arthur born Orpington). Peter Sr started his sweep business in Woolwich and relocated to Orpington, a semi-rural town ~10 miles south.
- **Arthur Martin** (13) is already working as a chimney sweep — child labour in the sweep trade continued despite Victorian legislation. At 13 he would be too large to climb flues, but would assist with equipment, soot collection, and smaller jobs.
