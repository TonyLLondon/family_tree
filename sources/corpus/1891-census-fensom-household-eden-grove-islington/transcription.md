## Notes

- This is **David William Fensom** (b. Oct 1864, Islington), father of [Annie Fensom](../../../people/annie-fensom-i291.md) (b. Jan 1893, not yet born at this census). His wife is **Minnie Flora Pope** (I293, b. 1865, Cranbrook, Kent). Age 25 here gives birth year ~1866, close to the GRO-registered 1865.
- The household is at **11 Eden Grove** — the same street where David's parents, [David Fensom](../../../people/david-fensom-i202.md) (I202) and Georgiana Grindlay (I203), would be recorded at **13 Eden Grove** in 1901. The extended family lived in adjacent houses on this street.
- Findmypast indexes the occupation as "Line worker", but the original handwriting appears to read **"Lime Worker"** — someone working with lime in the building trades. By the 1912 marriage of his daughter Annie, David gives his occupation as **plumber**, a progression within construction.
- Three children are present, all Annie's **older siblings** and previously unknown:
  - **David** (b. ~1887) — the eldest, born about a year after the marriage (8 Aug 1886).
  - **Minnie** (b. ~1889) — named for her mother.
  - **Fred** (b. ~1891) — an infant at this census.
- Annie Fensom (b. 29 Jan 1893) would arrive roughly two years after this snapshot.
- All family members are recorded as born in London, consistent with Islington-area births.
