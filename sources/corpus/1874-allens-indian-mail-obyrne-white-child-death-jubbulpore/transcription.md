# Death of Tommie White — Allen's Indian Mail, 10 October 1874

**Source:** Allen's Indian Mail, 10 October 1874. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast.

**FMP search:** [Allen's Indian Mail](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/search-newspapers/results?names=o%27byrne+white&newspaper=allen%27s%20indian%20mail&startdate=1874-10-10&enddate=1874-10-10&exactdate=true)

## Transcription

> DEATHS.
>
> WHITE — At Marwarra, Sept. 8, Tommie, child of W. O'Byrne White, Station Staff Surgeon, Jubbulpore, aged 1 year.

## Notes

- **Tommie White** died on **8 September 1874** at **Marwarra** (likely Narwar or Mhow, a hill station near Jubbulpore used by the garrison), aged 1 year — born c. September 1873, matching the **Irish Times birth announcement of 2 October 1873** from Jubbulpore.
- William is titled **"Station Staff Surgeon, Jubbulpore"** — confirming his posting and his promotion from Assistant Surgeon to Surgeon (1 March 1873 per the Army List).
- This is a previously unknown child death. The 1911 census records William as having had **8 children born alive, 6 still living** — Tommie was one of the two who did not survive.
- Infant mortality in the Central Provinces garrison was substantial; cholera, malaria, and enteric fever were the leading causes.
