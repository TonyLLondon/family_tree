# Old Resident Suffocated — Kilburn Times, 20 November 1908

**Source:** Kilburn Times, 20 November 1908. Also Paddington Times, 20 November 1908; Daily Telegraph, 17 November 1908. Via British Newspaper Archive / FindMyPast. [Scan (Kilburn Times)](../../media/docs/1908-kilburn-times-eliza-knecht-suffocation-inquest.png).

## Transcription

> **Old Resident Suffocated.**
>
> On Monday morning the death of Sarah Eliza Victoria Knecht, aged 65, the widow of a retired cabinet maker (formerly a well-known vestryman and Conservative in the Borough), of 19, Chichester-road, Paddington, was enquired into by Dr. Thomas. — Charles Knecht, of Cambridge-avenue, a son, said his mother enjoyed good health for her age, and had sufficient for all her wants. He last saw her alive on Sunday week, when she was apparently as usual, and very cheerful. He was informed of her death on Friday, and on going to the house found that there was a strong smell of gas, which appeared to be coming through the floor, from the kitchen below, in which there was a leakage in the pipe. The kitchen was unoccupied. Mrs. Margaret Smith, living in the same house, said the deceased slept beneath her and she was quite well when she last saw her alive on Thursday afternoon. She went to bed about 10.15 p.m. occupying the room alone. At 12.30 the next morning witness knocked at the door, but received no answer. She entered, and then saw her lying dead as though she was endeavouring to get up when she became unconscious. There was a smell of gas. — Jas. Paton, a labourer, said he was called in on Friday and found that a three-quarter compo pipe had broken, and fallen from the ceiling in the room below. A large quantity of gas had escaped, and got through the floor into the room. — Dr. Graves said he knew Mrs. Knecht, who was a very old resident of Paddington, well. Death was due to suffocation, following coal-gas poisoning, and the deceased must have been breathing this foul gas for hours. Otherwise, she was quite healthy. — The Coroner said he knew the late Mr. Knecht very well, and he was on the Vestry for many years. — The jury returned a verdict of "Accidental death."
>
> **THE FUNERAL**
>
> took place yesterday afternoon at Kensal Green Cemetery. The cortège left No. 24, Cambridge-avenue, Kilburn, amid many signs of neighbourly condolence, and the mourners who followed were Mr. and Mrs. Knecht, jun., Mr. Edwin Stephens, Mrs. Walker, Mrs. Creighton, Mrs. A. Stephens, Mrs. Bradley, and Miss Lott. Beautiful wreaths were placed on the coffin and these were sent by Jim and Ada, Selina and Ada Walker, Kate, Mr. and Mrs. A. C. Stephens, Mr. Edwin Stephens and family, Mr. and Miss Alice Lott, Mr. and Mrs. Embleton, Mr. and Mrs. H. B. Bradley, Mr. and Mrs. Roberts, Mr. and Mrs. Ingram, Mr. and Miss Dunsford, Mr. and Mrs. Cook and the maids. Mr. and Mrs. Knecht, of 24, Cambridge-avenue, desire to return their sincere thanks to neighbours and friends for the kind sympathy shown them in their bereavement.

## Notes

### What happened

Eliza died sometime between Thursday evening and Friday morning (probably overnight Thursday–Friday) in her room at 19 Chichester Road — the house she and George had lived in since at least 1881. A composition gas pipe in the ceiling of the unoccupied kitchen below her room broke and fell, releasing coal gas that seeped up through the floorboards. Mrs. Margaret Smith, who also lived in the house, found her at 12.30am — lying as though she had tried to get up but lost consciousness. Dr. Graves confirmed she had been breathing gas for hours and was otherwise healthy. The jury returned "Accidental death."

### Key biographical details

- **Full name confirmed:** Sarah Eliza Victoria Knecht (the "Sarah" and "Victoria" were not known from census records).
- **Age at death:** 65 — consistent with a birth c. 1843. This is younger than previous estimates from censuses (born ~1828 per baptism, or ~1840 per 1891 census). The age may be approximate or Eliza may have understated her age throughout life.
- **Son Charles Knecht** of **24 Cambridge Avenue, Kilburn** — gave evidence. This is the first identified adult child of George and Eliza beyond Dorothea. "Charles" does not match any of the children on the 1871 census (Francis Henry, Elizabeth, Dorothea, George William, Hannah, Louisa Sarah) — he may be a later child born after 1871, or "Charles" may be a middle name used by one of them.
- **The coroner** (Dr. Thomas) personally knew George Knecht: "he knew the late Mr. Knecht very well, and he was on the Vestry for many years" — independent corroboration of George's vestry service, from someone who knew him.
- **George described as** "a retired cabinet maker (formerly a well-known vestryman and Conservative in the Borough)" — the only source for George's Conservative political affiliation.
- **Funeral at Kensal Green Cemetery** — the same cemetery where George was buried in 1898. Eliza was buried with him.
- **Mourners** include "Mr. and Mrs. Knecht, jun." (presumably son Charles and his wife), Edwin Stephens, Mrs. Walker, Mrs. Creighton, Miss Lott — none of these are identifiable as Eliza's known children by their married names, but several could be daughters married under new surnames.
- **Wreath senders** include "Jim and Ada" and "Selina and Ada Walker" — "Jim" could be James (Francis Henry's possible name), and "Selina" is an otherwise unknown family name.
- **The house at 19 Chichester Road** was evidently subdivided — Mrs. Margaret Smith lived upstairs, and there was an unoccupied kitchen, suggesting lodgers or multiple tenants. Eliza had stayed in the house for a decade after George's death but may have been letting rooms.
