## Notes

This is a contemporary newspaper report listing the personnel of the British diplomatic mission to Persia, c. 1810. **"Mr. Cormick, Surgeon to the Caroni"** is almost certainly **John Cormick** — William Cormick's father — who went to Persia as a military/diplomatic surgeon and remained to serve the Shah.

"Caroni" is likely an OCR garble of a ship name or military unit designation; the original newspaper text would clarify.

Anna Saginian's 1880 interview (NYPL appendix) confirms the family tradition: "Dr. Cormick's father came to Persia to be on the medical staff of the Shah." This 1810 newspaper notice is the earliest independent press evidence of that journey — placing a Cormick surgeon in the Persian diplomatic service at the very start of the family's connection to the Qajar court.
