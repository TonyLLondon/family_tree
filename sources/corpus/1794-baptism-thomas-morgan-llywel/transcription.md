## Significance

This baptism identifies [Thomas Morgan](../../people/thomas-morgan-nantyglo.md) (I606) — the Nantyglo miner whose son [Keziah](../../people/keziah-morgan.md) married into the Cushen line — as a son of **Morgan Morgans** and **Lettice**. These are the same parents as [William Morgan](../../people/william-morgan-llanfairarybryn.md) (I657), baptised at the same parish on 15 September 1795 (FMP `R_732790335`). Thomas and William are therefore **brothers**, and two branches of the tree converge at a single couple.

The mother's name is spelled **Lettice** here and **Lettuce** on William's 1795 record — normal 18th-century spelling variation of the same name (Welsh form of Lettice, from Latin *Laetitia*). Same parish, same register batch (C08147-1), same FamilySearch place ID (10578868), same father.

This creates a **pedigree collapse**: Morgan Morgans (I659) and Lettice (I660) appear in two separate positions in the ancestor chart. See [Pedigree collapse — Morgan Morgans & Lettice](../../topics/pedigree-collapse-morgan-morgans.md).

The baptism date of 15 February 1794 makes Thomas about 4 years older than census records suggest (~1798). Census ages in this period are unreliable: the 1841 census rounded adult ages down to the nearest 5, and self-reported ages routinely varied by several years. The Thomas Morgan in the tree also gave his birthplace variously as "Llanworted" (Llanwrtyd) and "Lampeter" on different censuses — Llywel is a neighbouring parish in the same upper Usk valley.
