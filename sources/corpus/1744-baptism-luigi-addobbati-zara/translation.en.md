# Translation (English)

Literal English translation of the Italian baptismal entry. Bracketed material expands abbreviations or marks reading uncertainty; nothing is added that is not in the manuscript.

> *Addobati* &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; On the 11th [or 2nd?] day of November 1744
>
> I, Don Carlo Felicinouvich, Curate of this Metropolitan Church, have baptized a male infant born on the past 1st of October, son of Mr. Daniel Addobbati, citizen of this city, begotten in lawful matrimony with Mrs. Margarita Lanfranchi, to whom he gave the name **Anzolo Alloisio**. His godfather was Mr. Christoffolo Guardi.

The day of the month is genuinely ambiguous in the scribe's hand (`11` vs `ii` = 2); November is secure, both because of the abbreviation `N.bre` and because the entry refers back to the child's birth as "the past 1st of October" — the entry must therefore postdate 1 October 1744.
