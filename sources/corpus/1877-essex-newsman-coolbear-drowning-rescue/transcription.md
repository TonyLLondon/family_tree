# Gallant Rescue from Drowning

**Essex Newsman**, 1 September 1877

> **GALLANT RESCUE FROM DROWNING.** — One day last week, among seven children disporting themselves near the Beeleigh locks, were two named Rowe — Charles, aged 5, and his sister, 14. As the tide advanced the 5yr. old, Charlie, took a header, but getting beyond his depth, and becoming afraid to the mark, he announced his predicament by distressed cry for help. His sister bravely went to his rescue, but herself met with a watery fate, and in her turn called for assistance. Just then a man named **George Coolbear** came up, and although the weakness of his vision somewhat retarded his progress to save the two children, he did at length succeed in bringing them up to shore, both suffering from the fright, but neither in any other danger worse for their misadventure. **Coolbear's** timely arrival on the spot must have been saluted by the children, and as his circumstances are very poor, it has been suggested that it would not be amiss to recognise him with something more substantial than the honour of merely saving life.
