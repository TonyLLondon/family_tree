## Notes

### Earliest snapshot of the Griffiths household

This is the first census on which Thomas and Anne Griffiths appear as a family. They are already in Aberdare, living at **Clive Place** in the Cwmdare hamlet — the same street they would remain on for at least twenty more years (recorded as "Clive Street" in 1871 and 1881).

### Thomas's birthplace: Llywel

The 1861 enumerator wrote **"Llywel"** — the parish name — rather than the village name "Trecastle" that Thomas gave on later censuses. This matches the Non-Conformist baptismal register (TNA RG 4/1725), which records his birth at Llywel.

### Margaret Griffiths — the lost daughter

**Margaret** (age 6, b. ~1855) is the couple's eldest recorded child. She does not appear on the 1871 or 1881 censuses — she was [buried at St Fagan's, Aberdare on 2 December 1863](../1863-burial-margaret-griffiths-st-fagans-aberdare/transcription.md), age 8. The later daughter "Margaret A" (b. ~1866) was named after her.

### Ann's birthplace

The enumerator left Ann's birth town blank (transcribed as "...") but recorded her birth county as **Carmarthenshire**. Later censuses give her birthplace as "Llan Ferbyn" (1871) and "Llanfair" (1881).

### Thomas Thomas — boarder from Llywel

**Thomas Thomas** (29, blocklayer, born Llywel, Brecknockshire) is a boarder — not a family member but almost certainly someone Thomas Griffiths knew from home. Both men were born in Llywel within a year or two of each other and migrated to the same Aberdare street. The surname "Thomas" is distinct from "Griffiths".

### Elizabeth — infant

**Elizabeth** (age 1, b. ~1860) is later known as **Eliza** on the 1881 census, where she appears as a tinworker aged 21.
