# 1851 Census of England & Wales — 3 Bishop Town, Llywell, Brecknockshire

**Census date:** 30 March 1851.

**Address:** 3, Bishop Town, Llywell, Brecknockshire, Wales. "Bishop Town" is the English rendering of **Tresgob** (Welsh: "Bishop's homestead"), the same hamlet where the family appeared on the 1841 census.

**Archive reference:** TNA HO 107/2489, folio 113, page 15, schedule 36. Registration district: Brecknock. Sub-district: Devynnock. Enumeration district: 2-4. City/ward: Traiangaer.

## Notes

### David — a Llywel man, still labouring at 75

David Griffiths was born in **Llywel** parish itself, confirming he was local to the upper Usk valley. At 75 he was still described as a **Labourer** — agricultural work in a rural parish. His age (b. ~1776) is consistent with the 1841 census where he was recorded as 60 (the 1841 census rounded ages down to the nearest 5 for adults).

### Margaret — born in Cayo, Carmarthenshire

The 1851 census reveals Margaret's birthplace for the first time: **Cayo, Carmarthenshire**. This is the parish of **Cynwyl Gaeo** (often abbreviated "Caio" or "Cayo"), in the northern Tywi valley, about 15 miles west of Llandovery and 20 miles from Llywel across the watershed. She married into the Griffiths family from across the county boundary.

### Thomas has left home

Their son [Thomas Griffiths](../../people/thomas-griffiths.md) (b. 1830) is no longer in the household at age ~21. He had likely already migrated to the Aberdare coalfield — by the 1861 census he was an ironworks labourer there.

### An elderly couple alone

David (75) and Margaret (65) are the entire household. No servants, no lodgers, no other family members. They are an elderly rural couple in the hamlet of Bishop Town / Tresgob.
