# Marriage register — Antonio Zerauschek (sr.) & Anna Herenda, 26 January 1878, Pago

Marriage entry **N. 8** in the parish register of **Pago (Pag)**, Zara circle, Habsburg Dalmatia, on **26 January 1878**. Banns read on **1, 6, and 13 January 1878**.

## Register fields

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Entry number** | N. 8 |
| **Marriage date** | 26 January 1878 |
| **Parish** | Pag (Pago), Zara circle, Dalmatia |
| **Banns** | 1, 6, 13 January 1878 |
| **Groom** | Antonio (Giacomo Vincenzo) Zerauschek — *falegname* (carpenter), Catholic, bachelor; born **15 March 1850, Zara** |
| **Groom’s father** | Giuseppe Biagio Zerauschek (deceased; recorded under the patronymic form *Biaggio Zerauschek*) |
| **Groom’s mother** | Antonia Maria Guerino (deceased) |
| **Bride** | Anna (Petronilla) Herenda — *negoziante* (tradeswoman), Catholic, single; born **14 February 1859, Pago** |
| **Bride’s father** | Giovanni Erenda Or Marzo — merchant (*negoziante*), then living |
| **Bride’s mother** | Giuseppina Magassich (Magarich) — then living |
| **Witness 1** | Andrea Armiani — *civile*, Pago |
| **Witness 2** | Andrea Beveroni / Devercovi — merchant, Novigno |

The bride’s parents were both still alive at the wedding day, despite the family memoir’s recollection that Anna was “orphaned at sixteen” (an emotional rather than literal reading); see [Anna Herenda](../../people/anna-herenda.md). The groom’s parents were both dead by 1878, consistent with his independent standing as a Zara cabinet-maker by his late twenties.
