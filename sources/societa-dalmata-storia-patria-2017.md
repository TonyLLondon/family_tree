## Summary

Published volume of the Società Dalmata di Storia Patria. Contains the **1909 protest list** — a petition signed by over 500 Italian-speaking Dalmatian officials and civic figures opposing the introduction of Croatian into the Habsburg provincial administration. The signatories are listed with their official titles, providing a contemporaneous snapshot of who held what post in Zara.

**Addobbati entries (1909 protest list):**

| Name | Title |
|------|-------|
| **Marino Addobbati** | *Cancellista giudiziario* (judicial clerk) |
| **Pietro Pio Addobbati** | *Ufficiale postale superiore* (superior postal officer) |
| **Vincenzo Addobbati** | *Ispettore del catasto* (cadastre inspector) |

Three family members simultaneously in imperial posts spanning judiciary, postal, and land-registry branches of the administration.

## Use in this research

Primary evidence for Pietro Pio's grade as **Ufficiale postale superiore**. Demonstrates the Addobbati family's distribution across the Habsburg provincial apparatus in the final decade before WWI. The three-branch spread (judiciary / postal / cadastre) confirms the pattern of diversified institutional loyalty visible across the family's history.
