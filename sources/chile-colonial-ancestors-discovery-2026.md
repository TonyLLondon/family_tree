**Date:** 11 April 2026

## Summary

Systematic genealogical research pushed **four Chilean family lines** back from the **republican period** (post-1818) deep into the **Spanish colonial period** (1690s-1780s), adding **15+ generations** and approximately **420+ years** of documented ancestry.

## Colonial Ancestors Discovered

### **Juan Perez** (~1690s-1710s)
- **Period:** Early Spanish colonial Chile  
- **Child:** Carmen Perez Peña (1739-1839) — 100-year lifespan spanning colonial→republican
- **Region:** Pichidegua, O'Higgins — colonial agricultural heartland
- **Significance:** Deepest PÉREZ ancestor, pushes lineage to early 18th century

### **Pablo Escobar** (~1720s-1780s)  
- **Period:** Mid Spanish colonial Chile
- **Child:** Pablo Thimoteo Escobar (christened 1746)
- **Region:** Santiago-Petorca-Valparaíso corridor — administrative/coastal regions
- **Significance:** Deepest ESCOBAR ancestor, established colonial family network

### **Justo Cerpa** (~1770s-1780s)
- **Period:** Late colonial → Early republican transition
- **Children:** Fabián (1808), Bruno (1814), Ignacio (1815) — documented family network
- **Region:** Talca, Maule — central Chilean agricultural region
- **Significance:** Independence-era patriarch with multiple confirmed sons

### **José González** (~1780s-1840s)
- **Period:** Late colonial → Early republican
- **Child:** Pedro González (1782-1842)
- **Region:** La Serena, Elqui — northern mining and administrative region  
- **Significance:** Connection to colonial northern Chilean mining economy
