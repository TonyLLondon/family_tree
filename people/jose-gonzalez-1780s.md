# José González

**Period:** Late colonial Chile → Early republican Chile  
**Active:** ~1780s-1840s  
**Region:** La Serena, Elqui region, northern Chile  
**Significance:** Deepest documented ancestor in GONZÁLEZ lineage

## Family

- **Spouse:** Juana Lorca
- **Child:**
  - **Pedro González** (1782-1842) — documented through burial record showing parents

## Historical Context

José González lived through the **Chilean independence period**, representing the **deepest documented generation** in our GONZÁLEZ lineage. His presence in the La Serena/Elqui region suggests connection to Chile's **colonial mining economy** in the north.

## Geographic Significance  

The **La Serena-Elqui** region was a major **colonial administrative and mining center**:
- Spanish colonial copper and gold mining operations
- Administrative center for northern Chilean territories  
- Migration hub between mining regions and central Chile

The GONZÁLEZ family's later movement from Elqui → Illapel → Santiago reflects typical **internal Chilean migration patterns** from mining regions to administrative centers.

## Evidence

Pedro González burial record (6TN1-TGYG, 1842):
- "Father: José González, Mother: Juana Lorca"
- Burial: La Merced, La Serena, Elqui region
- Consistent with northern Chilean colonial presence
