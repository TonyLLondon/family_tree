# Morgan Morgans

**Morgan Morgans** was baptized on **27 December 1776** at **Llywel** parish church, Breconshire. His patronymic name — Morgan son of Morgan — follows the Welsh naming convention still used in the Brecon Beacons in the late eighteenth century. His parents were **Morgan** and **Rachel**, recorded in the Llywel parish register.

Morgan Morgans and [Lettuce](lettuce-llywel.md) had at least two sons baptised at Llywel: [Thomas Morgan](thomas-morgan-nantyglo.md) on **15 February 1794** and [William Morgan](william-morgan-llanfairarybryn.md) on **15 September 1795**. Thomas migrated to Nantyglo, Monmouthshire, and became an iron worker; William stayed in the upper Usk valley and farmed at Llanfair-ar-y-bryn. Both lines descend independently into the tree — making this couple the point where two separate branches converge.

By the 1841 and 1851 censuses, William's "Mother" in the household was **Anne Morgan** (b. ~1767, Llanfairdydogan, Carmarthenshire), a widow — likely Morgan Morgans' second wife following Lettuce's death.

By 1816 he had married a woman named **Margaret** — a son Rees Morgans was baptized at Llywel on 7 January 1816 with parents "Morgan Morgans" and "Margaret". Several Morgan Morgans marriages appear in the Wales marriage indexes (Margaret Nicholas 1793, Margaret Morgans 1794, Elisbth Jenkins 1798) but without parish locations it is unclear which, if any, is the same man.

He presumably lived in the Llywel–Llanfairarybryn area of the upper Usk valley. A burial of "Morgan Morgans" on 26 April 1845 in Breconshire (born ~1779, three years off from the 1776 baptism) is a plausible candidate for his death.

## Family

- Wife (1st): [Lettuce](lettuce-llywel.md) (Lettice) — named as mother in both baptisms (1794 and 1795). Date of death unknown; she may have lived well beyond 1795.
- Wife (2nd): **Margaret** — known from the 1816 baptism of Rees Morgans at Llywel. Surname unconfirmed.
- **Anne Morgan** (b. ~1767, Llanfairdydogan, Carmarthenshire) — listed as William's "Mother" on the 1841 and 1851 censuses. Her relationship to the family is uncertain: she may be Morgan Morgans' second wife (a stepmother to William), or Lettice's mother (William's maternal grandmother) — the census term "Mother" was sometimes used loosely for elderly female relatives. No marriage record for Anne and Morgan Morgans has been found.
