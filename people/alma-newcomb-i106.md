# Alma Newcomb

**Alma Newcomb** was born in the **April–June quarter of 1855** at **Tolleshunt Knights**, Essex (GRO birth index, Maldon Vol. 4a p. 186). Her parents were [James Newcomb](james-newcomb-i184.md), a labourer, and [Susannah South](susannah-south-i185.md). In 1851 the young couple were living with James's parents, [William](william-newcomb-i186.md) and [Elizabeth Newcomb](elizabeth-newcomb-i187.md), at Tolleshunt Knights, already with three children — Charles, James Jr, and Sarah A. Alma was their fourth surviving child, born into a family that lost several infants through the 1850s.

By the **1861 census** both parents had vanished from the record. Five-year-old Alma was living with **John and Elizabeth Cousins** at **Tiptree Heath**, Great Wigborough, listed as their granddaughter. Her older brother James Jr (16) was a servant in an oyster-dredger's household at nearby Tollesbury. The paternal grandmother Elizabeth Newcomb was a widow, alone and destitute, working as a charwoman on King Street, Chelmsford. Whatever happened to James and Susannah between 1857 — when their last recorded child was born — and 1861, it shattered the family completely.

By sixteen Alma was a **servant** on Park Farm in Tolleshunt Knights, working for John Tanner, a farmer of 117 acres. She married [George Gridley Coolbear](george-gridley-coolbear-i105.md) in **1879** (GRO, Maldon Vol. 4a p. 403) and had at least eleven children. After George's death in 1892 she remarried **Reuben South** — almost certainly a relative of her own mother's family, since the Souths and Newcombs were neighbours in Tolleshunt Knights in 1851. She was living in Little Totham by 1901.

## The South connection

The Newcombs and Souths were neighbours in Tolleshunt Knights. The 1851 census puts them on consecutive enumeration pages: William Newcomb's household (including James and Susannah) at schedule 47, with James South's household and Reuben South's household just a few schedules away. Both South families' wives came from **Layer Marney** — adjacent to Layer Breton, where grandmother Elizabeth Newcomb was born. The Souths trace back to **Abraham South** (b. ~1786) at Layer de la Haye in 1841.

When Alma married **Reuben South** after George Coolbear's death, she married back into her mother Susannah's natal family. Whether Reuben was Susannah's brother, nephew, or cousin remains to be confirmed.

## Open questions

- **What happened to James and Susannah?** Both vanish after 1857 (last child born). No James Newcomb death in the Maldon GRO index 1851–1861. He may have died outside the district (at sea, in a distant institution), or FreeBMD coverage is incomplete. Susannah also disappears — no Susannah Newcomb or Susannah South death in Maldon. A wider death search across all districts, 1857–1861, is needed.
- **Cousins connection:** Alma is a "granddaughter" of the Cousins. John Cousins (57, Norfolk) and Elizabeth Cousins (82, Suffolk) don't match as Susannah's parents by age or geography. The Cousins connection may run through Susannah's mother's maiden name, or through a more complex family arrangement.
- **Reuben South identification:** Which Reuben South did Alma marry? The elder Reuben (b. ~1817, Tolleshunt Knights) or a younger one (b. ~1850, Great Totham)?
- **Siblings' fates in 1861:** Charles (~17), Sarah A (~12), Arthur (~10), Emily (~9) — none found in the 1861 census under Newcomb in Essex. They may have been placed with different families or their names were recorded differently.
- Birthplace: "Tolleshunt Knights" (1871, 1891) vs "Tiptree" (1901) — resolved: Tiptree Heath is the hamlet where she grew up with the Cousins, straddling the Tolleshunt Knights / Great Wigborough parish boundary.
