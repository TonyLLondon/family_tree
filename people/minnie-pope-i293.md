# Minnie Flora Pope (1865–?)

Minnie Flora Pope was born in **1865** at **Cranbrook, Kent** (GRO birth index: Q4 1865, Cranbrook, Vol 2A, p. 571). She was the daughter of **George Pope**, a master shoemaker, and **Sarah Ann Quaife**, a dressmaker — both lifelong Cranbrook residents.

On **8 August 1886** she married **David William Fensom** (I292), a plumber from Islington, at **Islington** (GRO marriage index: Q3 1886, Islington, Vol 1B, p. 355). She was registered as "Minnie Florence Pope" on the marriage index — a trivial variant of her birth name "Minnie Flora."

The couple settled in **Lower Holloway, Islington**, raising eleven children over the next two decades. Minnie appears consistently in census records as a wife and mother; she never reported an occupation after marriage.

## Open questions

- **Death date and place** — not yet identified.
- **1881 Census** — Minnie would be ~15. Not found under Pope in the 1881 census searches so far; she may be in service elsewhere or still in Cranbrook under a variant spelling.
- **How she met David William Fensom** — she grew up in rural Kent; he grew up in Islington. By 1886 they married in Islington. Her route from Cranbrook to London is unknown.
- **Parents' later life** — George Pope (b. ~1825) and Sarah Ann Woolgar (b. ~1828–29) in the 1881 and later censuses.
- **Paternal grandparents** — William Pope (b. ~1801, Kent) and Sarah Pope (b. ~1806, Kent) identified in the 1841 Census. A great-grandfather William Pope Sr (b. ~1766) also in the household.
- **"Woolgar" debunked** — Findmypast displayed "mother's maiden name: Woolgar" for Minnie's birth index entry, but the actual GRO birth index page (Q4 1865, p. 853) has no mother's maiden name column (pre-1911 format). "Woolgar" does not appear on the original page. This was a Findmypast data enrichment error. The GRO marriage index is definitive: George Pope married **Sarah Anne Quaife** (Q4 1847, Cranbrook, Vol 5, p. 163).
