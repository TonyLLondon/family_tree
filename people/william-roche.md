# William Roche

**Crown Solicitor for the County and City of Limerick** and senior partner of **William Roche & Sons**, Solicitors, **72 Harcourt Street, Dublin**. A son of the **[Roche of Limerick](../sources/roche-of-limerick-forgotten-victorians.md)** Catholic merchant-gentry family documented in Burke's 1833, William built one of Dublin's prominent Catholic legal practices and held his Crown appointment from 1861 until retirement. He married **[Eliza Knight](eliza-knight.md)**, co-heiress daughter of "— Knight, Esq." Their children married into the legal and medical professions across Ireland and the Empire: one daughter ([Catherine Mary](catherine-mary-roche.md)) married military surgeon [William O'Byrne White](william-obyrne-white.md); another married the Crown Solicitor for Galway; another married a County Court Judge; a son became Professor of Medical Jurisprudence at the Catholic University.

## Life

- **~1806** — Born **Limerick** (GEDCOM says "bef 1794" but obituary records death "at the ripe age of 88" in April 1894, placing birth ~1806).
- **1 Mar 1832** — Marriage at **St Andrew's, Westland Row, Dublin** to [Eliza Knight](eliza-knight.md).
- **By 1833** — Described in Burke's *History of Commoners* as "William, a solicitor in Dublin, married Eliza, another daughter and co-heir of — Knight, esq."
- **1838** — Appears in Dublin court records on behalf of a Limerick client, still connected to his home city.
- **1850** — Address: **Upper Pembroke Street, Dublin** (birth announcement of a son, *Tipperary Free Press*, 19 Jun 1850).
- **1861** — Appointed **Crown Solicitor for the County and City of Limerick** when the Munster Circuit role was divided after the death of **Sir Matthew Barrington, Bart.** (*Cork Examiner*, 19 Apr 1861; *Cork Daily Herald*, 20 Apr 1861). Described as "long and intimately connected with the city of Limerick, for which he is Clerk of the Crown."
- **By 1864** — Firm operating from **72 Harcourt Street, Dublin** (property sale notices).
- **By 1877** — Firm styled **"William Roche and Son"**, later **"William Roche and Sons"**, also at **4 Stephen's Green North, Dublin**.
- **14 Apr 1894** — Died **72 Harcourt Street, Dublin**, aged **88**. Obituary in *Limerick Chronicle* ("Death of Mr. William Roche, Ex-Crown Solicitor"): admitted as solicitor **Trinity term 1829**; **President of the Incorporated Law Society**; **Chairman of the Solicitors' Benevolent Association**; **Solicitor for the War Department in Ireland**. "During his very long professional life he won the highest esteem of his legal brethren."

The pattern is striking: Crown Solicitor father, Crown Solicitor son-in-law (O'Farrell), County Court Judge son-in-law (Brereton Barry), medical professor son (Antony), J.P. son-in-law (Roche-Kelly), military surgeon son-in-law (O'Byrne White). A Catholic professional dynasty at the heart of Victorian Ireland's legal establishment.
