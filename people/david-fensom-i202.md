# David Fensom

**David Fensom** was born about **1836** in **Luton, Hertfordshire** (he gave Harpenden on the 1901 census — both are in south Hertfordshire, about five miles apart). He was a son of **William Fensom** (I210) and **Ann Brown** (I211), a family with deep Hertfordshire roots.

He married **Georgiana Talbot Grindlay** (I203) on **18 June 1861** and the couple settled in Islington. Their children included [Georgina Elizabeth](georgina-fensom-i205.md) (~1862), **David William** (Oct 1864, the father of [Annie Fensom](annie-fensom-i291.md)), **Annie** (~1870, not in the working tree), **Ellen** (I207, ~1875), and **Fanny** (I209, ~1877).

The **1881 Census** found the family at **8 Milton Place, Lower Holloway** — David a **house decorator** at 46, with all five children still at home. His son David William, sixteen, was already working as a **plumber**. By **1891** David William had moved two doors along Eden Grove (no. 11) while David Sr and Georgiana would settle at **no. 13** by 1901, by then a **general labourer** at 65. Their daughter [Georgina](georgina-fensom-i205.md) was in an institution nearby in Upper Holloway that year.

## Family

Wife: Georgiana Talbot Grindlay (I203, b. 14 June 1835; d. 1921, Edmonton). Parents: William Fensom (I210) and Ann Brown (I211), married 14 June 1821. Children (from the working tree + census): Georgina Elizabeth (~1862), David William (Oct 1864), Annie (~1870), Ellen (~1875), Fanny (~1877). The same tree listing also names a William Fensom (no dates) not found in the 1881 household.

By **1891** his son David William was living two doors down at **11 Eden Grove** with wife Minnie and three small children — the extended family clustered on a single street in Lower Holloway for at least a decade.
