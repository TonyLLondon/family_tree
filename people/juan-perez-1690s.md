# Juan Perez

**Estimated birth:** ~1690s-1710s  
**Period:** Spanish colonial Chile  
**Active:** ~1710s-1740s  
**Region:** Pichidegua, O'Higgins region, central Chile  
**Significance:** Deepest discovered ancestor in PÉREZ lineage, pushing documentation to early 18th century

## Family

- **Spouse:** Maria Peña
- **Child:**
  - **Carmen Perez Peña** (1739-1839) — lived 100 years spanning colonial → republican periods

## Historical Significance

Juan Perez represents our **deepest documented PÉREZ ancestor**, active during the **early-to-mid Spanish colonial period**. His daughter Carmen's remarkable lifespan (1739-1839) witnessed the entire **Chilean independence period** and early republican development.

## Geographic Context

**Pichidegua, O'Higgins region** was part of central Chile's **colonial agricultural heartland**:
- Spanish colonial agricultural estates (encomiendas/haciendas)
- Indigenous-Spanish mestizo population development
- Central valley settlement patterns

## Research Discovery

Found through **systematic FamilySearch extension** of the PÉREZ lineage:
Simon Perez (1763) → Ignacio Perez (1806) → Valentin Perez (1827) → Manuel Perez (1864)

**Breakthrough record:** Carmen Perez Peña burial 1839 (6RWR-2BPS)
- "Parents: Juan Perez + Maria Peña"
- Birth: January 1739 (colonial period)
- Death: January 1839 (republican period)
- **100-year lifespan** spanning Chilean history

## Historical Context

Juan Perez lived during **Spanish colonial consolidation**:
- **1700s-1740s:** Bourbon colonial administration
- **Pre-independence Chile** under Spanish Captaincy General
- **Agricultural/mestizo settlement** of central valleys

His descendants continued in the same region through the colonial period, independence, and into the republican era, representing **multi-generational Chilean continuity**.
