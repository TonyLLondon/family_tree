# Jean François Montagnon

Jean François Montagnon was born around 1667 and died on **15 September 1732** at Aubonne in the Pays de Vaud. He was buried the same day. His burial record gives his birth year as 1666; his baptism record, indexed as "François Montagnon" on **22 December 1667**, gives his father as **[Jean Montagnon](jean-montagnon.md)** — the earliest known bearer of the surname in Switzerland.

He married **[Jeane Marechal](jeane-marechal.md)** (b. 1665, d. 11 Jul 1742, Aubonne), who survived him by ten years. Her burial record names her as "Jeane Marechal Montagnon," following the Vaudois convention of joining maiden and married surnames.

Jean François is the **probable but unconfirmed** father of three Montagnon men who all lived at Aubonne: **[Jean Pierre](jean-pierre-montagnon.md)** (b. 1692, d. 1737 — our direct ancestor), a second **Jean François** (b. 1694, d. 1778), and **Jean Michel** (b. 1699, d. 1772). No record explicitly connects them. The case is circumstantial: no other Montagnon patriarch is attested at Aubonne in this era, and the birth spacing is consistent with a single household. The Aubonne baptismal registers for the 1690s appear unindexed by FamilySearch; a [FamilySearch Center visit](../sources/wishlist/montagnon-aubonne-registers.md) could resolve this.

## Montagnon origins

The surname Montagnon is concentrated in the Isère (Dauphiné) and Ardèche (Vivarais) regions of southeastern France. A striking parallel record survives from Isère: "Pierre Montagnon, baptised 6 Oct 1669 at Parmilieu, Isère, father François Montagnon" ([6W4V-FW6Z](https://www.familysearch.org/ark:/61903/1:1:6W4V-FW6Z)) — a near-contemporary François Montagnon in Huguenot country. Jean Montagnon's arrival in Vaud likely dates to the mid-1600s, coinciding with Huguenot resettlements in the Bernese territories.
