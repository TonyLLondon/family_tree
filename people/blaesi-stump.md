# Blaesi Stump

**Blaesi Stump**—Latin registers often read **Blasius**—was born about **1565** at **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, and died **7 October 1629**. He married twice, first to [Madalena Bruewschwyler](madalena-bruewschwyler.md) and later to [Helena Bruewschwyler](helena-bruewschwyler.md), likely sisters. He was father of at least six children and represents our deepest confirmed ancestor in the STUMP line, extending the genealogy back to Generation 13 (approximately 475+ years).

## Research Notes

**Generational Extension:**
This research has extended the STUMP line by 2-3 generations beyond Blaesi, reaching back to approximately 1550 with his potential parents Conradt Stumpp (d. 1560) and Margreth Zällerin (d. 1550).

**Multiple Marriages:**
Evidence shows Blaesi married twice to sisters Helena and Madalena Bruewschwyler, common in Swiss families of this period. The chronology of children suggests Madalena was the first wife (children 1591-1594), followed by Helena (children 1596-1601).

**Naming Patterns:**
His grandson was named Blasius (1631) following the Swiss tradition of honoring the paternal grandfather.
