# Sarah O'Bryen

**Sarah O'Bryen** was the daughter and co-heiress of [John O'Bryen](john-obryen.md), **chief of the O'Bryens of Arran** and a **lineal descendant of Brian Boru**, High King of Ireland. She married [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md) as his second wife and was the mother of [Thomas Roche](thomas-roche.md), the Limerick banker.

Through Sarah, the Roche family inherited both the **O'Bryen estates** at **Moyvanine and Clounties** in County Limerick and — if Burke's 1833 is correct — a direct blood descent from **Brian Boru** (c. 941–1014) through the **Clan Tiege** (Mac Tiege O'Brien) of the **Aran Islands**. Sarah also brought large estates at **Rathkeale, Co. Limerick** and **Tuam, Co. Galway**.

Burke's 1833 describes her father's lineage with a precision unusual even for that source:

> Sarah, daughter and co-heiress of **John O'Bryen, esq. of Moyvanine and Clounties**, both in the county of Limerick, **chief of the O'Bryens, of Arran, lineal descendants of Brien Borroimhe**.

The "Brien Borroimhe" is Brian Boru — and this claim is independently corroborated by Vallancey's *Collectanea* (vol. I, pp. 568–569), who names her father directly as "the chief and direct representative" and records "I have in my possession an authentic copy" of the 1588 certificate; by Hardiman's *History of Galway* (1820); O'Brien's *Irish Dictionary*; and Ferrar's *History of Limerick*.

## Life

- **~1740s–1750s** — Born at **Moyvanine** or **Clounties**, County Limerick.
- **~1768** — Married [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md) as his second wife. Stephen's first wife, Margaret Meade, had died or the marriage had ended by this date.
- **8 Nov 1786** — Died (per Burke's 1833). Stephen married a third time, to Mary-Anne Ankettle, after Sarah's death.

## The Brian Boru Lineage

The O'Bryens of Arran — the **Clan Tiege** (Mac Tiege) — were descended from **Teige Ó Briain**, great-grandson of Brian Boru, from whom they took their name. They had been the temporal lords of the Aran Islands in Galway Bay "time out of man's memory," protecting Galway harbour against pirates in exchange for an annual tribute of wine from the Tribes of Galway.

A certificate dated **30 March 1588**, signed by the Mayor and Bailiffs of Galway and addressed to Queen Elizabeth, attests the lineage of **Murrough mac Toirdelbach Ó Briain** (fl. 1575–1588), chief of the Clan Tiege. Burke describes Murrough as "the sixth progenitor of the said John" — Sarah's father. The original document was held by her son **James Roche of Cork**.

After losing the Aran Islands to the O'Flahertys in the 1580s, the chiefs of Clan Tiege retired to County Limerick. By the time Sarah married Stephen Roche, her father John was at Moyvanine and Clounties — the last recorded chief. See [John O'Bryen](john-obryen.md) for the full descent.

## Family

- Husband: [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md) (1724–1804).
- Father: [John O'Bryen](john-obryen.md) — chief of the O'Bryens of Arran, lineal descendant of Brian Boru.
- Brother: **Thady O'Brien** — "an officer of known valour in the queen of Hungary's service" (Vallancey, p. 569), serving in the army of Maria Theresa of Austria. His existence helps explain the "co-heiress" designation — Sarah likely inherited because Thady died without issue or remained abroad.
- Children include [Thomas Roche](thomas-roche.md) (direct ancestor).
