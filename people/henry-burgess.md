# Henry Burgess

English **banker, economist, and editor** (Schwartz, NYPL Bulletin) — father of **Charles Henry Burgess**, [Edward Burgess](edward-burgess.md), **Joseph**, and **George** by [Frances Ridsdale](frances-ridsdale.md). Far from a passive patriarch, Henry actively financed his sons' Persian trading ventures, managed their English accounts, guaranteed bills of exchange, and maintained correspondence across Persia, Constantinople, and Philadelphia for over twenty-five years. His sons' letters portray a literate, commercially astute, warm-hearted man whose driving of a horse down from Hampstead was still remembered fondly in Tabriz.

## Life

- **1781** — Born (graph).
- **6 Dec 1804** — Married [Frances Ridsdale](frances-ridsdale.md) at **York, Yorkshire**.
- **1827–36** — Financed Charles's Persian trading enterprise through the firm of **Hanson** (London & Constantinople). Paid insurance on goods (£53.17.6), honoured bills of exchange drawn from Persia, arranged credit, and sent commercial intelligence (silk, opium, gum prices). Employed **Twigg** as clerk and bookkeeper; Twigg accompanied Charles to Persia in 1830.
- **1830** — Living at **Hempstead**. In his letter to Charles (18 Oct 1830) he described writing on the just-opened **Liverpool & Manchester Railway** — "a great curiosity … any distinguished Englishman would consider it such" — evidence of his interest in public events and technology. The same letter shows him arranging £200 credit through the Hansons and forwarding intelligence on Eastern trade conditions.
- **1830s–50s** — Maintained a stream of letters, books, and parcels to his sons in Persia. Edward frequently praised his father's "quickness in conversation" and talent for drawing information from people. Charles noted Henry's involvement in an **iron works project** (1840s). Henry also appears connected to Edinburgh intellectual circles ("Ebro" = Edinburgh in the 1830 letter).
- **Before Feb 1855** — Wife [Frances](frances-ridsdale.md) died. Edward wrote to brother George about "the property my Mother left" and appointed George as agent to collect from uncle George Ridsdale.
- **Feb 1855** — Received Edward's deeply emotional letter: *"How shall I describe to you the emotions this letter inspired in me, thankfulness to God for having given me so good and kind a father and gratitude to you were my predominant feelings."* Henry had promised to care for [Anna Saginian](anna-saginian.md) and infant [Fanny](fanny-burgess.md) should Edward die — a promise Edward described as *"most kind, affectionate and fatherly."*
- **1863** — Died (graph).
