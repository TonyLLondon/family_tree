# Henry Burgess

English **banker, economist, and editor** (Schwartz, NYPL Bulletin) — father of **Charles Henry Burgess**, [Edward Burgess](edward-burgess.md), **Joseph**, and **George** by [Frances Ridsdale](frances-ridsdale.md). Far from a passive patriarch, Henry actively financed his sons' Persian trading ventures, managed their English accounts, guaranteed bills of exchange, and maintained correspondence across Persia, Constantinople, and Philadelphia for over twenty-five years. His sons' letters portray a literate, commercially astute, warm-hearted man whose driving of a horse down from Hampstead was still remembered fondly in Tabriz.

Born into a **Quaker** family at Hinckley, Leicestershire, Henry was the son of [Joseph Burgess](joseph-burgess.md) and [Sarah Airey](sarah-airey.md), whose own Quaker marriage at Kendal in 1769 united a Leicester meeting family with one from Westmorland. By 1804 Henry was resident in Leicester when he obtained an Archbishop of York marriage licence to wed [Frances Ridsdale](frances-ridsdale.md) at St John's, Wakefield. He settled in the City of London, where he lived at **St Swithins Lane** — in the heart of the financial district, steps from the Bank of England — for at least the last two decades of his life.

## Life

- **7 Jul 1780** — Born at **Hinckley**, **Leicestershire** (Quaker Monthly Meeting register, TNA RG6/750; a Quarterly Meeting copy gives 7 Jul 1781, TNA RG6/990). Father: Joseph Burgess; mother: Sarah.
- **6 Dec 1804** — Married [Frances Ridsdale](frances-ridsdale.md) by **licence** at **St John's, Wakefield**, Yorkshire. Henry aged 21, resident in **Leicester**; Frances aged 20, resident in **this parish** (Wakefield). Witnesses: **Edward Ridsdale** and **Elisa Ridsdale**. Archbishop of York marriage licence, Borthwick Institute for Archives (FMP `GBPRS/OR/YORK/M/00098493/1`); Bishop's transcripts (FMP `GBPRS/YORKSHIRE/MAR/183534/1`); Wakefield & District FHS transcription (FMP `GBPRS/M/343018975/1`, archive ref WDP 45/1/10).
- **1827–36** — Financed Charles's Persian trading enterprise through the firm of **Hanson** (London & Constantinople). Paid insurance on goods (£53.17.6), honoured bills of exchange drawn from Persia, arranged credit, and sent commercial intelligence (silk, opium, gum prices). Employed **Twigg** as clerk and bookkeeper; Twigg accompanied Charles to Persia in 1830.
- **1830** — In his letter to Charles (18 Oct 1830) he described writing on the just-opened **Liverpool & Manchester Railway** — "a great curiosity … any distinguished Englishman would consider it such" — evidence of his interest in public events and technology. The same letter shows him arranging £200 credit through the Hansons and forwarding intelligence on Eastern trade conditions.
- **1830s–50s** — Maintained a stream of letters, books, and parcels to his sons in Persia. Edward frequently praised his father's "quickness in conversation" and talent for drawing information from people. Charles noted Henry's involvement in an **iron works project** (1840s). Henry also appears connected to Edinburgh intellectual circles ("Ebro" = Edinburgh in the 1830 letter).
- **1841** — Census: **St Swithin Lane**, City of London, age 60 (b.~1781). Household includes Mary and others (Stiles/Burgess). TNA HO107, piece 723 (FMP `GBC/1841/0007987793`).
- **Before Feb 1855** — Wife [Frances](frances-ridsdale.md) died. Edward wrote to brother George about "the property my Mother left" and appointed George as agent to collect from uncle George Ridsdale.
- **1851** — Census: **Swithins Lane**, St Swithin London Stone, City of London. **Widower**, age 69 (b.~1782), born **Leicestershire**, occupation **"Trader On Dealer"**. TNA HO107, piece 1530 (FMP `GBC/1851/0004139829`).
- **Feb 1855** — Received Edward's deeply emotional letter: *"How shall I describe to you the emotions this letter inspired in me, thankfulness to God for having given me so good and kind a father and gratitude to you were my predominant feelings."* Henry had promised to care for [Anna Saginian](anna-saginian.md) and infant [Fanny](fanny-burgess.md) should Edward die — a promise Edward described as *"most kind, affectionate and fatherly."*
- **1861** — Census: **29 St Swithins Lane**, St Swithin, City of London. Head, **Widower**, age 80 (b.~1781), birthplace transcribed as **"Winster"**. Living with the Jeffery family (Alexander, porter; Elizabeth, housekeeper; Charlotte, servant). TNA RG09, piece 225 (FMP `GBC/1861/0001423441`, transcribed as "Bargess").
- **Q2 1863** — Died in the **London City** registration district. GRO death index vol 1C, p.68 (FMP `BMD/D/1863/2/AB/000394/004`).

## Editorial and banking career

Henry founded and edited **"Circular to Bankers"** (1828-~1850), a major financial publication headquartered at 81 Lombard Street, cost six guineas for subscription. As **Secretary of the Association of British Provincial Bankers**, he testified before Parliament's 1832 committee reviewing the Bank of England's charter. **Confirmed by newspaper death notices**: *Sun* (9 Jun 1863): *"Henry Burgess, for upwards of 20 years proprietor and [of the] Circular to Bankers..."* He died **6 June 1863, aged 82** at St. Swithin's Lane, after supporting Charles Henry financially for nearly two decades during his European exile.

**Financial burden of Charles Henry's betrayal:** Henry borrowed heavily to support Charles from 1836-1854, financing his health-seeking travels across European resorts after Charles abandoned Edward as a hostage to Persian creditors. This prolonged financial strain likely affected both Henry's business and family resources during the peak years of his banking publication.

## Open questions

- **Birth year:** The two Quaker register copies give 7 Jul **1780** (Monthly Meeting, Hinckley) and 7 Jul **1781** (Quarterly Meeting, Leicester). Census returns cluster around 1780–82. The original images would resolve which is the contemporaneous entry.
- **1861 birthplace "Winster":** Census transcription reads "Winster" — no such place in Leicestershire. Possibly a misread of the enumerator's hand, or refers to Winster in Westmorland (near his mother's Kendal Quaker roots) or Derbyshire. The 1851 census gives Leicestershire and the Quaker registers give Hinckley/Leicester.
- **Total financial cost** of supporting Charles Henry 1836-1854 — this may be quantifiable through Henry's business records or estate documents.
- His residence history before the 1841 census — the 1830 letter mentions writing from what may be Hampstead; earlier addresses in Yorkshire / Lancashire implied by Edward's references to Leeds "packing days" and manufacturing towns.
