# William Morgan

**William Morgan** was baptized on **15 September 1795** at **Llywel** parish church, Breconshire, son of [Morgan Morgans](morgan-morgans-llywel.md) and [Lettuce](lettuce-llywel.md) (Lettice). He became a substantial farmer at **Coedefaur** (also spelled Coedevan) in the neighbouring parish of **Llanfair-ar-y-bryn**, near Llandovery, where by 1851 he farmed **90 acres of arable land and 173 acres of mountain grazing**.

On **24 October 1831** he married [Sarah Watkins](sarah-morgan.md) at Llanfairarybryn parish church. Sarah was from the Builth/Llanddaysaint area on the Brecknockshire–Carmarthenshire border. Their eldest child [Anne](anne-morgan.md) (b. ~1829–1830) was born shortly before the marriage — a common situation in rural Wales. Sons David (~1832) and William Jr (~1835, later a druggist's apprentice) followed.

The 1841 and 1851 censuses show an elderly widow named **Anne Morgan** (b. ~1767, Llanfairdydogan, Carmarthenshire) living in William's household, listed as his "Mother." She is not the same person as **Lettuce/Lettice**, named as William's mother in the 1795 baptism. Anne's actual relationship to the family is uncertain: she may have been Morgan Morgans' second wife (William's stepmother), or she may have been Lettice's own mother (William's maternal grandmother) — the census term "Mother" was sometimes used for any senior female relative. There is no evidence that Lettice died young; she was alive at least through 1795 and may have lived for decades afterwards.

William died between the 1851 census and 1881, when Sarah was living as a widow with the Griffiths family in Aberdare.
