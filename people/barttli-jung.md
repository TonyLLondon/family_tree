# Barttli Jung (early 1600s)

**Barttli Jung** was a Swiss man from **Sulgen**, **Thurgau** in the early 17th century. He married [Barbel Schmidhusser](barbel-schmidhusser.md) and became the father of [Hans Jung](hanss-jung.md) (baptized 1631), establishing the Jung lineage in **Generation 12** that would span over 200 years in the same Swiss parish.

## Life

**c. 1600** — Born in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland** (estimated from son's birth date).

**c. 1625** — Marriage to [Barbel Schmidhusser](barbel-schmidhusser.md).

**19 May 1631** — Birth of son [Hans Jung](hanss-jung.md) (recorded as "Johanns Jung") in Sulgen.

**c. 1650** — Death (estimated, as son Hans appears as head of household in later records).

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Generation 12 Significance:**
Barttli Jung represents the deepest documented Jung ancestor, establishing the family presence in Sulgen back to approximately 1600. This places the Jung family settlement during the late Renaissance/early Reformation period in Switzerland.

**Name Etymology:**
"Barttli" is a diminutive form of Bartholomew (German: Bartholomäus), common in Swiss German naming traditions of the early 17th century. The form indicates likely informal community usage rather than formal church records.

**Early 17th Century Context:**
Living during the early 1600s, Barttli Jung would have experienced the religious and social changes following the Swiss Reformation, as well as the economic impacts of the Thirty Years' War period affecting neighboring regions.

**Genealogical Foundation:**
Barttli Jung serves as the foundational Generation 12 ancestor for a lineage that remained in Sulgen for over 200 years, eventually connecting to the STUMP family through his great-great-granddaughter Anna Margaretha Jung's marriage.
