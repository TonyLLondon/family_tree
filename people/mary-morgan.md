# Mary Morgan (c. 1854–aft. 1911)

Mary Morgan was born about **1854** at **Dowlais**, daughter of **Keziah Morgan** (b. 7 Mar 1824, Nantyglo, Aberystruth, Monmouthshire; collier) and **Jane** (b. ~1828, Dowlais). Keziah was [baptised](../media/docs/1824-baptism-keziah-morgan-aberystruth-monmouthshire.jpg) **4 July 1824** at **Aberystruth** parish — his father was **Thomas Morgan** (miner, Nantyglo) and his mother was **Charlotte Davies**. Thomas and Charlotte [married](../sources/corpus/1823-marriage-thomas-morgan-charlotte-davies-aberystruth/transcription.md) on **5 July 1823** at Aberystruth — Keziah was born exactly eight months later. The family lived at **Nantyglo**, a hamlet in the western Monmouthshire ironworks valleys near Blaina. Keziah moved to the Merthyr Tydfil area by the late 1840s and married Jane.

Mary grew up on **Pond Street** in Dowlais — the [1871 census](../media/docs/1871-census-keziah-morgan-household-dowlais-rg10-5397.jpg) records her at 17, one of six children, on RG 10/5397 page 200. The [Thomas Cushing household](../media/docs/1871-census-thomas-cushing-household-dowlais-rg10-5397.jpg), with 15-year-old David, is on page 201 — the families were neighbours. David married the girl from next door.

Mary married [David Cushen](david-cushen.md) in **Q1 1876** at Merthyr Tydfil (GRO: "David Cushin", Vol 11A p. 514; MarriageFinder confirms bride was Mary Morgan). By the [1881 census](../media/docs/1881-census-david-cushin-household-pond-street-caeharris-rg11-5314.png) they were at **31 Pond Street**, Caeharris — still on the same street as both families — with four young children. The family expanded to nine children by 1891, including [Elizabeth "Lily"](elizabeth-lilian-cushen.md) (b. 1886) and twins Harriet and Ellen.

David died in **Q3 1907**. By the [1911 census](../media/docs/1911-census-mary-cushen-widow-pond-street-dowlais-rg14-32453.jpg), Mary was a **widow** at **25 Pond Street**, heading the household at 58. She signed the 1911 census schedule herself: "Mary Cushen, 25 Pond St Dowlais." She stated she had been married 15 years [sic — likely meant "over 15"], with 9 children born alive and 6 still living.

## Open questions

- **Jane (Morgan) maiden name** — born ~1828, Dowlais. No maiden name found yet.
- **Keziah Morgan after 1871** — not found on 1881 or 1891 census in Merthyr Tydfil. A Keziah Morgan (b. ~1824) was buried at **Llangeinor** (Ogmore Valley) and a Kezia Morgan (b. ~1823) died Q4 1905 at Bridgend — same registration district. Did Keziah leave Dowlais for the Ogmore coal valleys?
- **Thomas and Charlotte (Davies) Morgan** — Keziah's parents. Thomas was a miner at Nantyglo. He married Charlotte Davies on **5 Jul 1823** at Aberystruth parish (Monmouthshire Marriages, p. 87). No Charlotte Davies baptism found at Aberystruth or Nantyglo — she may have been born in a neighbouring parish. An older Kezia Morgan (b. ~1802) was also baptised at Aberystruth with father "Thomas" — possibly Keziah's sibling or half-sibling from an earlier marriage, suggesting Thomas was in Nantyglo for at least 22 years.
- **Keziah's 1841 and 1851 census** — not yet located under his own name (the 1841 Bedwellty hit was a different, female Keziah). He was ~17 in 1841, likely working as a miner.
