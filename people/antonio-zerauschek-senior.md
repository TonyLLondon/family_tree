# Antonio Zerauschek (senior)

This is **`@I25@`**, the **Antonio who fathers the interwar hotel Antonio** [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md) (`@I14@`). He is **not** a duplicate record of the same man: in the export he is the **1850-born Zara native** who marries [Anna Herenda](anna-herenda.md) in **1878**, producing the junior Antonio who later marries [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md) and becomes Fulvia's father.

Keeping the two Antonios straight is the single most important editorial rule for the **Zara** branch: **senior = F13 husband of Anna Herenda**; **junior = F5 husband of Ester, father of Fulvia**.

## Life

- **15 March 1850** — Born **Zara, Dalmatia** (modern Zadar, Croatia).
- **24 August 1850** — His parents [Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md) and [Antonia Maria Guerino](antonia-maria-guerino.md) married in Zara (**F58**). Antonio's birth **precedes** that wedding by about **five months** — a common **premarital birth** pattern; confirm with **baptism** wording (legitimation clause, father named, etc.).
- **26 January 1878** — Marriage **Zara** to [Anna Herenda](anna-herenda.md) (**F13**).

Death date was not present in the INDI block inspected. Fulvia's 1996 memoir indicates he died when his youngest son Riccardo (Ginco) was only **three years old**, placing his death roughly in the 1890s-early 1900s. His son [Antonio](antonio-zerauschek.md) described him curtly as *"un falegname, ecco che cos'era"* — a carpenter. Fulvia heard from Zio Tanio that Antonio senior's father ([Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md)) had bought him a **furniture factory**. ([Fulvia memoir, 1996](../sources/famhist-nonna-memoir-1996.md).)
