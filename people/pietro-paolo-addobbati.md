# Pietro Paolo Addobbati

Pietro Paolo Addobbati spent his life in Zara (Zadar), moving through the layers of a Habsburg Dalmatian career—accountant, reviewer of the lieutenant-governor’s books, a man whose witnesses at his wedding were fellow officers of the Ragioneria. He was born in April 1815 and died in late December 1888, dates that line up with civil and genealogical scrapes, though one printed guide gives his death two years earlier; the gap is small enough to feel like editorial noise rather than a second life.

On 26 October 1845 he married Gerarda Rovero Brizzi in the Collegiate Church of Saint Simeon. He was thirty, an impiegato, a bachelor of that parish; she was twenty and still under the Austrian majority, so the praetor’s consent arrived hot on the heels of her birthday. The banns had been read without incident in high summer. Two accounts officers stood as witnesses—men whose titles sit comfortably beside the portrait Sabalich later drew of Pietro Paolo as *revidente contabile luogotenenziale* and as a lieutenant of the National Guard in 1848.

His father had been dead since 1839, yet the marriage record still named him “C. R. Impiegato,” imperial-royal service echoing down from a household that had already seen Nin nobility and Coltelli kin. Pietro Paolo’s own sons would include Pietro Pio and Simeone Gilberto, one bound toward medicine and the other toward a k.k. officer’s braid and a general’s retirement.

The Addobbati marriage alliances Sabalich catalogues—Lanfranchi, Plavinò, Sandri, Coltelli, Rovaro-Brizzi, and through Plavinò the tall Italian cousinage of Canova, Pasini, Marchio—read like a map of who could vouch for whom in a small Adriatic capital.

## Life

- **27 Apr 1815** — Born Zara, Dalmatia (primary register; Sabalich gives “(1815)” for the year).
- **26 Oct 1845** — Marriage at the Parrocchia di S. Simeone to Gerarda Rovero Brizzi; officiant D. Dom. Morovich (administrator). Banns 13, 20, and 27 July 1845. Witnesses Giuseppe Tenni Cupilli and Giuseppe Sebasticho, both *ufficiale dei conti della Ragioneria*.
- **1848** — *Tenente della guardia nazionale* (national guard lieutenant), per Sabalich.
- **Mid-century** — *Computista* at the Dalmatian accounts office; Sabalich styles him *Revidente Contabile Luogotenenziale*.
- **Death** — 29 Dec 1888 in Zara in the structured tree; Sabalich (p. 508) prints “† 1886.” Treat both as competing secondary captures until a single parish or civil image decides.

## Marriage alliances

Sabalich (p. 508) records that the Addobbati family, after arriving in Zara, intermarried with: **Lanfranchi, Plavinò, Sandri, Coltelli, Rovaro-Brizzi**; and through the Plavinò (authenticated genealogical trees, 1803, notary Giov. Grisogono de Franceschi): **Canova, Pasini, Marchio, Marchi** (test. 1589), **Carboncich, Ventura**.
