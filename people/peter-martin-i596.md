# Peter Martin

**Peter Martin** was a **chimney sweep** born about **1838** in **Bexley, Kent**. In 1861 he was a 23-year-old **travelling sweeper** living in sheds near Orpington with his first wife **Catherine** (also from Bexley) and infant son James. By 1871 he had settled at Frog Lane, Orpington with six children and an employee. By 1881 he was a **master chimney sweep**. Catherine died between 1871 and ~1877; Peter then lived with **Clara Webb** of Chelsfield, who bore him six more children. His youngest daughter **[Rosina](rosina-a-martin-i104.md)** married [Archibald Percy Coolbear](archibald-percy-coolbear.md), also a chimney sweep — almost certainly a trade connection through the Martin family.

Peter ran a sweep business at **Frog Lane, Orpington** (later called Alpine Cottage / 1 Alfrons Cottages) from at least 1871, when he already employed a journeyman sweep (William Deanson). By 1881 he had risen to **master chimney sweep** employing his sons.

## Family

- First wife: **Catherine Hosten** (b. ~1838, **Bexley**, Kent). Married Q1 1857, Lewisham registration district (Vol 1D, p. 703). Same age and parish as Peter — they grew up together. Died between 1871 and ~1877. Children: James (~1859, Bletchingley, Surrey), Samuel (~1862, Kent), Catherine (~1866, Orpington), Emma (~1868, Orpington), Peter Jr (~1870, Orpington), William (~1871, Orpington — died young).
- Second partner/wife: [Clara Webb](clara-webb-i597.md) (b. ~1854, Chelsfield, Kent). Listed as unmarried housekeeper in 1881; by 1891 listed as wife. No marriage record found. Children: Arthur (~1877), William (~1879), Albert (~1881), Harry (~1884), Edward (~1888), [Rosina](rosina-a-martin-i104.md) (1891).

## Open questions

- **Death:** Missing from 1901 census. Likely died between 1891 and 1901. Death index search in Bromley/Dartford registration district would confirm.
- **Catherine Hosten's fate:** Disappeared from the household between 1871 and ~1877. May have died (no death found yet) or separated from Peter — a Catherine Hosten remarried in Q4 1883 at Sculcoates (Hull), but unconfirmed as the same woman. Peter is listed as "Married" (not widower) in 1881, and Clara as "Unmarried" — consistent with Catherine being alive but absent. No divorce record found.
- **James Martin:** Eldest son (b. ~1859, Surrey). Not in 1881 or later censuses — left home or died young.
- **Birthplace resolved:** **Bexley** (1861, closest to birth) is the primary answer. Woolwich (1871, 1891) is the adjacent parish — easy to interchange. Sevenoaks (1881) was wrong.
