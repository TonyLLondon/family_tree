# D. Daniele Addobbati

Father of [Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati](luigi-anzolo-alloisio-addobbati.md) in **F16** with [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md). **"D."** = **Don** (honorific) in Dalmatian Catholic registers. Progenitor of the Zara Addobbati branch — one of three brothers who crossed the Adriatic from the Venetian Terraferma in 1733.

## Life

- **1702** — Born (GEDCOM year only; likely Bergamo or northern Italy based on family chain).
- **13 Nov 1733** — Admitted to the Zara council of citizens (*cittadini originari*) alongside brothers **Lorenzo** (1705) and **Giuseppe** (1709), sons of Giovanni (Ivan) Addobbati. They arrived as Venetian cavalry officers carrying the 1745 Bergamo testimonial documenting six generations of *cives originarios* lineage from Pietro de Adobatis (1495).
- Settled in Zara; married **Margherita Lanfranchi** (**F16**).
- **Five sons** received the *attestato di Cittadinanza di Zara* (Sabalich p. 503). The GEDCOM records only one son (Luigi), but civic records show the family branching widely in this generation.

## Family

- Wife: [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md) (**F16**).
- Father: [D. Ioanes Addobbati](d-ioanes-addobbati.md) (**F17**).
- Children: [Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati](luigi-anzolo-alloisio-addobbati.md) (I30) per **F16**; plus at least four others (not in GEDCOM) who received Zara citizenship — among them are likely **Lorenzo** (Conservatore degli ordini 1742), **Daniele** (Procuratore della Casa di Pietà 1762), and **Giuseppe** (Fonticaro 1781), based on civic appointment dates in Sabalich pp. 503–504.
- Brothers (not in GEDCOM): **Lorenzo Addobbati** (1705) and **Giuseppe Addobbati** (1709) — co-admitted to Zara *cittadini* 1733.

### Brother Lorenzo's line

Lorenzo (1705) received a Grimani land investiture at **Nadin** in the contado of Zara (1756); served as *Deputato foraneo di Sanità* during the plague at **Dobropolci** (1770). His son **Giuseppe** was *Capitano delle Craine* (border militia captain, 1770). The Dominican friar **Padre Daniele** (doctorate in Theology 1789, became *Priore*; portrait at the Biblioteca Paravia) was also from this branch (Sabalich p. 504).
