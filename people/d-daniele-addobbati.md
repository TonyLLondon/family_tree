# D. Daniele Addobbati

Father of [Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati](luigi-anzolo-alloisio-addobbati.md) in **F16** with [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md). **"D."** = **Don** (honorific) in Dalmatian Catholic registers. Progenitor of the Zara Addobbati branch — one of three brothers who crossed the Adriatic from the Venetian Terraferma, present in the Zara contado by 1728 (Turanj registers) and in Zara itself by 1730 (his marriage); formally admitted as *cittadini* 1733. His 1749 remarriage to **Laura Tori**, daughter of a Sienese nobleman, brought the first connection to Italian minor nobility into the Addobbati family — previously *cittadini* of artisan-officer origins from Bergamo.

## Life

- **1702** — Born (GEDCOM year only; likely Bergamo or northern Italy based on family chain).
- **By 1728** — An Addobbati from Bergamo appears in the **Turanj** parish registers (per DAZD inventory), predating the formal 1733 *cittadini* admission. This may be Daniele or one of his brothers already present in the Zara contado.
- **22 Nov 1730** — Marriage **Zara** to [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md) (**F16**). Recorded in Latin: Canon **Petrus Bado**, acting under licence from Archbishop **Vincenzo Zmajević**, married "D. Danielem Adobati et D. Margaritam filiam quondam Francisci Lanfranchi" — Margherita was the daughter of the **late** Francesco Lanfranchi (alive at her 1716 baptism, dead by 1730). Banns were dispensed ("Omissis Publicationibus") by archiepiscopal faculty. Witnesses: **Francesco Armanini** and **Pietro Pilarino** (the Pilarino/Plarinò family reappears as witnesses at Luigi's 1766 marriage — a multi-generational connection). The register confirms the Addobbati presence in Zara three years before the formal *cittadini* admission.
- **1731** — Two Addobbati births recorded in Zara (per DAZD inventory), further confirming the family's settled presence before formal admission.
- **13 Nov 1733** — Formally admitted to the Zara council of citizens (*cittadini originari*) alongside brothers **Lorenzo** (1705) and **Giuseppe** (1709), sons of Giovanni (Ivan) Addobbati. They arrived as Venetian cavalry officers; the 1745 Bergamo testimonial later documented six generations of *cives originarios* lineage from Pietro de Adobatis (1495).
- **Early 1749** — First wife [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md) died (see death register image).
- **12 Mar 1749** — Second marriage **Zara** to **Laura Tori**, daughter of the late **Salustio Tori**, *Nobile Senese* (Sienese nobleman). The **Tori** are a confirmed Sienese noble house with registered arms (*vaiato d'argento e di rosso* — vairy argent and gules; Archivio di Stato di Firenze; BNV ms. 314, p. 106). Officiated by **Don Pietro Garo**, Primicerio (chief dignitary of the Cathedral chapter), with dispensation from all three banns by the Archbishop. Daniele is described as **"Cittadino di Zara"**. Witnesses: **Giam Battista Lorenzo Adobatti** (a relative) and **Girolamo Lantana**. Recorded by **Don Giovanni Frangipani**, delegate and Curate of the Metropolitan Church. The remarriage came barely weeks after Margherita's death; the Tori connection brought Tuscan minor-noble blood into the Addobbati line. The characteristically Sienese given name "Sallustio" (from the Roman historian Sallust) entered the Addobbati family through this marriage: Daniele and Laura's son born 1751 was christened **Simone Sallustio**, and the name persisted for three generations.
- **Five sons** received the *attestato di Cittadinanza di Zara* (Sabalich p. 503). The GEDCOM records only one son (Luigi), but civic records show the family branching widely in this generation. Luigi (b. 1744) was from the first marriage; some of the other four sons may be from the second marriage with Laura Tori.

## Family

- Wife (1st): [Margherita Lanfranchi](margherita-lanfranchi.md) (**F16**) — full baptismal name **Regina Margaritta**, b. 26 Dec 1715 Zara, daughter of **Francesco Lanfranchi** and **Margaritta Doriceo**; d. 1749 Zara. Married **22 Nov 1730**; she was 14. Emergency-baptized at home 2 Jan 1716 by Don Giorgio Bonicelli (Curate of the Duomo) due to imminent danger at birth.
- Wife (2nd): **Laura Tori**, daughter of the late **Salustio Tori**, *Nobile Senese* (Sienese nobleman; Tori arms: vairy argent and gules, per ASFI/BNV). Married **12 Mar 1749**, Zara — weeks after Margherita's death. Their son **Simone Sallustio** (b. 1751) was named for Laura's father; other children from this marriage are not yet individually identified, though some of the "five sons" cited by Sabalich may belong here.
- Father: [D. Ioanes Addobbati](d-ioanes-addobbati.md) (**F17**).
- Children: [Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati](luigi-anzolo-alloisio-addobbati.md) (I30) per **F16**; plus at least four others (not in GEDCOM) who received Zara citizenship — among them are likely **Lorenzo** (Conservatore degli ordini 1742), **Daniele** (Procuratore della Casa di Pietà 1762), and **Giuseppe** (Fonticaro 1781), based on civic appointment dates in Sabalich pp. 503–504.
- Brothers (not in GEDCOM): **Lorenzo Addobbati** (1705) and **Giuseppe Addobbati** (1709) — co-admitted to Zara *cittadini* 1733.

### Brother Lorenzo's line

Lorenzo (1705) married **Anzola Plavino** — their son **Zuanne** was born in Zara in 1731 (per DAZD inventory §2.2), confirming the family was settled before the 1733 admission. Lorenzo received a Grimani land investiture at **Nadin** in the contado of Zara (1756); served as *Deputato foraneo di Sanità* during the plague at **Dobropolci** (1770). His son **Giuseppe** was *Capitano delle Craine* (border militia captain, 1770). The notary **Angelo Addobbati q.m Lorenzo** (DAZD sig. 56, 1806–1817) was from this branch. The Dominican friar **Padre Daniele** (doctorate in Theology 1789, became *Priore*; portrait at the Biblioteca Paravia) was also from this branch (Sabalich p. 504).
