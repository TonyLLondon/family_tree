# George Gridley Coolbear

**George Gridley Coolbear**—earlier registers spell the cluster **Coalbear** / **Colbear**—is the **Essex** grandfather who connects **Hazeleigh** to **[Archibald](archibald-percy-coolbear.md)**’s **Great War** dossier and, downstream, **[May](may-coolbear-i100.md)** and the **Evans** line. Born **1844** in **Hazeleigh**, he married [Alma Newcomb](alma-newcomb-i106.md) in **1879** and died **1892**, still on the cusp of seeing his son grow to enlist.

His parents were **George Coolbear** (earlier spelled "Coalbear"), a **journeyman carpenter** from **Hazeleigh**, and **Ann Gridley** from **Tillingham** on the Dengie Peninsula. The 1851 Census captures the family at **Butt Lane, Maldon** with George Gridley (age 6), his brother **Samuel Olonzo**, and infant sister **Elizabeth Ann**.

## Life

- **1844** — Born **Hazeleigh, Essex**. His father George was a journeyman carpenter.
- **1851** — Age 6, living with parents and siblings at Butt Lane, St Peter, Maldon. Brother **Samuel Olonzo** (4) and infant sister **Elizabeth Ann** also in the household.
- **By 1881** — Living at Bambridge Road, All Saints, Maldon. Working as a **bill poster and hawker** — an itinerant tradesman pasting advertisements and selling goods. Alma assists in the trade.
- **1892** — Died (year in tree); place and register image still open. Son Archibald Percy born the same year (Mar 1892).

## Family

They met as teenagers in the **Hamlet of Bicknacre, Danbury** — the 1841 census has George (15, apprentice carpenter) and Ann (15, farm servant) on consecutive pages of the same enumeration book.

**Note on marriage and children:** The GEDCOM records the marriage as 1879. In the **1871 Census** George is **unmarried and childless**, ruling out a prior marriage. Yet Ellen Alice (b. ~1868) and Charles (b. ~1871) appear as his children in 1881. Either: (a) Alma's age in 1881 is understated and she is older than 27, making her old enough to have had children from a prior relationship; (b) the children's ages are overstated in 1881; or (c) there is an unknown first wife/partner not reflected in the census.

## Open questions

- Death registration and burial for **1892**.
- **Ellen Alice and Charles:** The 1871 Census rules out a prior marriage for George. These older children remain unexplained — most likely Alma's from a prior relationship, or ages overstated in 1881.
- **1861 Census:** George Gridley would be ~17. Not yet found — may be working away from the family, just as his father was at the same age (apprenticed in Bicknacre in 1841).
- **Siblings' fates:** Samuel Olonzo Coolbear (~1847) and Elizabeth Ann Coolbear (~1851) — no records traced yet.
- Alma's parents: [James Newcomb](james-newcomb-i184.md) (b. ~1823, labourer) and [Susannah South](susannah-south-i185.md) (b. ~1824). Both vanished from the record between 1857 and 1861. See [Alma's page](alma-newcomb-i106.md) for the full Newcomb–South–Cousins reconstruction.
