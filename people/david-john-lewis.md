# David John Lewis

Welsh-born soldier, colonial administrator, and public-service leader. After a distinguished war in Italy — where he earned the **U.S. Silver Star** for heroism at the Po crossing — he returned to **Sirmione on Lake Garda**, where the 1st Welch were stationed after VE Day, and married **[Fulvia Zerauschek](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md)**. Together they raised five children across two decades in **HM Overseas Civil Service**: first in **Tripolitania (Libya)**, then across **Northern Rhodesia** as District Commissioner (Fort Jameson, Chinsali, Livingstone, Ndola), where he managed the **1960 Congo refugee crisis** from the Copperbelt, and rose through Deputy Provincial Commissioner to **Acting Permanent Secretary** in the Zambian government. He corresponded personally with **President Kenneth Kaunda**. Returned to Britain in 1969 as **Director-General of the Keep Britain Tidy Group**, receiving correspondence from **Buckingham Palace** and leading international anti-litter conferences. David died on 22 August 2000; Fulvia followed four months later on 20 December. They are buried together at **Sirmione** — the place where the war ended and their life together began.

Asked by his son Ivor what he had done to earn the Silver Star, David replied: ***"The same as everyone else; except someone saw me."***

## Education

- **1931–1937** — **Aberdare Grammar School** (school student treasurer; pianist).
- **1937–1939** — **University of Bristol** (interrupted by war).
- **1947–1948** — **University of Bristol** (resumed) — **BA Honours, Modern History** (2nd Class, 1st Division). Post-Graduate Certificate in Education. Boxing team; rugby second team.

## Life

- **2 Oct 1918** — Born **Merthyr Tydfil, Glamorgan, Wales**.
- **1939–1947** — Military service (see below). Fought in North Africa, Sicily, and Italy. Awarded U.S. Silver Star and Mentioned in Dispatches.
- **Late 1945** — 1st Battalion, The Welch Regiment stationed at **Sirmione, Lake Garda**, after the German surrender. Met **[Fulvia Ottilia Antonia Zerauschek](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md)** at Sirmione. Returned after demobilisation to marry her.
- **1948–1969** — HM Overseas Civil Service: Libya, Northern Rhodesia, Zambia. Five children raised in Africa; Ivor wrote that they were *"effectively brought up there."*
- **1969–c. 1987** — Director-General, Keep Britain Tidy Group, Brighton.
- **22 Aug 2000** — Died **Brighton and Hove, Sussex, England**, after a brief illness.
- **20 Dec 2000** — **[Fulvia](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md) passed away peacefully** — four months after David.
- **Burial** — **Sirmione, Province of Brescia, Lombardy, Italy** — the place where they first met.

## Military service (1939–1947)

- **1939** — Voluntary enlistment, No. 5675133, **Somerset Light Infantry**.
- **23 Aug 1940** — **Emergency commission** as **2nd Lieutenant**, Welch Regiment (army number **143587**). London Gazette Supplement 34928 (20 Aug 1940), p. 5145.
- **1942** — **Western Desert Campaign**.
- **Second World War — Italy** — **Major**, **1st Battalion, The Welch Regiment**. Full narrative: [David John Lewis — Sicily to the Po, and the U.S. Silver Star](../stories/david-john-lewis-italy-1945-silver-star.md).
  - **May 1943** — 1st Welch joined **168th (2nd London) Brigade**, attached to **50th Northumbrian Division**.
  - **Jul 1943** — **Sicily** (Operation Husky): Allied invasion. Lewis later recalled being pinned down by enemy guns on the beach near **Avola**, when Admiral Sir William Davies' light cruiser **HMS *Mauritius*** bombarded the shore — *"Probably saved my life."*
  - **Oct 1943** — 168th Brigade returned to **56th (London) Division** on the Italian mainland.
  - **Jan 1944** — **Garigliano crossing / First Battle of Monte Cassino**.
  - **Feb–Mar 1944** — **Anzio**: 168th Brigade detached to reinforce the bridgehead under 1st British Division / U.S. VI Corps. The brigade suffered **50% casualties** — the worst of the 56th Division's three brigades.
  - **1944** — Promoted to **Major**.
  - **Jul 1944** — Brigade rebuilt (including ex-RA anti-aircraft gunners retrained as infantry); landed **Taranto**.
  - **Aug–Sep 1944** — **Gothic Line / Battle of Gemmano** (Operation Olive). 56th Division took ~6,000 casualties, nearly half the Eighth Army total for Olive.
  - **Sep 1944** — 1st Welch reduced to **cadre**; left 168th Brigade.
  - **Winter 1944–45** — Battalion rebuilt; joined **61st Infantry Brigade, 6th Armoured Division**.
  - **13–19 Apr 1945** — **Battle of the Argenta Gap**: 6th Armoured Division part of V Corps breakthrough.
  - **23 Apr 1945** — **Bondeno** captured; linked with U.S. 10th Mountain Division at Finale.
  - **25 Apr 1945** — **Po bridgehead / Faccenda**: Lewis ordered to seize and hold hamlet on the right flank. Led platoon assaults against machine-gun positions and a counterattack. Awarded **U.S. Silver Star** (GO No. 285, 10 Nov 1945).
  - **2 May 1945** — German surrender in Italy.
  - **May 1945** — 6th Armoured Division moved to **Austria**. Lewis recalled that he **ended his war in Austria**. Division subsequently deployed to the **Trieste** border zone.
  - **4 Dec 1945** — Battalion stationed at **Sirmione, Lake Garda**. Battalion Orders (No. 113, Lt-Col D.L.C. Reynolds OBE commanding) formally announce the Silver Star under "Honours and Awards." It was here, at Sirmione, that David met **Fulvia Zerauschek**.
- **1947** — Demobilised with rank of **Major**. Returned to Sirmione to marry Fulvia.
- **23 May 1946** — **Mentioned in dispatches** (London Gazette, Supplement 37575, p. 2466).
- **18 Jul 1947** — Silver Star formally gazetted (London Gazette, Second Supplement 38018, p. 3319).
- **31 Oct 1953** — **Relinquished commission**; granted honorary rank of **Major** (London Gazette Supplement, p. 5761).
- **Decorations and medals:** U.S. Silver Star; Mentioned in Dispatches. Campaign medals: **1939–1945 Star**, **Africa Star**, **Italy Star**, **Defence Medal**, **War Medal 1939–1945** (all photographed in the personal archive).

### Wartime letter (16 Aug 1943)

A two-page typed letter home to *"Dear Parents and Keith"* from the Sicilian campaign — written on a captured Italian typewriter with a broken ribbon. David analyses the strategic situation with remarkable clarity, saves money for his parents in Defence Bonds, worries about them at home: *"Look after yourselves, because when I come home I want you to be young enough and fit enough to laugh and enjoy yourselves with me."* Of baby brother Keith (then aged 9): *"The more I hear of Keith the more interesting and attractive he becomes. He seems to have been a great improvement on the other red you had about the house."* Signs off as **"Dafydd John"** — the Welsh form of his name.

### Comrades

Brigadier **O.G. Brooke CBE DSO**, his former commanding officer, wrote to David in May 1985 recalling VE Day near **Caporetto** — *"The enemy did not believe in cessation!"* — and praising David's magazine articles. In a second letter (Sep 1985) he wrote: *"Wouldn't it be nice to escape from this filthy summer and escape to Sirmione?"* — forty years on, the Lake Garda posting still resonated.

## HM Overseas Civil Service (1948–1969)

- **1948–1950** — Seconded to **Foreign Office Administration of African Territories**. **District Officer in charge of Sabratha District, Libya**.
- **1951** — **Magistrate, Western Province, Tripolitania, Libya**.
- **Jun 1952** — **Oath of office** taken (certificate dated 6 Jun 1952).
- **1952–1955** — **District Officer and District Commissioner, Fort Jameson, Northern Rhodesia**.
- **1956** — **District Commissioner, Chinsali**.
- **1957–1958** — **District Commissioner, Livingstone**.
- **1958–1961** — **District Commissioner, Ndola**.
- **1961** — **Deputy Provincial Commissioner, Western Province (Copperbelt)**.
- **1962–1966** — **Assistant Secretary** in the Ministries of the Chief Secretary, Home Affairs, and Information and Postal Services.
- **1966** — **Acting Permanent Secretary, Ministry of Information and Postal Services** (Zambia).

Passed government examinations in **Law** and the **Nyanja language**. Italian speaker from wartime service and marriage to Fulvia.

### The Congo Crisis (July 1960)

As District Commissioner in Ndola, David was the senior administrative officer on the ground when the **Belgian Congo collapsed** in July 1960. Thousands of European refugees — men, women, children, a baby born in a car on a bush road — flooded across the border into Northern Rhodesia. David organised the reception and departure centres in Ndola, coordinating with the Royal Rhodesian Air Force (Dakotas and Pembrokes on 24-hour standby, Canadairs flying blankets and mattresses), the Women's Voluntary Service, the British Empire Service League, and local commerce.

**Andrew Gardner**, then a radio journalist with the Federal Broadcasting Corporation (who would go on to become one of Britain's most recognisable faces as newscaster on *News at Ten* from its first night in 1967), broadcast live from Ndola on 11 July 1960. His script names David directly:

> *"In charge of the operation in Ndola is the District Commissioner, Mr David Lewis, who with his team of helpers is organizing the reception and departure centres in the town."*

Gardner's broadcast describes crying children, worried parents, harassed airport officials, piles of luggage, French and English announcements, an urgent appeal for a doctor, and a trainload of refugees leaving south. In a personal letter to David dated 23 July 1960, Gardner wrote:

> *"Everybody down here, in the Corporation, is full of admiration for the truly magnificent way that the Provincial Administration on the Copperbelt dealt with the recent refugee problem. It was certainly organised very well and the general opinion here in Salisbury is that no praise is too high for those who were concerned."*

The archive also contains **12 pages of SECRET classified government documents** (Feb 1961), US Consulate correspondence, and UMHK (Union Minière du Haut-Katanga) mining company communications from the crisis period.

### Appointments in Zambia

Vice President, Northern Rhodesia European Civil Servants Association; member, Whitley Council. Municipal Councillor. **Director, Zambia Television Limited**. Director, Zambian Publishing Company. Chairman, National Obscene Literature Committee. Member: National Literature Committee, National Nutrition Committee, National Decimalisation Committee, National Stamp Design Committee.

Received a **DFID pension** (Department for International Development, successor to the Colonial Office / ODA), confirming pensionable colonial/overseas service.

### Correspondence with President Kaunda (1969)

In September 1969, writing from his new post at the Keep Britain Tidy Group, David wrote to **President Kenneth Kaunda** of Zambia after watching Kaunda's BBC Television interview about the government's participation in the copper mining industry. David described himself as *"one of your Former Under Secretaries"* and recalled that the President had once had *"a game of table tennis with my wife"* when visiting their house as Prime Minister.

Kaunda's Private Secretary replied on 6 October 1969:

> *"I recall that you once lived in Zambia for a considerable period during which time you held a prominent and high position in the Government. I am sure that you have a fair knowledge, if not fully conversant with our problems."*

The letter concluded: *"Indeed Zambia is counting on willing hands for their services should need arise."*

## Director-General, Keep Britain Tidy Group (1969–c. 1987)

- **From 1969** — **Director-General, Keep Britain Tidy Group**, based at **Bostel House, 37 West Street, Brighton BN1 2RE**. Later moved to **27 Queen Anne's Gate, London SW1**. Member, Government Working Group on Waste Management. Member, Packaging Council. Associate, Institute of Wastes Management.
- **Nov 1975** — Led the British delegation to the **Clean World International Conference** in **Berlin** (held in the Reichstag building), alongside **James C. Bowling** (Chairman, Keep America Beautiful) and **Gordon Oakes MP**.
- **Spring 1978** — Authored "The Keep Britain Tidy Group", a short communication in *Environmental Conservation* (Cambridge University Press), Vol. 5, No. 1, p. 56. DOI: [10.1017/S037689290003811X](https://doi.org/10.1017/S037689290003811X).
- **Aug 1981** — Received a personal telegram from **Queen Elizabeth II** thanking him for birthday wishes sent on behalf of KBT (*"I am extremely touched by your telegram of good wishes on my birthday"*). The **Queen Mother** was patron of the Group; her private secretary corresponded with David on organisational matters, including condolences on the death of co-founder **Sir Felix Brunner** (Nov 1982).
- **Sep 1984** — **Peter Walker**, Secretary of State for Energy, wrote praising the KBT annual report: *"Naturally I am delighted at the progress you continuously achieve and the impact you make in the country."*
- KBT was the UK partner of **Keep America Beautiful**; David hosted a reception at **Claridges** with **Sir Derek Ezra** (KBT Chairman) in June 1981 for the US parent organisation's Chairman William A. Fickling Jr.
- The Keep Britain Tidy Group was founded in 1955; registered as a charity in 1960. It became the **Tidy Britain Group** in 1987, then **ENCAMS** in 2002, reverting to **Keep Britain Tidy** in 2009. Lewis's tenure as Director-General placed him at the head of Britain's principal anti-litter charity through the 1970s and into the 1980s.
