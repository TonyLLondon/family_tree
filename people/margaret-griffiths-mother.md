# Margaret Davies (wife of David Griffiths)

**Margaret Davies** was born about **1787** in the parish of **Cayo** (Cynwyl Gaeo / Conwil-gaio), Carmarthenshire — a rural parish in the northern Tywi valley, about 20 miles west of Llywel across the county boundary. She was probably the "Margaret David" christened on **20 April 1787** at Conwil-gaio, daughter of **David Conwil** (i.e. David Davies, using a locative identifier). On **22 December 1815** she married [David Griffiths](david-griffiths-llywel.md) of Llywel at the parish church of Llywell (Church of England).

Their first child, **John**, was christened at the same parish church just six weeks later. A daughter, **Sarah**, was born about 1826 at Trecastle. Margaret and David were later members of the **Saron Independent Chapel** at Llywell, where two further sons named Thomas were baptised in 1828 and 1830 — the first presumably died in infancy.

On the 1841 census Margaret was 55, living at Tresgob with David (60) and their son Thomas (11). By the 1851 census she was 65, described as "Labourer's Wife", still at 3 Bishop Town with David (75).

David was buried in December 1857. By the 1861 census Margaret had moved to **Aberdare** to live with her daughter Sarah and son-in-law **John Price**, a haulier, at **Cynon Row, Cwmdare** — the same hamlet where her son Thomas was living at Clive Place. She was recorded as age 75, a widow, born "Cilycwm, Carmarthenshire" (the parish adjacent to Cayo).
