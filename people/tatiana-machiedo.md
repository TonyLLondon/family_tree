# Tatiana Machiedo (née Machiedo; Zerauschek)

Fulvia remembered her as **Tanie** or **Tania**—“she was not much older than us”—and guessed wrong once about her origins; the documents speak more clearly. **Tatiana** was Dalmatian Italian by milieu, from a **Machiedo** line that scholars have since drawn into the **Hvar–Zadar** paper trail ([Kovačić 2014](../sources/kovacic-2014-rod-machiedo-hvar.md)). She was wife to [Riccardo Zerauschek](riccardo-zerauschek.md) (**Zio Ginco**), mother to **Nora**, **Renato**, and **Luciano**, and a presence in the **1951 Trieste IRO file** alongside his displaced-persons narrative.

Published genealogy places her among the children of **[Juraj-Zorko Machiedo](juraj-zorko-machiedo.md)** and **[Graziella Nakic d’Osljak](graziella-nakic-d-osljak.md)**—with **Maksim** (short life, Florence), **[Sergije Makiedo](sergije-makiedo.md)** (jurist, partisan cadence, later Yugoslav diplomat), and **Isabella “Cinci”**. Fulvia’s “communist judge” brother is **Sergije**, not an unrelated namesake. **[Dr. Jerko Machiedo](jerko-machiedo.md)** was **uncle** on the father’s side—brother to Juraj-Zorko, both sons of **[Lauro Machiedo](lauro-machiedo.md)** and **[Magdalena Novak](magdalena-novak-machiedo.md)** “Bonaparte.”
