# D. Ioanes Addobbati

Giovanni in Italian, Ivan in Slavic orbit, **D. Ioanes Addobbati** closes the gap between a Bergamo line and the Adriatic generation that would settle Zara. Sabalich calls him *capitano dei corrazieri*—captain of cuirassiers—heavy cavalry in Venetian service, a gloss that gives human weight to the terse “Captain” that travelled with his name in a machine extract.

Born about 1676, he fathered three sons whose baptisms read like a march into Dalmatia: Daniele (1702), Laurentius (1705), Iosephus (1709). By 1728 an Addobbati surname turns up in Turanj parish registers; by 1730 Daniele is marrying in Zara itself; on 13 November 1733 the brothers appear among the *cittadini originari* in the capital’s book of citizens. The 1745 Bergamo testimonial in the family fonds names Giovanni as the father of that trio—the documentary spine of six generations from an earlier Pietro de Adobatis (1495).

His wife does not appear in the same structured import as his children; parish books in Bergamo may still hold her name.
