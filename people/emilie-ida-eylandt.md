# Emilie Ida Eylandt

Emilie Ida Eylandt was born on **20 March 1812** in Tallinn and baptised on **2 April 1812** at one of the city's Lutheran churches as "Ida Emilie Eylandt," the daughter of [Johan Jacob Eylandt](johan-jacob-eylandt.md) and [Maria Elisabeth Busse](maria-elisabeth-busse.md). She was the sixth of at least ten children, arriving into the household of a **Schneidermeister** (master tailor) in the Baltic German artisan quarter.

Her mother died when Emilie was eleven, and her father when she was nineteen. On **27 March 1839** she married [Hermann Eberhard Erbe](hermann-eberhard-erbe.md), the **Domschloßvogt** (cathedral castle warden) — a match that linked the Eylandt tailoring family to the Erbe administrative lineage stretching back to Thuringia. Their first child, Amalie, was born and died in Pärnu within days in October 1839, before the couple settled in Tallinn where [Olga Caroline](olga-caroline-erbe.md), [Eugen Edmund](eugen-edmund-erbe.md), and several other children followed through the 1850s.

Hermann died in **Riga** in **January 1872**. Emilie survived him by only six months, dying in **Tallinn** on **14 July 1872** and buried there on **18 July**. Whether the household had already fragmented geographically — Hermann in Riga, Emilie remaining in Tallinn — is worth noting when reconstructing the children's adult lives.
