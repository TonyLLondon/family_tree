# Jaqueline Suzanne Chenevard

Jaqueline Suzanne Chenevard was baptised on **26 October 1732** at **Céligny**, a Geneva-canton enclave on the north shore of Lac Léman. Her parents were **Benjamin Chenevard** (b. ~1688, d. 2 Dec 1747 at Céligny, son of **Antoine Chenevard**) and **Christine Nerfin** (daughter of **Humbert Nerfin**), who had married on 9 March 1720 at Céligny.

On **28 October 1761** she married **Jean François Louis Montagnon** (son of Jean Pierre Montagnon + Jeanne Cusin of Aubonne) at Céligny. Their first child Jean Gédéon was baptised at Céligny on 9 May 1762, after which the couple settled in **Aubonne**, Vaud, where their remaining children were born: Henri Samuel (1764), Jeanne (1767), Jean Isaac (1768), and Jean Louis François César (1776).

Her name appears in later records with multiple spellings — Cheneveau, Chanevaz, Chenevard — reflecting the fluid orthography of 18th-century parish scribes. The Chenevard surname clusters in **Jorat-Mézières** (Vaud) and **Céligny** (Geneva), both Reformed parishes.

Her husband Jean François Louis died on **4 February 1786** at Aubonne, aged about 49. Their son **Louis Montagnon** married Jeanne Françoise Dumartheray and fathered [Jeanne Bénigne Montagnon](jeanne-benigne-montagnon.md), who married [Hans Jacob Stump](hans-jacob-stump-1800.md) in 1832.

## Open questions

- Death date and place — not found in Aubonne burial indexes; possibly died elsewhere or the record is not yet indexed.
- Jean Pierre Montagnon (husband's father, b. 1692, d. 1737 Aubonne): burial found (6NQL-QVQK). His wife was **Jeane Elizabeth Cuzin** (b. 1685, d. 4 Dec 1753 Aubonne, 6NQK-33DR). His parents not explicitly confirmed, but a **Jean François Montagnon** (b. 1666, d. 15 Sep 1732 Aubonne) married to **Jeane Marechal** (b. 1665, d. 1742) is the likely father — same parish, right generation. Jean François's father was **Jean Montagnon** (baptism of son François 22 Dec 1667, 6NQK-Q676), the earliest known Montagnon in Swiss records. The Montagnon surname is concentrated in **Isère** (Dauphiné) and **Ardèche** (Vivarais) in southeastern France, both Huguenot strongholds — Jean Montagnon likely migrated from France to Vaud in the mid-1600s.
- Christine Nerfin's birth/baptism and death records not yet found. The Nerfin surname appears indigenous to the Vaud/Geneva region.
- Bernard Chenevard (Antoine's father, earliest confirmed Chenevard ancestor at Céligny) — no baptism or burial found. A French branch of Chenevards existed at **Seveux, Haute-Saône** (Franche-Comté), suggesting possible French origins for the family.
