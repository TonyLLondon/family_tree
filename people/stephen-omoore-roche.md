# Stephen O'Moore Roche

**Stephen O'Moore Roche**, known as **"Dov"** or **"the Black"** from his complexion, was the last Roche to hold land at **Castletown-Roche** in County Cork. The estate his family had held for five centuries — since Adam de Rupe arrived with the Norman invaders — was **entirely forfeited under William III** after the Williamite War (1689–1691). Dispossessed, he left Cork and rebuilt the family's fortunes from nothing in County Limerick, laying the foundation for his son [John Lysaght Roche](john-lysaght-roche.md)'s rise as a merchant prince.

Burke's 1833:

> Stephen Roche, known by the designation of **Dov, or Black**, from his complexion, whose estate, **already injured by composition in the time of Cromwell, was entirely forfeited under William III**. Compelled in consequence to leave the county of Cork, he retired to **Kilrush, in Clare**, and afterwards took up his abode at **Pallas, in the county of Limerick**, in the vicinity of his brother-in-law, **William Apjohn, esq.**

## Life

- **~1660** — Born at **Castletown-Roche** (or nearby **Curwarragher**), County Cork. Son of [Robert Roche](robert-roche.md) and [Juliana O'Moore](juliana-omoore.md).
- **~1685** — Married **[Anastasia Lysagt Burke](anastasia-lysagt-burke.md)**, elder daughter and co-heir of **Thomas Lysaght, Esq.**, of County Limerick. Anastasia's sister Catherine married **William Apjohn** — the same brother-in-law who would later shelter Stephen.
- **1688** — Birth of son [John Lysaght Roche](john-lysaght-roche.md). John took the Lysaght name from his maternal grandfather Thomas Lysaght.
- **1689–1691** — The **Williamite War**. The Catholic Jacobite cause was defeated at the Boyne (1690), Aughrim (1691), and the siege of Limerick (1691). In the aftermath, Catholic landowners who had supported James II were systematically stripped of their estates. Stephen's property at Castletown-Roche — "already injured by composition in the time of Cromwell" under his father [Robert](robert-roche.md) — was now **entirely forfeited**.
- **After 1691** — "Compelled to leave the county of Cork," Stephen moved first to **Kilrush, Co. Clare**, then to **Pallas, Co. Limerick**, near his brother-in-law William Apjohn. This was the migration that turned the Roches from Cork landowners into Limerick merchants — within a generation, his son John would be sending armed ships to the West Indies.

## The Pattern of Dispossession

Stephen's story encapsulates the destruction of Catholic Ireland across three generations:

| Generation | Event | Consequence |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| Grandfather [John Roche](john-roche.md) | 1641 rising; sat in the Catholic Parliament | Cromwellian conquest; estate "injured by composition" |
| Father [Robert Roche](robert-roche.md) | Survived the Cromwellian settlement | Retained diminished estate at Castletown-Roche |
| **Stephen** | Williamite War (1689–91) | Estate **entirely forfeited**; forced to leave Cork |

The family lost Castletown-Roche — the ancestral seat of the Roche lords of Fermoy for five hundred years — and never returned. But Stephen's marriage to Anastasia Lysaght gave his son an entrée to the Limerick merchant class, and John Lysaght Roche built from that base a fortune that would rival any of the Tribes of Galway.
