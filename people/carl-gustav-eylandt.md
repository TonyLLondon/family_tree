# Carl Gustav Eylandt

Carl Gustav Eylandt was born in the Hageri parish south of Tallinn and spent his adult life across the Estland–Livland border, part of the fluid movement of Baltic German artisan and estate families within the Russian imperial provinces. He married [Margarete Elisabeth Wiedau](margarete-elisabeth-wiedau.md) on **8 January 1768**, a union recorded in the Hageri church books. Both were born in the same parish — she in 1744, he the year before — suggesting a match rooted in local community ties.

Their son [Johan Jacob Eylandt](johan-jacob-eylandt.md) was born in **Leal (Lihula)** in 1774, one of at least twelve children in family **F143**. The siblings' birthplaces shift from Lihula to Kirbla Parish in the 1780s, tracking the family's move to the Kirrefer pastorat estate area on the western Estonian coast.

Carl Gustav outlived his wife Margarete by seven years. He died on **31 March 1826** in **Viljandi (Fellin)** — the same town where his son Johan Jacob had married Maria Elisabeth Busse in December 1799 — and was buried there on **3 April 1826**, aged 82.

## Open questions

Cited by Geni researchers Maris Lindau and Lea Kornel, not yet independently verified against images.
- **Date discrepancy:** Geni (Maris Lindau) gives birth ~1747 and death 1819 at Kirbla; the FamilySearch burial record 6D73-GFCY gives birth year 1743 and death 31 Mar 1826 in Viljandi. The Saaga baptism image at EAA.3154.2.2:177 would resolve the birth year. The Hageri parish registers (EAA catalog 620449) are online but unindexed for the 1740s. His son Johan Jacob’s parentage under Carl Gustav IS supported by the 1795 Kirbla family list (EAA.1864.2.V-67:227, cited by Geni researcher Andrey Masing).
- Baptism record for Carl Gustav in the **Hageri** parish registers (FamilySearch catalog 620449, online, covering 1711–1940) — would confirm parents Caspar Martin and Hedwig Helene Busch.
- **Carl Ludwig Eylandt** (b. 1762, d. 12 Feb 1843) — born 19 years after Carl Gustav but before Caspar Martin's 1766 death, could be a brother. See [corpus reference](../sources/corpus/fs-estonia-church-books-eylandt-busse/reference.md#carl-ludwig-eylandt--burial).
- Whether Carl Gustav held an **Amtman** (estate steward) role — an "Amtman Eyland" appears as father in burials from the 1790s–1800s.
