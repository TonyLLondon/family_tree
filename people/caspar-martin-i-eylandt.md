# Caspar Martin I. Eylandt

With his wife [Hedwig Helene Busch](hedwig-helene-busch.md) he was father of [Carl Gustav Eylandt](carl-gustav-eylandt.md). He was born about 1713 in Riga, Livland, and is recorded as marrying about 1729 at Nonnenhof, Livland, Estonia. He died on 20 November 1766 at Kirrefer in Wiek (Kirbla area, western Estonia), in the imperial-era placename form carried by the import.

The Geni profile (managed by Lea Kornel) describes him as a **blacksmith** (Estonian: *sepp*) at **Veltsa manor** in the 1750s, and notes that he came to Estonia from Latvia ("Eestimaale Latist"). Court or administrative records in the Estonian Historical Archives (EAA.3.1.491) document his presence at the manor.

Three of his children's baptisms are now indexed in FamilySearch under the "**Eiland**" spelling variant, all at Hageri parish: Casper Marten (bap. 23 Sep 1740), [Carl Gustav](carl-gustav-eylandt.md) (bap. 29 Sep 1743), and Anna Catharina (bap. 13 Feb 1748). Each record independently names both parents -- "Caspar Eiland" and "Hedwig Helena Busch" (with minor orthographic variation).
