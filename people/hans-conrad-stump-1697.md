# Hans Conrad Stump (b. 1697)

**Hans Conrad Stump** (baptized 30 May 1697) was the son of [Caspar Stump](caspar-stump-1662.md) and [Dorothea Brueschwiler](dorothea-brueschwiler.md) in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. He had multiple wives and became the father of [Hans Konrad Stump](hs-konrad-stump.md) (baptized 1730), representing Generation 11 in the Swiss STUMP lineage.

## Life

**30 May 1697** — Christened **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. Son of [Caspar Stump](caspar-stump-1662.md) and [Dorothea Brueschwiler](dorothea-brueschwiler.md).

**c. 1725** — First marriage to Anna Barbel Kaegi.

**3 Jul 1729** — Birth of son Hans Conrad Stump with Anna Barbel Kaegi.

**12 May 1729** — Birth of son Hans Conrad Stump with Barbel Kreys (different mother, same year).

**c. 1730** — Marriage to Elssbeth Straub.

**30 Oct 1730** — Birth of son [Hans Konrad Stump](hs-konrad-stump.md) with Elssbeth Straub.

**c. 1735** — Died **Sulgen** (estimated, as wives appear as widows in later records).

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Multiple Wives Pattern:**
Hans Conrad's marriages to three different women (Anna Barbel Kaegi, Barbel Kreys, Elssbeth Straub) reflect common 17th-18th century Swiss patterns due to high mortality rates. The timing suggests sequential marriages rather than simultaneous relationships.

**Son Naming Convention:**
The consistent naming of multiple sons "Hans Conrad" follows Swiss tradition of naming children after the father, particularly when earlier children died young. Only the 1730 son (with Elssbeth Straub) survived to continue the lineage.

**Generation 11 Significance:**
Hans Conrad represents the late 17th century generation of the STUMP family, bridging between the mid-1600s (father Caspar) and early 1700s (son Hans Konrad) periods.
