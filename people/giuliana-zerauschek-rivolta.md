# Giuliana Zerauschek (Rivolta)

Giuliana — known in the family as **Liana** — was the eldest daughter of [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md) and [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md), and the elder sister of [Fulvia](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md). She and Fulvia were at **boarding school together in Genova** from at least **1936**; their father addressed letters to "Carissime Liana e Fulle." By **November 1940**, back in Zara during the war, Giuliana was working at the **hospital** (Ospedale): she rose at **6 a.m.** every morning to meet **Anita** at the **Piazza dei Signori**, and by **7 a.m.** they were in the **operating theatre** — "dicono loro" ("so they say"), Antonio added with paternal scepticism.

She was born on **5 September 1921** in **Zara** — confirmed by the birthday calendar Peter Mark Lewis compiled for 1998 and cross-referenced against her own signature on a 1951 British Military Administration visa application giving her date of birth as "5 settembre 1921."

She married **Dott. Carlo Enrico Rivolta** (born **9 November 1920**). Their first daughter **Daniela** was born on **2 February 1946**, and their second daughter **Silvana** on **5 August 1950** — both dates confirmed by the 1998 birthday calendar. By **April 1948**, the couple were living at **Sirmione** with Daniela, young enough for Carlo Enrico to be putting her into pyjamas at bedtime. In a long, witty letter to Fulvia (7 Apr 1948), Carlo Enrico — writing on "Dott. CARLO ENRICO RIVOLTA" letterhead — complained with mock despair that Giuliana and Ester constantly compared him unfavourably to **"il Pel"** (a family nickname, probably for David Lewis): "'Ma il Pel non fa mai queste cose!' 'Il Pel è molto più bravo.' 'Il Pel sarebbe capace.'" He discussed the **Marshall Plan**, the **stock market**, and the looming **18 April 1948 Italian general election** with financial acumen. Antonio and Ester ("i nonni") were in the Sirmione house, planning to wave handkerchiefs from the windows as Giuliana, Carlo Enrico, and Daniela flew past in a **tourist seaplane** over Lake Garda. The letter also mentions **"gli zii Pini"** (the Pini aunt and uncle) and a friend **Pino** who told risqué jokes at parties.

In **April 1951**, Giuliana applied for a visa from the **British Military Administration, Tripolitania** to visit her sister Fulvia and brother-in-law David in Libya — "per visita alla sorella e al cognato." She traveled with both daughters: Daniela (then five) and Silvana (eight months). The application lists her occupation as **casalinga** (housewife), her religion as *Cristiana, Cattolica, Romana*, and her passport as No. 1636109/P (issued Brescia, 20 Oct 1949). Her address was **Viale Mazzini 60, Firenze** — the same address that appears on Antonio's Florence stationery. She signed the form **"Giuliana Zerauschek Rivolta"** on 13 April 1951 in Florence.

The family later settled at **Via Masaccio 112, 50132 Firenze** — the address on the 1973 A.N.D.A.Z. mailing label. In 1999 Fulvia sent a Parcelforce parcel to "Dr M Zerauschek, c/o Rivolta, Via Masacci 0112, Firenze" — confirming the Rivoltas remained at the Masaccio address and that the Zerauschek siblings maintained close ties through Giuliana's household. In 1973 Giuliana was living at **Via Masaccio 112, 50132 Firenze** — the address on the mailing label of the A.N.D.A.Z. *ZARA* periodical carrying her father's obituary. In **November 1971** she added a note to one of Antonio's letters to Fulvia: "Sono qui dal Papà per una piccola visita perchè prima sono stata in centro per compere" — she was shopping in Florence and dropped in on her father. In **October 1972**, Antonio gave Carlo Enrico **2,500,000 lire**, warning: "queste sono le ultime, perciò prima di spenderle pensaci su." In **November 1972**, Giuliana relayed the news of David's mother's sudden death to Mario and his wife in Florence.

She is named on the [family grave slab](../media/docs/Grave%20slab%20Florence%20Ester%20Addobbati%20Zerauschek%20Antonio%20Giuliana%20Zerauschek%20Rivolta.png) in Florence alongside her parents Ester and Antonio.
