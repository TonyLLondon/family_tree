# Susannah South

**Susannah South** (b. ~1824, Essex) married [James Newcomb](james-newcomb-i184.md) in the **January–March quarter of 1844** at Maldon (GRO, Vol. 12 p. 229). The couple had at least seven children between 1844 and 1857. By the 1861 census both Susannah and James had vanished, and their surviving children were dispersed — [Alma](alma-newcomb-i106.md) to the Cousins at Tiptree Heath, young James to service in Tollesbury.

Susannah almost certainly came from one of the **South families of Tolleshunt Knights**. In 1851 the Newcomb and South households were neighbours on consecutive census pages: James South (b. ~1809, labourer) and Reuben South (b. ~1817, labourer) both lived in Tolleshunt Knights, both married to women from **Layer Marney**. These South brothers, together with Abraham South (b. ~1786) at Layer de la Haye in 1841, formed a tight rural clan in the Layer–Tolleshunt–Maldon corridor. Susannah (b. ~1824) fits as a daughter of the older generation — possibly of Abraham or of an elder sibling not yet found.

Decades later, Alma married **Reuben South** after George Coolbear's death — marrying back into Susannah's natal family.

## Evidence

- **1851 Census** — Tolleshunt Knights: Susannah Newcomb, daughter-in-law, married, 27, born Essex. In the household of William Newcomb with her husband James and children Charles, James Jr, Sarah A.
- **1861 Census** — Not found. No Susannah Newcomb or Susannah South in Essex.
- **GRO index** — No Susannah Newcomb death in Maldon 1851–1867. No Susannah South death in Maldon for this period either.

## Open questions

- **Natal family:** Which South household was she born into? James South (b. ~1809) or a sibling of James and Reuben? Abraham South (b. ~1786, Layer de la Haye) is the patriarch.
- **Cousins connection:** Alma is listed as "granddaughter" of John and Elizabeth Cousins in 1861. If the Cousins are maternal grandparents, Susannah's mother (not father) was a Cousins — meaning Susannah's full maiden name might have been Cousins, with "South" being an error or alternate family name in the working tree. Alternatively, the Cousins connection runs through a different route.
- **Fate after 1857:** Like James, Susannah vanishes. Death may be registered outside Maldon, or under a remarried name.
