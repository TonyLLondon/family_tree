# James Newcomb

**James Newcomb** (b. ~1823, Essex) was a labourer at **Tolleshunt Knights**, Essex, and the father of [Alma Newcomb](alma-newcomb-i106.md). He married [Susannah South](susannah-south-i185.md) in the **January–March quarter of 1844** (GRO, Maldon Vol. 12 p. 229). The couple had at least seven children between 1844 and 1857, several of whom died in infancy. Both James and Susannah vanish from the record after mid-1857 — their last child, Mary Ann, was registered in the April–June quarter of that year. By the 1861 census their surviving children had been scattered: Alma to the Cousins at Tiptree Heath, young James to service in Tollesbury.

James's parents were [William Newcomb](william-newcomb-i186.md) (b. ~1793, labourer) and [Elizabeth](elizabeth-newcomb-i187.md) (b. ~1792, Witham / Layer Breton). In 1851 James and Susannah were still living in William's household at Tolleshunt Knights — a three-generation labourer's cottage with eight people.

## Evidence

- **GRO marriage index** — James Newcomb, Q1 1844, Maldon Vol. 12 p. 229.
- **1841 Census** — Possibly James Newcomb (b. ~1825), St Osyth, Tendring, Essex, living with James and Martha — a childhood household before his move to Tolleshunt Knights (FMP: GBC/1841/0001362135). The two-year birth discrepancy is within normal range.
- **1851 Census** — Tolleshunt Knights, Maldon (HO 107/1778): James Newcomb, son, married, 28, labourer, born Essex. In the household of his father William Newcomb (head, 58) with wife Susannah (daughter-in-law, 27) and children Charles (7), James (5), Sarah A (2).
- **1861 Census** — Not found. No James Newcomb of the right age appears anywhere in the census in Essex or nationally.
- **GRO death index** — No James Newcomb death in Maldon 1851–1861. The 1842 Maldon death (Vol. 12 p. 107) predates his 1844 marriage and is a different individual.

## Open questions

- **Fate after 1857:** James and Susannah's last child was born Q2 1857. By 1861 the family has disintegrated. James's death has not been found in Maldon — he may have died at sea, in a distant institution, or the FreeBMD record is incomplete.
- **1841 identification:** The St Osyth household (James, b. ~1825, with James and Martha) is a plausible match but not confirmed. The elder James Newcomb (b. ~1816) at St Osyth on the same census page may be his father or uncle — but these are in Tendring district, not Maldon.
- **Occupation:** Listed as "labourer" in 1851. No indication of maritime work, though the Cousins household Alma was placed with included a "mariner's wife."
