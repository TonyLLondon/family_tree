# Ann Mainwaring (1826–1875)

Ann Mainwaring was born **10 June 1826** and [baptised](../media/docs/1826-baptism-ann-mainwaring-st-mary-swansea.jpg) **16 July 1826** at **St Mary's, Swansea**. Her father was **Evan Mainwaring** (b. ~1781), a **mason**, and her mother was **Margaret** (b. ~1785). The census consistently records her birthplace as **Llansamlet** — the parish immediately east of Swansea.

Evan and Margaret both married and were buried at St Mary's, Swansea. Margaret died in **Q4 1841**, shortly after the [1841 census](https://www.findmypast.co.uk/image-viewer?id=GBC/1841/1426/0085) which found Evan (aged ~56) at Swansea with children Rachel, John, Martha, and William. Ann was 15 and likely already in service. Evan survived as a widower until about **1848**, when he too was buried at St Mary's. By then Ann had already left Swansea and married Thomas Cushen in the Pontypool area.

She married [Thomas Cushen](thomas-cushen.md) (b. ~1821, Cork, Ireland) about 1847. Their first child Mary was born at Abersychan (~1847), near where Thomas's brother Patrick had settled. By 1851 the couple had moved to Dowlais, where Thomas worked as a blast furnace filler and Ann raised a growing family of boys.

The [1861 census](../media/docs/1861-census-thomas-cashin-household-dowlais-rg9-4056.jpg) lists her as Ann Cashin (34, Llansamlet); by [1871](../media/docs/1871-census-thomas-cushing-household-dowlais-rg10-5397.jpg) she was Anne Cushing (47, Llansamlet) with six sons at home. She died in **July 1875** at Merthyr Tydfil (GRO Vol 11A p. 227) and was buried at **Dowlais**. Thomas was a widower on the 1881 census.

Her father Evan's trade as a **mason** passed to her son [David Cushen](david-cushen.md), who worked as a bricklayer and mason at Dowlais — grandfather to grandson through a Welsh-Irish marriage.

## Open questions

- **Evan Mainwaring's origins** — born ~1781. The Mainwaring surname is English/Cheshire in origin, unusual in Swansea. No baptism found in Glamorganshire. He may have migrated from England or his family may have been established in the Swansea area for some time.
- **Siblings' fates** — Rachel, John, Martha, William from the 1841 census. Did any of them stay in the Swansea/Llansamlet area?
- **Evan's death year** — GRO has two candidates: Q2 1848 and Q4 1849, both Swansea. The burial indexes give birth ~1781, confirming identity.
- **Evan's marriage to Margaret** — Glamorgan Marriage Index Pre-1837 lists an Evan Mainwaring marriage in Glamorganshire, but no image is available. The St Mary's Swansea banns (1831) are for a younger Evan marrying Eleanor Bowen — a different man. Ann's father's marriage to Margaret has not yet been found as a parish register image.
