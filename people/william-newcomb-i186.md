# William Newcomb

**William Newcomb** (b. ~1793, Essex) was a labourer at **Tolleshunt Knights**, Essex. He and his wife [Elizabeth](elizabeth-newcomb-i187.md) (b. ~1792, Witham / Layer Breton) were the paternal grandparents of [Alma Newcomb](alma-newcomb-i106.md).

In the **1851 census** William headed a household of eight at Tolleshunt Knights: himself and Elizabeth, their married son [James](james-newcomb-i184.md) (28) with his wife [Susannah](susannah-south-i185.md) (27) and three grandchildren, plus their unmarried son Charles (21, labourer). It was a crowded labourer's cottage — three generations under one roof.

William died before the **1861 census**: Elizabeth is listed as a **widow** on King Street, Chelmsford, alone and working as a charwoman. His death is not clearly identified in the Maldon GRO index, though a William Newcomb death in Q4 1868 at age 78 (Maldon Vol. 4a p. 141) is too late — Elizabeth was already widowed by 1861. The William Newcomb death in Q4 1850 (Maldon Vol. 12 p. 116) also doesn't match, as William was alive for the 1851 census. His death likely fell between 1851 and 1861, possibly outside the Maldon district.

- **1861 Census** — Not found. Elizabeth is a widow in Chelmsford, confirming William's death.
