# Thomas Griffiths

**Thomas Griffiths** was born about **1830** at **Trecastle**, a village in the upper Usk valley of **Breconshire**. By 1861 he had migrated south to the coalfield, settling in **Aberdare** where he spent the rest of his documented life. On the 1871 census he was an **ironworks labourer** at 40; by 1881, at 51, he had shifted to **colliery labourer** — the iron age giving way to coal. He married [Anne Morgan](anne.md) of Llanfair, Carmarthenshire; her widowed mother **Sarah Morgan** (75, described as having "house property") was living with the family in 1881.

His daughter [Catherine Griffiths](catherine-griffiths.md) married [David John Lewis](david-john-lewis-1857.md) in Q1 1884 at Merthyr Tydfil.

## Life

- **~1830** — Born **Trecastle**, Breconshire (1871 census: "Trecastle, Breconshire"; 1881: "Trecastle, Brecknockshire").
- **1861** — Age 30, head of household, Aberdare (RG09/4068).
- **1871** — Age 40, **ironworks labourer**, Aberdare (RG10/5405). Household: wife Anne (41), children Elizabeth (11), Catherine (9), Margaret (5), David (2) — all children born Aberdare.
- **1881** — Age 51, **colliery labourer**, Aberdare (RG11/5320). Still with Anne (52); daughters Eliza (21), Catherine (18), Margaret A (15) all **tin workers**; son David (12) a scholar. Mother-in-law Sarah Morgan (75, widow) and nephew David Morgan (16, coal miner) also present.
