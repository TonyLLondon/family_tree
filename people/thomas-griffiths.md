# Thomas Griffiths

**Thomas Griffiths** was born on **16 June 1830** in the parish of **Llywel**, Breconshire — the upper Usk valley parish that includes the village of **Trecastle**, which he named as his birthplace on every census. He was the son of [David Griffiths](david-griffiths-llywel.md) and [Margaret](margaret-griffiths-mother.md), baptised on 18 July 1830 at **Saron Independent Chapel** — a Welsh Congregationalist meeting house in Llywell. An older brother of the same name, Thomas Griffith, had been baptised at Saron on 20 July 1828 but presumably died in infancy.

By the mid-1850s Thomas had migrated south to the Aberdare coalfield, where he married [Anne Morgan](anne-morgan.md) of Llanfair, Carmarthenshire. Their first recorded child, **Margaret**, was born about 1855 in Aberdare but died at age 8 and was buried at **St Fagan's, Aberdare** on 2 December 1863. The couple's four surviving children — Elizabeth/Eliza, [Catherine](catherine-griffiths.md), Margaret A, and David — were all born in Aberdare between ~1860 and ~1869.

The family lived on **Clive Street** (called "Clive Place" in 1861), in the **Cwmdare** hamlet of Aberdare. Thomas worked as an **ironworks labourer** in 1861 and 1871; by 1881 the iron age was giving way to coal and he had shifted to **colliery labourer**. In 1861 a boarder named **Thomas Thomas**, also from Llywel, shared the house — likely an acquaintance from home. By 1881 the household had expanded to ten people: the nuclear family, Anne's widowed mother **Sarah Morgan** (75, from Builth, Brecknockshire), a nephew **David Morgan** (16, coal miner), a niece **Margaret Griffiths** (16, brick worker), and Anne's cousin **John Morgan** (34, coal miner, born Llanfair, Carmarthenshire).

## Life

- **16 Jun 1830** — Born **Llywel** (Trecastle), Breconshire.
- **18 Jul 1830** — Baptised **Saron Independent Chapel**, Llywell. Father: David Griffiths; Mother: Margaret.
- **1841** — Age 11, living with parents at **Tresgob, Llywell** (HO107/1367). The only child at home.
- **~1854** — Married [Anne Morgan](anne-morgan.md), probably in the Merthyr Tydfil registration district.
- **~1855** — Daughter **Margaret** born, Aberdare.
- **1861** — Age 30, head of household, **Clive Place, Cwmdare**, Aberdare (RG09/4068, folio 71, page 24, schedule 114). **Labourer in the Iron Works**. Household: wife Ann (31), daughter Margaret (6, scholar), daughter Elizabeth (1). Boarder: Thomas Thomas (29, blocklayer, born Llywel, Brecknockshire).
- **2 Dec 1863** — Daughter **Margaret** buried at **St Fagan's, Aberdare**, age 8.
- **1871** — Age 40, head, **Clive Street, Cwmdare**, Aberdare (RG10/5405, folio 12, page 17, schedule 76). **Iron Works Lab**. Household: wife Anne (41), children Elizabeth (11), Catherine (9), Margaret (5), David (2).
- **1881** — Age 51, head, **11 Clive Street**, Aberdare (RG11/5320, folio 50, page 6, schedule 409). **Colliery Lab**, married. Household: wife Ann (52); daughters Eliza (21, tinworker), Catherine (18, tin worker), Margaret A (15, tin worker); son David (12, scholar); nephew David Morgan (16, coal miner); niece Margaret Griffiths (16, brick worker); mother-in-law Sarah Morgan (75, widow, "house property", born Builth, Brecknockshire); cousin John Morgan (34, coal miner, born Llanfair, Carmarthenshire).
