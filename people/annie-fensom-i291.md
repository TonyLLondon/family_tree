# Annie Fensom

Daughter of **David William Fensom** (`I292`, b. Oct 1864 Islington, d. Jun 1929 Islington) and **Minnie Florence Pope** (`I293`, b. 1864 Hammersmith). Parents' marriage **8 August 1886** at Islington (**F137**). The Fensom family were lifelong Islington residents: David William appears in every census from 1871 to 1911 at Islington addresses, ending at **Lower Holloway London N** in 1911. His father **David Fensom** (`I294`) was born about 1836 in **Harpenden, Hertfordshire** — a rural migrant to London.

Wife of [Alfred Edward William Evans](alfred-edward-william-evans.md) — marriage **25 December 1912** (**F50**). Mother of [Joseph Evans (c. 1914)](joseph-evans-i99.md). Fensom parents in [family-tree.json](../family-tree.json): **F137** (**I292** / **I293**).

By the **1939 Register** (29 Sep 1939; TNA ref RG101/0291F/005, schedule 61, sub 2), Annie was living with Alfred in Islington. Her listed occupation was **Printers Maker** — manufacturing components for the printing trade, one of Clerkenwell's established industries. Annie was born, married, raised her children, and died in Islington — seventy-nine years in the same borough.

**Narrative:** [The Spencers and the Evans: Five Generations Between Clerkenwell and Islington](../narratives/evans-islington-clerkenwell.md).
