# Annie Fensom

**Annie** lived almost her whole life inside **Islington**—born **29 January 1893** in the borough where her **Fensom** ancestors had put down roots from **Hertfordshire** soil. She married [Alfred Evans](alfred-evans-i101.md) on **Christmas Day 1912**, a join of **printing-trade** Clerkenwell and **Fensom** Holloway; their children [Alfred Charles](alfred-charles-evans-i576.md), [Joseph](joseph-evans-i99.md), and [Annie](annie-evans-i577.md) continued the Evans name toward the **1940** generation.

By the **1911 Census** Annie had already left home at **eighteen**, boarding on the second floor of **17 North Road, Holloway** with her friend **Rose Elizabeth Maud Watts** — also eighteen, also a brush maker at the **Star Brush Factory** on Eden Grove. Rose would stand as witness at Annie's wedding twenty months later. The Star Brush factory sat on the same Eden Grove where Annie's father and grandparents had lived.

The **1921 Census** (19 June) found her at **4 Bratton Street, Islington** with Alfred and three children — Alfred Charles (8), Joseph David (6), and Annie (4). She was a **brush maker** at the **Star Brush Company**, Eden Grove, Holloway — her workplace within walking distance of the Fensom family's Lower Holloway streets.

The **1939 Register** found her with Alfred at **37 Oldershaw Road, Lower Holloway** — just north of the Holloway Road, back in the Fensom family's home territory. Her occupation was **printers maker** — fashioning parts for presses in an industry that had fed **Exmouth Market** and **Clerkenwell** for generations. She died about **February 1972**, still in Islington, nearly **eighty** years a Londoner within a few Islington streets.

**Line narrative:** [The Spencers and the Evans](../stories/evans-islington-clerkenwell.md).

Parents named as **Edward Pope** and **Emma Day** (Hammersmith) in an working tree belong to a **different** family than Minnie’s.
- The family was indexed as **"Fenson"** (not Fensom) in 1901 and 1911, which is why earlier searches failed.
