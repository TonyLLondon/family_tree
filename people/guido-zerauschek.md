# Guido Zerauschek

Guido was a son of [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md) and [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md), brother of [Fulvia](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md), [Giuliana](giuliana-zerauschek-rivolta.md), and [Mario](mario-zerauschek.md). At school in wartime Zara his marks were uneven: in **November 1942** his father recorded them as "piuttosto bruttina — tre cinque" (three marks of 5/10); by **May 1940** he had scored **otto** (8/10) in an Italian essay, showing improvement when he applied himself. In **February 1943** he had a **liver attack** in Zara; Antonio wrote that they were awaiting the doctor's response.

In **April 1957** he was doing **military service** near **Ascoli Piceno** in central Italy; his uncle Roberto, writing from Cape Town, noted that it was "molto buono" they hadn't sent him to Sicily and urged him to think about getting married. The family photographs from 1962 show him at the seaside — a stocky man in a rowboat at what appears to be the Tuscan coast, and with his wife and two small children on the beach. A photograph captioned "Zia Mimi" shows a woman holding a baby in the sand at the same beach outing — "Mimi" was apparently a family nickname for his wife or another aunt.

By **November 1971** Guido was living in Florence near his father. He added a note to one of Antonio's letters: **"Nicola"** had gone on a trip to **Venice** with **Sandra**, and **"Simone"** had stayed home "con un poco di invidia." These are his two sons — **Nicola Zerauschek** and **Simone Zerauschek** — confirming the "NZ" and "SZ" initials on the 1962 and 1992 photographs.

The 1962 photograph "NZ-Fiumetto-62" shows Nicola as a child on an inflatable ring at **Fiumetto** (near Forte dei Marmi, Tuscany). The 1992 photo "SZ-HPW-92" shows Simone, a young man in glasses, in the garden at **Hove Park Way** (the Lewis family home in Hove) — visiting his aunt Fulvia and uncle David.
