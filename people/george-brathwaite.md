# George Brathwaite

**George Brathwaite** (baptised 7 May 1637, Hawkshead – buried 4 March 1681, Swarthmoor) was a Quaker of the Swarthmoor Monthly Meeting, Lancashire. He married **Agnes Benson** and was father of [Ann Braithwaite](ann-braithwaite.md), who married [Joseph Ayrey](joseph-ayrey.md) into the Quaker Airey family of Kendal.

## Life

- **7 May 1637** — Baptised at **Hawkshead**, Lancashire, son of **William Braithwait**. Parish register (FamilySearch batch P00596-1). An earlier George, also son of William, was baptised 26 Apr 1635 at the same church — the reuse of the name indicates the first child died in infancy.
- **~1660s** — Settled at **Swarthmoor** (Ulverston), about 4 miles south of Hawkshead. Became a member of the Swarthmoor Monthly Meeting (Society of Friends).
- **21 Sep 1663** — Daughter [Ann](ann-braithwaite.md) born at Swarthmoor. Quaker birth register (TNA RG6/1084). Three years before the recorded Quaker marriage — George and Agnes may have had a civil or informal union first, or Ann was born before they joined the Society.
- **~1664** — George's sister ("second daughter of William Brathwaite") married **Barnard Benson son of Francis Benson** of Westmorland "in the house of George Brathwaite" (TNA RG6/1085, same page as George's own marriage). George was already established as a householder at Swarthmoor.
- **1 Jul 1666** — Married [Agnes Benson](agnes-benson.md), daughter of **Barnard Benson** of Longriggs, at **Swarthmoor** Monthly Meeting. Quaker marriage register (TNA RG6/1085, p. 29). The register reads: "George Brathwaite son of William Brathwaite of the parish of Hawkshead in the county of Lancaster and Agnes Benson daughter of Barnard Benson..."
- **2 Jul 1667** — Son **Edward** born at Swarthmoor. Quaker birth register (TNA RG6/1085). Father George, mother Agnes.
- **4 Jun 1669** — Daughter **Agnes** born at Swarthmoor. Quaker birth register (TNA RG6/1085). Father George, mother Agnes.
- **10 Feb 1671** — Daughter **Dorothy** born at Swarthmoor. Quaker birth register (TNA RG6/1085). Father George, mother Agnes.
- **4 Mar 1681** — Buried at **Swarthmoor**, Quaker burial ground. Quaker burial register (TNA RG6/1085, p. 49). The register reads: "George Brathwaite of [Colthouse?]... was buried the fourth day of the first month Anno Domini 1681." A different George Brathwaite (son of Rowland) was buried at the same meeting on 12 Mar 1687.
- **1684** — Son **Edward** buried at Swarthmoor, aged only ~17 (TNA RG6/1085). "Edward son of George Brathwaite... was buried the fourth day of the fourth month Anno Domini 1684."
- **5 Jul 1694** — Agnes died. Buried **7 Jul 1694** at Swarthmoor (TNA RG6/1616). The burial record names her spouse as George, confirming identification. She survived George by 13 years.

## Context

The Swarthmoor area was the heartland of early English Quakerism. **Swarthmoor Hall**, home of Judge Thomas Fell and his wife **Margaret Fell** (later Margaret Fox), served as George Fox's base from 1652. George Brathwaite's family would have been among the first generation of Quakers in the Furness district. Hawkshead, where George was baptised in the Anglican parish church, is about 4 miles north of Swarthmoor — a conversion from Anglicanism to the Society of Friends in the 1650s–60s was typical for this area.
