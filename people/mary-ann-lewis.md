# Mary Lewis (b. 1886)

**Mary Lewis** — identified as **Miss M. A. Lewis** in her own postcard portrait — was the eldest recorded child of [David John Lewis (1857)](david-john-lewis-1857.md) and [Catherine Griffiths](catherine-griffiths.md), born in **1886** in **Aberdare**. She was a year older than her brother [Samuel](samuel-lewis.md), who arrived in June 1887.

She grew up in the household at **41 Glen Road, Aberdare**, the same address that appears on the **1911** census return where her mother's fertility line — twelve births, seven deaths, five surviving — recorded the weight of the coalfield on one family. Mary was among the survivors.

The middle initial **A** on her Edwardian studio postcard (c. 1910) is the only trace of a second name — possibly **Ann** or **Anne** — and the "Miss" confirms she was unmarried at the time of sitting. Samuel's 1967 obituary names nephews and nieces with surnames **Hennessey**, **Evans**, and **Southey**: one of these married names likely belongs to Mary.
