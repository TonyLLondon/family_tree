# Thomas Cushen (c. 1821–1889)

Thomas Cushen was born about **1821** in **Cork**, Ireland. He emigrated to south Wales with his older brother **Patrick Cushen** (b. ~1811, Cork), arriving at **Trevethin** near **Pontypool** by about 1839. The [1841 census](../media/docs/1841-census-cushen-household-trevethin-pontypool-ho107-744.jpg) found them together in a cluster of Irish families — Patrick with his wife Helen and two young children, Thomas listed as 15 (the 1841 census rounded ages). Patrick's eldest son Michael had been born in Cork ~1838; the family's first Welsh-born child arrived ~1840, dating their crossing.

By 1847 Thomas had married **[Anne Mainwaring](ann-mainwaring.md)**, a Welsh woman from **Llansamlet** near Swansea. Their first child Mary was born at **Abersychan** (near Trevethin), showing Thomas was still in the Pontypool area. But by 1851 he had moved thirty miles west to **Dowlais**, the great ironworks town on the northern rim of Merthyr Tydfil. The [1851 census](../media/docs/1851-census-thomas-cushin-household-dowlais-ho107-2459.png) records him as a **filler** — the man who charged blast furnaces with ore and coke — with three Cork lodgers sharing his house. Dowlais was then the largest ironworks in the world, drawing labour from across Ireland and rural Wales.

Thomas spent the rest of his life at Dowlais. The [1861 census](../media/docs/1861-census-thomas-cashin-household-dowlais-rg9-4056.jpg) shows him as a **fireman** (tending the furnace fire) with five sons including **[David](david-cushen.md)** (5). By [1871](../media/docs/1871-census-thomas-cushing-household-dowlais-rg10-5397.jpg) he was 50, still a blast furnace filler, with six sons — three of the eldest already working as labourers. Anne died between 1871 and 1881; Thomas appears on the 1881 census as a **widower** at 60, living with his eldest son Thomas Jr and described as born **Cork, Cork, Ireland**.

He died in **Q3 1889** at Merthyr Tydfil, age about 70, and was buried at **Dowlais** — nearly fifty years after he left Cork.

## The surname

The name was transcribed differently at every census:

| Year | Spelling |
|------|----------|
| 1841 | Cushan |
| 1851 | Cushin |
| 1861 | Cashin |
| 1871 | Cushing |
| 1881 | Cushin |
| Death 1889 | Cushen |

All are phonetic renderings of the Irish surname **Coisín** / **Cuisín** (anglicised Cushen, Cushion, Cashin, Cushing). The GRO death record uses **Cushen**, which became the family's settled spelling by David's generation.

## Open questions

- **Cork parish** — which parish in Cork? Both Thomas and Patrick are from Cork. The 1881 census specifies "Cork, Cork" (city of Cork, County Cork). Pre-1864 Irish civil records don't exist; a Catholic parish baptism register might anchor the ~1821 birth.
- **Ann Mainwaring's death** — GRO death index confirms Jul 1875, Vol 11A p. 227. Buried Dowlais.
- **Patrick Cushen's death** — after 1881. GRO search for Patrick Cushen/Cushion, Merthyr, 1882–1900.
