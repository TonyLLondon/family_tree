# Lettuce (Lettice)

**Lettuce** — a Welsh form of the name **Lettice** (from Latin *Laetitia*) — is known from two baptism records at **Llywel** parish church, Breconshire. She and her husband [Morgan Morgans](morgan-morgans-llywel.md) had at least two sons:

- [Thomas Morgan](thomas-morgan-nantyglo.md), baptised **15 February 1794** — father "Morgan Morgans", mother "Lettice".
- [William Morgan](william-morgan-llanfairarybryn.md), baptised **15 September 1795** — father "Morgan Morgans", mother "Lettuce".

She does not appear on any census record (the earliest, 1841, was 47 years after Thomas's baptism). By 1841, William's "Mother" in the household at Coedefaur was **Anne Morgan** (b. ~1767, Llanfairdydogan, Carmarthenshire, widow). Anne's relationship to the family is uncertain: she may have been Morgan Morgans' second wife (a stepmother to both boys), or she may have been Lettice's own mother (William's maternal grandmother) — the census term "Mother" was sometimes applied loosely to elderly female relatives. No marriage record for Morgan Morgans and Anne Morgan has been found, and the age gap (Anne was 9 years older than Morgan Morgans) is unusual for a second marriage.

There is no evidence that Lettice died young. She was alive at least through September 1795 (William's baptism) and may have lived for many years afterwards — her absence from the 1841 census only proves she was dead or elsewhere by that date, when she would have been in her sixties or seventies.

Her maiden name is unknown. No marriage record for Morgan Morgans × Lettice has been found.
