# Thomas Morgan

**Thomas Morgan** was baptised **15 February 1794** at **Llywel** parish church, Brecknockshire, the son of [Morgan Morgans](morgan-morgans-llywel.md) and [Lettuce](lettuce-llywel.md) (Lettice). He was the elder brother of [William Morgan](william-morgan-llanfairarybryn.md) (bapt. 15 Sep 1795, same parents, same parish), making two distinct branches of the tree — the Nantyglo Morgans and the Llanfairarybryn Morgans — siblings from the same Llywel family.

Thomas migrated roughly thirty miles south-east to the booming iron and coal district around **Nantyglo**, in the parish of Aberystruth, western Monmouthshire.

On **5 July 1823** he [married](../sources/corpus/1823-marriage-thomas-morgan-charlotte-davies-aberystruth/transcription.md) **[Charlotte Davies](charlotte-davies-aberystruth.md)** at Aberystruth parish church; both were described as "of Aberystruth." Their son [Keziah](keziah-morgan.md) was born **7 March 1824** at Nantyglo and [baptised](../media/docs/1824-baptism-keziah-morgan-aberystruth-monmouthshire.jpg) there on 4 July 1824. Thomas's occupation at the baptism was **Miner**. Charlotte appears to have died shortly after — she is absent from all later records and Thomas had remarried by 1826.

By the 1841 census Thomas was head of household at **Nantyglo** with his second wife **Mary** (b. ~1803, Glamorganshire) and seven children: Ann (~1826), David (~1827), Margaret (~1830), Elizabeth (~1832), Phoebe (~1834), William (~1839), and Jane (~1839, twins with William). Keziah (then 17) had already left home.

In 1851 the family was at **Ivy Cottages**, Aberystruth. Thomas, now 52, was a **Moulder** (iron moulder) — he had moved from mining to the foundry. By 1861 he was 63, a **Labourer**, at **Club Houses, Henwain Row**, Aberystruth.

Thomas was buried on **26 December 1862** at Aberystruth, age 64.

## Open questions

- Birthplace on censuses: 1851 says "Lampeter" and 1861 says "Llanworted" (Llanwrtyd) — both in Brecknockshire but different places (~15 miles apart). The baptism confirms Llywel parish, which borders both areas. "Lampeter" likely refers to Lampeter-Pont-Steffan as misheard by the enumerator.
- Charlotte's death — no record found. She likely died ~1824–1825 and is buried at Aberystruth.
- Burial age (64 in Dec 1862) implies birth ~1798, four years later than the 1794 baptism — a common discrepancy with estimated ages at burial.
