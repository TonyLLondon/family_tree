# Vincenzo Giovanni Domenico Valentino Addobbati

Father of [Pietro Paolo Addobbati](pietro-paolo-addobbati.md) and **Anna** with [(Domenica) Vincenza Coltelli](domenica-vincenza-coltelli.md) in **F14** (marriage **25 Feb 1811**, **Zara**). Long **quadruple** given-name stack matches the GEDCOM **NAME** line and **FamilySearch** id **GW1S-CGK**. Styled **Ivan Vicko** in Croatian sources — identified by Granić as one of the two sons of **Lujo (Luigi) Addobbati** formally inscribed as nobles of Nin on **24 June 1804**.

## Life

- **24 Mar 1781** — Born **Zara, Dalmatia**. The 1811 marriage register records him as **"d'anni 30 c.a"** (approximately 30), consistent with 1781 but not with the earlier Sabalich claim of 1776. The DAZD analytical inventory (HR-DAZD-342) independently cites **24 Mar 1781** from the Archbishop's baptism book 1778–1791.
- **24 Jun 1804** — Formally inscribed as noble of **Nin** (*Ivan Vicko Addobbati*) alongside brother **Dr. Petar**, sons of Lujo Addobbati. The Nin municipal confirmation is preserved in the Addobbati fonds as **sig. 19** (1 Jul 1804).
- **25 Feb 1811** — Marriage **Zara** to Vincenza Coltelli (**F14**), at 6 PM in the bride's house, officiated by D. Antonio Can.co Grazovich (Curato of the Metropolitana), with dispensation from banns. Described as **Possidente** (landowner). Witnesses: five civic officials including Nicolò Sandri (Justice of the Peace), Francesco Medin (Capo de Dalmati ex Veneti), and Alessandro Sanfici (Public Notary).
- **28 Jan 1839** — Died **Zara, Dalmatia**. His son Pietro's 1845 marriage register posthumously lists Vincenzo's occupation as **"C. R. Impiegato"** (Imperial-Royal Employee), whereas the 1811 register described him as **Possidente** (landowner) — he transitioned from landowning to Austrian government service.

## Family

- Wife: [(Domenica) Vincenza Coltelli](domenica-vincenza-coltelli.md).
- Children: [Pietro Paolo Addobbati](pietro-paolo-addobbati.md) and **Anna** (per DAZD inventory §2.2) per **F14**.
- Parents: [Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati](luigi-anzolo-alloisio-addobbati.md); [Maddelena Plarino](maddelena-plarino.md) (**F15**). Both parents described as **"fù"** (deceased) in the 1811 marriage register.
- Brother: **Dr. Petar Addobbati** (not in GEDCOM) — also admitted Nin 1804; Knight of the Golden Spur 1786.
