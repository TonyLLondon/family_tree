# Vincenzo Giovanni Domenico Valentino Addobbati

Vincenzo Giovanni Domenico Valentino Addobbati bore one of those baroque name-stacks that clerks copied faithfully and descendants shorten to a whisper: in Croatian noble lists he appears as **Ivan Vicko**, son of Lujo (Luigi), one of two brothers admitted to the council of Nin in 1804. He was born on 24 March 1781 in Zara—an exact date the archbishop’s baptism book and the state archives inventory agree on, even when an older guide tried to nudge him five years earlier.

As a young *possidente* he married Vincenza Coltelli one winter evening in 1811, in her house, with a curate’s dispensation from the banns and five solid citizens as witnesses, from a justice of the peace to a public notary. By then his parents were both *fù*—gone—but their names still anchored the line: Luigi Anzolo Alloisio and Maddalena Plarinò. Pietro Paolo was the son who lived to carry the accounting ink into the next generation; a daughter Anna sits in the archival inventory waiting for her own record to surface.

He died in Zara on 28 January 1839. The 1845 marriage of his posthumous “voice” in the register calls him an imperial-royal employee where the 1811 act had called him a landowner—small semantic drift for a life lived across the hinge of Napoleonic and Habsburg Dalmatia. His brother **Dr. Petar** shared the Nin diploma and the Golden Spur story; he does not appear in the same tree import as Vincenzo but belongs to the same breakfast table in the sources.

## Life

- **24 Mar 1781** — Born Zara (1811 marriage age “d'anni 30 c.a.”; DAZD inventory cites Archbishop’s baptism book 1778–1791).
- **24 Jun 1804** — Inscribed noble of Nin (*Ivan Vicko Addobbati*) with brother Dr. Petar; municipal confirmation in fonds sig. 19 (1 Jul 1804).
- **25 Feb 1811** — Marriage Zara to Vincenza Coltelli, 6 PM, bride’s house; officiant D. Antonio Can.co Grazovich; *Possidente*; witnesses include Nicolò Sandri (Justice of the Peace), Francesco Medin, Alessandro Sanfici (notary).
- **28 Jan 1839** — Died Zara. Son Pietro’s 1845 marriage lists his occupation as *C. R. Impiegato*; the 1811 act had *Possidente*.
