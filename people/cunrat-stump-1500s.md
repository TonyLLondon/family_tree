# Cunrat Stump (c. 1500-1510)

> **⚠️ NOT A PROVEN DIRECT ANCESTOR.** Cunrat Stump is documented in Swiss records but there is **no proven link** connecting him to [Hanssen Stump](hanssen-stump-1580.md) (c. 1580, Sulgen), our earliest confirmed direct ancestor. The connection is hypothetical, based on the shared STUMP surname and the suggestive **Zällerin** surname overlap. See [Research Notes](#research-notes-2026-04-11) below.

**Cunrat Stump** (born c. 1500-1510) is the **earliest documented STUMP surname-bearer** with confirmed family records. He lived in the **Zürich region** of Switzerland, married **Margreth Zällerin**, and was the father of **Elsbeth Stump** (baptized 1534). He died in 1560. While he is *not* a proven direct ancestor, the **Zällerin surname** shared with later Thurgau STUMP families makes him a strong candidate for the extended lineage.

## The Gap: Why This Link Is Unproven

**Our proven direct lineage** ends with **Hanssen Stump** (c. 1580, Sulgen, Thurgau) — named as Jacob Stump's father in Jacob's 1606 baptism record. To connect Cunrat to the direct line we would need:

1. **Hanssen's baptism record** naming his father — not yet found
2. **A son of Cunrat** who matches a father/grandfather of Hanssen — not yet found  
3. **Migration evidence** linking Zürich STUMP families to Thurgau — circumstantial only

**The gap:** ~80 years and two different cantons (Zürich → Thurgau) with no connecting records.
