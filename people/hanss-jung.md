# Hans Jung (c. 1640s)

**Hans Jung** was a Swiss man from **Sulgen**, **Thurgau** in the mid-17th century. He married [Anna Marstetter](anna-marstetter.md) and became the father of [Johannes Jung](johannes-jung-1667.md) (baptized 1667), establishing the Jung lineage that would span multiple generations in Sulgen and eventually connect to the STUMP family genealogy.

## Life

**19 May 1631** — Christened **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland** (recorded as "Johanns Jung"). Son of [Barttli Jung](barttli-jung.md) and [Barbel Schmidhusser](barbel-schmidhusser.md).

**c. 1665** — Marriage to [Anna Marstetter](anna-marstetter.md).

**11 Aug 1667** — Birth of son [Johannes Jung](johannes-jung-1667.md) in Sulgen.

**c. 1690** — Death (estimated, as son Johannes appears as head of household in later records).

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Jung Family Origins:**
Hans Jung represents the earliest documented Jung ancestor in Sulgen, establishing the family presence in this Thurgau parish by at least the 1640s. This places the Jung family settlement during the aftermath of the Thirty Years' War period.

**Swiss Rural Community:**
His marriage to Anna Marstetter suggests integration with other established local families. The Marstetter surname appears in contemporary Thurgau records, indicating marriage within the established parish community.

**Genealogical Foundation:**
Hans Jung serves as the foundational Generation 11 ancestor for a lineage that remained in Sulgen for over 150 years, demonstrating remarkable family stability in one Swiss location through multiple social and economic changes.

**17th Century Context:**
Living during the mid-1600s, Hans Jung would have experienced the transition from the post-Reformation period into the early modern era in Switzerland, including economic recovery and population growth in rural communities.
