# Clara Webb

**Clara Webb** was born about **1851** in **St Paul's Cray**, Kent (civil birth: Q2 1851, Bromley, Vol 5, p. 64), daughter of **[Richard Webb](richard-webb-i652.md)**, an agricultural labourer from Eynsford, and **[Charlotte Willoughby](charlotte-willoughby-i653.md)** of St Paul's Cray. Her parents married at St Paul's Cray on 7 June 1851 — Clara may have arrived just before the wedding. Later censuses give her birthplace as "Chelsfield," the next parish south of St Paul's Cray; the 1861 census (the earliest record listing her) says St Paul's Cray. She became housekeeper to the widowed chimney sweep **[Peter Martin](peter-martin-i596.md)** at Alpine Cottage, Frog Lane, Orpington. In the 1881 census she is listed as **unmarried** with surname **Webb**, and her three children with Peter — Arthur, William, and Albert — also carry the Webb surname. By 1891 she is listed as Peter's wife and all children have been re-surnamed **Martin**.

Her youngest child **[Rosina](rosina-a-martin-i104.md)** (b. 1891) married [Archibald Percy Coolbear](archibald-percy-coolbear.md), a chimney sweep like her father.

## Open questions

- **1901 Census:** Missing along with the rest of the Martin family. May have remarried after Peter's death, or be listed under Webb again.
- **Birthplace:** "St Paul's Cray" (1861 census, the earliest record; also "St Pancras Cray" — same place) vs "Chelsfield" (1881 census). The parishes are ~4 miles apart, both in the Bromley registration district. The 1861 record is closer to the event and more reliable; the family may have moved to Chelsfield by the time Clara was old enough to remember, or the later enumerator simply recorded the wrong adjacent parish.
