# Edward Burgess

British merchant, translator, and official who spent his adult life in Qajar Persia and married into the Armenian Saginian military-diplomatic circle. He anchors the **Burgess → Fanny → Bottin → Stump** line that later ties Tehran to the European engineer and dentist generations.

## Life

- **1831** — Arrived in Persia and remained there (NYPL Burgess papers narrative).
- **By 1844** — In **Tabriz**: translator of English newspapers for **Prince Bahman Mirza**; later in royal and intellectual circles under Qajar princes.
- **Detained in Persia** — His brother (name unknown) was sent by the Persian government to England with several thousand tomans to purchase merchandise, and Edward stood as **guarantor for his brother's honour**. The brother defaulted and abandoned Edward to the consequences. Edward was therefore unable to leave Persia; he wrote four times to Colonel Sheil (British minister) without receiving an answer. Dr. Joseph Wolff, who met him in Tabriz in January 1844, described him as "an interesting and unfortunate young gentleman… well acquainted with the Persian language" and noted he was sustained by the British consul-general Bonham, the Russian consul-general, and Greek merchants. Source: *Narrative of a Mission to Bokhara* (1845), Ch. XXVI — now in corpus.
- **1851** — Married [Anna Saginian](anna-saginian.md); marriage year from consolidated narrative and NYPL-linked research.
- **1855** — Died en route between **Tehran** and **Tabriz**, leaving infant [Fanny Burgess](fanny-burgess.md).
