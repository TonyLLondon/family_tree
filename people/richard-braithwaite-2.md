# Richard Braithwaite (of Hurworth)

**Richard Brathwait** (1588–1673) — English **poet**, author of *Drunken Barnaby's Four Journeys* (1638), *The English Gentleman* (1630), *The English Gentlewoman* (1631), and more than twenty other works. His 1613 book *The Yong Mans Gleanings* contains the **first known use of the word "computer"** (meaning an arithmetician). He has entries in the DNB, ODNB, and *Encyclopædia Britannica*.

Born at **Burneside** (Burneshead), near Kendal, Westmorland, he was the **second surviving son** of [Thomas Braithwait](thomas-braithwait-of-worcop.md) and [Dorothy Bindlose](dorothy-bindlose.md). He entered **Oriel College, Oxford** in 1604 aged 16, later studied law at Cambridge (under Lancelot Andrewes at Pembroke) and at the Inns of Court in London. After his father's death in 1610 he inherited the family estates and returned to Westmorland. His elder brother, **Sir Thomas Brathwaite**, died in 1618, making Richard head of the family. He served as **deputy-lieutenant of Westmorland**, **justice of the peace**, and captain of a company of foot in the trained bands. He was **lord of the manor of Catterick** and is believed to have served with the Royalist army in the Civil War.

## Life

- **~1588** — Born at **Burneside**, parish of Kendal, Westmorland. Date from portrait inscription "An° 1626, Æt. 38" and Anthony à Wood's statement that he entered Oxford "A.D. 1604, aged 16."
- **1604** — Entered **Oriel College, Oxford** as a commoner, matriculated as "a gentleman's son."
- **1610** — Father [Thomas](thomas-braithwait-of-worcop.md) died; Richard inherited estates in Westmorland.
- **1611** — Published first work, *The Golden Fleece*, dedicated to his uncle Robert Bindlosse and elder brother Sir Thomas Brathwaite.
- **4 May 1617** — Married [Frances Lawson](lady-frances-lawson.md), daughter of [James Lawson of Nesham](james-lawson-of-nesham-abbey.md), at **Hurworth**, near Darlington. She bore him **nine children, five of them sons**.
- **1618** — Elder brother Sir Thomas Brathwaite died; Richard became head of the family.
- **1630–31** — Published *The English Gentleman* and *The English Gentlewoman*, his best-known conduct books.
- **7 Mar 1633** — Wife [Frances](lady-frances-lawson.md) died. His poem *Anniversaries upon his Panarete* (1634) memorialises her.
- **27 Jun 1639** — Second marriage to a widow, daughter of **Roger Crofts of Kirtlington**, Yorkshire. Their son, **Sir Strafford Brathwaite**, was killed in a sea-fight with Algerine pirates.
- **1638** — Published *Barnabæ Itinerarium* (*Drunken Barnaby's Four Journeys*) under the pseudonym "Corymbæus." Southey pronounced the Latin "the best piece of rhymed Latin in modern literature."
- **12 Apr 1673** — Still active as a trustee of the Catterick free school.
- **4 May 1673** — Died at **East Appleton**, near Catterick, aged 85. Buried three days later on the **north side of the chancel**, **Catterick parish church**.

## Open questions

- **Christening 29 Jan 1591 at Strickland Ketel** in the export — this conflicts with a 1588 birth from the portrait and Wood. Strickland Ketel is adjacent to Burneside; the register image would resolve whether this is an older sibling or a registration anomaly.
- The "Lady" prefix for Frances Lawson — the DNB simply says "Frances, daughter of James Lawson of Nesham." The courtesy title may be legitimate (the Lawsons held Nesham Abbey) or a later Geni addition.
