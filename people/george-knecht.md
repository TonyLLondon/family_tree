# George Knecht

**George Knecht** was a German immigrant who settled in London and raised a family in Islington. Born about **1829** in **Germany**, he married **[Eliza Sarah Glasscock](eliza-knecht.md)** in **Q3 1856** (July–September) at **Islington** (GRO marriage index: Vol 1B, p. 267).

The **1871 Census** (RG10/263, schedule 316) records him as head of household at **Pembroke Street, Upper**, parish of St Michael Islington, age 42, occupation listed as a worker **at gas works**.

The elder children — Francis Henry (born 1857, St Giles) and Elizabeth (born 1859, Bloomsbury) — place the family in the **St Giles / Bloomsbury** area of central London before 1860, part of the German quarter around Fitzrovia and Tottenham Court Road where mid-Victorian German migrants clustered. By 1861 the family had moved north to Islington, where all subsequent children were born.

His daughter [Dorothea](dorothea-knecht.md) married [William Evans](william-evans-i289.md) in 1878 and became the mother of [Alfred Evans](alfred-evans-i101.md).

## Family

Wife: [Eliza Sarah Glasscock](eliza-knecht.md) (born ~1833, Ireland; m. Q3 1856, Islington). Children (from 1871 census): Francis Henry (~1857), Elizabeth (~1859), [Dorothea](dorothea-knecht.md) (~1861), George William (~1864), Hannah (~1866), Louisa Sarah (~1870).

The **1881 Census** finds the family in **Paddington** (Mary Magdalene parish), where George is recorded as a **Cabinet Maker**, age 45, born **Germany, Hesse Nassau** — the first record to specify a German state. Only Eliza and son George V. remain in the household; Dorothea had married William Evans in 1878. By the 1901 Census the family had returned to Islington, back on Pembroke Street.
