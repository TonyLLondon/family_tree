# Barbara Muntz (c. 1675)

**Barbara Muntz** was a Swiss woman from **Sulgen**, **Thurgau** in the late 17th century. She married [Johannes Jung](johannes-jung-1667.md) and became the mother of [Hans Ulrich Jung](hanss-ulrich-jung.md), establishing a crucial generational link in the Jung lineage that would eventually connect to the STUMP family genealogy.

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Muntz Family Background:**
The Muntz (also spelled Munzen) surname appears in multiple Swiss records of the late 17th century in Thurgau canton. Barbara's father Jacob Muntz was established in the Sulgen community, as evidenced by marriage record documentation.

**Generational Bridge:**
Barbara Muntz serves as a crucial generational bridge, connecting the mid-17th century Jung ancestry (through her husband) with the early 18th century Jung descendants who would eventually marry into the STUMP lineage.

**Swiss Marriage Patterns:**
Her marriage to Johannes Jung demonstrates typical Swiss rural marriage patterns: unions between established local families within the same parish, creating multi-generational community stability.

**Maternal Heritage:**
Through Barbara Muntz, the Jung and eventual STUMP descendants carry Swiss Muntz ancestry from the late 1600s, adding another layer of established Thurgau heritage to the family genealogy.
