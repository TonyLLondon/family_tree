## Roche of Limerick ancestry (Burke’s 1833)

Burke’s *History of Commoners* (1833) documents the **Roche of Limerick** Catholic merchant-gentry line. Catherine Mary Roche’s parents fit exactly:

| Generation | Person |
|---|---|
| c. 1688 | John Roche (b. 1688) m. Anne Stacpole — Limerick/Rotterdam trading empire |
| b. 1724 | Stephen Roche m. Margaret Meade |
| fl. early 19th c. | Thomas Roche of Limerick m. Hellen Ankettle |
| fl. 1820s–30s | **William Roche**, solicitor, Dublin m. **Eliza Knight** |
| 1841 | **Catherine Mary Roche** |

The family descends from Castletown-Roche (Cork); forfeited under William III; relocated to Clare and Limerick. Seat: **Granagh Castle, Kilkenny**. Arms: *Gules, three roaches naiant in pale.*

The legal profession ran in the line: her father William was a Dublin solicitor; her son [Gerald Sebastian White](gerald-sebastian-white.md) became a solicitor and Registrar of the High Court of Madras.
