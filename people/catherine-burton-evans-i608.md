# Catherine Burton (Evans)

**Catherine Burton** was born about **1824** in **Clapton**, a hamlet straddling the boundary of Hackney parish and the Middlesex/Essex border. Census enumerators recorded her birthplace variously as "Clapton, Middlesex" (1851), "Hackney, Middlesex" (1861), and "Clapton, Essex" (1871) — all referring to the same place. She married [Alfred Evans](alfred-evans-i434.md) (b. ~1821, Shoreditch, house painter) before 1849 — their eldest known child, Jessie, was born that year. Her maiden name is confirmed as **Burton** by the GRO birth registration of her son [William Evans](william-evans-i289.md) (Q1 1857, Islington, mother's maiden name Burton).

In 1851 she was described as a **Needle Woman** and in 1861 as doing **Needle Work** — domestic sewing or piecework, a common occupation for wives in artisan households. She raised at least eight children across three decades in the Islington East sub-district, from Windsor Street (1851) to Paradise Place (1871–1881).
