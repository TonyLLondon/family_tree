# John Lysaght Roche

John Roche, known as **John Lysaght Roche** from his mother's family, was a **Limerick merchant prince** — the man who transformed a family dispossessed under William III into one of the wealthiest Catholic trading dynasties in Munster. He married [Anna Stackpole](anna-stackpole.md) of Mountcashell, County Clare, and was father of [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md).

In **October 1758** he fitted out the **Catherine**, a letter of marque "mounting fourteen, six and four pounders," which "sailed for Barbadoes" — Ferrar records she was **"the first ship of force that belonged to Limerick."** The Limerick Chronicle called him a "merchant prince." He died on 18 January 1760 and was buried at **St Mary's**, County Limerick.

## Life

- **1688** — Born in the **Limerick / Rathkeale** area of County Limerick. His father, [Stephen O'Moore Roche](stephen-omoore-roche.md) — known as **"Dov"** or **"Black"** from his complexion — had been forfeited under William III and forced to leave County Cork, retiring first to **Kilrush, Clare**, then to **Pallas, County Limerick**, near his brother-in-law William Apjohn.
- **~1710s** — Married **[Anna Stackpole](anna-stackpole.md)**, youngest daughter of **Philip Stacpole, Esq., of Mountcashell, Kilneen, and Kilcoman** in County Clare. Anna's mother was **Christian Creagh**, daughter of **John Creagh of Ballyvolane**, a colonel in the Irish army (1642). Through Anna, the Mountcashell estate eventually passed to the Roche family — Burke's 1833 notes the fee was held by grandson [Thomas Roche](thomas-roche.md).
- **By 1740s** — Established as a major Limerick merchant, trading provisions and goods with the continent and the West Indies. Operated **oil mills near Sixmilebridge, Co. Clare** (advertised for lease in the *Dublin Journal*, Apr–May 1758).
- **October 1758** — Fitted out the **Catherine**, a letter of marque mounting fourteen, six, and four pounders, which sailed for Barbados. Ferrar (p. 128) records she was "the first ship of force that belonged to Limerick." The Limerick Chronicle (2018) describes her as "a huge ship, the largest of its kind ever seen in Limerick" with "14 sixteen-pounder guns." A major capital venture during the Seven Years' War, demonstrating the scale of the Roche trading operation in an era when Catholic merchants were still constrained by penal law restrictions.
- **18 Jan 1760** — Died **Limerick**. Buried at **St Mary's, County Limerick**. Probate the same year. Succeeded by his son [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md).

## Children

Burke's 1833 and the Limerick Chronicle (2018) together give the following children. The sons "all became independently wealthy in their own right":

| Child | Dates | Career & family |
|-------|-------|-----------------|
| **Philip Roche** | ~1740–1797 | Stayed in Limerick; m. Margaret Kelly (dau. of John Kelly, merchant). Built the **Granary** on Michael Street (1787), still standing today. "One of the most successful businessmen in southern Ireland." Took a 999-year lease from Edmond Sexton Pery in 1779. In 1792, two ships he sent to the West Indies under the Dutch flag were seized by the British — goods valued at £15,000. Died at his seat at Newcastle, 7 Jun 1797. Buried in the family vault at **Drumbanny Churchyard**. |
| **John Roche** | fl. 1750s | m. Miss Harold, cousin of General Harold of the Saxon service. Had a daughter Mary-Anne, who married John Meade, Esq. of Limerick — mother of **Captain Roche Meade** of the 21st Regiment, deputy-adjutant-general. John continued the family business in **Dublin**. |
| **[Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md)** | 1724–1804 | **His heir.** Succeeded his father 1760. Three marriages. "The oldest merchant in that city" at death. **Direct ancestor.** |
| **Thomas Roche** | fl. 1750s; d. before 1797 | Went to **Rotterdam** to trade. Died before brother Philip, who provided in his will to repay Thomas's debts. |
| **Jane Roche** | fl. 1750s | m. John Sheehy, Esq. of Cork. Had a daughter who m. Bryan Keating, Esq. — mother of **General Keating**. |
| **Christiana Roche** | fl. 1750s | m. James Lombard, Esq. of County Cork. Had several daughters: the youngest married **Daniel O'Connell, Esq., of Ivragh, Co. Kerry** (of the same family as Daniel O'Connell "The Liberator") — mother of **Charles O'Connell, M.P.** for Kerry. The eldest m. Daniel Cronin, Esq. of the Park, Co. Kerry. |
| **Anastasia Roche** | fl. 1750s | m. **Ignatius Nihell**, a Limerick merchant who "lost his fortune and ended up in debtor's prison in England in 1769." |

The O'Connell connection through Christiana explains the close friendship between William Roche M.P. and Daniel O'Connell "The Liberator" — they were kin through the Lombard marriage.

## Family

- Father: [Stephen O'Moore Roche](stephen-omoore-roche.md) — known as "Dov" (Black); forfeited under William III; m. [Anastasia Lysagt Burke](anastasia-lysagt-burke.md), elder daughter and co-heir of Thomas Lysaght, Esq.
- Grandfather: [Robert Roche](robert-roche.md) (1631–1690), of Castletown-Roche, Cork; m. [Juliana O'Moore](juliana-omoore.md).
- Great-grandfather: [John Roche](john-roche.md) of Castletown-Roche — member of the **Catholic Parliament** held at Kilkenny during the civil wars (1641); descended from the **Viscounts Fermoy**.

### Heraldry (Burke's 1833)

- **Arms:** *Gules, three roaches naiant in pale* — three roach fish swimming vertically on a red field.
- **Crest:** A rock proper, thereon a fish eagle with wings displayed, argent, membered or, in the claw a roach.
- **Motto:** *Mon Dieu est ma roche* — "My God is my rock."
- **Estates:** Counties Kilkenny, Limerick, and Clare.
- **Seat:** Granagh Castle, Kilkennyshire (held by grandson George Roche, 1833).
