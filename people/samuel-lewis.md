# Samuel Lewis

Father of [David John Lewis](david-john-lewis.md) and Brynmor Keith Lewis. Coal miner turned insurance man; chapel deacon and Oddfellow. The longest-held address was **32 Penydarren Street, Aberdare** (almost 40 years, c. 1927–1966).

**Line narrative:** [Lewis in Aberdare and Merthyr — coalfield labour and child mortality](../narratives/lewis-aberdare-merthyr-coalfield.md) — 1911 fertility return (mother's twelve births, seven deaths), underground mining at ages 13–23, out of the pit by 1939.

## Life

- **23 Jun 1887** — Born **Aberdare, Merthyr Tydfil, Glamorganshire, Wales**, son of [David John Lewis (1857)](david-john-lewis-1857.md) and [Catherine Griffiths](catherine-griffiths.md).
- **1901** (age 13) — Already working as a **coal miner** below ground, enumerated in his father's Aberdare household (RG13/5035).
- **1911** (age 23) — Still mining below ground, **41 Glen Road, Aberdare** (RG14/32495, schedule 77).
- **1917** — Marriage to [Elizabeth Lilian Cushen](elizabeth-lilian-cushen.md), **Merthyr Tydfil** (**F7**).
- **c. 1922** — Became **Sunday school superintendent** at Carmel Calvinistic Methodist Church, Trecynon (held for 10 years).
- **c. 1927** — Settled at **32 Pendarren Street, Aberdare** (the family home for almost 40 years).
- **1932** — Made **deacon (Blaenor)** at Carmel; served until death (35 years).
- **1939 Register** — Listed as **assurance agent**, Aberdare/Merthyr (RG101/7310F/013/9).
- Employed by the **Scottish Legal Life Assurance Society** as **district manager** until retirement in **1954**.
- Also a **church secretary** at Carmel for approximately 30 years and **corresponding secretary (Ysgrifennydd Gohebol)**.
- Active in the **Independent Order of Oddfellows, Lily of the Valley Lodge** — committeeman for seven years and held some of the Order's highest offices.
- **c. early 1966** — Moved with Elizabeth to **Maesyffynnon**, a care home in Aberaman.
- **7 Aug 1967** — Died at **Maesyffynnon, Aberaman**, aged 80. Funeral cremation **Friday 11 August**, 11 a.m. at **Glyntaff Crematorium**. Officiated by the **Rev. Glyndwr Richards, M.A., B.D.**, former pastor of Carmel. No flowers; donations to the Blind Institute. Attended by Dr. A. Richards.

The GEDCOM death date of 8 Jul 1967 is a month/day transposition: the newspaper death notice confirms **7 August 1967**, and the funeral on **Friday 11 August** is consistent (11 Aug 1967 was a Friday).

At the Sunday evening service at Carmel following the funeral, **Mr. E. R. Walters** paid tribute to Samuel's "tremendous sincerity and loyalty and his unfailing willingness to serve God and his church."
