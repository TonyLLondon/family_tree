# David John Lewis (1857)

He is **not** the major who crossed the **Po** in **1945**—that is his grandson [David John Lewis](david-john-lewis.md), born **1918**. **This** David is the **Aberdare** miner whose name repeats down the census like a drum: **Gelligaer** boy, **Glen Road** head of house, **coal hewer** underground while [Catherine Griffiths](catherine-griffiths.md) kept a **tin-worker**’s wage in the register’s memory of **1881**. Baptised **the day he was born**—**11 August 1857** at **Aberafon**—he married Catherine in **Merthyr** about **February 1884** and fathered the crowded brood whose **1911** return still shocks: twelve births, seven deaths, Samuel among the survivors who would descend into the earth at thirteen.

Military papers tag a **South Wales Borderer** stint in **1884**—service number in the export, full **WO** bundle not yet in this repo. He died in **May 1925** in **Merthyr** registration district, still administratively bound to the coalfield that had shaped every line of his life.
