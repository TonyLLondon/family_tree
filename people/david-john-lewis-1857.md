# David John Lewis (1857–1925)

Baptised the day he was born — **11 August 1857** at **Aberavon**, Glamorganshire — David John Lewis grew up not in the coalfield but on the coast, in the port town near Briton Ferry where his father [Lewis Lewis](lewis-lewis.md) worked as a **porter**. The [1871 census](../sources/1871-census-aberavon-lewis-lewis.md) catches him at thirteen, still a scholar, the eldest of nine children in a crowded Aberavon household. Sometime in the following decade he migrated inland to the **Cynon Valley** — by the 1890s he was head of a crowded household on **Glen Road, Aberdare**, listed as **coal hewer underground**, while [Catherine Griffiths](catherine-griffiths.md) — whom he married in **Merthyr** about **February 1884** — kept a tin-worker's wage in the register's memory of **1881**. On every subsequent census he gave his birthplace as "Aberdare" rather than "Aberavon" — the two names easily confused, both "Aber-" towns in the same county.

By the **1921** census, taken during the great coal lockout of that summer, David was a **widower** and a **surface labourer** at **Bwllfa Colliery** — aged out of underground work at 62, with Catherine gone sometime in the preceding decade. Samuel, Thomas John, and even the little grandson David John (age two years eight months) were all under the same roof at 41 Glan Road, and every miner in the house was out of work.

The **1911** census return still shocks: **twelve births, seven deaths**, five children surviving. Among them was [Samuel](samuel-lewis.md), who would descend into the earth as a miner at thirteen.

Military papers tag a **South Wales Borderer** stint beginning in **1884** — service number **1082** in the export, full **WO** bundle not yet in this repo. David died in **May 1925** in **Merthyr** registration district, administratively bound to the coalfield that had shaped every line of his life.

> *Not to be confused with his grandson [David John Lewis (1918–2000)](david-john-lewis.md), the major who crossed the Po in 1945.*
