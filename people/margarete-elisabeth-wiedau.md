# Margarete Elisabeth Wiedau

Margarete Elisabeth Wiedau married [Carl Gustav Eylandt](carl-gustav-eylandt.md) on **8 January 1768** in the Hageri parish, where both had been born — she in 1744, he the year before. Sharing a birthplace and marrying within the same parish was typical of the Baltic German estate communities in Harrien, where endogamy inside the local network of tenant farmers, pastors, and minor officials kept families tightly interlinked.

The couple had at least twelve children, born first in **Lihula (Leal)** from the late 1760s through the mid-1770s, then in **Kirbla Parish** from the 1780s onward — tracking their move to the Kirrefer pastorat area on western Estonia's coast. Their son [Johan Jacob Eylandt](johan-jacob-eylandt.md) was born in Lihula in 1774 and eventually settled in Tallinn as a master tailor.

Margarete died in **1819** at Kirrefer. Her husband Carl Gustav survived her by seven years, dying in Viljandi in 1826.
