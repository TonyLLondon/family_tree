# Abraham Glasscock (c. 1735–?)

**Abraham Glasscock** was baptised in **1735** at **St Michael, Bishop's Stortford**, Hertfordshire (FMP: GBPRS/B/71381316/1, register page 246). He came from a family deeply rooted in Bishop's Stortford — the Glasscocks appear in the parish registers from at least 1587.

Two earlier Abrahams baptised at the same church died as infants: Abraham (bapt. 1724, buried 1725) and Abraham (bapt. 1733, buried 1733). The 1735 Abraham has no corresponding burial at Bishop's Stortford — he survived and migrated to London.

On **17 January 1768** he married **Mary Thompson** by banns at **St Botolph Aldgate**, London (FMP: R_849941289). The marriage is also indexed in Boyd's Marriage Index as "Abr Glascock / Mary Tomson" at Enfield (FMP: GBPRS/M/752824554/2). The family settled at **Colchester Street, Whitechapel**, where they raised at least five children.

## Bishop's Stortford — a Glascock stronghold

The Glasscocks were one of the established families of Bishop's Stortford, on the Essex–Hertfordshire border, from the late sixteenth century. The parish register of St Michael records Glascock marriages from 1587, baptisms from the 1630s, and a dense cluster of births, marriages, and burials through the 1700s. Branches spread into surrounding parishes: Little Hallingbury, Sawbridgeworth, Broxted, Epping, and into southwest Essex (South Weald, Navestock, Brentwood, Stondon Massey).

Abraham's migration to London — 30 miles south — placed him in the Whitechapel/Stepney area where another Glascock family was already established: Robert Glascock (labourer) and his wife Sarah, whose children were baptised at St George in the East between 1740 and 1747. That family is a parallel branch, not Abraham's parents.
