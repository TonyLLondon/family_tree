# Daoud Khan Saginian ("Mayor Khan")

Georgian-born military commander who fled Russian-controlled Georgia in **1811**, joined the Qajar army, integrated fully into the Armenian community in Iran, and rose to the rank of **Sartip (Brigadier General)**, commanding Qajar forces in the provinces of **Shiraz and Isfahan** under Fath Ali Shah. Patrilineal anchor for both [Anna Saginian](anna-saginian.md) and [Tamar Saginian](tamar-saginian.md). The family sits in the **military-administrative elite**, not the commercial periphery of Qajar Iran.

## Georgian origin and flight to Iran

- Born in Georgia (Tbilisi region), c. 1790s; Georgian form of surname **Saginskilli** (also transliterated as *Saginean* in the Wikipedia article on Solayman Khan Saham al-Dowleh; related to the Georgian noble family Saginashvili).
- Served in the **Russian army** after the dissolution of the Georgian kingdom — explaining Dr. Wolff's 1843 identification of him as "a Colonel in the Russian service."
- In **early 1811**, fled Russian-controlled Georgia with his brother **Zaal Saginean** and their companion **Solayman Khan Saham al-Dowleh** (an Armenian-Georgian nobleman), motivated by fear of forced relocation to Russia and religious conversion. They obtained leave from their Russian commander on a pretext (a wrestling match), traveled via Yerevan, Akhalkalaki, and Gyumri, and arrived in **Tabriz**.
- Joined the army of Crown Prince **Abbas Mirza** under Qajar service; over subsequent decades rose through the military hierarchy and integrated into the Armenian diaspora community in Iran. The surname shifted from Georgian **Saginskilli** to Armenian **Saginian** in this period.

## Qajar military career

- Title: **Sartip** (سرتیپ) — Brigadier General, the senior non-royal military grade in the Qajar army.
- The Persian caption on his portrait photograph reads: *“Sartip Daoud Khan Sakinian, who during the reign of **Fath Ali Shah** was **commander of the Iranian forces in the provinces of Shiraz and Isfahan**, and died at the age of **78**.”* (Source: [sartip-daoud-khan-sakinian-photograph.md](../sources/sartip-daoud-khan-sakinian-photograph.md))
- Served under **Fath-Ali Shah Qajar** (r. 1797–1834); commanded troops under **Sayf ol-Dowleh**.
- **1834 Isfahan conflict** — Sayf ol-Dowleh divided his army; one division was led by **David (Daoud) Saginian**.
- Armenian military literature names **Davit Khan Saginian** as head of the Isfahan army — same figure.
- **Death age 78** — born c. 1795 implies death c. 1873; born c. 1790 implies c. 1868. Both fit the pattern of long Qajar service.

## Dr. Wolff's dual identification (1843)

Dr. Joseph Wolff visited Tabriz in late 1843 and recorded two passages that at first appear contradictory but in fact both refer to this figure:

1. *"I visited again Daoud Khan, a Colonel in the Russian service. He is a genuine Georgian."* — correctly noting his origin and prior Russian service.
2. *"Bonham, Osroff, Dr. Casolani, **Daoud Khan the Armenian**, all the Russian Attachés and the Greek merchants, accompanied me a long distance."* — reflecting his community identity by 1843.

Both passages are in *Narrative of a Mission to Bokhara* (1845), Chapter XXVI. The apparent contradiction resolves entirely given his Georgian birth and subsequent Armenian community integration.

## Family

- Brother: **Zaal Saginskilli** (also *Zaal Saginean*) (fled Georgia with him in 1811; further career unknown).
- Daughters: [Anna Saginian](anna-saginian.md); [Tamar Saginian](tamar-saginian.md) (married [Dr. William Cormick](william-cormick.md)).

## Burial

- **Armenian cemetery outside the walls of Tabriz** — Anna Burgess’s 1880 interview states: “my dear father’s grave is four or five English yards from [Edward’s grave]; it is among the graves of all my relations. And old Mr. & Mrs. Cormick and the Europeans are all buried there.” (Source: [nypl-burgess-appendix-anna-interview.md](../sources/nypl-burgess-appendix-anna-interview.md))
- His mother died before him; the cemetery also held the Cormick family (including the elder Cormicks).
