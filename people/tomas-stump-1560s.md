# Tomas Stump (c. 1560s)

**Tomas Stump** (born c. 1560) was another early patriarch of the STUMP family in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. He was the father of **Ulrich Stump** who died during the **1629 plague epidemic**, making him part of the founding generation of STUMP families established in Sulgen during the late 16th century. He likely died during the same 1629 epidemic that devastated the STUMP families.

## Life

**c. 1560** — Born in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland** (estimated from son's death age in 1629).

**c. 1590** — Marriage and family formation in Sulgen.

**c. 1605** — Birth of son Ulrich Stump (died aged c. 24 in 1629).

**c. 1629** — Died during or just before the **European plague epidemic** that killed his son Ulrich and multiple other STUMP family members.

## Family

**Children (documented):**
- **Ulrich Stump** (born c. 1605, died 16 Aug 1629, Sulgen) - first STUMP victim of 1629 plague epidemic

**Extended STUMP Connections:**

- **Augustin Stumpp** (c. 1560, lived to 1679) - contemporary patriarch

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Generation 17 Patriarch:**
Tomas represents another of the **earliest STUMP patriarchs** (Generation 17) in Sulgen, contemporary with Blaesi Stumpp (c. 1560). His establishment in Sulgen contributed to the multiple STUMP lineages documented in the 1590s-1600s.

**1629 Plague Impact:**
The death of Tomas's son Ulrich on **16 August 1629** marked the beginning of the four-month STUMP family mortality crisis during the European plague epidemic. Tomas may have died shortly before or after his son, as suggested by the "Thomma Stump" burial in nearby Langrickenbach on 4 October 1629.

**Geographic Connection:**
The potential burial in **Langrickenbach** (rather than Sulgen) suggests either:
- **Family connections** in surrounding Thurgau parishes
- **Epidemic displacement** as families fled affected areas
- **Property holdings** across multiple locations

**Contemporary STUMP Families:**
Tomas's presence alongside Blaesi, Augustin, and other STUMP patriarchs in late 1500s Sulgen indicates a **coordinated establishment** of the surname in this parish during a specific historical period.

## Historical Context

**Late 16th Century Sulgen:**
Tomas's generation (c. 1560) established the STUMP families during:
- **Post-Reformation period** in Swiss Reformed territories
- **Agricultural expansion** in Thurgau region  
- **Population growth** before Thirty Years' War disruptions

**Legacy Through Crisis:**
While Tomas's direct line ended with Ulrich's death in 1629, the **collective STUMP establishment** he helped create enabled other lines (particularly through Jacob Stump → Augustin Stump) to continue the family presence in Sulgen for centuries.

## Connection to Documented Lineage

**Generational Contemporary:**
Tomas was likely a **brother, cousin, or close relative** of other early STUMP patriarchs, particularly:
- **Hanssen Stump** (c. 1580) - father of Jacob Stump (1606) in our direct line
- **Blaesi Stumpp** (c. 1560) - contemporary with multiple children 1591-1601

The **20-year age gap** between Tomas (c. 1560) and Hanssen (c. 1580) suggests either father-son or uncle-nephew relationship, potentially making Tomas a **direct ancestor** in our lineage.
