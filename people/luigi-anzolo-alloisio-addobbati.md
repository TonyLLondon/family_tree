# Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati

Luigi Anzolo Alloisio Addobbati was baptized with the saint’s names that posterity would compress to “Luigi” and Croatian sources would render as **Lujo**—a Zara *cittadino* who moved from sworn citizenship in 1762 through priories and procuracies to the Nin noble body and the deputation that rowed out to meet the Austrians in 1797. He married Maddalena Plarinò in her father’s house on 4 April 1766 while physicians signed as witnesses; by 1811 both he and Maddalena were *fù*, already dead when their son Vincenzo married Vincenza Coltelli.

Granić’s 1817 article gives him another role: petitioner to Venice in 1793 for family admission to Nin’s council, alongside brothers Salustio and Don Giovanni whose ecclesiastical and chancellery careers Sabalich documents in parallel. His sons Dr. Pietro and Ivan Vicko (Vincenzo) took the 1804 Nin diploma that sig. 19 in the family fonds confirms with imperial and communal arms.

The civic chronicle is dense—*Casa di Pietà*, *Università* of the citizens, lawsuits appealed to Doge Manin—but the human scale returns in one baptismal godfather, Cristoffo Guardi, and in a single register line: a boy born 1 October 1744 to Daniel the citizen and Margherita Lanfranchi, named Anzolo Alloisio at the font.

## Life

- **1 Oct 1744** — Born Zara; baptized 2 Nov [?] 1744 Metropolitan Church (Don Carlo Felicinouvich, Curate); godfather Cristoffo Guardi; parents Daniel Addobbati *cittadino* and Margaritta Lanfranchi (Sabalich p. 505; register image).
- **27 Sep 1762** — Swore citizen’s oath (*giurò come Cittadino*) — Sabalich p. 505.
- **4 Apr 1766** — Marriage Zara to [Maddelena Plarinò](maddelena-plarino.md); bride’s house; Don Simeone Tatovich; banns dispensed; witnesses physicians Dr. Giacomo and Dr. Antonio Danieli; bride “figlia del Sig.r Pietro Plarinò”.
- **21 Mar 1772** — *Procurator e coadjutor*, attest from Carin provincial — Sabalich p. 506.
- **10 Jun 1783** — *Priore della Casa di Pietà* — p. 505.
- **1786** — *Procuratore e capo dell'Università* [of citizens] — p. 505.
- **13 Jan 1788** — *Giustiziere*; resignation and Doge Manin suit via envoys Calvi and Addobbati — p. 505.
- **1789** — *Procurator provinciale*; *causidico*; mandate for Ugljan (pp. 504–506).
- **1789** — Aggregated to *Corpo nobile di Nona* (Nin).
- **1793** — Petition for Nin council; brothers Salustio and Don Giovanni admitted — Granić / Sabalich.
- **6 Jul 1797** — *Procurator e capo dell'Università dei cittadini* — p. 506.
- **22–27 Jul 1797** — Extraordinary civic congress; deputations including sea mission to Austrian forces — p. 506.
- **24 Jun 1804** — Sons Dr. Pietro and Vincenzo (Ivan Vicko) inscribed Nin nobles.
- **Before 1811** — Died (predeceased son’s marriage register).
