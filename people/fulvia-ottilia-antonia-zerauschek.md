# Fulvia Ottilia Antonia Zerauschek

Fulvia is the hinge where the Adriatic line meets the Welsh one: the only child of [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md) and [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md) who carries this vault forward into [David John Lewis](david-john-lewis.md) and their English descendants. She was born in **Zara** in **1923**, when the city still wore the Italian kingdom’s flag and her father’s name was on cigarette papers and café awnings. A young woman on the **loggia of Villa Ester** at Sirmione—marble, cypress, lake light—survives in the family photograph; the villa would pass out of the family and into the story of **Maria Callas**, but Fulvia’s smile on that terrace is the human-sized memory.

She died at **Hove** in **Sussex** in **2000** and was laid to rest at **Sirmione**, beside the Garda shore, not far from the worlds she had left and kept returning.
