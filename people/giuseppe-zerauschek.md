# Giuseppe Zerauschek

Giuseppe is the father of [Antonio Zerauschek (senior)](antonio-zerauschek-senior.md). His marriage to [Antonia Maria Guerino](antonia-maria-guerino.md) in **1850** joined the Carniolan Zerauschek line to a Venetian family settled in Zadar. Their son Antonio would marry [Anna Herenda](anna-herenda.md) at Pag in **1878**.

The **1850 marriage register** (Entry 30, Sv. Stošija, Zadar) records him as **"Zerauschek M. Biagio vulgo Giuseppe"** — his baptismal name was **Biagio**, but he was commonly known as **Giuseppe**. He shared his baptismal name with his father, which likely explains the everyday use of an alternative. The register gives his birth as **11 November 1807** in **Vindol** (Sindol), **Carniola** (now Slovenia), and his occupation is listed in the column as **Industriosi**. His parents—**Biagio Zerauschek** (deceased by 1850) and **Maria Schinkouz**—are both recorded as *Ambi di Vindol*.

Fulvia's memoir gives him a **Viennese prologue**—sent south "to reorganize the town archives," arriving in his **own carriage**—and the registers confirm a Carniolan rather than Viennese origin: Sindol lies in the Tolmin district of the Duchy of Carniola, so the journey south to Dalmatia fits.

He was **dead** before the 1878 wedding of his son; the Pago register lists **Biagio Zerauschek**, coachman, **defunto**, as the groom's father.

## Life

**11 November 1807 — born at Sindol, Carniola** (Kranjska), Duchy of Carniola, Habsburg Austria. Recorded in the 1850 Zadar marriage register.

**24 August 1850 — marriage in Zara** to Antonia Maria Guerino. Register lines: father **Biagio**, *defunto*; mother **Maria Schinkovz**, living. Bride's parents both "di Venezia."

**Before 26 January 1878 — death** (father listed deceased on son's Pago marriage).
