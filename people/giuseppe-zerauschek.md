# Giuseppe Zerauschek

Giuseppe stands one generation above **Nonno’s father**: the man whose wedding to [Antonia Maria Guerino](antonia-maria-guerino.md) in **1850** ties the **Zerauschek** patronymic to the **Guerino** circle; whose son [Antonio Zerauschek (senior)](antonio-zerauschek-senior.md) would marry [Anna Herenda](anna-herenda.md) at Pag in **1878**. The export began from **marriage backward**, so his own birth date may still sleep in a Zara book.

Fulvia gives him a **Viennese prologue**—sent south “to reorganize the town archives,” arriving in his **own carriage**—and the **1878 groom’s extract** calls him **cocchiere**, coachman: archivist and driver may be two seasons of one life. He was **dead** before that 1878 wedding; the register lists **Biaggio Zerauschek**, coachman, **defunto**, as the groom’s father.

Patronymic proof: his son Antonio’s **1850 birth line** reads **Zerauschek [qu.] Biagio, Giuseppe**—Giuseppe, son of Biagio.

## Life

**24 August 1850 — marriage in Zara** to Antonia Maria Guerino. Register lines: father **Biagio**, *defunto*; mother **Maria Schinkovz**, living.

**Before 26 January 1878 — death** (father listed deceased on son’s Pago marriage).
