# Archibald Percy Coolbear

First World War **soldier-turned-airman** (**RAF official number 125257**) whose paper trail — **RAF service file (AIR 79/1122), medal index cards, award rolls, pensions card, and finding-aid captures** — anchors the **Coolbear** side of the [Evans · Cerpa Pérez](../lines/evans-cerpa-perez-london-chile.md) line. He enlisted in the **Essex Regiment** as a 22-year-old **chimney sweep** in December 1914, fought at **Gallipoli** and in **France**, and transferred to the **Royal Flying Corps** in January 1918 where he served as an **Instructor**. On **1 April 1918** the RFC became the **RAF**; he crossed that threshold with the institution — one of its **founding generation**. He married an Islington **Martin** daughter in **1911**; their daughter **May** married into the Holborn **Evans** family in **1940**. Indexed records sometimes spell the surname **Colbear** or split it **Cool Bear**; treat as the same man when the service number matches.

## Life

### Before the war

- **Mar 1892** — Born **Maldon, Essex** (GEDCOM / tree). Parents: [George Gridley Coolbear](george-gridley-coolbear-i105.md) and [Alma Newcomb](alma-newcomb-i106.md); marriage **F52** recorded as **1879** in [family-tree.json](../family-tree.json).
- **Civilian occupation** — **Chimney sweep** (RAF service form, field 5a).
- **28 Oct 1911** — Marriage to [Rosina A Martin](rosina-a-martin-i104.md) (**F51**). Town on the service form is partially legible (possibly Bottlewell / Southwell). The GEDCOM records December 1911; the form's "28.10.11" is primary evidence and takes precedence.
- **Mar 1916** — Daughter [May Coolbear](may-coolbear-i100.md) born (tree).
- **Physical description** (RAF form, field 8) — Height **5 ft 7 in**, chest **39 in**.

### Army service (Essex Regiment, 1914–1918)

- **29 Dec 1914** — Enlisted **Essex Regiment** as **Private**, age **22** (WWI service summary; RAF form field 3b). Regimental number **16694** (Victory Medal MIC; character page; Pensions card). A second number, **4677**, appears on the 1914–15 Star medal index card and the Blandford Star roll — probably his original battalion number before the Army's 1917 regiment-wide renumbering.
- **20 May 1915** — Deployed to **Gallipoli** with the Essex Regiment (character page: "16694: Gallipoli, 20.5.15").
- **7 Jun 1915** — Official date of entry into the **Mediterranean Expeditionary Force** (WO 372 medal index card, roll B76/2C p. 109).
- **4 Oct 1915** — Admitted with **rheumatism** (SH), General Hospital Zagazig, Alexandria (medical boards column).
- **c. Dec 1915** — Admitted with **scarlatina** (SH), hospital 1784, Alexandria.
- **1 Jan 1916** — **Fit to duty** (medical board). Gallipoli had been evacuated by January 1916; the Essex Regiment regrouped in Egypt.
- **21 Apr 1916** — Transferred to **France** (character page: "France, 21.4.16"). A second number "S. 8855C" appears on this France entry — possibly an attached-unit or MGC designation. The 1st Essex remained with the **88th Brigade, 29th Division**; the division attacked **Beaumont-Hamel** on **1 July 1916** (first day of the Somme). Coolbear's service record does not itemise individual engagements but places him with the unit at the time.
- **23 Jul 1916** — Admitted with **dysentery**, 98th Hospital, Rouen.
- **3 Nov 1916** — Evacuated to **Mont Dore Military Hospital, Bournemouth** (back in England).
- **20 Aug 1917** — Admitted with **severe diarrhoea**, 4th General Hospital, Camiers (France). Same day: observation for **mild dysentery**, 24th General Hospital, Étaples.
- **31 Aug 1917** — Admitted to hospital in Fordham (or Frodsham) — back in England.

### Transfer to RFC/RAF (1918–1919)

- **26 Jan 1918** — Transferred to the **Royal Flying Corps** (RAF form field 3c; character page: "Trans to R.F.C. 26.1.18"). Per the [RAFCommands service-number table](https://www.rafcommands.com/reference/raf-service-numbers/), numbers **125001–128000** were allocated in **February 1918** specifically for **RFC transfers**. Coolbear received **125257**.
- **1 Apr 1918** — The **Royal Air Force** formed from RFC + RNAS. Coolbear crossed that threshold automatically (RAF form field 3c: "R.A.F. 1.4.18"); he belongs to the RAF's **founding generation**.
- **Trade** — **Instructor** (character and trade proficiency page). Corporal's pay noted from c. Feb 1918.
- **Postings** — Movements column records **18 Y.S.** (likely 18th Training Squadron) and **Spittalgate** (an RFC/RAF training airfield near Grantham, Lincolnshire), c. Jun 1918.
- **3 Mar 1919** — **Transferred to RAF Reserve** (form field 9).
- **Discharge address** — **69 Milton Street, Southend-on-Sea, Essex** (form field 10).
- **Next of kin** (field 7) — Wife **Rosina Coolbear**, 33 Army Road, Southend.

### After the war

- Post-war residence initially at **Southend-on-Sea, Essex** (discharge address); later at **Hendon, Middlesex** (death registration) and the widow's pension address at **10 Queensland Road, Holloway, N.4** (Ministry of Pensions card P.F. 23 M.).
- **1928** — Died **Hendon, Middlesex**, age roughly 36 (GEDCOM / tree). No **CWGC** commemoration — he survived the war. No **London Gazette** commission, mention-in-dispatches, or casualty notice found (consistent with enlisted rank throughout).
- **1929** — **Ministry of Pensions** card (P.F. 23 M.) processed for widow **Mrs Coolbear**, 10 Queensland Road, Holloway N.4. Awards reference **11/W/42429**. Card copies stamped **3 Aug 1929**; entries dated 3 May 1929 and 7 Aug 1929.
- The RAF service file notes: **"Last Card Destroyed 13.1.21"** — referring to a prior administrative record, not the file itself.
- Probate likely Q3 1928 London (probate calendar to be searched).

### Surname origins — Colbia / Coolbar / Colbear / Coolbear (Mundon, Essex)

The Coolbear surname traces to a single Essex cluster. A [Wilkinson–Ransome family tree](https://www.wilkinsonransomefamilytree.co.uk/fam9112.html) documents **John Colbia** (b. c. 1807, Purleigh, Essex) and **Mary Barnard** (b. c. 1807, Tollesbury, Essex), married 29 Sep 1832 at Tollesbury. Their children were recorded under evolving spellings: **Coolbar** (1841 census), **Colbear** (birth index), **Coolbear** (1851 census) — all in **Mundon**, near Maldon. One child, **David Coolbear** (b. 1843, Mundon), emigrated to Utah with his mother; another, **John Colbear** (b. 1840, Mundon), stayed in Essex. The [SurnameDB entry](https://www.surnamedb.com/Surname/coolbear) traces the name to Old English "Cole" + "baer" (pasture), originating in Devon before migrating to Essex. **George Gridley Coolbear** (Archibald's father) fits squarely into this Maldon-district lineage — the same parish cluster where the Colbia/Coolbear family is documented from the 1830s.

Labour detachment?) or a War Office administrative reference. The full WO 363 attestation papers (if they survive) would clarify.
- **Marriage town:** The RAF form field 6a is partially legible. The GRO marriage certificate would confirm the place and correct the GEDCOM date (Dec 1911 vs form's 28 Oct 1911).
- **Cause of death (1928):** Died at ~36, ten years after the war. The repeated hospitalisations for dysentery, diarrhoea, and rheumatism across Gallipoli, France, and England suggest long-term health consequences of service. The death certificate would clarify.
