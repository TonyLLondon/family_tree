# Barbel Schmidhusser (early 1600s)

**Barbel Schmidhusser** was a Swiss woman from **Sulgen**, **Thurgau** in the early 17th century. She married [Barttli Jung](barttli-jung.md) and became the mother of [Hans Jung](hanss-jung.md) (baptized 1631), establishing the maternal lineage in **Generation 12** that would span over 200 years in Swiss family genealogy.

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Schmidhusser Family Background:**
The Schmidhusser surname (also spelled Schmidhauser) appears frequently in Swiss records of the early 17th century, particularly in Thurgau canton. The name suggests occupational origins related to metalworking or smithing ("Schmied" = smith).

**Maternal Heritage Contribution:**
Through Barbel Schmidhusser, subsequent generations of the Jung and eventual STUMP lineages carry Swiss Schmidhusser ancestry from Generation 12. Her heritage contributed to over 200 years of documented descendants in the Sulgen area.

**Early 17th Century Swiss Women:**
Barbel Schmidhusser's life exemplifies early 17th-century Swiss rural women's roles: marriage within the local community, childbearing during challenging historical periods, and family continuity through social and religious changes of the Reformation era.

**Generation 12 Significance:**
