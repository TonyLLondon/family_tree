# Antonio Zerauschek

**"Trgovac, bankar, veleposjednik i tvorničar"** — trader, banker, large landowner, and factory owner in **Zara** (Kingdom of Italy). Hotels (**Hotel Zerauschek**, **Caffè Centrale**, **Hotel Bristol / Excelsior**), cigarettes (**Manifattura Zaratina Sigarette** — Calypso brand), chocolate (**Fabbrica Cioccolato e Cacao Ausonia**). In the MyHeritage GEDCOM export he is **`@I14@`**, father of [Fulvia Ottilia Antonia Zerauschek](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md). His parents in the GEDCOM chain are [Antonio Zerauschek (senior)](antonio-zerauschek-senior.md) and [Anna Herenda](anna-herenda.md) (**F13**). That earlier Antonio (`@I25@`) must not be confused with this file's subject (`@I14@`).

## Life

The contemporary Italian obituary in *Difesa Adriatica* (issue **XXVII, no. 3**, **25 March–3 April 1973**) is the richest narrative source in this vault: signed **„g. z."**, it places his death in **Florence** in the **last days of February 1973**, aged **84**, which matches the GEDCOM **28 February 1973** date. The piece stresses that he died **far from Zara**, roughly **eighteen months** after the death of his wife **[Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md)**—a loss the author treats as having broken his health despite the care of children and grandchildren.

Socially, the obituary sketches a figure already visible before the First World War: tall, elegant, a strong dancer at balls run by the **„Società Ginnastica"** in Zara; after the war he was part of the circle around the **„Circolo Arturo Colautti"**. Politically he is called a **patriot** who followed the city's fortunes. Economically it casts him as a **self-made** operator: starting from **import–export**, he rose to a leading place in Zara's **commerce and industry**, helped by the **free port** regime, holding **agency** lines for foreign goods (flour, oils, petrol, cars, and similar). Outside business he is portrayed as **family-centred**, modest, and widely **liked** across Zara society; the author recalls pre-war hospitality in a **house he had designed** shortly before the Second World War, then the **anguish** of leaving Zara under **Allied bombing** and abandoning industries, home, and city. **English translation** (use for wording; cite scan + Italian transcript for authority): [translation (EN)](../sources/corpus/difesa-adriatica-1973-antonio-zerauschek-obituary/translation-antonio-zerauschek-obituary-1973.en.md).

### Manifattura Zaratina Sigarette

On **27 May 1928** Zerauschek acquired the **Manifattura Zaratina Sigarette** from **Niccolò Peristeridis**, a Greek-origin tobacco operator whose family also ran factories in Durazzo (Albania). The company originated as **Manifattura Zaratina Tabacchi e Sigarette "N. Peristeridis"** (Calle Giuseppe Sabalich 2), producing **Calypso** and **Samos**; renamed 1922 and moved to Calle Catena 6. Under Zerauschek it also absorbed the **Fabbrica Sigarette Diadora** and produced: **Calitea, Calypso, Diadora, Flavia, Lampo, Radio, Raminga, Samos, Samos Export, Si**. Zara sat outside the Italian customs line — these cigarettes were classified as "foreign" in mainland Italy. A surviving c. 1940 Calypso hinged-box pack is documented in the zigsam.at collector's database. **Sources:** [sigaretteitaliane.org](../sources/sigaretteitaliane-zara-1920-43.md) · [zigsam.at](../sources/zigsam-at-calypso-cigarette-packs.md).

The obituary separately names him a **founder** of the **Manifattura Tabacchi Orientali** (MTO) — a larger, state-majority manufacturer on Riva 4 Novembre. He later struck out independently with the Manifattura Zaratina; the two companies were distinct entities.

### Fabbrica Cioccolato e Cacao Ausonia

The **Ausonia** chocolate and cocoa factory stood in **Voštarnica** (Ceraria), built on the newly developed land that opened after the 1928 bridge over the Jazine inlet. By **1929** the factory was **exporting chocolate to Italy and Western European countries**. It won **numerous quality awards**. A 2011 Croatian business article speculates that **Luxardo** (est. 1821 in Zadar) probably used Ausonia products for its bonbon and chocolate lines. The Zerauschek family also erected modern residential and commercial buildings across Zadar in the 1930s. **Sources:** [Zadarski tjednik (2024)](../sources/zadarskitjednik-ausonia-tvornica-cokolade.md) · [Poslovni dnevnik (2011)](../sources/poslovni-hr-2011-ausonia-zadar-zerauschek.md).

### Built works (interwar)

He demolished an older building on **Široka ulica** and commissioned a modern hotel/café complex (**1937–1938**), architect **Umberto Nordio** (Trieste) — [narratives/zerauschek-zadar.md](../narratives/zerauschek-zadar.md). **Hotel Bristol → Excelsior** (~1936): takeover and rename per Adriatic press/hospitality histories linked there.

### Villa Ester, Sirmione (Lake Garda)

Before the war, Antonio acquired the **Villa Giannantoni** on the tip of the Sirmione peninsula — Via Caio Valerio Catullo 7, overlooking the Grotte di Catullo — and renamed it **Villa Ester** after his wife. The family used it as their Lake Garda retreat; a [photograph of Fulvia on the loggia](../media/docs/Fulvia%20Zerauschek%20at%20Villa%20Ester%20Sirmione.png) survives from this period. In the immediate post-war years, after the family's exile from Zara, Antonio sold the villa to **Angelo Marinoni** (electric-oven manufacturer, Verona). Marinoni in turn sold it in **December 1957** to **Comm. Giovanni Battista Meneghini**, who gave it to his wife **Maria Callas** — it became the famous **Villa Callas**. **Source:** *Incom* reportage by Savino Mariani, 10 May 1958 · [corpus](../sources/corpus/gbopera-2009-telefoni-e-rose-sirmione-callas/). **Narrative:** [Villa Ester — from the Zerauscheks to Maria Callas](../narratives/zerauschek-villa-callas-sirmione.md).

### After WWII

Zara passed to Yugoslavia; Italian-speaking business families **emigrated**. The cigarette factory — partly destroyed by Allied bombing — was **confiscated** by Yugoslav authorities. The Manifattura Zaratina Sigarette attempted to reconstitute in Italy, convening its shareholders' assembly in **Florence**; the assembly voted to convert into an *s.r.l.*, but the **Tribunale di Firenze annulled** the resolution and the company ceased to exist. The Ausonia chocolate factory was likewise **shut down by the incoming Yugoslav authorities**; no chocolate was produced in Zadar again for 82 years. The obituary's emotional centre is exile and memory of Zara; **property and legal succession** in Yugoslavia/Italy still need document-by-document work (parish/civil scans under `media/collections/zerauschek/`).
