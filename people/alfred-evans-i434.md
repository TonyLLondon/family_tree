# Alfred Evans

**Alfred Evans** was born about **1821** in **Shoreditch**, Middlesex — recorded consistently across four censuses (1851–1881). He worked his entire adult life as a **house painter and glazier** in Islington, raising a family of at least six children with his wife [Catherine Burton](catherine-burton-evans-i608.md).

The family lived in the **Islington East** sub-district for at least three decades: **Windsor Street** in 1851, **1 Clarks Place, Anglers Gardens** in 1861, and **Paradise Place** from 1871 through at least 1881. All of these addresses fall within the parliamentary borough of Finsbury, on the boundary between Islington and Clerkenwell — the same working-class neighbourhood where his son [William](william-evans-i289.md) would later settle with [Dorothea Knecht](dorothea-knecht.md).

Alfred was sixty in the 1881 census, still heading the household and working as a painter. His death has not yet been traced — he does not appear in the 1891 census (not yet searched), so he likely died in the 1880s.

Seven persons in household.

**Note on birthplace "Hoxton":** The 1861 census was indexed by FMP as "Horton" but the original image reads **Hoxton** — the northern part of Shoreditch parish. Entirely consistent with "Shoreditch" in the 1851, 1871, and 1881 censuses.

**Note on 1881 household:** The 1881 census shows two children not present in 1871: **Thomas** (17, Printer) and **Catherine May** (12, Scholar). Thomas's birth year (~1864) matches Walter Evans from the 1871 census — they may be the same person using different given names. Catherine May (b. ~1869) should have appeared in the 1871 census at age ~2 but did not — she may have been staying with relatives on census night.
