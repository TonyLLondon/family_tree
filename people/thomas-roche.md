# Thomas Roche

Thomas Roche of **George's Street, Limerick** was a **banker**, Catholic civic leader, husband of [Hellen Ankettle](hellen-ankettle.md), and father of [William Roche](william-roche.md) (Crown Solicitor for Limerick). Son of [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md) ("the oldest merchant" in Limerick, d. 1804) and [Sarah O'Bryen](sarah-obryen.md), from Stephen's second marriage. His parentage through the Roche of Limerick line is documented in Burke's *History of Commoners* (1833) and independently confirmed by the 1883 marriage notice for granddaughter Maria Josephine Roche: "granddaughter of the late **Thomas Roche, Esq.**, of this city [Limerick]."

In 1801 Thomas and William founded the **[Bank of Thomas and William Roche](../topics/bank-of-thomas-and-william-roche.md)** on Charlotte's Quay, Limerick — one of the Catholic private banks of the city, issuing its own banknotes (examples survive in the British Museum). The bank moved to **George's Street** in 1806, where William built his famous **Hanging Gardens** — terraced gardens rising seventy feet above street level, growing pineapples and grapes in heated hothouses, at a cost of £15,000.

When the **Munster Banking Crisis of 1820** destroyed every other private bank in Limerick — Maunsell's fell, and the brothers' own Cork bank (run by siblings Stephen and James) collapsed on 25 May 1820 — Thomas held firm. The Limerick bank's legal separation from the Cork operation saved it. By 1824, Thomas Roche & Co. was the **only bank** in Limerick. It was acquired by the Provincial Bank in 1825; Thomas became a **Local Director** of the new joint-stock branch.

Thomas was also active in Limerick public life for decades: he seconded **Daniel O'Connell** at a Munster Catholic meeting in 1825, chaired Catholic meetings alongside William, and served on the Board of Management of the Provincial Bank's Limerick branch (1844–1846).

## Life

- **~1769** — Born **Limerick** (died Dec 1855 "in his eighty-seventh year").
- **1796** — Marriage at **Limerick** to [Hellen Ankettle](hellen-ankettle.md), daughter of **John Ankettle**, of **Dingle, Kerry** (Farrar's Index to Irish Marriages 1771–1812; bride's name indexed as "Catherine" — likely a transcription of "Hellen" or a second given name).
- **1801** — Founded the **[Bank of Thomas and William Roche](../topics/bank-of-thomas-and-william-roche.md)** on **Charlotte's Quay**, Limerick, with brother William. Bank named alongside Maunsell's in the 1802 citizens' meeting (*Sun (London)*, 7 Jan 1802).
- **~Feb 1804** — Death of father [Stephen John Roche](stephen-john-roche.md), "in his 80th year... the oldest merchant in that city."
- **1804** — Purchased **George's Street** premises for the bank.
- **1806** — Announced transfer of bank to George's Street. Advertising the sale of his **house on Patrick Street, Limerick** (*Limerick Gazette*, Apr–Sep 1806).
- **1808** — William built the **Hanging Gardens** behind the George's Street bank (£15,000). Managing the **Clounties estate** (Co. Limerick, Barony of Lower Conello) with brothers Stephen (of Cork) and William; estate inherited from mother Sarah O'Bryen's family (*Limerick Gazette*, Jan–Mar 1808).
- **1813** — Calling Catholic meetings in Limerick alongside brother William (*Limerick Gazette*, 6 Aug 1813).
- **25 May 1820** — **Munster Banking Crisis**: Roches' Cork Bank (brothers Stephen and James) collapsed, triggering a chain of failures. Maunsell's Bank in Limerick fell. **Thomas held the Limerick bank steady** — the only bank in Limerick to survive. Legal separation from the Cork bank proved decisive.
- **1824** — Thomas Roche & Co. listed as the **only bank** in Limerick (Pigott's Trade Directory).
- **1 Nov 1825** — Bank acquired by **The Provincial Bank**, which opened its Limerick branch on that date. Thomas became **Local Director** of the new branch. Seconded a resolution by **Daniel O'Connell** at the Munster Catholic Provincial Meeting (*Southern Reporter*, 27 Oct 1825; *Belfast News-Letter*, 8 Nov 1825).
- **1826** — Thomas proposes resolutions at a meeting chaired by brother William (*Limerick Chronicle*, 22 Jul 1826).
- **1828** — Chairs a Limerick Catholic meeting (*Limerick Chronicle*, 21 Jun 1828). Birth of a child recorded (*Limerick Evening Post*, 29 Jul 1828).
- **1832** — Described as father-in-law of **Daniel Ryan Keane, Esq., Barrister, Commissioner of Bankruptcy** (*Warder and Dublin Weekly Mail*, 26 Sep 1832) — confirming Burke's: daughter Helen m. D.R. Kane.
- **1841** — Speaking at a Limerick meeting to address Lord Morpeth (*Dublin Morning Register*, 23 Aug 1841).
- **1844–1846** — On the **Board of Management** of the Limerick branch, listed alongside brother William Roche, J.P. (*Limerick Chronicle*, 20 Jan 1844; *Limerick Reporter*, 20 Oct 1846).
- **22 Mar 1849** — Death of wife [Hellen Ankettle](hellen-ankettle.md), "in the 77th year of her age," at George's Street, Limerick (*Southern Reporter*, 27 Mar 1849).
- **27 Apr 1850** — Death of brother and banking partner **William Roche, M.P.**, aged 75, at Lower Glentworth Street, Limerick. Multiple papers across Britain and Ireland.
- **1 Apr 1853** — Death of brother **James Roche**, aged 83, in Cork. "Formerly an eminent banker, and brother of Thomas Roche" (*Dublin Evening Packet*). Called "the Roscoe of Cork" — author, antiquary, director of the National Bank.
- **15 Dec 1855** — Died at his house in **George's Street**, Limerick, "after a short illness, in his 87th year." The *Limerick Chronicle* obituary read: "Thomas Roche, Esq., elder brother of William Roche, Esq., M.P. for this city, deceased, and surviving partner of the late highly respectable and independent Bank of Thomas and William Roche, of Limerick, **the only one, probably, in the South of Ireland, that passed through the disastrous commercial crash of private banks 40 years ago, with perfect honor and integrity**. Mr Thomas Roche was like his brothers, a gentleman of highly cultivated mind and of strict moral rectitude in all his extensive business transactions. His domestic life was pure and irreproachable, his politics ever liberal and consistent."

## Open questions

- Whether the Farrar's Index "Catherine" is a transcription error for "Hellen" or a second given name. The bride's father (John Ankettle) and residence (Limerick) match exactly.
- Location and date of Thomas's burial in Limerick — possibly the family vault at Drumbanny Churchyard (where brother Philip Roche was interred in 1797).
- The 1867 Wexford Independent article "THE LATE THOMAS ROCHE, ESQ" — likely concerns a different, later Thomas Roche (this Thomas died 1855).
