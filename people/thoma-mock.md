# Thoma Mock (early 1600s)

**Thoma Mock** was a Swiss man from **Sulgen**, **Thurgau** in the early-to-mid 17th century. He married [Anna Sprenger](anna-sprenger.md) and became the father of [Johannes Mockh](johannes-mockh-1662.md) (baptized 1661), establishing the Mockh lineage in **Generation 12** that would connect to the Jung and STUMP families through subsequent marriages.

## Life

**c. 1630** — Born in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland** (estimated from son's birth date).

**c. 1655** — Marriage to [Anna Sprenger](anna-sprenger.md).

**27 Sep 1661** — Birth of son [Johannes Mockh](johannes-mockh-1662.md) (recorded as "Johanness Mock") in Sulgen.

**c. 1680** — Death (estimated, as son Johannes appears as head of household in later records).

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Mock/Mockh Family Establishment:**
Thoma Mock represents the earliest documented Mockh ancestor in Sulgen, establishing the family presence back to approximately 1630. This places the Mockh family settlement during the post-Reformation period in Switzerland.

**Name Variations:**
"Thoma" is a Swiss German form of Thomas, and the surname appears as both "Mock" and "Mockh" in contemporary records - typical of spelling variations in 17th-century parish documentation.

**Mid-17th Century Context:**
Living during the mid-1600s, Thoma Mock would have experienced the aftermath of the Thirty Years' War (1618-1648) and the subsequent period of Swiss economic recovery and population growth in rural communities.

**Genealogical Bridge:**
Thoma Mock serves as a crucial genealogical bridge, connecting the mid-17th century Mockh ancestry to the 18th and 19th-century Jung and STUMP lineages through his son's marriage alliances.

**Generation 12 Foundation:**
As a Generation 12 ancestor, Thoma Mock established Mockh family roots that would remain in Sulgen for over 100 years, demonstrating remarkable family stability through multiple social and economic changes.
