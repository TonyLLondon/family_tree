# Johan Jacob Eylandt

Johan Jacob Eylandt was born on **11 February 1774** in **Leal (Lihula)**, a small town in the Wiek district of western Estonia. He was the son of [Carl Gustav Eylandt](carl-gustav-eylandt.md) and [Margarete Elisabeth Wiedau](margarete-elisabeth-wiedau.md), who had married in Hageri six years earlier and by the 1770s were living in the Lihula area. Johan Jacob was one of at least twelve children — his siblings' birthplaces shift from Lihula to Kirbla Parish during the 1780s, tracking the family's move to the Kirrefer pastorat.

He became a **Schneidermeister** (master tailor) and settled in **Tallinn**, where he married [Maria Elisabeth Busse](maria-elisabeth-busse.md) on **27 December 1799** in her home town of **Viljandi (Fellin)**. The couple returned to Tallinn and raised a large family. FamilySearch church book records document at least six of their children's baptisms between 1800 and 1814, all in Tallinn's Lutheran churches.

Maria Elisabeth died in **February 1823**, leaving Johan Jacob with children ranging from toddlers to young adults. He survived her by eight years, dying on **7 August 1831** in Tallinn. The parish of his burial — whether St Nicholas, the Holy Spirit church, or a suburban chapel — remains unidentified.
