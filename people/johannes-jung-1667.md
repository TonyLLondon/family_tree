# Johannes Jung (1667-1726)

**Johannes Jung** (baptized 11 August 1667) was the son of [Hanss Jung](hanss-jung.md) and [Anna Marstetter](anna-marstetter.md) in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. He married [Barbara Muntz](barbara-muntz.md) and became the father of [Hans Ulrich Jung](hanss-ulrich-jung.md), establishing the Jung lineage that would later connect to the STUMP family through his granddaughter [Anna Margaretha Jung](anna-margaretha-jung.md).

## Life

**11 Aug 1667** — Christened **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. Son of [Hanss Jung](hanss-jung.md) and [Anna Marstetter](anna-marstetter.md).

**c. 1695** — Marriage to [Barbara Muntz](barbara-muntz.md).

**c. 1700** — Birth of son [Hans Ulrich Jung](hanss-ulrich-jung.md).

**4 Apr 1726** — Died **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, age 58 years. Buried at Sulgen.

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Jung Family Continuity:**
Johannes Jung represents the third documented generation of the Jung family in Sulgen, spanning from his father Hanss (c. 1640s) through his granddaughter Anna Margaretha (1731). This 90-year documented presence demonstrates remarkable family stability in one Swiss parish.

**Swiss Community Integration:**
The Jung family's consistent residence in Sulgen, combined with marriages to local families (Marstetter, Muntz, Mockh), illustrates the tight-knit nature of Swiss rural communities where families intermarried within the parish across multiple generations.

**Genealogical Bridge:**
Johannes Jung serves as a crucial genealogical bridge, connecting the mid-17th century Jung ancestry to the 18th-century STUMP lineage through his granddaughter's marriage to Hans Konrad Stump.
