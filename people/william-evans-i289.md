# William Evans (London)

**William Evans** was born in the first quarter of **1857** in **Islington**, London — registered in the Islington district (GRO Vol 1B, p. 253; mother's maiden name Burton). He grew up in a house-painter's family: his father [Alfred Evans](alfred-evans-i434.md) (b. ~1821, Shoreditch) and mother [Catherine Burton](catherine-burton-evans-i608.md) (b. ~1824, Clapton, Hackney) raised at least eight children in the Islington East sub-district across three decades.

William married [Dorothea Knecht](dorothea-knecht.md) in **Q4 1878** at **Pancras** (GRO marriage index: Vol 1B, p. 143). The 1911 Census records them as married **32 years**, consistent with late 1878. Dorothea was seventeen; William was about twenty-one.

By 1871 (age 14) William was working as a **printer** in his parents' household at Paradise Place, Islington. Within a few years he shifted to the brass trades — recorded as a **brass founder** in 1881 and thereafter as "Brass Trimmer" (1901, 1921) and "Brass Finisher" (1911). Both printing and brasswork were concentrated in the Clerkenwell/Finsbury corridor, so the transition was geographical as much as vocational. By 1912, when his son [Alfred](alfred-evans-i101.md) married [Annie Fensom](annie-fensom-i291.md), William's trade was recorded on the marriage register as **horse dealer** — whether a change of career or a sideline, it places him in the world of Smithfield and the costermonger barrows.

The **1881 Census** places the couple at **3 Swinton Street, St Pancras** — just the two of them, married two years, no children yet, William working as a brass founder in the workshops between Gray's Inn Road and Clerkenwell Green. The family later settled in Islington — at **44 Pembroke Street** by 1901 (the same street where Dorothea had grown up in the Knecht household), then at **124 Barnsbury Road** by 1911. William and Dorothea raised at least ten children. He was still heading the household in the **1921 Census** at **6 Gibson Square, Islington** (age 64, brass trimmer — out of work from Din Cross & Co, Acton), but was absent by the **1939 Register** — Dorothea appears as a widow at **61 Theberton Street**, living with sons Ernest (electrician) and Francis (bus conductor).

### Father's later census

- [1881 Census — Alfred Evans household, 6 Paradise Place, Islington](../sources/corpus/1881-census-alfred-evans-household-islington/transcription.md) — RG11/265, folio 9, p. 12: Alfred (60, Painter), Catherine (58), Arthur (26, Cabinet Maker), William (24), Reuben, Catherine May (12), Thomas (17, Printer). Seven persons. Our William already at Swinton Street — the "William" here may be a dual enumeration or indexing confusion.

**Correction note:** An older working tree linked this William to a Louisa Spencer (marriage 26 Jul 1891, old `I290` / `F136`). Four census returns (1901, 1911, 1921) and the 1939 Register consistently record his wife as **Dorothea**, married since ~1878. The Louisa Spencer marriage belongs to a **different** William Evans. Louisa is **out of this tree** (see [corpus extract — Ancestry christening index](../sources/corpus/louisa-spencer-england-select-births-and-christe-c5d42f5463/extracted.pdf.md) for the index entry only).

**Note on birthplace:** Later censuses (1901, 1911) give William's birthplace as "Clerkenwell." The GRO birth registration and 1871 census both say Islington. The two parishes are contiguous — the family lived on the Islington side of the boundary, but "Clerkenwell" was the broader neighbourhood name for the area's metalworking quarter.
