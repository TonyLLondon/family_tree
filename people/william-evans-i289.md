# William Evans (London)

**William Evans** was born about **1857** in **Clerkenwell**, London — not Wales, despite the surname. He married [Dorothea Knecht](dorothea-knecht.md) in **Q4 1878** at **Pancras** (GRO marriage index: Vol 1B, p. 143). The 1911 Census records them as married **32 years**, consistent with late 1878. Dorothea was seventeen; William was about twenty-one.

William's lifelong trade was **brass trimming and finishing** — recorded as "Brass Trimmer" in the 1901 and 1921 censuses, "Brass Finisher" in 1911. The brass trades were central to Clerkenwell's economy: the district was dense with workshops producing gas fittings, door furniture, scientific instruments, and decorative metalwork. By 1912, when his son [Alfred](alfred-evans-i101.md) married [Annie Fensom](annie-fensom-i291.md), William's trade was recorded on the marriage register as **horse dealer** — whether a change of career or a sideline, it places him in the world of Smithfield and the costermonger barrows.

The **1881 Census** places the couple at **3 Swinton Street, St Pancras** — just the two of them, married two years, no children yet, William working as a **brass founder** in the workshops between Gray's Inn Road and Clerkenwell Green. The family later settled in Islington — at **44 Pembroke Street** by 1901 (the same street where Dorothea had grown up in the Knecht household), then at **124 Barnsbury Road** by 1911. William and Dorothea raised at least ten children. He was still heading the household in the **1921 Census** (age 64, brass trimmer), but was absent by the **1939 Register** — Dorothea appears as a widow, living with sons Ernest and Francis.
