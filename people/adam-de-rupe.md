# Adam de Rupe (Adam de la Roche)

**Adam de Rupe** — **Adam de la Roche** in many Irish and Welsh sources — is the **traditional founding name** on the **patriline** that became the **Roche** dynasty of **Fermoy** and **Castletownroche** in County Cork. Modern pedigrees cite him as the man who brought the **de la Roche** identity from **Wales** into the **Anglo-Norman** settlement of **Munster**, usually with **Robert FitzStephen**. **Sir Bernard Burke** (**1866**) even gives **1196** for the Irish establishment — a **later** date than the classic **1169–1171** invasion window — so chronology is **not** uniform across handbooks. He is **not** entered in `family-tree.json`: too remote and too dependent on **secondary genealogies** for a structured ID.

## Wales

The [Monastic Wales](https://www.monasticwales.org/person/88) database describes **Adam de la Rupe (Roche)** as married to **Blandina**, founder of **Pill Priory**, and the figure **first associated with Roch Castle** in **Pembrokeshire**. The entry summarises a **Flemish** background via **Godebert** (Pipe Roll **1130**) and a **barony of Roche** crystallising about **1100×1130** around **Pill** and **Roche**. Above Adam, Victorian charter scholarship (notably **Hunter & Traherne**, *Archaeologia Cambrensis*, **1852**) is the usual place editors reconstruct **FitzGodebert** generations — see the [topic article](../topics/adam-de-rupe.md) for the full **ancestry table** and **Wexford** sibling tradition.

## Ireland

Irish reference works (including [Dictionary of Irish Biography](https://www.dib.ie/) articles on later Roches, recycled in handbook form) repeat that the **Viscounts Fermoy** and the **Old English** Roches of Cork descend from **Adam de Rupe**, from Wales, with **Robert FitzStephen**. Burke **1866** (p. **454**) states explicitly that the Roche family was **established in Ireland** by Adam **of Roch Castle, Pembrokeshire**, with FitzStephen, in **1196** — excerpted in the footnotes of [David Roche, 7th Viscount Fermoy (Wikipedia corpus)](../sources/corpus/wikipedia-david-roche-7th-viscount-fermoy/extracted.web.md). Whether the **first Munster grant** belongs to **Adam’s** lifetime or to a **son** is a question for **feudal specialists**; Eithne Donnelly’s JCHAS articles (**1935–1937**) move onto **safer ground** with **fourteenth-century** lords from **Carew**, the **Book of Fermoy**, and the **Irish rolls**. For this vault, Adam remains the **named root** of the **published stem** that Burke’s **1833** *Commoners* later compresses as **“descended from the Viscounts Fermoy.”**

## In this tree

The **proved** patriline begins at [John Roche](john-roche.md) of **Castletown-Roche** (~1600), Confederate **Commons** in **1641**. Burke states he is **descended from the Viscounts Fermoy**; the **exact fork** below those viscounts is **unknown**. **Above** that fork, the handbooks supply a single ladder of fathers ending in **Adam**.
