# Richard Webb

**Richard Webb** was an **agricultural labourer** born about **1827** in **Eynsford**, a village in the Darent valley, north-west Kent. On **7 June 1851** he married **[Charlotte Willoughby](charlotte-willoughby-i653.md)** at **St Paul's Cray**, a parish about 8 miles east of Eynsford. Their daughter [Clara](clara-webb-i597.md) was born in the same quarter — Clara may have arrived just before the wedding.

In the **1851 Census** (taken 30 March, before the marriage) Richard was still living as a "Son" with his parents at **Mandy Cottages, Crockenhill** (a hamlet within Eynsford parish). The household also included members surnamed Spire, suggesting his mother may have remarried. His father's name was **William Webb**, recorded on the marriage register.

By **1861** the family had settled at **Crocker Hill Road, Crockenhill**, in the ecclesiastical parish of All Souls. Richard (34, ag. labourer) and Charlotte (29) had one child at home: Clara (9), born St Paul's Cray.
