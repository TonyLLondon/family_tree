# Antonia Maria Guerino

Antonia Maria Guerino was the wife of [Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md) and mother of [Antonio Zerauschek (senior)](antonio-zerauschek-senior.md). She was born on 2 January 1825 in Zara and married Giuseppe there on 24 August 1850.

## Life

- **2 Jan 1825** — Born **Zara, Dalmatia** (Habsburg **Kingdom of Dalmatia** period).
- **24 Aug 1850** — Marriage **Zara** to [Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md).
- **BEF 26 Jan 1878** — Deceased. The 1878 marriage register of her son Antonio lists his mother as **"Antonia Guerino"**, Catholic, **deceased**. The 1850 marriage register confirms her parents: father **Guerino Antonio** (*padre defunto* — deceased), mother **Zuajetto Maria** (*madre defunta* — also deceased by 1850).

Giuseppe Zerauschek (her husband) was **sent from Vienna to Zara to reorganize the town archives**, travelling in his own horse-drawn carriage. He met Antonia Maria at an event in the **Archbishop's palace** in Zara. She had a sister who was the **Superior of a Convent** elsewhere in Dalmatia; the family's grandchildren used to spend holidays at the convent. (Fulvia memoir, 1996 — attributes meeting to "X Zerauschek" and "X Guerrino"; the structured tree matches Giuseppe and Antonia Maria as the couple.)

Italian-speaking, she reportedly lived **60 years on the island of Pago** and never learned a word of Croatian — a detail the family treasured as illustrating Italian identity within the Slavic hinterland. (Fulvia memoir, 1996.)
