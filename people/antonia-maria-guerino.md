# Antonia Maria Guerino

Mother of [Antonio Zerauschek (senior)](antonio-zerauschek-senior.md). She was born **2 January 1825** in **Zara** and married [Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md) there on **24 August 1850** (**F58**).

## Life

- **2 Jan 1825** — Born **Zara, Dalmatia** (Habsburg **Kingdom of Dalmatia** period).
- **24 Aug 1850** — Marriage **Zara** to [Giuseppe Zerauschek](giuseppe-zerauschek.md) (**F58**).
- **Death**: flagged `DEAT Y` in export without a dated line in the snippet—complete from parish or civil books.

Giuseppe Zerauschek (her husband) was **sent from Vienna to Zara to reorganize the town archives**, travelling in his own horse-drawn carriage. He met Antonia Maria at an event in the **Archbishop's palace** in Zara. She had a sister who was the **Superior of a Convent** elsewhere in Dalmatia; the family's grandchildren used to spend holidays at the convent. (Fulvia memoir, 1996 — attributes meeting to "X Zerauschek" and "X Guerrino"; the GEDCOM generation mapping places Giuseppe and Antonia Maria as the couple.)

Italian-speaking, she reportedly lived **60 years on the island of Pago** and never learned a word of Croatian — a detail the family treasured as illustrating Italian identity within the Slavic hinterland. (Fulvia memoir, 1996.)
