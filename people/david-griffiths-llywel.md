# David Griffiths

**David Griffiths** was born about **1776** in the parish of **Llywel**, Brecknockshire, in the upper Usk valley. He spent his entire recorded life in this parish, working as an agricultural **labourer**. On **22 December 1815** he married [Margaret Davies](margaret-griffiths-mother.md) at Llywell parish church (Church of England). Margaret had come from **Cayo** (Cynwyl Gaeo), Carmarthenshire, about 20 miles across the county boundary.

Their first child, **John**, was christened at Llywel parish church just six weeks later, on 4 February 1816. A daughter, **Sarah**, was probably born about 1826 at Trecastle. They later attended the **Saron Independent Chapel** (Congregationalist) in Llywell, where two more sons named Thomas were baptised in 1828 and 1830 — the first presumably died in infancy, and the second became [Thomas Griffiths](thomas-griffiths.md) of Aberdare.

The family lived at **Tresgob** (Bishop Town), a hamlet in Llywell parish. On the 1841 census David was 60, Margaret was 55, and only their son Thomas (11) was still at home — John had left or died. By the 1851 census David was 75, still labouring, living alone with Margaret at 3 Bishop Town.

David was buried on **12 December 1857** at age 83, according to the Brecknockshire parish register (FS `KCVB-F2X`, b. 1774). The register image is restricted to FamilySearch Centers and the parish has not been confirmed, but the date, age, and county fit.
