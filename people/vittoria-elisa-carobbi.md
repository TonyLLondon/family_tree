# Vittoria Elisa Carobbi

Wife of [Simeone Vincenzo Boara](simeone-vincenzo-boara.md) in **F11**; mother of [Ottilia Anna Vincenza Boara](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md). Geni scrape and **@S12@** agree on **1828** birth and **1902** death.

## Context

Vittoria came from the **Carobbi** family, whose roots connect Senj, Kotor, and Zara — three key Adriatic port cities of the Habsburg Empire. Her father [Casimiro Carobbi](casimiro-carobbi.md) was born in **Senj** (Italian: Segna), a town on the Croatian Military Border where numerous Italian families had settled from the 15th century onward, drawn by maritime commerce and military service along the Adriatic. Italian noble families in Senj were inscribed in the city's "Golden Book" and received coats of arms; the Carobbi presence fits this pattern of Italian merchant or military families operating along the Habsburg Adriatic coast. Casimiro married [Marianna Billicich](marianna-billicich.md) in **Kotor** (1820) — another Adriatic port — before settling in Zara, where Vittoria was born in 1828.

The marriage of Vittoria and Simeone in **1849** united two of Zara's established Italian civic families — the Boara (landowners and intellectuals, Simeone served as assessore comunale) and the Carobbi. They married at the **Church of Sv. Šime** (Saint Simeon), one of Zadar's most prominent churches, famous for housing the silver sarcophagus of Saint Simeon the Prophet (commissioned 1380 by Queen Elizabeth of Hungary). The union was part of a broader web of intermarriage among Zara's Italian-speaking elite: Boara, Carobbi, [Addobbati](pietro-pio-addobbati.md), Coltelli, and Sandri.

Vittoria survived Simeone by six years (he died 1896; she died 1902) and witnessed the full unfolding of the Boara inheritance drama: Eveline's scandal-provoking marriage to a Croat, the gendered will that cut out the women, the three sons' bequests to the **Lega Nazionale** (the Italian irredentist cultural association founded 1891 in Trieste), and the comfortable final years of Casimiro and Carmela Boara — the last of the line.

**Memoir discrepancy:** Fulvia's 1996 memoir gives dates "(1820–1911)" for "Vittorie Carobbi" — the **1820** is likely Simeone Boara's birth year, and **1911** may be a misremembered death year (GEDCOM gives 1902). The documentary dates (1828–1902) from GEDCOM/Geni/FamilySearch are preferred.

## Family

- Husband: [Simeone Vincenzo Boara](simeone-vincenzo-boara.md).
- Parents: [Casimiro Carobbi](casimiro-carobbi.md); [Marianna Billicich](marianna-billicich.md) (**F31**, marriage **5 May 1820**, **Kotor**).
- Children: **eight** per Fulvia memoir (1996), including [Ottilia Anna Vincenza Boara](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md), **@I62@** (Artemisia), **Eveline** (married a Croat — the scandal that triggered the gendered inheritance will), **Casimiro Boara** (famous for lectures on the classics), and **Carmela Boara** (last of the Boara line). Three unmarried Boara sons left their inheritances to the **Lega Nazionale**, the Italian irredentist cultural association (founded 1891, Trieste; established Italian schools and cultural circles across Habsburg Dalmatia, Istria, and the Littoral). Casimiro and Carmela lived together in comfort; the last Boara land was sold by Casimiro to [Antonio Zerauschek](../people/antonio-zerauschek.md). When Carmela died, she left something to the Addobbati who had assisted her.
