# D. Ivanes Baptista Addobbati

In the long chain that links Bergamo’s notarial benches to Zara’s council lists, **D. Ivanes Baptista Addobbati** appears as a seventeenth-century notary—a *D.* for *don* in the local Catholic idiom, a profession that left traces in civic memory if not always in the scans we have today. Sabalich, leaning on the 1745 Bergamo genealogical testimonial, gives his year as 1630 and his craft in plain Italian: *notajo*.

He stands in the vault as father of D. Daniel in the patriline that eventually sends captains and *cittadini* across the Adriatic; the mother of that union does not surface in the export used here. For a man whose work was witnesses and contracts, the irony is how thin the paper trail can feel four centuries on.
