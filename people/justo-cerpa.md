# Justo Cerpa

**Estimated birth:** ~1770s-1780s  
**Period:** Late colonial Chile → Early republican Chile  
**Region:** Talca, Maule region, central Chile  
**Significance:** Patriarch of documented CERPA lineage, father of at least 3 sons

## Family

- **Spouse:** Rosario Mendez
- **Children:**
  - **Fabián Cerpa** (1808-1888) — father of Francisco Cerpa, continuing main lineage
  - **Bruno Cerpa** (1814-1894) — documented in civil registration  
  - **Ignacio Cerpa Mendez** (1815-1885) — married Francisca Fernandez

## Historical Context

Justo Cerpa lived through the **Chilean independence period** (1810-1818), transitioning from Spanish colonial rule to the Chilean Republic. His children were born during the early republican period, establishing the CERPA family as documented Chilean citizens in the Talca region.

## Evidence

Multiple civil death registrations (1888-1894) consistently identify **Justo Cerpa** as father:
- Fabián Cerpa death 1888 (Q241-CBB4): parents "Justo Cerpa + Rosario Mendez"
- Bruno Cerpa death 1894 (Q241-8DL9): father "Justo Cerpa"  
- Ignacio Cerpa Mendez death 1885 (Q241-CYQS): parents "Justo Cerpa + Rosario Mendez"

This **family network evidence** confirms Justo as the patriarch of a significant CERPA presence in 19th-century Chile.
