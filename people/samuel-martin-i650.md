# Samuel Martin

**Samuel Martin** was a **chimney sweep** working in the **Bexley Heath** area of north-west Kent. He is known from the 1840 baptism record of his son [Peter Martin](peter-martin-i596.md), where he is named as father with occupation "Sweep."

A Samuel Martin (b. ~1805, Woolwich, widower) appears in the **1851 Census** as a lodger at Dog Yard, Woolwich (HO 107/1589, folio 124, p. 21, schedule 87), with children Mary, William, James, Benjamin, John, and George — but not Peter, who at eleven was likely already apprenticed out as a sweep. Samuel is listed without an occupation and as a widower, indicating his wife **[Marianne](marianne-martin-i651.md)** had died by 1851. Woolwich and Bexley are adjacent parishes; Peter gave both as his birthplace across different censuses.

## Family

- Wife: [Marianne Martin](marianne-martin-i651.md) (d. before 1851).
- Son: [Peter Martin](peter-martin-i596.md) (b. 1840, Bexley; chimney sweep).
