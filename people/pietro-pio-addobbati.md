# Pietro Pio Addobbati

Pietro Pio was the father of [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md) and Fulvia's maternal grandfather. He worked as a Habsburg postal official (**I.R. Assistente alla Direzione delle Poste**) in [Zara](../topics/zara-italy-dalmatia.md). Born in **1852**, he died on **8 April 1919**, four years before Fulvia's birth — she never knew him, but his children fill her memoir: a doctor, teachers, a piano teacher, nuns, and postal clerks.

## Life

**5 May 1852 — Zara.** He grew up in a family that took its *cittadini* standing seriously. They first aimed him at the **seminary**; Fulvia passes down the story that he left after putting a rat on a visiting bishop's plate — "considered unsuitable and luckily released (or we wouldn't be here)." After that he became a **lawyer's secretary**, though money was always tight. [Ottilia](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md) borrowed regularly from her **Boara** relations — **Zio Casimiro** and **Zia Carmela** — and sent the sums back through a daughter's hands each month; Casimiro always forgave the debt (Fulvia, 1996).

**12 February 1890 — marriage at the Basilica Metropolitana, Zara.** He married [Ottilia Anna Vincenza Boara](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md) under Monsignor Stefano Franc[avieri?], delegated by **Dr. Marco [S/Z]accini**. Pietro Pio was **37**, listed as **Imperial Assistant at the Directorate of Posts**, Catholic, bachelor; Ottilia was **24**, listed as **civile**. Banns: **19 and 26 January**, **2 February 1890**. Witnesses included **Mariano Rovaro Brizzi** (Lieutenancy accountant, kin to [Geralda Rovero Brizzi](geralda-rovero-brizzi.md)); **[Simeone Addobbati](simeone-gilberto-addobbati.md)**, captain in Vienna; **Francesco Kirschner**, imperial councillor; and **Opaludo** of Zara. A later hand noted in the margin **† 8.4.1919**.

**8 April 1919 — death in Zara**, months after the collapse of the empire he had served his whole career.

The Addobbati, despite their civic standing, broke the gentry convention of not working. Pietro Pio's children all had professions: the eldest son became a doctor (qualified in Vienna); Noemi worked at the Poste & Telegraphs; Else was a piano teacher; Irma and "Bill" were teachers; Villosie (later **Suor Daniela Addobbati**, born Zara 22 November 1903) served 25 years as [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md)'s secretary and administrator before entering religious life at the **Istituto "B.V. Maria"** (the Dame Inglesi / English Ladies) in Rovereto, rising to Sister Superior; she died there on **17 August 1972**; Bice married and settled in Rome. In Zara it was said as a compliment: *un vero Addobbati*. (Fulvia memoir, 1996.)

## Family

- Parents: [Pietro Paolo Addobbati](pietro-paolo-addobbati.md) (1890 register: retired resident accountant, deceased); [Geralda Rovero Brizzi](geralda-rovero-brizzi.md) (pensioner, living).
- Brother: [Simeone Gilberto Addobbati](simeone-gilberto-addobbati.md) — witness in 1890; Habsburg captain in Vienna.
- Wife: [Ottilia Anna Vincenza Boara](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md).
- Children (per Fulvia memoir — "wife and 7 children"; handwritten tree NTree01 names seven daughters plus the eldest son): **Pietro** (doctor; died ~30 in Trieste), **Noemi** (Poste & Telegraphs), [Ester Addobbati](ester-addobbati.md) (m. Antonio Zerauschek), **Irma** (teacher), **Elsa** / Else (piano teacher), **Vittoria** / Villosie (b. Zara 22 Nov 1903; religious name **Suor Daniela**; Istituto "B.V. Maria" / Dame Inglesi, Rovereto; d. Rovereto 17 Aug 1972), **Beatrice** / Bice (m., lived in Rome). Of the sisters, only Bice and Ester married. The handwritten tree (NTree01) also shows a name that reads as **Rosatina** among the daughters.
- The eldest son (**Pietro Addobbati**) served in the Austrian army during WWI; in the Officers' mess he slapped a superior officer and was saved from court martial only by [Simeone Gilberto Addobbati](simeone-gilberto-addobbati.md)'s position and influence. He later practised medicine in Trieste and died within 24 hours of blood poisoning, aged about 30. **DAZD sig. 59 (1915)** preserves a copy of his medical degree, described as belonging to "Pietro Addobbati, nephew of Simeone."
