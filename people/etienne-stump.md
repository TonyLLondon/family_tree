# Étienne Stump

**Swiss** dentist. Persian memoirs and Anglophone reference works often mislabel him **Austrian** or **German** (e.g. Najm-Abadi: Austrian origin with Swiss citizenship; Encyclopaedia Iranica: “Austrian”; Mustawfi: “German” for his dentist) — this vault records **Swiss** as his nationality. In Persian text he most often appears as **دکتر اشتومپ** with spelling swings (**اشتمپ، استومپ، استامپ، استمپ**); first name is sometimes rendered **اتکین** (approximation of *Étienne*). Persian-language scholarship still casts him as a central figure in **early modern dentistry in Tehran** from the late Qajar period through the early reign of Mohammad Reza Shah. He ties the Bottin-Stump line to **elite residential history** (Mahmoudieh) as well as medicine. A convenient synthesis attributed to **iran-newspaper.com** is **[canonical on Beytoote](https://www.beytoote.com/art/negah-gozashte/dentist-atabak2-ahmadshah.html)** (ingested: [corpus extract](../sources/corpus/beytoote-stump-dentist-atabak-ahmadshah/extracted.web.md)); the same copy is widely **syndicated** (e.g. [Sarpoosh](https://www.sarpoosh.com/culture-thought/history-civilization/dentistry-ahmadshah-atbek970805.html), [Fekrshahr](https://fekrshahr.ir/fa/news/48950/%D8%AF%D9%86%D8%AF%D8%A7%D9%86%D8%B3%D8%A7%D8%B2-%D8%A7%D8%AA%D8%A7%D8%A8%DA%A9-%D9%88-%D8%AF%D9%86%D8%AF%D8%A7%D9%86%D9%BE%D8%B2%D8%B4%DA%A9-%D8%A7%D8%AD%D9%85%D8%AF%D8%B4%D8%A7%D9%87)). Annotated English walkthrough: [translation.en.md](../sources/corpus/beytoote-stump-dentist-atabak-ahmadshah/translation.en.md).

## Life

- **Baltic birth and family (primary register digest).** [Europe-only narrative — Thurgau to Tallinn](../stories/stump-thurgau-tallinn-baltic-line.md) · [Estonian Biographical Center report (2005)](../sources/estonian-biographical-center-stump-report-2005.md) quotes **Tallinn Dome** parish: born **7 March 1880** (9 p.m.), Tallinn; baptized **6 April 1880**; godparents include **von Landesen** and **Marie Erbe** née von Landesen. A **1910** Tallinn newspaper obituary for **Louise Stump** (interpreted in the report as Marc’s sister or sister-in-law) lists mourners including **“Dr Etienne Stump and wife in Teheran.”** Older **GEDCOM** exports in this vault sometimes show **3 July 1880** — treat as **transposed day/month** pending image of the grave or parish page.
- **Chicago — dental education and license refusal (1902).** Graduated from the **German-American Dental College** (incorporated 1891 as the "North American-German Dental College", 569 Wells Street, Chicago). On **30 April 1902** the **Illinois State Board of Dental Examiners** refused to license Stump on the ground that the college was not of sufficient standing and repute. Stump petitioned for a writ of **mandamus**; **Judge Chetlain** dismissed the petition on **9 August 1902**, excoriating the college's dean **Dr. Huxmann** for running a sham institution — no faculty meetings, no matriculation books, an 18-month course instead of the required duration, and furnishing exam answers in advance (*Chicago Tribune*, 10 Aug 1902; repr. [*Dental Register* 56:582–583](../sources/dental-register-stump-mandamus-1902.md)). Photo: [Etienne — Chicago 1902](../media/albums/henderson/Etienne%20-%20Chicago%201902%20(1).jpg).
- **Iran — arrival and role (per Najm-Abadi, Beytoote, Elgood).** The Chicago license refusal likely prompted Stump's departure from the United States. Hired in **New York** as personal dentist to **Mirza Ali-Asghar Atabak** (*Amin al-Sultan*) during Atabak’s and **Mehdi-Qoli Hedayat** (*Mokhbar al-Saltaneh*) **1904** American tour; arrived Iran **c. 1904** (late Mozaffar al-Din Shah). Remained after **Atabak’s assassination (1907)**. **Ahmad Shah’s court dentist**; **Cyril Elgood** records him as the **Shah’s dentist** and **first teacher of dentistry** at **Dar al-Funun** (*A Medical History of Persia*, 1951 — purchase lead: [Stump wishlist](../sources/wishlist/stump.md)).
- **Clinic and homes.** Clinic at corner of **Manuchehri and Ferdowsi**; **Dr. Mohsen Sayyah** (c. 1927) adds a large garden **north of Ferdowsi Square** and clinic on **Ala al-Dowleh**. **Seyyed Mohammad Kamareh’i** (1296–1299 SH / 1917–1920) describes a busy practice toward the **end of Lalezar**, near **Moshir al-Dowleh**’s house, with assistant **Monsieur Gol** (later **Dr. Gol**, who ran the office after Stump’s death); Kamareh’i’s diary also mentions **porcelain denture sets** priced about **35 tomans**. Syndicated Persian articles spell his surname **کمره‌ای** or **کمرهای** — treat as the **same MP/diarist** until the printed diary is collated. Residence also associated with a large garden between **Fisherabad and Iranshahr** known by his name; **Sayyah** describes a **villa in old Mahmoudieh** between **Tajrish and Evin**, antiques, and carpets. **Mahmoudieh land chain** (Muhammad Shah’s *Muhammadieh* → **Ala-ud-Dowleh** → **Mahmoud Alamir** / *Ehtesham al-Saltana* → **Sahebdivan** / **Amin al-Zarb** → Stump): after Stump’s death his heirs subdivided the estate into residential lots, creating the modern **Mahmoudieh neighbourhood**; the southwestern corner became the site of the **Royal Tehran Hilton** (1962, now **Hotel Esteghlal**). Sources: [mustawfi-mahmoudieh-stump.md](../sources/mustawfi-mahmoudieh-stump.md), [Hamshahri (2022)](../sources/hamshahri-mahmoudieh-stump.md), and [Mahmoudieh house](../topics/mahmoudieh-house.md).
- **Peers and institutions.** Counted with **Hartioun Stepanian** and **Falak Kush Melczarski** (after **Bertrand Hybenette**, Naser al-Din Shah’s Swedish dentist) among **foreign-trained dentists** who introduced modern methods. **Rivalry with Melczarski** (Reza Shah’s dentist) reportedly kept him off examining boards and out of the founding of the state dental school. With **Stepanian**, submitted a **dental-school proposal** (curriculum and site) to the **Ministry of Education** (document **1334 AH / c. 1916**, National Archives of Iran) — **years before** Melczarski’s school; initiative did not succeed at the time.
- **World War I.** In **1294 SH / 1915**, arrested with the **Ottoman ambassador** and **Austrian military attaché** at **Vardavard** (Karaj–Tehran road), handed to **General Baratov**; sent to **Qazvin**, then **released** and returned to Tehran (Swiss nationality noted in the release account).
- **Public profile.** **Hygiene article** in ***Nasvan*** (**1301 SH / 1922**); **International College of Dentists** Fellow **1308 SH / 1929** (per Yazdi’s history of Iranian dentistry). **Abolhassan Ebtehaj** (student **1295 SH / 1916**) met him through **American** households in Tehran. **Simin Daneshvar’s *Savushun*** uses the idiom of getting **«a proper set of teeth from Dr. Eshtamp»** — evidence of **popular fame** (exact English wording varies by translation).
- **Wealth and lore.** By the **1940s**, very wealthy: **land in north Tehran**, **brewery at Majidieh, Shemiran**; cited in **Majles** debate as owning **one of the first cars** in Iran (claim that only two cars existed in **1912** — Shah’s and Stump’s — repeated as **probably exaggerated** but indicative of reputation). **Mustawfi** calls him **«German»** in one dental anecdote while naming him owner of Mahmoudieh — see [mustawfi-mahmoudieh-stump.md](../sources/mustawfi-mahmoudieh-stump.md).
- **Death and chronology.** Beytoote (citing Najm-Abadi): died **Tehran 1330 SH / 1951**, age **83** (implies birth **c. 1868**). **GEDCOM** had **3 Jul 1880** (superseded for vault narrative by **Tallinn parish 7 Mar 1880** per EBC); **grave marker**: died **1 Nov 1951** (age **71** if **1880** birth year holds). Treat **83** as **secondary-source conflict**, not as established fact.
- **Encyclopaedia Iranica** (*Dentistry*) names him with **Melczarski** as foreigners who **treated and trained** in Iran — [iranica-dentistry.md](../sources/iranica-dentistry.md). Iranica’s chronology can read like a **later** arrival (**c. 1921**); that sits in **open questions** against the **1904** hiring narrative.
