# Dr. Pietro Addobbati

Dr. Pietro Addobbati was Zara’s great juridical personality at the hinge of republic, empire, and kingdom—advocate, judge, protector of prisoners, man of the Extraordinary Civic Congress, and eventually *primo assessore* of the commune. Born in 1769, he studied at Padua; in 1786 Bishop Stratico invested him with the Papal Golden Spur and the title of Lateran Count, honours his niece’s memoir still links to a miniature passed down the generations.

The career Sabalich unfolds across Venetian, Austrian, Napoleonic Italian, and restored Habsburg administrations reads less like a curriculum vitae than a city history told through one desk: criminal advocate for the poor in the 1790s, civic head during the fall of Venice, associate judge under Count Thurn, Nin noble in 1804 beside his brother Ivan Vicko (Vincenzo), councillor and charitable administrator as the decades turned. Enemies praised him in print; Napoleon made him substitute judge; the Metropolitana’s fabric committee knew his signature.

He died in Zara on 4 November 1832. The registers quiet; the briefs and decrees do not—his afterlife is mostly paper, and the memoir’s small gold disk.

## Life

- **1769** — Born Zara (Sabalich p. 506).
- **1786** — Golden Spur and Lateran Count title at Padua (Stratico); family memoir on miniature and hundred-knight tradition.
- **1790** — Doctorate; *Avvocato* on Venetian decrees (Ugljan villagers).
- **1793** — Accademia Economo-Agraria, Venetian Dalmatia.
- **1794** — *Avvocato criminale*; *avvocato dei poveri prigionieri*.
- **1797** — *Capo e Procuratore dell'ordine civico*, Extraordinary Civic Congress.
- **1798** — Habsburg *Giudice assessore del Tribunale di I. istanza*, Zara.
- **24 Jun 1804** — Nin nobility with brother Vincenzo; diploma *D.r Petri et Joanes Vincentis fratres Addobbati q m Aloysium*.
- **1806** — Municipal councillor and *Amministratore aggiunto* (Dandolo).
- **1807** — Kingdom of Italy: *Protetor legale dei carcerati*; first-class patron for Zara district.
- **1810** — Curnis encomium in Venice case.
- **1812** — *Giudice supplente* (Napoleonic).
- **1815** — *Deputazione sanitaria*.
- **1824–1826** — *Primo Assessore* communale; beneficence; cathedral fabric; *Commissione di Ripartizione* (from 1816).
- **4 Nov 1832** — Died Zara.
