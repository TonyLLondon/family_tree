# Vera Obolensky

**Vera**—**Nemtchinova** before the world insisted on **Princess Obolensky**—fled the **1917** storm south to **Tehran**, where she ran what functioned as a **forty-room hotel** on **[Mahmoudieh](../topics/mahmoudieh-house.md)**: European beds, Russian tongues, **[Étienne Stump](etienne-stump.md)**’s land beneath her lease and later his name beside hers in marriage. Her son **Georges**, born **1920** in the same city, would write the memoir that saves the guesthouse from anecdote.

## Life

- **Born** c. 1895, Russian Empire (age 79 at death in Nov 1974; NYT death notice). Maiden name recorded as **Nemtchinova** (per [Ancestry record](https://www.ancestry.com/genealogy/records/georges-obolensky-24-1tby3yk) for son Georges). **Not** the ballerina [Vera Nemtchinova](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vera_Nemtchinova) (1899–1984), a different person.
- **Marriage to Prince Yuri Obolensky** — before or during WWI. The Obolenskys were a prominent Russian princely family. The NYT death notice describes Yuri as "**a relative of Tolstoy**," connecting the Obolensky line to the Tolstoy family.
- **Flight to Persia** — after the 1917 Revolution, Vera and Yuri fled south through the Caucasus to Iran. By **1920** they were living in Tehran.
- **1920** — son **Georges (Yuri) Obolensky** born in Tehran.
- **c. 1925** — Prince Yuri dies (malaria). Vera remains in Tehran as a widow.
- **1920s** — manages a **40+ room guesthouse** on the [Mahmoudieh estate](../topics/mahmoudieh-house.md), catering to European business travelers. Effectively one of Tehran's first foreign-run hotels. Dr. Stump provided the property and local connections; Vera ran operations.
- **Marriage to Dr. Étienne Stump** — date unknown; occurred in Tehran after the guesthouse was established. Vera became Stump's partner and then wife (after his first wife [Henriette Bottin](henriette-bottin.md)).
- **c. 1951** — after Stump's death, Vera leaves Iran for **Frankfurt**, Germany, to join her son and daughter-in-law.
- **1950s–1960s** — settles in **New Jersey**, USA.
- **6 November 1974** — dies in New Jersey. Death notice published in the *New York Times*, **14 November 1974**.

## Family

- First husband: **Prince Yuri Obolensky** (d. c. 1925, Tehran, malaria).
- Second husband: [Étienne Stump](etienne-stump.md).
- Son: **Georges (Yuri) Obolensky** (b. 1920, Tehran).

## Context: Russian émigrés in Iran

Vera was part of the first wave of Russian refugees (White Army officers and nobility) who fled to Iran after the 1917 Revolution and Civil War. Hundreds of thousands of Russians passed through; some settled permanently. The community built the **St. Nicholas Orthodox Church** in Tehran (opened 1945). Academic research by **Marcus James** (ANU) documents a second wave of refugees in the 1930s and their eventual emigration in the 1950s — see ["Exploring the Russian Refugee Diaspora in Iran, 1930–1955"](https://www.peripheralhistories.co.uk/post/exploring-the-russian-refugee-diaspora-in-iran-1930-1955) and ["Mechanics Fleeing Communism"](https://cais.cass.anu.edu.au/research/publications/mechanics-fleeing-communism-russian-refugees-iran-and-their-resettlement) (open-access paper). Also: [Kaveh Farrokh / RT report on St. Nicholas Church](https://www.kavehfarrokh.com/uncategorized/a-view-of-russian-emigres-to-iran/) and Wikipedia [Russians in Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russians_in_Iran).
