# Robert Roche

**Robert Roche** of **Castletown-Roche**, County Cork, was the eldest son of [John Roche](john-roche.md) — the member of the 1641 Catholic Parliament — and husband of [Juliana O'Moore](juliana-omoore.md). He lived through the two great upheavals that destroyed the Old English Catholic gentry of Munster: the **Cromwellian conquest** (1649–1653) and the **Williamite war** (1689–1691).

Burke's 1833:

> His eldest son, **Robert Roche**, espoused **Juliana O'Moore**, daughter of Alexander O'Moore, of Ballina, in the county of Kildare, and was succeeded by his eldest son, Stephen Roche.

## Life

- **~1631** — Born at **Castletown-Roche**, County Cork. His father [John Roche](john-roche.md) was then sitting in the Confederate Parliament at Kilkenny.
- **1649–1653** — The Cromwellian conquest devastated Catholic Cork. Castletown-Roche castle itself was besieged — Lady Roche (of the main Viscount line) defended it for several days in 1649 before it was confiscated. Robert's branch of the family had their estate "injured by composition" — meaning they paid a heavy fine to retain any portion of their lands.
- **~1660s** — Married **[Juliana O'Moore](juliana-omoore.md)**, daughter of **Alexander O'Moore of Ballina, County Kildare**. The O'Moore (Ó Mórdha) family were Gaelic lords of Laois — Alexander's presence in Kildare likely reflects the same pattern of dispossession and relocation that would soon affect the Roches themselves.
- **~1690** — Died in Ireland. His son Stephen would face the final blow — total forfeiture under William III.

## Family

- Wife: [Juliana O'Moore](juliana-omoore.md) (b. ~1636, Ballina, Co. Kildare; d. ~1689).
- Father: [John Roche](john-roche.md) — of Castletown-Roche; member of the 1641 Catholic Parliament.
- Son: [Stephen O'Moore Roche](stephen-omoore-roche.md) ("Dov") — forfeited under William III.
