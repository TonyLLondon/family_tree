# Ester Addobbati

Ester was born into one of the old **Addobbati** households of Italian Dalmatia—the pen-and-ink respectability of the Habsburg post, parish weddings at the **Basilica Metropolitana**, and a family habit of working for a living when the “better” families thought they should not. She married the younger **Antonio Zerauschek**, who would become the public face of interwar Zara: hotels at the centre of the city, cigarette papers and chocolate factories at its industrial edge, a life measured in employment and skylines. Her name is on **Villa Ester** at Sirmione, the refuge on Lake Garda that her husband bought and named for her—a small, stubborn poetry against exile.

She was mother to [Fulvia Ottilia Antonia Zerauschek](fulvia-ottilia-antonia-zerauschek.md) and the other children of that marriage. She died in **Florence** in **September 1971**. Her husband’s obituary in *Difesa Adriatica* (1973), speaking from the Italian exile community, treats her loss as the wound that never closed: Antonio, it suggests, folded something essential when she left him.

## Life

Ester spent her first years in **kingdom-era Zara** (now Zadar, Croatia), anchored to [Pietro Pio Addobbati](pietro-pio-addobbati.md) and [Ottilia Anna Vincenza Boara](ottilia-anna-vincenza-boara.md). On **30 October 1920** she married [Antonio Zerauschek](antonio-zerauschek.md) in the same civic world her parents had documented in ink and wax. The remainder of her life tracks the family’s move from Dalmatian grandeur to Italian refuge—**Florence** as last chapter.
