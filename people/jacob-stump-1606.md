# Jacob Stump (c. 1606)

**Jacob Stump** (baptized 23 October 1606) was the son of [Hanssen Stump](hanssen-stump-1580.md) and [Annae Rued](annae-rued.md) in **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. He married [Barbel Cuntz](barbel-cuntz.md) and became the father of [Augustin Stump](augustin-stump-1628.md) (baptized 1628), representing Generation 15 in the Swiss STUMP lineage and extending the family's documented presence in Sulgen back to the early 1600s.

## Life

**23 Oct 1606** — Christened **Sulgen**, **Thurgau**, **Switzerland**. Son of [Hanssen Stump](hanssen-stump-1580.md) and [Annae Rued](annae-rued.md).

**c. 1625** — Marriage to [Barbel Cuntz](barbel-cuntz.md).

**22 Dec 1628** — Birth of son [Augustin Stump](augustin-stump-1628.md) in Sulgen (Jacob age 22).

**c. 1654** — Died **Sulgen** (probable death record 20 Sep 1654 per FamilySearch 68VT-BJQB).

## Research Notes (2026-04-11)

**Generation 15 Identification:**
Based on chronological analysis, Jacob (baptized 23 Oct 1606) with parents **Hanssen Stump** & **Annae Rued** is most likely the father of Augustin Stump (1628). The alternative Jacob (16 Nov 1606) with parents **Baschli Stump** & **Adelheitt Duesler** is less likely due to Baschli's death in September 1629, just months after Augustin's baptism.

**STUMP Origins Discovery:**
Research uncovered multiple STUMP families established in Sulgen by the 1590s:
- **Blaesi Stumpp** - children 1591-1599 (potentially related to later Blaesi STUMP d. 1629)
- **Augustin Stumpp** - children 1595-1599  
- **Andreas Stumpp** - children 1591
- **Anstett Stumpp** - children 1596-1599

**1629 Epidemic Impact:**
Multiple STUMP family members died in 1629 (Baschli, Blaesi, Ulrich, Steffan, Hanssli), suggesting plague or epidemic affecting the established families.

**Next Generation Research:**
Jacob's parents **Hanssen Stump** and **Annae Rued** represent Generation 16 (c. 1580s), potentially connecting to the late 1500s STUMP families already established in Sulgen.

## Historical Significance

**STUMP Origins in Sulgen:**
Jacob potentially represents the founding generation of the documented STUMP lineage in Sulgen parish. His establishment in the community provided the foundation for over 200 years of continuous family presence through the 1800s.

**Early Modern Swiss Context:**
Living during the early 1600s, Jacob's generation experienced:
- Post-Reformation religious adjustments
- Development of Swiss cantonal independence
